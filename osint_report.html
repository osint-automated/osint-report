Concurrently, U.S. Southern Command Commander Gen. Francis L. Iran conducted live-fire drills in the Strait of Hormuz in collaboration with Russia, while the U.S. moved the aircraft carrier USS Gerald R. Ford closer to the Mediterranean Sea. Furthermore, geopolitical tensions contributed to a rise in oil prices, reflecting market concerns about supply disruptions amid ongoing military developments.   • AFRICOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated significant military and geopolitical developments in Sudan, where a UN fact-finding mission determined that recent actions by Sudanese rebels in el-Fasher bore "hallmarks of genocide." This report followed a campaign of destruction targeting non-Arab communities in the region. Concurrently, at least 37 individuals died due to carbon monoxide poisoning linked to illegal mining operations in Nigeria, prompting government intervention to shut down the site involved. Additionally, reports emerged of a drone strike in Sudan that killed 28 people in a central market, highlighting ongoing violence between the army and paramilitary groups. The situation in Libya remained precarious, with the UN Security Council warning that the country could "explode again" without progress in its stagnant political process.
