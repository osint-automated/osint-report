Open Source Intelligence (OSINT) Report

Date of Access: 07 November 2025

Date of Information: 07 November 2025

Reporting Period: 06 November - 07 November 2025

Distribution: Approved for public release; distribution is unlimited.

Combatant Commands: SOUTHCOM, EUCOM, PACOM, CENTCOM, AFRICOM

Intent: The intent of this report is to provide a high-level overview of significant military and geopolitical developments during the reporting period. The report consists of open-source reporting and updates on evolving conflicts, diplomatic initiatives, and strategic military actions involving countries of interest in the following Combatant Commands: SOUTHCOM, EUCOM, PACOM, CENTCOM, AFRICOM.

Executive Summary: During the reporting period, significant military and geopolitical developments unfolded across multiple regions. In SOUTHCOM, the U.S. government under President Trump contemplated military action against Venezuela to unseat President Maduro but later reported to Congress about the lack of legal justification for strikes. The U.S. military conducted 16 drone strikes in the Eastern Pacific targeting drug trafficking, resulting in multiple casualties. In EUCOM, Poland and Romania enhanced defenses against Russian drone incursions, while Ukrainian forces remained vigilant amid Russian military buildups in Belarus, and ongoing conflict in Pokrovsk raised security concerns. In PACOM, President Marcos Jr. of the Philippines declared a state of emergency following Typhoon Kalmaegi, which caused over 240 fatalities. North Korea intensified tensions by firing artillery rockets as U.S. Defense Secretary Hegseth visited the region, while the Philippines bolstered defense ties with Canada against Chinese assertiveness. CENTCOM reported an escalation of Israeli airstrikes on Hezbollah positions in southern Lebanon, with rising military expenditures by Israel amid ongoing conflicts in Gaza. In AFRICOM, South Africa investigated its citizens’ connections to mercenaries in Ukraine, while Mali faced an economic siege from JNIM, and piracy increased off the Somali coast as the humanitarian crisis in Sudan deepened.

  • SOUTHCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that the United States, under President Donald Trump, considered various military actions against Venezuela to remove President Nicolás Maduro from power, including potential airstrikes and the seizure of oil fields. On 6 November, Trump’s administration informed Congress that it lacked legal justification for immediate strikes. Meanwhile, the Senate prepared to vote on a war powers resolution aimed at preventing such actions. The U.S. military also conducted 16 drone strikes in the Eastern Pacific against alleged drug trafficking operations from September to early November, resulting in multiple casualties. The geopolitical landscape turned tense as Trump’s rhetoric around regime change pointed to a revival of U.S. interventionist policies, prompting bipartisan discussions on the implications of military escalation.

  • EUCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that Poland and Romania initiated the deployment of a new weapons system to enhance their defenses against increasing Russian drone incursions into NATO airspace. The South African government announced an investigation into the circumstances surrounding 17 of its citizens who became trapped in the Donbas region amid the ongoing Russia-Ukraine war. Additionally, Ukrainian forces remained on high alert as the Russian Federation continued to augment its military presence in Belarus with the deployment of Oreshnik ballistic missiles, raising concerns about potential threats to Ukraine's security. In a related note, Russia's defense ministry claimed significant advances by its troops in the city of Pokrovsk, engaging in intense combat against Ukrainian forces.

  • PACOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that Philippine President Ferdinand Marcos Jr. declared a state of emergency on 24 October following Typhoon Kalmaegi, which resulted in at least 241 fatalities and left many missing in central provinces. Concurrently, the storm regained strength and moved toward Vietnam, prompting the evacuation of over 200,000 residents in anticipation of winds exceeding 92 mph (149 km/h). On 4 November, North Korea fired multiple artillery rockets shortly before a visit by U.S. Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth, heightening tensions on the Korean Peninsula. Additionally, the geopolitical landscape saw the Philippines and Canada strengthening defense ties in response to China's assertive regional posture, while Taiwan fortified its military capabilities amid escalating concerns over potential aggression from Beijing.

  • CENTCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that Israeli airstrikes on southern Lebanon escalated, targeting Hezbollah positions after the group violated a ceasefire. Israeli jets attacked three towns in southern Lebanon on 6 November while urging residents to evacuate, marking a continuation of near-daily strikes. The Israel Defense Forces (IDF) confirmed targeted actions against Hezbollah operatives in the Tyre area, focusing on preventing the group's military capabilities from being rebuilt. Concurrently, in Vienna, Austria, authorities discovered a weapons cache linked to Hamas, raising concerns about support for Palestinian militant activities. In Gaza, Israeli forces intensified military actions, with casualties reported in the Bureij refugee camp, amid a fragile ceasefire that has shown signs of strain. Israel's military expenditure rose significantly in response to ongoing conflicts, with reports indicating an allocation of $76.3 billion since October 2023. Additionally, Israeli officials declared an area along the Egypt border a closed military zone to address concerns over weapons smuggling.

  • AFRICOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that South Africa initiated an investigation into how 17 of its citizens became involved with mercenaries in the Russia-Ukraine war. The country's President Cyril Ramaphosa confirmed the situation, with uncertainty surrounding whether these individuals fought for Russia or Ukraine. In Mali, the al-Qaeda-affiliated group JNIM imposed an economic siege on Bamako, raising concerns about the potential collapse of government control in the region. Additionally, suspected Somali pirates attacked a Malta-flagged tanker off the coast of Somalia, continuing a resurgence in maritime piracy. In Sudan, over 80,000 individuals were displaced as the humanitarian crisis deepened following the capture of el-Fasher by the Rapid Support Forces (RSF). Meanwhile, the United States announced the termination of temporary protected status for citizens of South Sudan, citing improved conditions as a reason.
