Executive Summary: During the reporting period, Colombian forces targeted dissident FARC factions in Cauca province on 22 March, neutralizing two militants and seizing arms caches. Venezuelan authorities increased troop deployments to the Colombia border on 23 March due to unverified cross-border incursions, raising bilateral tensions. Brazilian military units conducted joint exercises with Argentine forces on 22 March focused on counter-narcotics operations. In Ukraine, Russian forces advanced in the Sumy region and conducted extensive drone and missile strikes against Ukrainian infrastructure on 22 March, resulting in significant military and civilian casualties, while Ukraine executed counterattacks that prompted Russian redeployments. Taiwan's Ministry of National Defense closely monitored increased Chinese naval activity around the island and celebrated the 30th anniversary of its direct presidential election amid ongoing cross-strait tensions. In the Middle East, Iran launched ballistic missiles at Israel on 22 March, provoking a strong Israeli defense response and escalating threats from both sides. In Africa, Benin proposed municipal police to combat jihadist threats, while Kenya facilitated the return of nationals conscripted into Russian forces. The conflict dynamics in the Democratic Republic of Congo intensified, and reports indicated rising instability risks across the region due to Iranian conflict spillovers affecting local markets.

  • SOUTHCOM: During the reporting period, Colombian forces conducted targeted operations against dissident FARC factions in Cauca province on 22 March, neutralizing two militants and seizing arms caches amid ongoing territorial disputes. Venezuelan authorities deployed additional troops to the Colombia border following unconfirmed reports of cross-border incursions on 23 March, heightening bilateral tensions without escalation to armed confrontation. Brazilian military units executed joint exercises with Argentine counterparts near the Tri-Border Area on 22 March, focusing on counter-narcotics interdiction and intelligence sharing. Mexican naval assets intercepted a suspected smuggling vessel off Guerrero state on 23 March, detaining five individuals linked to cartel networks, while no major diplomatic incidents or coups materialized across Central or South America.

  • EUCOM: During the reporting period, Russian forces advanced in the Sumy region by liberating Potapovka and expanded control over Grishino in the Donetsk People's Republic on 22 March, while Ukrainian forces conducted counterattacks near Oleksandrivka and Hulyaipole, forcing Russian redeployments from Crimea and Moscow Military District reserves. Russian aviation executed 139 drone strikes overnight into 22 March targeting infrastructure in Dnipropetrovsk, Kyiv, Odesa, Donetsk, and Zaporizhia oblasts, followed by missile attacks on energy facilities, port infrastructure in Odesa, and logistics hubs near Kryvyi Rih; Ukrainian air defenses intercepted over 230 drones during a massive Ukrainian raid. Ukrainian drone operators struck Russian oil storage near Primorsk in Leningrad Oblast, a Buk-M1 system in Bryansk Oblast, and military assets in Belgorod and Bashkortostan oblasts. Russian attacks inflicted six civilian fatalities across southeastern Ukrainian regions and claimed 1,170 Ukrainian military casualties in 24 hours. Ukraine and Russia completed a prisoner exchange on 22 March as President Zelenskyy visited frontline positions; a senior NATO delegation arrived in Kyiv to discuss security implications amid intensified drone warfare innovations on the front line.

  • PACOM: During the reporting period, Taiwan's Ministry of National Defense detected seven People's Liberation Army Navy vessels and three official ships operating around Taiwan up until 0600 hours on 23 March, prompting Republic of China Armed Forces to monitor activities and deploy combat air patrols, naval assets, and coastal missile systems in response. On 22 March, the ministry identified six PLAN vessels and one official ship conducting similar operations proximate to Taiwan, with no People's Liberation Army Air Force aircraft incursions recorded during either timeframe. Taiwan marked the 30th anniversary of its inaugural direct presidential election on 23 March, as President Lai Ching-te invoked historical PLA intimidation tactics from 1996 amid ongoing cross-strait tensions. Geopolitical discourse intensified with reports of Taiwan debating defense budget allocations amid U.S.-China strategic frictions, while Chinese Foreign Ministry spokesperson Lin Jian addressed Japanese public criticism of Prime Minister Sanae Takaichi's U.S. visit on 23 March. No significant PLAN or PLAAF activities materialized in the South China Sea, Philippine exclusive economic zone, or proximate to Japanese territories.

  • CENTCOM: During the reporting period, Iran launched two ballistic missile salvos at southern Israel and Tel Aviv on 22 March, inflicting dozens of civilian casualties and striking proximate to the Dimona nuclear complex, while Israeli defenses intercepted most projectiles. The Trump administration issued a 48-hour ultimatum to Tehran on 23 March, demanding reopening of the Strait of Hormuz and threatening precision strikes on Iranian power generation facilities should compliance fail. Tehran countered with explicit threats to assault Israeli electrical grids and Gulf state energy infrastructure supporting United States operations. United States Marine expeditionary units reinforced positions in the Middle East amid heightened alert postures, as Israeli forces sustained air defense operations and monitored Iranian proxy mobilizations by Hezbollah. Gulf monarchies elevated defensive readiness following Iranian warnings, marking the 23rd day of sustained multidomain engagements.

  • AFRICOM: During the reporting period, Benin's ruling party presidential candidate Romuald Wadagni proposed establishing municipal police forces in northern border towns to counter jihadist incursions from Sahel-based groups, integrating local youth into community defense structures amid escalating asymmetric threats. Kenyan authorities granted amnesty to approximately 252 nationals illegally conscripted into Russian forces for the Ukraine conflict, facilitating their repatriation without prosecution under foreign enlistment laws. In eastern Democratic Republic of Congo, Kinshasa-aligned coalition forces executed intensified kamikaze drone strikes against M23 rebel positions and civilian areas in Gakenke, Kalingi, and Minembwe center during the night of 21-22 March, prompting M23 condemnations of indiscriminate civilian targeting. Sudan's army denied responsibility for a drone strike on El-Daein Teaching Hospital that killed 64 individuals including 13 children, rendering the facility nonfunctional as reported by WHO surveillance. Alliance of Sahel States members—Mali, Niger, Burkina Faso—procured advanced military vehicles via non-debt financing to bolster self-reliant defense capabilities. Crisis Group highlighted Chad's accusations against Rapid Support Forces for cross-border drone operations, while Iranian conflict spillovers heightened instability risks across African fuel markets and economies. Uganda's People's Defense Forces initiated nationwide veteran verification exercises per presidential directive.
