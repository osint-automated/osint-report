Intent: The intent of this report is to provide a high-level overview of significant military and geopolitical developments during the reporting period. The report consists of open-source reporting and updates on evolving conflicts, diplomatic initiatives, and strategic military actions involving countries of interest in the following Combatant Commands: SOUTHCOM, EUCOM, PACOM, CENTCOM, AFRICOM.

Executive Summary: During the reporting period, Russian-flagged oil tanker Anatoly Kolodkin docked at Matanzas port in Cuba on 31 March, delivering 730,000 barrels of crude oil, marking the first significant shipment since the United States imposed an energy blockade in January. Mexican President Claudia Sheinbaum asserted Mexico's right to supply fuel to Cuba for humanitarian or commercial reasons. Ukrainian forces conducted long-range drone strikes, damaging key Russian military assets and significantly reducing crude oil flows, while Russian forces launched extensive drone operations causing infrastructure damage in Ukraine. In the South China Sea, the People's Liberation Army Navy engaged in confrontational maneuvers with the Philippine Navy, highlighting increasing tensions driven by territorial disputes. US forces targeted military sites in Iran with heavy munitions, prompting missile retaliation that injured civilians in Israel. Additionally, Iranian and regional responses escalated, with operations against US bases and retaliation to Israeli air strikes. In Africa, coordinated offensives by Congolese and allied forces targeted positions of the Alliance Fleuve Congo, while the Somali National Army captured a key city amid a leadership change, reflecting ongoing instability in the region.

  • SOUTHCOM: During the reporting period, Russian-flagged oil tanker Anatoly Kolodkin docked at Matanzas port in Cuba on 31 March, delivering 730000 barrels of crude oil as the first major shipment since United States authorities imposed an energy blockade in January, while United States forces permitted passage despite sanctions to avert humanitarian crisis escalation. Mexican President Claudia Sheinbaum asserted Mexico's sovereign prerogative to dispatch fuel supplies to Cuba for humanitarian or commercial purposes on 31 March. Colombian military personnel executed a rescue operation in the rainforest, recovering six siblings who evaded capture by FARC dissidents. Venezuelan navy personnel initiated 72-hour sea trials of diesel-electric submarine B-46 from Puerto Cabello with Cuban technicians embarked at 0500 local time on 31 March, prompting United States Southern Command to monitor via carrier strike group positioned 650 nautical miles northeast of Caracas, Colombia to launch K-8 surveillance sorties over Gulf of Venezuela waters, and Panama to deploy three coastal patrol cutters along La Guaira-Margarita corridor; security forces reported no hostile contacts.

  • EUCOM: During the reporting period, Ukrainian forces conducted long-range drone strikes that damaged a 50,000-ton oil tank at Ust-Luga port in Leningrad Oblast and targeted Russian military assets in occupied Luhansk Oblast, including Buk-M3 and Tor air defense systems, power substations, and the Alchevsk Metallurgical Plant, while additional strikes hit oil terminals at Ust-Luga and Primorsk for the sixth consecutive night, reducing Russian crude oil flows by 1.75 million barrels per day and oil income by over one billion dollars; Ukrainian medium-range weapons destroyed four Tornado-Smerch multiple launch rocket systems in occupied Crimea. Russian forces launched 289 drones and one missile overnight from 30 to 31 March, with Ukrainian defenses downing 267 drones that struck infrastructure in Sumy, Odesa, and Poltava oblasts, causing power outages across multiple regions; Russian air defenses intercepted 255 Ukrainian unmanned aerial vehicles and six guided aerial bombs. Ukrainian forces advanced in northeastern Petropavlivka east of Kupyansk, western Udachne southwest of Pokrovsk, near Slovyansk, and in the Kostyantynivka-Druzhkivka area, while Russian forces advanced in southeastern Kostyantynivka, eastern outskirts of Kostyantynivka, near Hulyaipole, and western Zaporizhia Oblast, alongside claims of capturing Novoosynove and Luhivske, inflicting losses on Ukrainian units in Sumy, Kharkiv, Donetsk, and Zaporizhia regions. A Russian An-26 military transport plane crashed in Crimea on 1 April, killing 29 personnel. Geopolitically, Russian President Vladimir Putin demanded Ukrainian withdrawal from remaining Donetsk Oblast territory within two months; Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelenskyy characterized this as creating false urgency; Russia's Central Election Commission asserted it would facilitate voting by Ukrainians in Russia for Ukrainian elections to undermine Kyiv's sovereignty; Ryazan Oblast Governor Pavel Malkov decreed forced employee selection by businesses for Ministry of Defense contracts; Russia's Higher Education Ministry directed universities to send two percent of students to military service; and Russia warned of retaliation if Ukraine utilized foreign airspace for Baltic port attacks.

  • PACOM: During the reporting period, People's Liberation Army Navy guided-missile frigate Jingzhou executed a close-range pass against Philippine tank landing ship BRP Benguet near Thitu Island in the South China Sea on 31 March, prompting mutual accusations of dangerous maneuvers from Beijing's Southern Theater Command and Manila's Western Command. PLAN deployed newly commissioned vessels to the South China Sea for combat-oriented drills encompassing defensive maneuvers, counter-attacks, weapons firing, and emergency response training to integrate new crews with advanced systems. Chinese military authorities issued statements rebutting Philippine claims of fire-control radar illumination by PLAN corvette Guang'an near Sabina Shoal earlier in March while highlighting multiple Philippine aircraft intrusions into Chinese-claimed airspace over Huangyan Dao and Meiji Jiao on 18 March and 20 March. PLA Daily published analysis asserting Japan possessed sufficient plutonium stocks for 5,500 nuclear warheads amid heightened tensions. Taiwan's leadership reacted to a U.S. intelligence report detailing People's Republic of China intentions toward the island, while Philippine senators urged diplomatic protests against observed increases in Chinese vessel presence in the West Philippine Sea.

  • CENTCOM: During the reporting period, US forces deployed 2000-pound bunker-buster munitions against ammunition depots and facilities in Isfahan on 31 March, while President Trump disseminated uncaptioned video footage of the explosions via Truth Social; Israeli Air Force aircraft struck hundreds of targets across Iran, including weapons production sites, command centers, security headquarters, pharmaceutical facilities, and steel plants in Isfahan and Farokhshar. Iran responded with missile barrages targeting central Israel, injuring eight civilians lightly in Bnei Brak, Ramat Gan, and Petah Tikva, and damaging a storage tank at Haifa's Bazan refinery; IRGC forces launched the 87th wave of Operation True Promise 4 against US bases in Bahrain, UAE, and Saudi Arabia, struck the Kuwaiti oil tanker Al-Salmi in Dubai port, and executed UAV attacks on Dubai port, Sharjah telecommunications infrastructure, and Al-Kharj residential areas, while explosions rocked Tehran, Isfahan, and Zanjan from retaliatory strikes. Hezbollah operatives killed four IDF soldiers in southern Lebanon via anti-tank missile on 31 March, prompting IDF forces to eliminate dozens of Hezbollah fighters and seize weapon stockpiles there over the prior day; Yemen's Houthis fired a ballistic missile at southern Israel, drones targeted Kuwait airport, and two UN peacekeepers died in southern Lebanon amid Israeli strikes near An-Najariyah and Tebnine. Geopolitically, Secretary of Defense Pete Hegseth reported the lowest Iranian missile and drone launches in 24 hours alongside destruction of a command bunker; Israel aligned operations with Trump's forthcoming decisions on escalation or cessation; Trump announced potential US withdrawal from the conflict in two to three weeks without Hormuz reopening; Iranian Foreign Minister Abbas Araghchi confirmed message exchanges with the US but rejected negotiations, affirmed Strait of Hormuz accessibility for non-belligerents, and dismissed fears of US ground invasion; UK forces extended Typhoon jet deployments to Qatar and dispatched Sky Sabre air defenses to Saudi Arabia.

  • AFRICOM: During the reporting period, Congolese Armed Forces alongside FDLR, Wazalendo militias, mercenaries, and Burundian National Defense Forces launched coordinated offensives against Alliance Fleuve Congo/March 23 Movement positions across eastern Democratic Republic of Congo on 31 March, deploying heavy artillery and combat drones that targeted villages including Rugezi, Kalingi, Gakenke, Bidegu in Minembwe axis, Lubimbishi in Kalehe axis, and Kabesebese, Mutondi in Lubero axis. Somali National Army captured a key city in Southwest State on 31 March as the state leader resigned amid the operation.
