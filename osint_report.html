  • SOUTHCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that Ecuadorian voters rejected a referendum proposal aimed at permitting foreign military bases in the country, impacting President Daniel Noboa's influence and U.S. regional ambitions. The political landscape in Chile shifted as the country prepared for a presidential runoff in December between leftist candidate Jeanette Jara and far-right contender José Antonio Kast, following an inconclusive first-round vote. In military developments, the USS Gerald R. Ford, the largest U.S. aircraft carrier, entered the Caribbean Sea on 16 November to support anti-narcotics operations, leading to heightened tensions with Venezuela. President Donald Trump hinted at potential discussions with Venezuelan President Nicolás Maduro as the U.S. designated the Cartel de los Soles, allegedly led by Maduro, as a Foreign Terrorist Organization. This significant escalation in U.S. military presence and rhetoric marked an increase in pressure on Venezuela amid ongoing geopolitical developments in the region.

  • EUCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated a rise in military and geopolitical activity amid the ongoing Russia-Ukraine conflict. Mysterious drones were spotted at night near airports across Western Europe, raising concerns about potential hybrid warfare tactics. In Ukraine, the 93rd Separate Rifle Brigade reported that its ground robots assisted drone teams in identifying Russian convoys during dense fog conditions. Former U.S. Secretary of State Mike Pompeo joined the advisory board of Fire Point, a leading Ukrainian defense company focused on developing long-range drones amid ongoing corruption investigations. Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelenskyy visited Paris to secure additional air defense assistance and discussed energy deals with Greece. In related developments, Russia deployed over 10,000 North Korean soldiers to support its efforts in Ukraine, incurring significant casualties. Additionally, Russian strikes in the Kharkiv region resulted in three civilian fatalities. These events occurred against the backdrop of escalating international tensions, with President Trump expressing support for sanctions against countries conducting business with Russia.

  • PACOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that a formation of China Coast Guard ships sailed through the waters of the Japan-administered Senkaku Islands on 16 November, as part of a "rights enforcement patrol," heightening tensions between Beijing and Tokyo. Additionally, regional powers, including actors in Sudan, utilized "good enough" drones for geopolitical leverage, demonstrating the evolving nature of military capabilities in contemporary conflicts. Concurrently, Asian markets faced challenges due to concerns over central bank policies and the potential for an economic bubble, impacting investor sentiment across the region.

  • CENTCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that various military and geopolitical developments unfolded across the globe. Emirates Airlines announced an order of 65 Boeing 777-9 aircraft during the Dubai Air Show, reflecting ongoing aviation investments. In Israel, the Israeli Defense Forces (IDF) targeted and eliminated a Hezbollah terrorist in Al-Mansouri, South Lebanon. U.S. President Donald Trump endorsed a bill to impose severe sanctions on countries cooperating with Russia, reiterating a tough stance against foreign alliances with Moscow. As the UN Security Council prepared to vote on a U.S.-drafted resolution to deploy an international force in Gaza, Israeli leaders expressed strong opposition to a Palestinian state ahead of the vote. Meanwhile, reports from Europe revealed mysterious drone sightings near airports and military installations, raising concerns about potential hybrid warfare activities. The Iranian government continued grappling with a severe drought, declaring it an existential crisis, while asserting that no undeclared nuclear enrichment activities existed within its facilities.

  • AFRICOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated notable shifts in military and geopolitical dynamics. The United States displayed a changing foreign policy approach toward Africa, reflecting a broader trend of multipolarity, moving away from a unipolar influence. Regional powers in Sudan leveraged 'good enough' drones as geopolitical tools amid ongoing conflicts. U.S. President Donald Trump mentioned a willingness to engage in discussions with Venezuelan President Nicolás Maduro, as the U.S. positioned its most advanced aircraft carrier in the Caribbean. In Ukraine, Russian forces neared the capture of Pokrovsk, which could threaten national unity in Ukraine. Meanwhile, the U.S. Ambassador to the United Nations, Mike Waltz, praised Nicki Minaj for highlighting the persecution of Christians in Nigeria, further intertwining military and humanitarian narratives in U.S. diplomacy.
