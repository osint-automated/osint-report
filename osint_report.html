Open Source Intelligence (OSINT) Report

Date of Access: 04 December 2025

Date of Information: 04 December 2025

Reporting Period: 03 December - 04 December 2025

Distribution: Approved for public release; distribution is unlimited.

Combatant Commands: SOUTHCOM, EUCOM, PACOM, CENTCOM, AFRICOM

Intent: The intent of this report is to provide a high-level overview of significant military and geopolitical developments during the reporting period. The report consists of open-source reporting and updates on evolving conflicts, diplomatic initiatives, and strategic military actions involving countries of interest in the following Combatant Commands: SOUTHCOM, EUCOM, PACOM, CENTCOM, AFRICOM.

Executive Summary: During the reporting period, significant military and geopolitical developments emerged across multiple regions. The U.S. administration suspended immigration applications for citizens from 19 countries, citing national security, which left many in legal uncertainty. In Venezuela, President Maduro rejected U.S. pressure, while the Trump administration planned airstrikes against regional drug traffickers. Ukrainian officials accused Russia of pretending to seek peace amid ongoing tensions, as NATO committed to continued military support for Ukraine. In the South China Sea, China's military presence increased with 19 warships spotted, prompting concerns from the Philippines, while Japan advanced its naval capabilities. In Israel, a ceasefire led to the reopening of the Rafah border for Palestinians to exit Gaza, yet violence persisted with over 350 Palestinian deaths reported. Lastly, instability grew in West Africa following a coup in Guinea-Bissau, reflecting broader regional conflicts affecting governance and security.

  • SOUTHCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated significant military and geopolitical developments involving the United States and Latin America. The Trump administration canceled citizenship ceremonies for migrants from 19 countries affected by travel bans, citing national security concerns. In a related move, it halted all immigration applications for these nations, including Afghanistan, Iran, and Somalia, leaving thousands in legal limbo. Tensions escalated in Venezuela, where President Nicolás Maduro rejected US military and political pressure while relying on support from Russia and China. The administration announced plans for airstrikes on drug traffickers in the Caribbean, asserting any nation involved in drug trafficking could face military action. Concurrently, allegations of war crimes surfaced against Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth regarding strikes on suspected drug boats, heightening scrutiny of the US’s military operations in the region. Furthermore, Colombia's President Gustavo Petro accused the US of murder following the death of a fisherman in a US maritime strike, leading to legal complaints against US officials.

  • EUCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that Ukraine and its European allies accused Russian President Vladimir Putin of feigning interest in peace following five hours of talks with U.S. envoys at the Kremlin. Belgium rejected a proposal to utilize frozen Russian assets to support Ukraine’s economy, citing risks involved. In diplomatic developments, French President Emmanuel Macron initiated a three-day state visit to China to discuss trade ties and ongoing hostilities in Ukraine. Meanwhile, Putin's visit to India highlighted the country’s increasing imports of discounted Russian oil amid U.S. sanctions. Ukrainian officials prepared to regroup with European and American allies in Brussels. NATO Secretary-General Mark Rutte confirmed that the alliance would persist in procuring weapons for Ukraine, notwithstanding U.S. peace efforts. Lastly, Russia's recent announcement of a complete capture of the city of Pokrovsk in eastern Ukraine underscored escalating military actions in the region.

  • PACOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated several significant military and geopolitical developments. In the South China Sea, China's legal maneuvers were reshaping regional dynamics and heightening global business risks. Concurrently, 19 Chinese warships were spotted in the West Philippine Sea, raising concerns in the Philippine Navy. The Chinese company CNOOC commenced production at its Weizhou 11-4 project, targeting a plateau output of approximately 16,900 barrels of oil equivalent per day by 2026. In Venezuela, a U.S. court mandated the forced sale of Citgo to settle debts, which the Venezuelan government criticized as part of a broader U.S. military buildup against it. Malaysia, over a decade after the disappearance of Flight MH370, announced plans to resume its search operations on 30 December in a targeted area of the Indian Ocean. Additionally, Japan's Prime Minister Sanae Takaichi appeared to challenge China's territorial claims by approaching sensitive issues related to Taiwan. Meanwhile, India advanced its military capabilities, with the third nuclear submarine undergoing sea trials, and preparations for acquiring six stealth submarines under Project 75 India neared completion.

  • CENTCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that Israel announced its intention to allow Palestinians to leave Gaza through the Rafah border crossing, reopening it as part of a U.S.-backed ceasefire agreement. Israeli authorities indicated that this crossing would open in the coming days, but only for Palestinians wishing to exit Gaza. Meanwhile, the United Nations General Assembly passed resolutions urging Israel to withdraw from the Golan Heights, which Israel's ambassador to the UN described as a reflection of the assembly's detachment from reality. In parallel, Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu pressed for a security buffer zone extending into Syria, an initiative met with firm rejection from Lebanese officials. Amid ongoing hostilities, Israeli forces reportedly killed over 350 Palestinians during the last 50 days of ceasefire in Gaza. In humanitarian terms, Hamas criticized Israel's restrictions on aid entering Gaza, while investigations uncovered Hamas's infiltration of NGOs operating in the region.

  • AFRICOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that the Trump administration paused all immigration applications for nationals from 19 countries, citing national security and public safety concerns. This action affected green card and citizenship processing for individuals from Afghanistan, Iran, Libya, Somalia, Sudan, Yemen, and others, aligning with previous travel bans. In geopolitical developments, the United Nations General Assembly called on Israel to withdraw from the Golan Heights, which Israeli officials dismissed as detached from reality. In West Africa, a coup in Guinea-Bissau highlighted the region's ongoing instability, as further conflicts in the Sahel drove herders to flee to coastal cities. In Sudan, the Rapid Support Forces faced criticism for lacking a coherent governance strategy while seeking new funding sources to support their operations amid worsening conditions.
