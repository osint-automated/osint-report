Date of Access: 05 December 2025

Date of Information: 05 December 2025

Reporting Period: 04 December - 05 December 2025

Distribution: Approved for public release; distribution is unlimited.

Combatant Commands: SOUTHCOM, EUCOM, PACOM, CENTCOM, AFRICOM

Intent: The intent of this report is to provide a high-level overview of significant military and geopolitical developments during the reporting period. The report consists of open-source reporting and updates on evolving conflicts, diplomatic initiatives, and strategic military actions involving countries of interest in the following Combatant Commands: SOUTHCOM, EUCOM, PACOM, CENTCOM, AFRICOM.

Executive Summary: During the reporting period, significant military and geopolitical developments unfolded globally. In South America, the U.S. faced allegations of extrajudicial killings following a military strike in Colombia, while escalating tensions with Venezuela prompted an increased military presence in the Caribbean, characterized by the deployment of warships and troops. In Europe, Russian forces faced economic strain and internal criticism regarding the Ukraine invasion, with threats of severe consequences for EU actions on Russian assets. NATO leaders emphasized financial support for Ukraine amid persistent threats from Putin. In Asia, Japan prepared for broader defense operations as China demonstrated military strength in East Asian waters. Concurrently, Iranian military readiness was bolstered by upcoming naval exercises, and the U.S. prepared ground strikes in Venezuela against drug traffickers. In the Middle East, Israeli airstrikes escalated regional tensions amid humanitarian crises in Gaza. In Africa, diplomatic efforts were made to foster investment amid escalating violence linked to terror groups.

  • SOUTHCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that the family of a Colombian fisherman killed in a U.S. military strike at sea filed a human rights complaint arguing the incident constituted an extrajudicial killing. Additionally, U.S. military drone interceptors engaged Russian forces in the skies above Ukraine, escalating conflict in the region. In Venezuela, President Nicolás Maduro confirmed a "cordial" phone call with U.S. President Donald Trump, amid heightened U.S. military presence in the Caribbean, where Trump deployed 11 warships and 15,000 troops under the guise of a counter-narcotics operation. The U.S. Treasury sanctioned affiliates of the Venezuelan terrorist organization Tren de Aragua, while bipartisan efforts in Congress sought to restrict Trump's military actions in Venezuela without congressional approval. Furthermore, Russia began evacuating its citizens from Venezuela, indicating a preparation for potential instability stemming from increased U.S. pressures.

  • EUCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that Col. Gen. Vladimir Chirkin criticized the Russian leadership for its unpreparedness regarding the invasion of Ukraine, stating that they were misled about local support. Economically, Russia faced a significant decline in oil export revenues, dropping 50% this year, thereby impacting its funding for the war against Ukraine. In geopolitical responses, Russia threatened severe reactions to any illegal European Union actions concerning its frozen assets. President Vladimir Putin ruled out rejoining the G7 during a visit to India on 24 October, amidst discussions to boost trade and pressure to resolve the Ukraine conflict. Meanwhile, France and China pledged increased cooperation on global issues, including the Ukraine crisis, as NATO Secretary-General Mark Rutte advocated for substantial financial support for Ukraine's military. As tensions escalated, Putin reaffirmed intentions to seize Ukraine's Donbas region by force if necessary, dismissing peace talks that lacked substantial progress.

  • PACOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated significant military and geopolitical developments. Japan's Prime Minister Sanae Takaichi asserted the need for Japan to prepare for defense operations beyond its borders while navigating heightened tensions stemming from the Trump administration's recent foreign policy maneuvers regarding Ukraine and China. China showcased its military presence by deploying over 100 naval and coast guard vessels across East Asian waters, marking a substantial demonstration of maritime strength. In the Persian Gulf, the Islamic Revolution Guards Corps (IRGC) Navy announced plans for a two-day military exercise, emphasizing Iran's regional military readiness. In the Caribbean, the United States, under President Donald Trump, prepared to initiate ground strikes against Venezuelan drug traffickers, raising concerns about escalating conflict in the region. Turkey's energy minister expressed urgency in protecting oil and gas supplies in the Black Sea following recent attacks on Russian tankers, highlighting the heightened vulnerability of energy security in the area. Furthermore, India finalized a $2 billion leasing deal for a nuclear submarine from Russia, enhancing its naval capabilities as President Vladimir Putin visited New Delhi.

  • CENTCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated significant military and geopolitical developments in the Middle East. On 9 December, organizers of the Eurovision Song Contest convened in Geneva, Switzerland, to discuss Israel's future participation amid calls for a boycott over its military actions in Gaza. Concurrently, Israeli air strikes on multiple towns in southern Lebanon escalated, with one strike causing the deaths of five Palestinians at the al-Mawasi camp in Khan Younis, Gaza. The UN Security Council sent a delegation to Syria for the first time since President Bashar al-Assad’s fall, aiming to enhance diplomatic relations. Amid ongoing regional tensions, Israel confirmed the identification of the remains of a Thai national among the last hostages returned, while reports highlighted the socio-economic devastation facing Gaza, exacerbated by the blockade and military operations. Clashes also erupted between Yemeni army forces and the Southern Transitional Council in the Hadramout governorate as instability persisted.

  • AFRICOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated significant developments in U.S. military and geopolitical affairs. President Donald Trump had his name added to the United States Institute of Peace building in Washington, D.C., coinciding with a scheduled signing of a peace deal between leaders of the Democratic Republic of Congo and Rwanda, which aimed to enhance American investments in the region. Meanwhile, the Trump administration enacted a pause on immigration applications from 19 non-European countries, citing national security concerns. Concurrently, U.S. Secretary of State Marco Rubio announced that Trump would involve himself in efforts to negotiate peace concerning the ongoing civil war in Sudan. In Africa, the UN warned of escalating violence in Mozambique, driven by Al-Shabaab militants, while Nigeria's National Security Adviser Nuhu Ribadu emphasized the need to protect Nigeria's democracy from terrorism and internal disunity.
