Intent: The intent of this report is to provide a high-level overview of significant military and geopolitical developments during the reporting period. The report consists of open-source reporting and updates on evolving conflicts, diplomatic initiatives, and strategic military actions involving countries of interest in the following Combatant Commands: SOUTHCOM, EUCOM, PACOM, CENTCOM, AFRICOM.

Executive Summary: During the reporting period, significant military and geopolitical updates emerged. In South America, the abduction of Nancy Guthrie was linked to cartel activities, while U.S. border law enforcement responded to the detection of illegal crossings at the Rio Grande. In Europe, Kenya’s intelligence agency reported over 1,000 citizens may have been misled to fight for Russia in Ukraine, while tensions rose over Slovakia's threats to cut electricity supplies to Ukraine and Moscow’s warnings to South Korea regarding arms support. In the Middle East, U.S. military presence expanded amidst Israeli airstrikes in Lebanon that killed Hezbollah members, heightening regional tensions. In the Pacific, trilateral maritime activities off the Philippines underscored military cooperation, and Indonesia finalized a trade agreement with the U.S. In Africa, violence in Nigeria resulted in over 50 casualties, while Kenya faced recruitment issues linked to Russia.

  • SOUTHCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated ongoing military and geopolitical developments. On 23 February, the Mexican military killed Nemesio Ruben Oseguera Cervantes, known as El Mencho, the leader of the Jalisco New Generation Cartel, in a military operation near Guadalajara with support from U.S. intelligence and Navy SEAL training. The operation triggered widespread cartel violence across western Mexico, prompting the U.S. State Department and other governments to issue shelter-in-place orders for citizens in affected areas including Puerto Vallarta, where tourists became trapped as narco gangs set fires and engaged in street battles. Seperately, a private investigator in California suggested that the abduction of Nancy Guthrie might be linked to a cartel-operated money-making venture, asserting she was likely taken north of Tucson, Arizona.

  • EUCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that Kenya's intelligence service warned over 1,000 Kenyans may have been recruited under false pretenses to fight for Russia in Ukraine. Former UK Prime Minister Boris Johnson called on allies to deploy noncombat troops to Ukraine to demonstrate commitment against Russian aggression. Slovakia threatened to cut emergency electricity supplies to Ukraine over disputes regarding halted Russian oil transit, which has caused tensions between Slovakia, Hungary, and Ukraine. Moscow issued warnings to South Korea regarding potential setbacks in relations if it provided arms to Ukraine amid NATO discussions. Concurrently, Ukraine's President Volodymyr Zelensky reported both Russia and the U.S. urged Ukraine to withdraw from the Donbass region. Russian artillery attacks continued in Kharkiv, resulting in civilian casualties, while Ukraine targeted a missile factory deep within Russian territory. NATO and the U.S. engaged in discussions to enhance defense capabilities within Europe, amidst ongoing complex geopolitical dynamics.

  • PACOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that three partner nations conducted a multilateral maritime cooperative activity off the west coast of Luzon, Philippines, demonstrating military collaboration. The upcoming negotiations regarding the South China Sea code of conduct were scheduled for the last week of February in Singapore, highlighting ongoing diplomatic efforts in the region. A Philippine senator, during a visit to Thitu Island, emphasized the need to strengthen defense relations and counter China's territorial claims in the South China Sea. Additionally, the U.S. military reported multiple strikes against vessels in the eastern Pacific Ocean, allegedly involved in drug trafficking, resulting in the deaths of three individuals. In geopolitical developments, Indonesia and the United States finalized the Agreement on Reciprocal Trade, facilitating tariff exemptions for numerous Indonesian products. Furthermore, a British nuclear-powered attack submarine arrived in Perth, Australia, to participate in joint military exercises under the AUKUS framework.

  • CENTCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated a significant buildup of U.S. military presence in the Middle East as President Donald Trump considered potential actions against Iran. This military expansion coincided with intense Israeli airstrikes in eastern Lebanon, which killed at least 10 Hezbollah members, including senior officials, and marked some of the deadliest attacks in recent weeks. The strikes drew condemnation from Arab nations and amplified regional tensions, particularly in response to remarks by U.S. Ambassador to Israel Mike Huckabee, which asserted Israel's historical claim to much of the Middle East. In Iraq, Iranian officials reported a collapse of their currency, prompting citizens to shift assets to bitcoin amid economic turmoil. Notably, Hezbollah acknowledged the need for resistance following Israeli attacks, while former U.K. Prime Minister Boris Johnson urged allies to dispatch noncombat troops to Ukraine in anticipation of a ceasefire amidst ongoing conflict.

  • AFRICOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that Kenya's intelligence service warned that over 1,000 citizens may have been recruited to fight for Russia in Ukraine under false pretenses. In Nigeria, a lawmaker reported that gunmen on motorcycles killed at least 50 individuals in northwest Nigeria, with a list of missing persons still being compiled. Meanwhile, South Africa experienced improvements in its economic outlook as S&P Global Ratings upgraded its sovereign credit rating by one notch to double B, elevating investor confidence. Additionally, the UN Emergency Relief Coordinator visited South Sudan to assess one of the world's most under-reported humanitarian crises, where ongoing clashes between government and opposition forces persisted in Jonglei State.
