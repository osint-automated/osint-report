Heightened tensions between the U.S. and Venezuela led to increased military operations, including U.S. strikes in the Caribbean that resulted in multiple deaths related to drug trafficking concerns. The U.S. urged citizens to evacuate Venezuela, signaling deteriorating relations, while Colombian leadership warned against violations of sovereignty amid potential U.S. actions. U.S. Central Command adjusted its strategy in the Middle East, deploying new drone units and reinforcing U.S.-brokered ceasefire violations in Lebanon and Gaza amidst rising casualties.

U.S. military strikes in the Caribbean resulted in the deaths of four individuals related to alleged drug trafficking, corroborating concerns raised by lawmakers about the implications of such actions, including strikes on "shipwrecked sailors" (4 December). The U.S. Southern Command announced these operations amidst a growing scrutiny of the Trump administration's approach toward Venezuela, characterized by a new national security strategy that emphasized regional dominance (5 December). Concurrently, the U.S. State Department urged American citizens to evacuate Venezuela, signaling deteriorating relations (date not specified). Amidst the backdrop of a potential U.S. military strike on Caracas, Venezuelan citizens expressed anxiety over the escalating situation (25 November).   • EUCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that Russia implemented a ban on Snapchat and Apple's FaceTime, citing their use in organizing terrorist activities. Concurrently, the Russian Defense Ministry announced that its forces took control of Bezymyannoye settlement in the Donetsk People's Republic. German President Frank-Walter Steinmeier visited Coventry Cathedral in England to commemorate WWII atrocities, reinforcing Europe's collective stance against Russia. Meanwhile, Russian President Vladimir Putin pledged uninterrupted fuel supplies to India during a summit, signaling continued partnerships amid ongoing U.S. sanctions related to the Ukraine conflict. Ukrainian officials condemned diver Sofiia Lyskun's switch of allegiance to Russia, stripping her of her medals as tensions between nations escalated. Defense experts pointed out that the reliance on AI in military applications remains a challenge for Ukrainian forces, as conditions in the warzone evolved.

  • PACOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated significant military and geopolitical developments across Asia and Europe. The Philippines announced enhancements to its naval capabilities with the acquisition of two new frigates featuring vertical launch systems for missiles. In Australia, the defence minister prepared for a visit to Japan to engage with the recently inaugurated administration of Prime Minister Sanae Takaichi, signaling enhanced diplomatic and military cooperation. Taiwan reported that China had deployed warships as part of extensive military operations across the Yellow Sea and South China Sea, raising security concerns in the region. China also showcased a substantial naval presence, deploying over 100 vessels in a show of force, which prompted tensions, particularly with Japan and Taiwan. In response, the United States committed to collaborating with Indo-Pacific partners to uphold stability in the Taiwan Strait. Additionally, NATO announced its plans to revise regional boundaries to bolster Euro-Atlantic defense capabilities.

  • CENTCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that a number of European countries, including Ireland, Spain, the Netherlands, and Slovenia, withdrew from the 2026 Eurovision Song Contest in protest against Israel's participation. In Syria, thousands celebrated in Hama, expressing hope for a future free from Bashar al-Assad's regime, marking one year since his forces' withdrawal from the area. The United Nations Security Council (UNSC) delegation met with Lebanese President Michel Aoun, emphasizing the need for Israel to adhere to the November 2024 ceasefire, which they claimed had been repeatedly violated. Israeli airstrikes targeted civilian areas in southern Lebanon shortly after U.S.-brokered talks, raising tensions further. Meanwhile, Israel set its military budget at $34 billion as operations in Gaza continued, despite international condemnation. The situation in Gaza remained dire, with reports of ongoing airstrikes and civilian casualties triggering criticism from international leaders, including UN Secretary-General Antonio Guterres, who accused Israel of neglecting civilian deaths. In the geopolitical arena, Egypt reaffirmed its strategic partnership with Russia amidst regional tensions, while U.S. Central Command deployed a new "suicide drone" squadron in the Middle East to counter escalating threats.

  • AFRICOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that the South African Rugby Federation extended head coach Rassie Erasmus' contract until the end of the 2031 Rugby World Cup. In the realm of U.S. immigration policy, Homeland Security Secretary Kristi Noem announced a new travel ban blocking entry from over 30 countries. The U.S. Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) ramped up operations in Minneapolis-St. Paul, targeting Somali immigrants following President Trump's remarks, which heightened tension in the community. In geopolitical developments, U.S. President Donald Trump facilitated a peace deal signing between the leaders of the Democratic Republic of Congo and Rwanda amidst ongoing conflict in their region. Additionally, Nigeria's National Security Adviser Nuhu Ribadu stressed the importance of safeguarding democracy as the Sahel region faced a surge in military coups. In another notable incident, reports surfaced from Sudan regarding the killing of dozens, predominantly children, attributed to "deliberate suicide-drone attacks."
