Date of Information: 05 November 2025

Reporting Period: 04 November - 05 November 2025

Distribution: Approved for public release; distribution is unlimited.

Combatant Commands: SOUTHCOM, EUCOM, PACOM, CENTCOM, AFRICOM

Intent: The intent of this report is to provide a high-level overview of significant military and geopolitical developments during the reporting period. The report consists of open-source reporting and updates on evolving conflicts, diplomatic initiatives, and strategic military actions involving countries of interest in the following Combatant Commands: SOUTHCOM, EUCOM, PACOM, CENTCOM, AFRICOM.

Executive Summary: During the reporting period, significant military and geopolitical developments occurred across multiple regions. In South America, U.S. President Trump ruled out military strikes on Venezuela, while Defense Secretary Hegseth confirmed a U.S. strike against drug trafficking boats and indicated upgrades to a naval base in the Caribbean for potential interventions. In Europe, Ukraine escalated its drone campaign targeting Russian industrial sites, while Russian forces intensified their encirclement of Ukrainian troops in Pokrovsk. North Korea dispatched construction troops to Russia, signaling military cooperation amid ongoing geopolitical negotiations between China and Russia. In the Pacific, China cautioned the U.S. against crossing critical diplomatic "red lines," and South Korea enhanced its military posture with nuclear submarines. In the Middle East, violence in Gaza escalated, with Israeli airstrikes leading to a significant Palestinian death toll amid fragile ceasefire conditions, while Iran increased its support for Iraqi militias. In Africa, Sudan faced heightened tensions as warlord Dagolo consolidated control and violence surged, prompting U.S. evacuations from Mali due to security threats.

  • SOUTHCOM: During the reporting period, President Donald Trump stated on 31 October 2025 that he was not considering military strikes on Venezuela, amid concerns that a U.S. military buildup in the region signaled intentions for regime change in Caracas. On 2 November 2025, U.S. Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth announced that three individuals were killed in a U.S. strike against alleged drug trafficking boats in the Caribbean. Concurrently, the U.S. military began upgrading a defunct Cold War naval base in the Caribbean, indicating preparations for potential military interventions in Venezuela. The Iranian Foreign Ministry disclosed that while messages were exchanged between Iran and the U.S. via intermediaries, there was currently no negotiation process underway. Additionally, internal polling suggested diminishing Republican support for a military operation in Venezuela, as public awareness of the military implications grew.

  • EUCOM: During the reporting period, Ukrainian long-range drones struck industrial facilities deep within Russia, including a petrochemical plant approximately 1,500 kilometers (932 miles) from the border, reflecting an escalation in Kyiv's long-range air campaign aimed at crucial Russian infrastructure. Concurrently, Ukraine's drone operations intensified, with efforts underway to mass-produce interceptor drones capable of combating Russian Shahed drones. On the ground in Ukraine, Russian forces tightened their encirclement around Ukrainian troops in Pokrovsk, a significant transport and logistics hub, through sustained air strikes that reportedly targeted civilian infrastructure. Meanwhile, North Korea dispatched around 5,000 construction troops to Russia to assist with infrastructure reconstruction, highlighting ongoing military cooperation between the two nations. In geopolitical developments, the Chinese leader met with Russia's Prime Minister in Beijing to discuss deepening collaboration amid challenging global conditions, while Russian officials indicated plans to enhance rare earth metals extraction capabilities, underscoring the country's intent to solidify its economic base despite Western sanctions.

  • PACOM: During the reporting period, China’s Ambassador to the United States, Xie Feng, urged the U.S. to avoid crossing four “red lines,” including issues related to Taiwan, democracy, human rights, and China’s political system. Following Bangladesh's accession to the UN Water Convention, concerns arose regarding potential adverse effects on relations with India. Typhoon Kalmaegi caused significant devastation in the central Philippines, leading to at least 26 reported deaths and trapping survivors on roofs and in submerged vehicles. In a move to address rising tensions with North Korea, South Korea advanced its military capabilities by embracing nuclear-powered submarines. Defense chiefs from the Philippines, Australia, Japan, and the United States expressed apprehension over China’s destabilizing actions in the East and South China Seas, while discussions around a newly signed Philippines-Canada military pact raised concerns in Beijing regarding intentions to target Chinese military activities. Additionally, Australia’s Defense Minister stated that China's military build-up necessitated a response due to increased risks to Australia’s sea trade routes.

  • CENTCOM: During the reporting period, the violent situation in Gaza worsened, with the Palestinian death toll nearing 70,000 as renewed Israeli airstrikes continued despite a fragile ceasefire. Gaza’s Health Ministry condemned Israel's actions, particularly regarding the alleged mistreatment of Palestinian bodies and prisoners. In Israel, a political scandal erupted following the resignation and subsequent arrest of the military's top lawyer, linked to a leaked video showing potential abusive conduct by soldiers. Meanwhile, Iran intensified its military support for Iraqi militias, signaling a shift in strategic focus amidst its ongoing regional influence efforts, particularly in light of Iraq's upcoming parliamentary elections on 11 November. U.S. Ambassador to Türkiye, Tom Barrack, reassured that Türkiye and Israel would not engage in conflict, while Palestinian prisoners were joyfully reunited with families in Gaza after a recent release. The geopolitical landscape remains tense, characterized by competing narratives and military preparations across the region.

  • AFRICOM: During the reporting period, military and geopolitical tensions escalated in Sudan as Mohamed Hamdan Dagolo, a prominent warlord, consolidated control over significant territories in the country. Reports indicated that Dagolo planned either to declare a breakaway state or to exert full control over Sudan, prompting concerns among international observers. Meanwhile, violence surged in the Kordofan region following a drone strike that targeted a funeral, killing multiple mourners and further aggravating the ongoing conflict. In a related development, the United States ordered the evacuation of all non-emergency personnel and their families from Mali due to the deteriorating security situation caused by conflicts with al-Qaida-linked militants. Additionally, Norway announced a contribution of USD 4.5 million aimed at providing humanitarian assistance in Sudan, highlighting the international community's response to the crisis.
