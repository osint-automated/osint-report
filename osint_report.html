Intent: The intent of this report is to provide a high-level overview of significant military and geopolitical developments during the reporting period. The report consists of open-source reporting and updates on evolving conflicts, diplomatic initiatives, and strategic military actions involving countries of interest in the following Combatant Commands: SOUTHCOM, EUCOM, PACOM, CENTCOM, AFRICOM.

Executive Summary: During the reporting period, Colombian authorities investigated a tragic Fuerza Aérea Colombiana C-130H Hercules aircraft crash in Putumayo on 23 March, resulting in the deaths of 34 to 66 military personnel, with ongoing rescue efforts for four missing individuals. In Cauca, Colombian Ejército Nacional troops faced a civilian asonada by the GAO-r Estructura Carlos Patiño after the destruction of a drug lab, leading to a tactical withdrawal to secure vital positions. Concurrently, the Colombian Quinta División redirected operational maneuvers to counter threats in critical areas. On the same day, Ecuadorian Fuerzas Armadas executed Operation Impacto against illegal mining, neutralizing over 200 hectares of clandestine operations. In Ukraine, Russian forces conducted approximately 700 strikes causing civilian casualties, while Ukrainian forces launched drone strikes against strategic targets, maintaining pressure on Russian operations. In the South China Sea, People's Liberation Army Navy forces moved southward, heightening tensions with the Philippines amid maritime frictions. Simultaneously, Iran targeted Israeli sites with ballistic missiles, provoking Israeli airstrikes and diplomatic negotiations with the United States. The situation in Sudan escalated with a drone strike on a hospital, resulting in significant civilian casualties, while geopolitical tensions in Zimbabwe arose with the detention of an opposition leader amidst controversial constitutional amendments.

  • SOUTHCOM: During the reporting period, Colombian authorities investigated a Fuerza Aérea Colombiana C-130H Hercules aircraft crash in Putumayo department on 23 March, which killed between 34 and 66 military personnel according to conflicting reports from regional governor and armed forces chief, with rescue operations ongoing and four personnel missing. Colombian Ejército Nacional troops in Cauca department's Mercaderes municipality encountered civilian asonada orchestrated by the GAO-r Estructura Carlos Patiño following destruction of a pasta base de coca laboratory on 23 March, prompting organized tactical withdrawal to secure positions along the Panamericana highway while maintaining situational control. The Colombian Quinta División redirected operational maneuvers toward Cauca, Huila, Tolima, and Eje Cafetero on 23 March to neutralize threats and bolster critical area defense. Ecuadorian Fuerzas Armadas executed Operation Impacto against illegal mining in Imbabura, Carchi, and Esmeraldas provinces on 23 March, deploying aerial firesupport, mortars, and BM-21 multiple rocket launchers to neutralize over 200 hectares of clandestine camps, shafts, and logistics networks. U.S. Southern Command conducted a kinetic strike on a narcolancha in the Eastern Pacific on or around 23 March as part of ongoing counter-narcotics operations.

  • EUCOM: During the reporting period, Russian forces launched approximately 700 strikes across 39 settlements in Zaporizhzhia Oblast on 23 March, employing nearly 400 FPV drones alongside artillery and airstrikes that killed two civilians and injured eight others, while broader Russian aerial campaigns across Ukraine killed nine people and wounded dozens more. Ukrainian forces conducted long-range drone strikes that damaged the Primorsk oil terminal on the Baltic Sea and a refinery in Ufa over 1400 kilometres from the border on 24 March, with satellite imagery confirming fires and structural harm. Commander-in-Chief Oleksandr Syrskyi announced that Ukrainian defenders repelled Russian assaults on multiple eastern front sectors, inflicting more than 6090 personnel casualties on Russian units over four days of intensified operations concluding 24 March. President Volodymyr Zelenskyy revealed intelligence assessments showing Russian preparations for a new massive offensive, the establishment of long-range drone control stations in Belarus, and irrefutable evidence of Russian intelligence provision to Iran. U.S. and Ukrainian delegations finalised bilateral discussions in Florida on 23 March, covering security guarantees, humanitarian concerns, and prospective prisoner exchanges.

  • PACOM: During the reporting period, People's Liberation Army Navy forces maneuvered seven vessels from the Yellow Sea southward through the Taiwan Strait into the South China Sea on 24 March, as Taiwan's Ministry of National Defense detected and reported their presence near Philippine waters. China declared clearing operations in designated sectors of Scarborough Shoal, prompting the Philippine Coast Guard to dismiss the proclamation and maintain patrols. Intelligence analysts tracked multiple disguised People's Republic of China maritime militia vessels operating in the Taiwan Strait and South China Sea approaches. Divergences surfaced in the China-Philippines provisional arrangement at Second Thomas Shoal, where Beijing enforced tacit restrictions on Philippine resupply missions. Manila advanced hard balancing measures against Chinese maritime claims, escalating South China Sea frictions within the PACOM area of responsibility.

  • CENTCOM: During the reporting period, Iran launched two ballistic missiles at Israeli targets in Arad and Dimona on 23 March, inflicting casualties on at least 36 civilians including children and prompting investigations into Israeli air defense interception failures near the Dimona nuclear reactor. Israel executed airstrikes in Tehran on 23 March, contributing to reported blackouts in targeted power grid infrastructure. United States President Donald Trump deferred planned strikes on Iranian power plants and energy facilities for five days, citing productive negotiations with Tehran authorities, while simultaneously signaling potential deal opportunities following consultations with Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu. Iranian officials threatened retaliatory actions against Israeli power plants, Gulf state energy and desalination systems, and United States regional bases in response to any escalation over the Strait of Hormuz closure. Tehran fired additional missiles at Israel amid ongoing United States-Iranian talks, as Trump adjusted prior ultimatums to prioritize diplomatic outcomes over immediate military intensification.

  • AFRICOM: During the reporting period, Sudanese Armed Forces launched a drone strike against El-Daein Teaching Hospital in East Darfur, Sudan on 23 March, which killed 64 individuals including 13 children and medical staff while wounding 89 others and rendered the facility non-functional. Rapid Support Forces engaged Zaghawa Joint Force units allied with Sudanese Armed Forces in clashes along the Chad-Sudan border on 23 March, which inflicted hundreds of casualties, disrupted cross-border trade, and impeded humanitarian operations. Zimbabwean police detained opposition leader Tendai Biti alongside activists on 23 March amid heightened geopolitical tensions over cabinet-backed constitutional amendments that extended presidential terms from five to seven years and enabled lawmakers to select the president, potentially retaining Emmerson Mnangagwa in power until 2030. Chadian forces reinforced deployments near Al-Tina on the Sudan border following prior incidents, while state militaries in Mali and Burkina Faso advanced against Jama'at Nusrat al-Islam wal-Muslimin positions amid persistent insurgent counterattacks.
