Open Source Intelligence (OSINT) Report

Date of Access: 17 November 2025

Date of Information: 17 November 2025

Reporting Period: 16 November - 17 November 2025

Distribution: Approved for public release; distribution is unlimited.

Combatant Commands: SOUTHCOM, EUCOM, PACOM, CENTCOM, AFRICOM

Intent: The intent of this report is to provide a high-level overview of significant military and geopolitical developments during the reporting period. The report consists of open-source reporting and updates on evolving conflicts, diplomatic initiatives, and strategic military actions involving countries of interest in the following Combatant Commands: SOUTHCOM, EUCOM, PACOM, CENTCOM, AFRICOM.

Executive Summary: During the reporting period, military and geopolitical tensions escalated significantly across various regions. In the Caribbean, the U.S. intensified its military presence near Venezuela, executing Operation Southern Spear and raising the prospect of direct military action as the USS Ford was deployed. Tensions rose as Venezuela maintained support from China, complicating U.S. objectives. In Europe, ongoing conflict in Ukraine saw increased Russian aggression, including a substantial drone attack and missile strikes, while President Zelensky condemned these actions. In Asia, a pivotal defense pact between Japan and the Philippines marked a strategic shift, prompting China to respond with warnings against joint military exercises in disputed territories. Meanwhile, in the Middle East, efforts to normalize relations between Saudi Arabia and Israel faced hurdles; Iran ceased uranium enrichment amid international pressures while Israeli forces engaged in confrontations in Lebanon. In Africa, migrant tragedies continued off the Libyan coast, alongside ambushes by jihadists in Nigeria, highlighting the region's persistent instability.

  • SOUTHCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated a significant increase in military and geopolitical tensions in the Caribbean and Latin America. The United States intensified its military presence near Venezuela, conducting Operation Southern Spear, which involved sinking 20 suspected drug boats since September. President Donald Trump suggested he was close to deciding on military action against Venezuela, coinciding with the positioning of the USS Ford aircraft carrier in the region. Venezuelan President Nicolás Maduro criticized the U.S. exercises with Trinidad and Tobago as "irresponsible," while Latin American countries prepared potential responses to U.S. aggression. In Colombia, the government announced a $4.3 billion deal to procure Swedish fighter jets amidst escalating tensions with the U.S. Meanwhile, U.S. Marines engaged in a shootout with gang members in Port-au-Prince, Haiti, underscoring the volatile security situation in the region. The geopolitical landscape shifted as China sought to support Maduro's government, defying U.S. sanctions, thus reinforcing the complexities of international relations in America's backyard.

  • EUCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that Finnish President Alexander Stubb warned a ceasefire in Ukraine is unlikely before spring 2026, urging European allies to maintain their support despite ongoing corruption scandals in Kyiv. On 14 November, a Russian missile barrage struck an apartment block in Kyiv, resulting in six fatalities, with President Volodymyr Zelensky condemning the attacks as acts of terror. Meanwhile, Russia increased its military pressure, launching its largest drone attack to date, employing 430 drones and 18 missiles against Ukraine. Ukrainian defenses intercepted a significant number of these threats, but the conflict continued to escalate with reports of Russian forces capturing two villages in the Zaporozhye region. The Kremlin's economy faced challenges as oil revenues decreased, prompting a shift to consumer taxation to address budget deficits. Additionally, discussions regarding prisoner exchanges resumed between Ukraine and Russia, with Zelensky stating they aimed to secure the return of approximately 1,200 Ukrainian prisoners.

  • PACOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated significant developments in military and geopolitical dynamics in Asia. A new defense pact between Japan and the Philippines, announced on 24 October, allowed the Japanese military to deploy to the Philippines for the first time since World War II, aiming to bolster regional security against China's advances. Concurrently, Chinese authorities urged the Philippines to cease provocative actions in the South China Sea, as tensions escalated following joint patrols between the Philippines, the United States, and Japan. China conducted a bomber formation patrol in the region, which it claims dominance over, further warning against the escalation of joint military exercises. Additionally, a China Coast Guard formation sailed through the Senkaku Islands, exacerbating tensions with Japan after comments from the Japanese Prime Minister regarding potential military responses to a Chinese attack on Taiwan. In this context, the United States reaffirmed its commitment to Taiwan by proposing a US$330 million arms sale, aimed at maintaining Taiwan's military readiness amid the shifting security landscape in the region.

  • CENTCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated several significant military and geopolitical developments. President Donald Trump sought to persuade Saudi Crown Prince Mohammed bin Salman to normalize relations with Israel, although results remained uncertain. A large-scale rebuilding campaign began in Gaza, supported by residents, volunteers, and U.N. personnel. Iran's foreign minister announced that the nation ceased uranium enrichment at all sites, responding to Western pressure following Israeli and U.S. bombings. Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu declared his opposition to any Palestinian state ahead of a crucial U.N. Security Council vote on 15 November regarding Trump's Gaza peace plan. Simultaneously, UN peacekeepers reported that Israeli forces fired on them in southern Lebanon as tensions escalated in the region. Additionally, Hezbollah asserted its right to reorganize following Israeli aggression, and Indonesia prepared 20,000 troops for a potential U.S.-backed stabilization force in Gaza, emphasizing health and infrastructure support.

  • AFRICOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that at least four migrants died when two boats capsized off the coast of Libya while carrying nearly 100 individuals attempting to reach Europe. The Libyan Red Crescent reported that they rescued 91 survivors from Bangladesh, Sudan, and Egypt. In Nigeria, jihadists affiliated with the Islamic State ambushed Nigerian security forces in Borno state, resulting in the deaths of two soldiers and two militia members. Protests occurred in Charlotte, North Carolina, as residents opposed federal immigration enforcement actions under President Donald Trump, announced by Homeland Security official Tricia McLaughlin. In Gaza, reports emerged of a group named Al-Majd ‘Europe’ facilitating the exit of Palestinians through Israeli-coordinated channels. In recent sports updates, South Africa achieved a significant Test victory against India in Kolkata, while Nigeria prepared for a decisive World Cup qualifying match against DR Congo in Rabat.
