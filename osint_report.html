Intent: The intent of this report is to provide a high-level overview of significant military and geopolitical developments during the reporting period. The report consists of open-source reporting and updates on evolving conflicts, diplomatic initiatives, and strategic military actions involving countries of interest in the following Combatant Commands: SOUTHCOM, EUCOM, PACOM, CENTCOM, AFRICOM.

Executive Summary: During the reporting period, significant military and geopolitical developments emerged across multiple regions. Venezuelan authorities released French national Julien Fevrier, further complicating diplomatic relations with France. In Ecuador, the withdrawal of the ambassador from Colombia following inflammatory remarks marked heightened interstate tensions. Ukraine demonstrated tactical proficiency by neutralizing Russian military capabilities and participating in multinational discussions to enhance maritime defense, while fatality reports revealed substantial losses among African mercenaries in the conflict. In East Asia, Japan scrambled fighter jets in response to aggressive PLA maneuvers, as China's military conducted nuclear simulations in preparation for potential conflicts in the Taiwan Strait. The situation in the Middle East escalated following Israel's extensive airstrikes against Hezbollah, which prompted Iran to disrupt oil shipments through the Strait of Hormuz. Additionally, ongoing violence in Sudan and Nigeria showcased the deteriorating security landscape, with increased drone strikes and militant attacks resulting in significant civilian casualties. The United Nations continued efforts to facilitate dialogue amid escalating conflicts, highlighting the urgent need for diplomatic resolutions in volatile regions.

  • SOUTHCOM: During the reporting period, Venezuelan authorities released French national Julien Fevrier after 15 months of detention on 8 April, prompting French Foreign Ministry confirmation of his return. Ecuador withdrew its ambassador from Colombia on 8 April following critical remarks on former Vice President Jorge Glas, heightening interstate diplomatic frictions. Argentine legislators approved a bill authorizing mining in glacier zones on 9 April, which demonstrators contested amid environmental concerns. Colombian Army units engaged Jaime Martínez dissidents in Valle del Cauca clashes involving drones on 8 April, resulting in soldier injuries. Mexican Federal Forces repelled a Jalisco New Generation Cartel assault in Michoacán on 8 April, securing a substantial weapons cache. No large-scale military operations or border incidents transpired elsewhere in Central or South America.

  • EUCOM: During the reporting period, Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelenskyy praised the US-Iran ceasefire for enabling diplomatic pathways and urged Russian forces to cease strikes on Ukrainian infrastructure while affirming Kyiv's readiness for reciprocal de-escalation. Zelenskyy announced Ukraine's participation in multinational discussions on the Strait of Hormuz, wherein Ukrainian specialists proffered Black Sea operational expertise to bolster collective maritime defense capabilities. Ukrainian defense units established new benchmarks for neutralizing Russian military personnel and materiel, thereby inducing operational instability across Russian command structures. Independent monitors disclosed that fatalities among African mercenaries recruited by Russian President Vladimir Putin surpassed official disclosures, with Cameroon confirming 16 deaths but analysts estimating at least 94 Cameroonian and over 300 total African losses in Ukrainian theaters. Ukrainian precision strikes targeted Russian oil export facilities, enforcing economic sanctions and curtailing petroleum revenues that sustained Moscow's war effort. Putin extended matériel assistance to Madagascar's military hierarchy, advancing Russian geopolitical leverage in Africa amid protracted conflict dynamics. NATO's senior command credited Ukrainian engagements with refining alliance protocols for drone-centric and hybrid warfare training scenarios.

  • PACOM: During the reporting period, Japan's Air Self-Defense Force scrambled F-15 fighters from the Southwest Air Defense Command to intercept People's Liberation Army aircraft approaching East China Sea airspace on 8 April. People's Liberation Army Eastern Theater Command conducted nuclear response simulations oriented toward the Taiwan Strait and Japan-facing directions. Assessments identified over 100 PLA Navy warships executing large-scale gray-zone armada operations in the Western Pacific. Beijing reserved extensive offshore airspace for 40 days starting early second-quarter 2026 to support elevated PLA operational activities.

  • CENTCOM: During the reporting period, Israel launched over 100 airstrikes against Hezbollah targets in Lebanon on 8 April, striking residential and commercial areas in Beirut and two key border crossings, which killed 112 civilians and militants including a Hezbollah leader's secretary. Iran responded by closing the Strait of Hormuz to tankers, halting oil shipments and announcing alternative shipping routes amid sea mine risks. Netanyahu declared Israel's readiness to resume full-scale operations at any moment, while US forces maintained positions encircling Iran until final negotiations concluded. Trump affirmed the two-week ceasefire brokered via Pakistan held tentatively despite discrepancies in terms, as Iran formalised new Hormuz tolls and the US paused energy infrastructure attacks. Hezbollah fired retaliatory drones toward Israel, intercepted without casualties, and Gulf states reported minor missile interceptions linked to Iranian proxies.

  • AFRICOM: During the reporting period, Rapid Support Forces conducted a drone strike on the Chadian border town of Tine on 8 April, killing 17 civilians as Sudan's civil war spilled over. Boko Haram and Islamic State West Africa Province launched simultaneous assaults on Maiduguri in Nigeria on 8 April. Unknown actors executed a drone strike on Kutum in North Darfur, Sudan, killing 12 civilians including six children. Sudanese Armed Forces and Rapid Support Forces intensified drone and loitering munition attacks on civilian infrastructure in Darfur and Kordofan, contributing to 480-500 fatalities since January. United Nations Personal Envoy Pekka Haavisto met Rapid Support Forces commander Mohamed Hamdan Dagalo in Nairobi on 8 April to pursue de-escalation pathways. James Swan assumed command of MONUSCO in the Democratic Republic of Congo on 8 April to revitalize peace initiatives amid eastern insurgencies. Kenyan security forces dismantled an Al-Shabaab logistics network in Nairobi's Eastleigh district on 8 April, confiscating camouflage uniforms and apparel. Ethiopian federal police arrested human trafficking ringleader Yetbarek Dawit and nine associates in Shire on 8 April for orchestrating Libya-bound operations involving torture and deaths. A Yale University analysis disclosed Ethiopian military provision of sanctuary and support to Rapid Support Forces. Zimbabwean opposition parties condemned Zanu-PF's constitutional amendments on 8 April, which sought to eliminate presidential elections and prolong incumbency terms. Central African Republic authorities released detained aid worker Joseph Figueira Martin on 8 April after nearly two years on espionage charges.
