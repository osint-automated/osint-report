Intent: The intent of this report is to provide a high-level overview of significant military and geopolitical developments during the reporting period. The report consists of open-source reporting and updates on evolving conflicts, diplomatic initiatives, and strategic military actions involving countries of interest in the following Combatant Commands: SOUTHCOM, EUCOM, PACOM, CENTCOM, AFRICOM.

Executive Summary: During the reporting period, key military and geopolitical developments unfolded across multiple regions. In South America, President Trump ruled out military strikes against Venezuela while the U.S. military upgraded a Cold War naval base in the Caribbean amidst ongoing geopolitical tensions with China, which defended its partnership with Venezuela. In Europe, Ukraine utilized long-range drones to strike deep within Russia, as Russian forces escalated attacks on civilian infrastructure and tightened encirclement of Ukrainian troops. In the Middle East, Israeli military operations in Gaza resulted in a significant death toll as Iran armed Iraqi militias, raising concerns about regional stability. In Africa, escalating violence in Sudan prompted UN calls for intervention, and the U.S. ordered the evacuation of personnel from Mali due to terrorist threats, while political unrest in Tanzania threatened critical trade routes.

  • SOUTHCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that President Donald Trump, while aboard Air Force One on 31 October 2025, stated that he was not considering military strikes against Venezuela, amidst concerns in Caracas over a U.S. military buildup in the region. On 2 November, U.S. Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth announced that the United States conducted a strike against alleged drug trafficking boats in the Caribbean, resulting in the deaths of three individuals. In a parallel geopolitical context, China's government defended its cooperation with Venezuela, rejecting U.S. threats of force in international dealings. Additionally, the U.S. military began upgrading a long-abandoned Cold War naval base in the Caribbean, indicating preparations for potential military interventions in Venezuela. Reports also revealed that recent military strategies, including an uptick in operations against "narco-terrorists," had eroded Republican support for a military invasion of Venezuela.

  • EUCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that Ukrainian long-range drones struck an industrial plant approximately 1,300 kilometers (800 miles) deep within Russia on 4 November, showcasing Ukraine's capability to target significant Russian infrastructure as the conflict in Ukraine continued. Concurrently, Ukrainian forces reported intensified drone-on-drone engagements and preparations to mass-produce interceptor drones to counter Russian Shahed drones. Russian military actions also escalated, with forces reportedly tightening their encirclement of Ukrainian troops in Pokrovsk, and launching air strikes against civilian infrastructure in Ukraine, leading to multiple casualties on 3 November. In a separate geopolitical development, the Chinese and Russian leaders pledged to strengthen bilateral ties amidst external pressures, while North Korea sent approximately 5,000 construction troops to Russia, indicating potential military collaboration.

  • PACOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that China urged the United States to avoid crossing its "red lines," which included issues related to Taiwan, democracy and human rights, its political system, and development rights, according to Chinese Ambassador to the US Xie Feng. In response to Bangladesh's recent accession to the U.N. Water Convention, concerns emerged regarding potential conflicts with India over shared water resources. The Philippines experienced severe devastation from Typhoon Kalmaegi, resulting in at least 46 fatalities and leaving victims trapped on roofs in flooded areas. Philippines, Australia, Japan, and the United States expressed concerns over China's destabilizing actions in the East and South China Seas, prompting Australia's defense minister to underscore the need for a response to China's military buildup. Meanwhile, the Philippines and Canada signed a military pact aimed at addressing China's military activities, though analysts characterized it as more symbolic than strategically significant.

  • CENTCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated significant military and geopolitical developments in the Middle East. Israeli military operations in Gaza continued, culminating in a death toll nearing 70,000 Palestinians since October 2023, while Israeli forces bombed various locations despite a fragile ceasefire. The Israeli military's former top lawyer, Maj. Gen. Yifat Tomer-Yerushalmi, resigned amid a scandal over alleged abuses in a military prison and was subsequently arrested. Concurrently, Iran reportedly armed Iraqi militias in anticipation of potential conflicts with Israel, while the Iranian Foreign Minister declared the continuation of the country's uranium enrichment program. In Iraq, parliamentary elections set for 11 November provoked concern that Iran aimed to maintain its regional influence following a reduced stature during the Gaza conflict. Meanwhile, tensions escalated near the Lebanon-Israel border as the Lebanese army mobilized in response to Israeli movements, further heightening regional instability.

  • AFRICOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that Mohamed Hamdan Dagolo, a prominent warlord in Sudan, aimed to either establish a breakaway state or assert control over the entire country, adding to the ongoing conflict. A drone strike in Sudan killed many mourners at a funeral, with blame directed at the paramilitary Rapid Support Forces as hostilities intensified in the Kordofan region. In a separate incident, reports emerged of the RSF detaining hundreds of men and boys near El Fasher, further heightening the humanitarian crisis in the area.
