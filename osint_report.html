  • Venezuela: During the reporting period, significant military and geopolitical developments unfolded in the Caribbean and surrounding regions. The USS Gerald R. Ford, a nuclear-powered aircraft carrier valued at $13 billion, was deployed to the Caribbean amid escalating tensions involving Venezuela. Following the arrival of U.S. warships in Trinidad and Tobago, Venezuela announced a suspension of its energy agreements with the island nation, citing U.S. military maneuvers as a provocation. Claims arose from Venezuela's government, asserting the capture of CIA-linked mercenaries allegedly plotting a false-flag attack against a U.S. warship. Concurrently, the U.S. military conducted multiple strikes against alleged drug-smuggling boats in the eastern Pacific Ocean, resulting in the deaths of 14 individuals, a continuation of a broader anti-drug operation. U.S. defense officials underlined the operation's connection to ongoing drug trafficking concerns emanating from Venezuela. Discussions of possible land strikes in Venezuela also surfaced among U.S. lawmakers, suggesting an intensifying U.S. stance towards the Maduro regime. The military buildup and operational focus on Venezuela reflect a significant escalation in U.S. foreign policy under the current administration.

  • Russia: During the reporting period, Russian lawmakers endorsed a bill on 24 October mandating year-round military conscription, seeking to bolster military recruitment amid ongoing challenges in the Russia-Ukraine conflict. Concurrently, Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelenskyy emphasized the necessity for sustained European support for ongoing military efforts, particularly in light of recent gains by Russian forces in regions such as Pokrovsk. On the geopolitical front, a heavy Russian cargo plane landed in Caracas, Venezuela, amid heightened tensions with the United States, which signifies Russia's continued engagement in Latin America as it faces Western sanctions. Additionally, a UN probe revealed new evidence of crimes against humanity committed by Russian forces against civilians in Ukraine, further complicating Russia's international relations. In military developments, Ukrainian forces reported increased challenges from small Russian infiltration units, indicating a shift in tactics on the front lines. Meanwhile, oil prices fluctuated amid investor concerns over Russia's sanctions and OPEC+ output plans, with prices dipping as market sentiment remained cautious about the ongoing geopolitical instability.

  • Ukraine: During the reporting period, U.S. Treasury Secretary announced sanctions against Russian oil giants Rosneft and Lukoil on 22 October, prompting Lukoil to divest its foreign assets. On 24 October, Russian lawmakers endorsed a bill to implement year-round military conscription to address personnel shortages in the ongoing conflict. A UN report released during this time accused Russia of committing crimes against humanity by targeting Ukrainian civilians with drones, forcing population displacement in regions controlled by Kyiv. Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelenskyy stated on 24 October that Ukraine needed continued EU support for two to three more years of fighting against Russia, as military exchanges between the two nations intensified, including drone attacks along the front lines. Additionally, reports indicated that Ukrainian strikes on Russian oil refineries decreased Moscow's refining capacity by 20%. Hungary's Prime Minister Viktor Orbán sought to form a coalition with Czechia and Slovakia to address EU policies on Ukraine, indicating rising tensions within the region concerning support for Kyiv.

  • China: During the reporting period, significant military and geopolitical developments emerged. The United States and China made attempts to negotiate a deal regarding rare earths, tariffs, and soybeans ahead of high-level meetings between Presidents Donald Trump and Xi Jinping scheduled for Thursday, 2 November, in Busan, South Korea. Concurrently, Japan and the U.S. strengthened their alliance, signing a rare earths agreement on 31 October to secure supply amid ongoing trade tensions. In military advancements, China demonstrated its capability to launch delta-wing ASN-301 drones from a truck-mounted multiple launch system, showcasing its increasing precision strike abilities. Additionally, Myanmar's military conducted operations against a major online scam center, prompting a stream of refugees crossing into Thailand. These developments indicate a complex interplay of military readiness and diplomatic endeavors in the region.

  • Israel: During the reporting period, Israel increased military operations against Hamas in the Gaza Strip, with Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu ordering "intensive" attacks in response to perceived violations of a ceasefire agreement. Israeli forces conducted airstrikes in Gaza, killing at least 26 individuals, including children, amidst ongoing hostilities. Simultaneously, Israel accused Hamas of breaching the ceasefire by returning remains that belonged to a hostage already recovered two years prior. In the West Bank, Israeli military actions resulted in the deaths of three suspected militants planning an attack. Meanwhile, Lebanon's President Michel Aoun called for an end to Israeli strikes, which have allegedly violated a ceasefire agreement established in November 2024. On the diplomatic front, Israel faced increasing scrutiny as accusations arose regarding its handling of humanitarian aid and treatment of Palestinian prisoners. Key geopolitical implications emerged as the U.S. navigated its role in the ongoing conflict, while tensions remained high between Israel and Hezbollah in Lebanon.

  • Iran: During the reporting period, four Indian nationals who were allegedly held captive in Iran returned to Gandhinagar, Gujarat, following their ordeal, which included receiving videos of torture on 26 October. In Iran, a private bank's recent bankruptcy highlighted extensive state corruption and ongoing economic challenges. Iraq secured a deal with U.S. company Excelerate Energy for its first floating LNG import platform, aimed at enhancing its gas supply and energy security. Iranian officials stated that they and Russia planned to sign a significant railway construction deal soon, underscoring their ongoing collaboration. Meanwhile, China signaled its readiness to respond to sanctions against Iran that threaten its interests, reinforcing the geopolitical complexities in the region. Additionally, Iranian forces accused a detained American couple of espionage, raising concerns amidst escalating tensions. In Sudan, paramilitary groups reportedly executed over 2,000 unarmed civilians in El-Fasher, indicating a dire humanitarian situation.

  • North Korea: During the reporting period, Russian President Vladimir Putin hosted North Korean Foreign Minister Choe Son-hui in Moscow on 27 October, reinforcing the strategic partnership between their nations amid ongoing military developments in Ukraine. Both foreign ministers reaffirmed their commitment to the Comprehensive Strategic Partnership Treaty, highlighting recent agreements between Russian and North Korean leaders. During his tour of Asia, U.S. President Donald Trump met with Japan's new Prime Minister Sanae Takaichi on 28 October, where they signed crucial agreements on rare earths and missile supplies for Japan's F-35s, signaling a deepening of U.S.-Japan defense ties. Trump expressed strong support for Takaichi’s leadership and Japan's military capabilities. Concurrently, South Korea faced pressures as the U.S. demanded a $350 billion investment, stirring public concern over potential economic implications.
