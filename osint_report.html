Combatant Commands: SOUTHCOM, EUCOM, PACOM, CENTCOM, AFRICOM

Intent: The intent of this report is to provide a high-level overview of significant military and geopolitical developments during the reporting period. The report consists of open-source reporting and updates on evolving conflicts, diplomatic initiatives, and strategic military actions involving countries of interest in the following Combatant Commands: SOUTHCOM, EUCOM, PACOM, CENTCOM, AFRICOM.

Executive Summary: During the reporting period, critical military and geopolitical updates included heightened tensions between the United States and Venezuela following President Trump's proclamation of a "total and complete blockade" against the country, aimed at sanctioned oil tankers and underscoring long-standing U.S. interests in Venezuelan oil. The U.S. conducted military operations resulting in casualties, while Maduro condemned such actions as attempts to destabilize his government amidst an escalating humanitarian crisis. In Europe, the Russia-Ukraine conflict continued to evolve with Ukraine's President Zelensky expressing concerns over prolonged warfare, as EU leaders discussed utilizing frozen Russian assets for Ukraine’s defense amid increased Black Sea tensions. Notably, the U.S. announced a substantial arms sale to Taiwan, aggravating China. In Asia, developments included a U.S. Navy warship's successful drone launch and China's condemnation of Philippine activities in the South China Sea. In Africa, M23 fighters withdrew from Uvira in the DRC amid U.S. mediation efforts, and civil unrest in Sudan intensified, exacerbating a humanitarian crisis.

  • SOUTHCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated heightened tensions between the United States and Venezuela, exemplified by President Donald Trump's declaration of a "total and complete blockade" against the country on 16 December. This move aimed to target sanctioned oil tankers, reflecting ongoing U.S. claims over Venezuelan oil interests which date back to the nationalization of the industry in the 1970s. Notably, U.S. military actions in the Pacific claimed four lives in a strike on an alleged drug boat, adding to an escalating death toll that approached 100 from recent U.S. strikes. The U.S. House of Representatives also rejected attempts to limit military action against Venezuela, while Russia warned of the dangers of U.S. military engagement in the region. Venezuelan President Nicolás Maduro condemned U.S. actions as attempts to overthrow his government and warned against the consequences of military aggression. Additionally, reports highlighted the exacerbating humanitarian crisis in Venezuela, with increased uncertainty among the population due to these escalating geopolitical tensions.

  • EUCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated significant military and geopolitical developments surrounding the ongoing Russia-Ukraine conflict. Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelensky addressed concerns that Russia was preparing for another year of war, despite ongoing peace talks. In Belgium, EU leaders pressed for the utilization of frozen Russian assets to fund Ukraine’s defense amid rising tensions in the Black Sea, where Turkey warned both Russia and Ukraine to exercise caution following the downing of a drone by Turkish forces. Concurrently, the United States announced an arms sale to Taiwan valued at over $10 billion, encompassing advanced missile systems, which elicited a critical response from China. In a notable advancement, a United States Navy warship in the Middle East executed a one-way attack drone launch, marking a significant milestone in military capabilities. Meanwhile, Russian leaders reiterated their commitment to military objectives in Ukraine, underscoring the persistent volatility in the region as the EU debated methods to support Ukraine using frozen Russian assets amidst these escalating tensions.

  • PACOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that a U.S. Navy warship in the Middle East launched a one-way attack drone, marking a significant milestone in the deployment of affordable drones for American forces. On 18 October, China condemned the Philippines for alleged illegal activities at Xianbin Jiao in the South China Sea, asserting sovereignty over the area and characterizing Manila's claims as false accusations. Additionally, the Chinese People's Liberation Army (PLA) Navy's aircraft carrier Fujian transited through the Taiwan Straits on 17 October, a movement perceived as part of a routine enhancement of military capabilities. The Armed Forces of the Philippines announced their ongoing commitment to protect Filipino fisherfolk in the West Philippine Sea, highlighting regional security dynamics. In related developments, China issued an arms control white paper aiming to reshape global security governance, reflecting on its strategic intentions amidst rising geopolitical tensions.

  • CENTCOM: No significant updates found.

  • AFRICOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that M23 fighters withdrew from the key town of Uvira in the Democratic Republic of the Congo. The withdrawal followed an announcement from the government, which claimed it aimed to distract a US mediation team. In Nigeria, the Air Force apologized for a military flight incident that resulted in the detention of 11 servicemen in Burkina Faso; all personnel were subsequently released. In Sudan, civil war intensified, with the paramilitary Rapid Support Forces accused of mass killings and attempting to conceal their actions, while reports noted a rising humanitarian crisis impacting millions. Additionally, South Africa increased its coal exports to Israel by 87% despite international criticism related to the ongoing Gaza conflict, while drone strikes led to blackouts in major Sudanese cities as civilian suffering escalated.
