Intent: The intent of this report is to provide a high-level overview of significant military and geopolitical developments during the reporting period. The report consists of open-source reporting and updates on evolving conflicts, diplomatic initiatives, and strategic military actions involving countries of interest in the following Combatant Commands: SOUTHCOM, EUCOM, PACOM, CENTCOM, AFRICOM.

Executive Summary: During the reporting period, Ecuadorian President Daniel Noboa implemented a nationwide curfew and mobilized over 40,000 Armed Forces personnel to target criminal entities across four provinces until 1 April. Russian offensive operations in northern Kharkiv Oblast on 15 March yielded no confirmed territorial gains, while Ukrainian forces executed successful maneuvers near Slovyansk and targeted various Russian assets, resulting in significant operational losses for Russian units. Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelenskyy expressed readiness for trilateral peace talks involving the US and Russia, amid geopolitical developments including France’s commitment to supply advanced anti-aircraft systems to Ukraine. Concurrently, the People's Liberation Army intensified aerial and naval operations around Taiwan, indicating a resurgence in gray-zone tactics, while US and Israeli airstrikes targeted Iranian cities in response to missile attacks on their interests. In broader regional developments, South Africa acknowledged Israel's legal response concerning Gaza, and French military deployments to Kenya hinted at evolving defense agreements, alongside political developments in the Republic of Congo amid ambivalent electoral conditions.

  • SOUTHCOM: During the reporting period, Ecuadorian President Daniel Noboa decreed a nationwide curfew effective 15 March and directed the Armed Forces to deploy over 40,000 personnel to strategic locations across four provinces, where contingents executed direct intervention operations targeting criminal structures, drug caches, illicit machinery, and narco-camps until 1 April.

  • EUCOM: During the reporting period, Russian forces conducted offensive operations in northern Kharkiv Oblast on 15 March but achieved no confirmed advances, while Ukrainian forces advanced near Slovyansk in southern Yampil and on the southern outskirts of Kostyantynivka, and Russian elements marginally progressed in the Kostyantynivka-Druzhkivka tactical area northwest of Stupochky. Russian forces launched 97 drones, including Shahed, Gerbera, and Italmas variants, targeting Chernihiv, Sumy, and Kharkiv oblasts overnight from 14 to 15 March, with Ukrainian air defenses neutralizing 90; subsequent Russian missile and drone strikes inflicted six fatalities across Ukraine, damaging energy infrastructure near Kyiv alongside residential and educational sites. Ukrainian forces executed long-range strikes against Russian targets, including a missile depot near Dovzhansk in Luhansk Oblast, manpower concentrations near Kupyansk, Shakhove, Udachne, and Pokrovsk, a logistics and drone warehouse in occupied Zaporizhia Oblast, radar stations, and an S-400 launcher in occupied Crimea on 15 March, alongside oil and energy facilities such as the Tikhoretsk pumping station in Krasnodar Krai and Belgorod infrastructure overnight from 14 to 15 March. The Russian 144th Motorized Rifle Division lost combat effectiveness near Kupyansk due to personnel and equipment attrition. Geopolitically, Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelenskyy affirmed readiness for the next trilateral peace talks involving the United States, Ukraine, and Russia on 15 March, pending agreement between Washington and Moscow on venue after Russia rejected a US-hosted meeting, while France committed the newest SAMP/T NG anti-aircraft system for Ukrainian battlefield testing against Russian ballistic missiles following Zelenskyy's discussions with President Emmanuel Macron on 14 March.

  • PACOM: During the reporting period, People's Liberation Army (PLA) forces executed a significant surge in aerial and naval operations around Taiwan, deploying 26 aircraft sorties that penetrated Taiwan's northern, central, and southwestern air defense identification zones (ADIZ) with 16 crossings of the Taiwan Strait median line, while seven People's Liberation Army Navy (PLAN) vessels maneuvered in adjacent waters. Taiwan's Ministry of National Defense detected these activities up until 0600 hours on 15 March, prompting Republic of China Armed Forces to scramble aircraft, dispatch surface combatants, and position missile systems for monitoring and response. This operation followed a two-week lull in comparable PLA incursions, signaling renewed gray-zone coercion tactics to normalize high-tempo presence and test Taiwanese response protocols. PLA units additionally conducted a joint landing and beach assault drill on 15 March, though specific location and participating elements remained undisclosed. No kinetic engagements or escalations beyond routine patrols occurred in the South China Sea or broader PACOM area.

  • CENTCOM: During the reporting period, US and Israeli forces executed airstrikes on Iranian cities including Tehran, Hamadan, and Isfahan on 15 March, while Iranian forces launched counterattacks that inflicted damage across several Israeli cities. Iran deployed its Sejjil solid-fuel ballistic missile for the first time in retaliatory strikes against US and Israeli positions on 15 and 16 March. On 16 March at 04:30 GMT, US and Israeli aircraft struck Tehran, generating smoke plumes from at least two locations, and simultaneously pounded Hamadan with heavy ordnance. Iranian drones targeted Gulf states throughout 16 March, prompting Saudi Arabia to intercept 60 drones in its eastern region, Bahrain to activate air raid alarms, Qatar to neutralize incursions in its airspace, and Kuwait to sustain radar damage at its international airport. Drones assaulted US facilities in Iraq on 16 March at 04:15 GMT near the Baghdad embassy and Balad Airbase, with an earlier hit on Victory Base wounding five personnel and igniting a fire. A drone strike near Dubai International Airport on 16 March at 04:25 GMT sparked a contained fuel fire and halted flights temporarily. Iran's Foreign Minister Abbas Araghchi rejected US President Donald Trump's assertion of Tehran's interest in truce talks, as Trump demanded NATO allies reopen the Strait of Hormuz and claimed US operations neutralized Iran's air force and defenses. Israel affirmed ample missile interceptors remained available and identified thousands of additional Iranian targets for engagement.

  • AFRICOM: During the reporting period, South African officials noted Israel's response filing to the International Court of Justice on the Gaza case on 15 March. African states maintained cautious approaches toward repatriating citizens recruited by Russian networks for Ukraine operations, according to Reuters analysis published on 15 March. Rwandan authorities warned of possible withdrawal of their troops from Mozambique's Cabo Delgado province amid persistent insurgency threats on 15 March. Three French warships delivered 800 soldiers to Mombasa port in Kenya, signaling advancement of a prospective defence agreement on 15 March. Republic of Congo authorities conducted presidential elections on 15 March, where incumbent Denis Sassou Nguesso pursued mandate extension despite weak opposition, subdued turnout, and internet disruptions.
