Open Source Intelligence (OSINT) Report

Date of Access: 05 November 2025

Date of Information: 05 November 2025

Reporting Period: 04 November - 05 November 2025

Distribution: Approved for public release; distribution is unlimited.

Combatant Commands: SOUTHCOM, EUCOM, PACOM, CENTCOM, AFRICOM

Intent: The intent of this report is to provide a high-level overview of significant military and geopolitical developments during the reporting period. The report consists of open-source reporting and updates on evolving conflicts, diplomatic initiatives, and strategic military actions involving countries of interest in the following Combatant Commands: SOUTHCOM, EUCOM, PACOM, CENTCOM, AFRICOM.

Executive Summary: During the reporting period, critical military and geopolitical developments emerged globally. In South America, President Trump announced on 31 October 2025 that the U.S. would not pursue military strikes on Venezuela despite a significant military buildup in the region. Concurrently, U.S. forces killed three individuals in the Caribbean during anti-drug operations, and the military began upgrading a Cold War naval base. In Europe, Ukraine's long-range drones targeted a facility deep inside Russia, while Russian forces intensified encirclement efforts around Ukrainian troops. Germany planned to increase military aid to Ukraine by €3 billion in 2026. In the Asia-Pacific, China warned the U.S. against crossing four "red lines," raising concerns among regional allies about China's military actions. Hostilities in Gaza continued, causing significant civilian casualties and complicating political dynamics, while Sudan's violence escalated, prompting international calls for a ceasefire and leading to the U.S. evacuation of non-emergency personnel from Mali due to increasing insecurity.

  • SOUTHCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that President Donald Trump announced on 31 October 2025 that the United States was not considering military strikes on Venezuela amid concerns of a major U.S. military buildup in the region perceived as a potential regime change initiative. On 2 November 2025, U.S. Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth confirmed that U.S. forces killed three individuals during a strike against alleged drug trafficking boats in the Caribbean. Furthermore, the U.S. military commenced upgrades on a previously abandoned Cold War naval base in the Caribbean, signaling preparations for potential interventions in Venezuela. As U.S. tensions with Venezuela escalated due to military actions, Republican support for such interventions appeared to decline, according to a recent poll. In contrast, communications between Iran and the United States indicated that, while messages were exchanged, no formal negotiations had begun.

  • EUCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that Ukrainian long-range drones struck an industrial plant approximately 1,300 kilometers (800 miles) deep inside Russia, marking a significant escalation in Ukraine's aerial operations. In a related development, Ukraine prepared to increase the production of interceptor drones to enhance its defense against Russian Shahed drones. Meanwhile, Russian forces escalated their military efforts, reportedly tightening their encirclement of Ukrainian troops in Pokrovsk, Ukraine. Concurrently, discussions between China and Russia in Beijing focused on strengthening bilateral ties amid challenging external circumstances. In response to ongoing territorial and energy conflicts, Germany announced plans to raise its military aid to Ukraine by an additional €3 billion for 2026.

  • PACOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated significant military and geopolitical developments. China urged the United States to avoid crossing four “red lines,” which included Taiwan, democracy and human rights, its political system, and development rights, according to Ambassador to the US Xie Feng. This coincided with concerns expressed by defense leaders from the Philippines, Australia, Japan, and the United States regarding China's destabilizing actions in the East and South China Seas. Meanwhile, Australia's Defense Minister acknowledged that China's military build-up required a strategic response, particularly in protecting maritime trade routes. In South Asia, Bangladesh's accession to the UN Water Convention stirred apprehensions regarding potential conflicts with India. Additionally, Typhoon Kalmaegi devastated regions of the central Philippines, resulting in at least 26 fatalities and trapping survivors on roofs and inside vehicles. The situation prompted disaster response efforts and raised awareness of the ongoing challenges posed by natural disasters amidst geopolitical tensions.

  • CENTCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that hostilities continued to escalate in the Gaza region, with the death toll nearing 70,000 since October 2023, predominantly affecting women and children. The Israeli military conducted multiple aerial attacks within Gaza despite an ongoing ceasefire. Additionally, Israel released five Palestinian prisoners, leading to emotional reunions with their families, while political tensions rose with the arrest of a former top military lawyer over allegations of prisoner abuse. In Lebanon, the military mobilized forces near the southern border in response to increased Israeli military activity. Iran demonstrated its ongoing strategic influence in Iraq, as it prepared to arm militias amid parliamentary elections set for 11 November. Tehran's Foreign Minister asserted that Iran would persist in its uranium enrichment program, warning of repercussions for Western aggression and positioning itself as a key player in the region. Meanwhile, the U.S. Ambassador to Türkiye maintained that no war between Türkiye and Israel was imminent, signaling potential diplomatic rapprochement. In parallel, Hamas announced the discovery of an Israeli soldier's body previously held captive, further complicating the situation.

  • AFRICOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated significant developments in Sudan, where Mohamed Hamdan Dagolo, a warlord controlling substantial territory, sought either to establish a breakaway state or assert national dominance. Notably, a drone strike attributed to the paramilitary Rapid Support Forces (RSF) targeted a funeral in the Kordofan region, resulting in numerous fatalities among mourners. Concurrently, reports detailed the RSF rounding up hundreds of young men and boys near El-Fasher, highlighting the escalating violence and human rights violations in the area. The UN Secretary-General António Guterres condemned the spiraling violence and called for an urgent ceasefire in Sudan, reflecting international concern over the deteriorating situation. Additionally, the U.S. State Department ordered the departure of non-emergency personnel from Mali due to ongoing conflict involving al-Qaida-linked groups, signaling heightened insecurity in the region. Meanwhile, Nigeria's Foreign Minister Yusuf Tuggar refuted claims of religious persecution made by former President Donald Trump, asserting that Nigeria's constitution safeguards against such actions.
