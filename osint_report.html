Russian forces executed multiple drone strikes against Kharkiv on 2 April, injuring six civilians, and conducted further assaults across Pavlohrad and Sumy regions, damaging industrial infrastructure. Meanwhile, Ukrainian forces targeted Russian military positions in occupied Crimea, destroying high-value assets and inflicting significant personnel losses on Russian troops. In the Taiwan Strait, People's Liberation Army aircraft executed 25 sorties, with 16 breaching the median line, prompting an accelerated response from Taiwan, including defensive enhancements on Pratas Islands. Concurrently, US and Israeli forces carried out airstrikes on Iranian infrastructure, inflicting casualties and destruction while Iran retaliated with missile strikes against Israeli and US military targets, escalating regional tensions. Human Rights Watch reported that Burkina Faso's military executed more civilian fatalities than jihadist groups, further highlighting the prevailing security challenges in the region.

  • SOUTHCOM: During the reporting period, the United States Treasury Department lifted sanctions against Venezuelan interim President Delcy Rodríguez on 2 April, facilitating her administration's preparations to assume control of Citgo Petroleum's US subsidiary boards. FBI investigators arrived in Cuba on 2 April to probe a deadly 25 February shootout between Cuban Border Guard Troops and a US-flagged speedboat carrying arms and personnel, marking unusual bilateral cooperation amid ongoing tensions. No additional military deployments, border clashes, or coups occurred across Central and South America.

  • EUCOM: During the reporting period, Russian forces executed multiple drone strikes against Kharkiv on 2 April, conducting 11 attacks that injured six civilians, while additional drone assaults targeted Pavlohrad and Sumy regions, damaging industrial infrastructure. Russian aircraft dropped four guided aerial bombs on Druzhkivka in Donetsk Oblast on 2 April, wounding nine residents. Ukrainian forces conducted coordinated strikes on Russian military positions in occupied Crimea, destroying three Pantsir-S1 anti-aircraft systems and other high-value targets. Russian air defenses intercepted 147 Ukrainian drones over Russian territory as reported by the Ministry of Defence on 2 April. An An-26 transport aircraft crashed in occupied Crimea, killing a Russian general according to BBC reports. Ukrainian General Staff reported Russian personnel losses exceeded 1,300 killed or wounded and 59 artillery systems destroyed in the prior day ending 2 April. A failed Ukrainian mechanized assault near Pokrovsk sparked online debate over tactical execution. Russian aerial operations inflicted two fatalities across Ukraine amid preparations for an Easter prisoner exchange.

  • PACOM: **During the reporting period, People's Liberation Army aircraft executed 25 sorties around Taiwan, with 16 penetrating the median line into northern, central, and southwestern air defense identification zones, while People's Liberation Army Navy vessels operated nine surface combatants and two official ships in contiguous zones up to 0600 hours UTC+8 on 2 April; Taiwan's Ministry of National Defense monitored these incursions and calibrated responses accordingly. Taiwan accelerated defensive enhancements on Pratas Islands, including wharf renovations and deployment of Anping-class corvettes equipped for anti-ship missile integration, countering intensified People's Republic of China grey-zone operations featuring coast guard and maritime militia encroachments since prior year. Philippines' National Maritime Council condemned People's Republic of China maritime forces' aggressive maneuvers against Philippine vessels at West Philippine Sea reefs during March, as Beijing countered with infringement accusations against Manila. Reports surfaced detailing People's Republic of China dredging and island-building on an obscure South China Sea reef, expanding it into a potential major military outpost with airstrip and harbor capabilities.**

  • CENTCOM: During the reporting period, United States and Israeli forces executed multiple airstrikes on Iranian infrastructure, including two attacks on the B1 bridge linking Tehran and Karaj on 2 April that severed the structure and killed eight personnel, alongside precision strikes on steel plants near the capital and a century-old medical research centre in Tehran using bunker-buster munitions exceeding 2,200 kilograms near the Strait of Hormuz. President Donald Trump addressed the nation on 2 April, asserted that operational objectives against Iran neared completion, threatened escalation targeting bridges and electric power plants to revert Iran to a pre-industrial state, and disseminated video footage of the bridge destruction on 3 April. Iran retaliated with Wave 91 of Operation True Promise 4, launching ballistic missiles and drones at Tel Aviv and other Israeli cities on 3 April that forced millions into bunkers, struck United States military facilities in Kuwait and Saudi Arabia, and targeted the USS Abraham Lincoln carrier along with a covert United States Navy office and a United Arab Emirates radar installation. Iranian officials mocked Trump's rhetoric, vowed crushing counterstrikes preserving concealed capabilities, and reported over 2,000 fatalities from cumulative United States-Israeli actions since February while mobilising seven million reserves against potential ground incursions. Gulf neighbours absorbed Iranian missile impacts, exacerbating disruptions in the Strait of Hormuz and global energy supplies.

  • AFRICOM: During the reporting period, Human Rights Watch documented that Burkina Faso's military and allied forces executed more civilian fatalities than jihadist groups, surpassing 1,800 deaths since 2023, while Malian troops alongside Russian paramilitaries inflicted 918 civilian casualties in 2025 alone. Allied Democratic Forces militants conducted an assault in northeastern Democratic Republic of Congo, slaying at least 43 civilians according to Congolese army statements. United Nations experts disclosed that a Libyan military commander maintained combat drones notwithstanding the prevailing UN arms embargo.
