Date of Information: 06 March 2026

Reporting Period: 05 March - 06 March 2026

Distribution: Approved for public release; distribution is unlimited.

Combatant Commands: SOUTHCOM, EUCOM, PACOM, CENTCOM, AFRICOM

Intent: The intent of this report is to provide a high-level overview of significant military and geopolitical developments during the reporting period. The report consists of open-source reporting and updates on evolving conflicts, diplomatic initiatives, and strategic military actions involving countries of interest in the following Combatant Commands: SOUTHCOM, EUCOM, PACOM, CENTCOM, AFRICOM.

Executive Summary: During the reporting period, U.S. Southern Command hosted the inaugural Americas Counter Cartel Conference in Florida, where senior U.S. leadership urged regional partners to intensify operations against transnational criminal organizations while signaling potential unilateral U.S. action if threats persist. In Europe, Ukrainian forces conducted drone strikes on Russian military assets in occupied Crimea as Russia continued attacks on Ukrainian rail infrastructure; both sides completed a 500-person prisoner exchange, and Kyiv postponed U.S.-brokered peace talks while offering air defense support against Iranian Shahed drones in the Middle East. In the Indo-Pacific, China announced a 7% increase in its 2026 defense budget, condemned U.S.-Israeli strikes on Iran, and maintained diplomatic engagement with Washington ahead of planned trade talks and a leadership summit. In the Central Command theater, U.S. and Israeli forces continued large-scale strikes on Iranian regime infrastructure while Iran and proxy groups conducted reduced but persistent missile and drone attacks against Israel and U.S. regional facilities, contributing to ongoing disruptions to regional airspace, shipping, and energy infrastructure. In Africa, the United States temporarily closed its embassy in Abuja due to protest threats as regional leaders assessed security and economic impacts from Middle East escalation, while a landslide at a rebel-controlled mine in the Democratic Republic of the Congo killed approximately 200 individuals.

  • SOUTHCOM: During the reporting period, SOUTHCOM hosted the inaugural Americas Counter Cartel Conference at its headquarters in Doral, Florida, where Secretary of Defense Pete Hegseth addressed defense and security leaders from Caribbean, Central American, and South American nations to urge aggressive offensive actions against drug cartels and transnational criminal organizations, emphasizing that the United States stood ready to act unilaterally if partners failed to confront threats to hemispheric and homeland security. SOUTHCOM Commander Gen. Francis L. Donovan reaffirmed U.S. support for a free, secure, and prosperous Venezuela through alignment with the United States to counter transnational threats, enhance citizen benefits, and promote Western Hemisphere stability in coordination with the U.S. Embassy in Venezuela. No additional operational announcements, kinetic strikes, or direct military engagements occurred within SOUTHCOM's area of responsibility, and SOUTHCOM maintained no reported involvement in concurrent U.S. military activities against Iran in the Middle East.

  • EUCOM: During the reporting period, Ukrainian forces conducted targeted drone strikes against Russian assets in occupied Crimea, damaging production workshops at the Yevpatoria Aircraft Repair Plant and destroying two Pantsir-S2 air defense systems near a military airfield in Dzhankoi. Russia intensified attacks on Ukrainian railway infrastructure, launching drone strikes that damaged a train in Mykolaiv Oblast and attempted to hit moving trains in other regions, while Ukrainian defenses intercepted numerous threats including Shahed drones. Both sides completed a major prisoner exchange over two days, with Ukraine repatriating 500 servicemen and civilians (200 on the first day and 300 on the second) in a reciprocal swap coordinated through recent trilateral negotiations. Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelenskyy announced postponement of planned U.S.-brokered trilateral peace talks with Russia due to the ongoing U.S.-Israeli operations against Iran, which diverted necessary diplomatic signals. Zelenskyy also directed Ukrainian support for U.S. and Gulf state air defense efforts against Iranian Shahed drones in the Middle East, including provision of interceptor drones and specialists. No confirmed major frontline territorial changes occurred in eastern or southern sectors, though Russian forces maintained pressure in directions such as Slovyansk and Pokrovsk amid reports of marginal positional adjustments and high attrition; northern Sumy Oblast remained stable without advances.

  • PACOM: During the reporting period, China announced a 7% increase in its 2026 defense budget during the opening of the annual Two Sessions to support military modernization efforts amid heightened strategic competition with the United States. China condemned ongoing U.S.-Israeli military operations against Iran as unacceptable violations of sovereignty, regime-change attempts, and infringements on international law, with Foreign Ministry spokespersons and officials urging an immediate halt to strikes, prevention of further escalation, and return to dialogue while expressing grave concern over spillover risks to Gulf states' security, civilian infrastructure, and global energy flows through the Strait of Hormuz critical to Chinese oil imports. Beijing pledged to take necessary measures to safeguard its energy security and protect Chinese nationals in the region amid reported evacuations, while reaffirming opposition to the use of force in international relations without direct military involvement or support for Iran. China maintained diplomatic communications signaling continued multi-level engagement with the United States, including preparations for mid-March trade discussions in Paris and the anticipated Trump-Xi summit in Beijing at the end of March, while upholding core interests on sovereignty, security, and development. No direct U.S.-China military incidents, confrontations, exercises, or developments occurred in the Indo-Pacific, Taiwan Strait, or South China Sea domains.

  • CENTCOM: During the reporting period, U.S. and Israeli forces sustained intensive airstrikes on Iranian regime infrastructure, including broad-scale waves targeting Tehran command centers, underground bunkers associated with the late Supreme Leader Khamenei, remaining air defense elements, missile production sites, and naval assets, while the U.S. military reported ongoing destruction of mobile ballistic missile launchers and maintained air superiority over key Iranian regions. Israel expanded operations against Hezbollah in Lebanon with at least 11 to 26 rounds of strikes on southern Beirut suburbs, hitting executive council headquarters, high-rise buildings, drone warehouses, and other command sites, prompting evacuation warnings and heavy civilian displacement amid retaliatory rocket and drone launches toward northern Israel. Iran conducted continued but reduced retaliatory barrages, launching ballistic missiles and drones that targeted Israel—with intercepts and impacts near Tel Aviv causing injuries and minor damage—and struck or attempted strikes on U.S. military facilities across Gulf states, including confirmed hits or proximity effects on bases in Qatar (Al Udeid), Bahrain (Fifth Fleet area), Kuwait (Camp Arifjan and others with prior casualties), UAE, Saudi Arabia, and additional sites, though defensive intercepts mitigated many threats and no major new large-scale missile waves materialized beyond prior intensity. Proxy forces including Iraqi militias and Hezbollah maintained supporting attacks on U.S. positions and Israel, while disruptions persisted in regional airspace, shipping through the Strait of Hormuz, and energy infrastructure; cumulative casualties from strikes on Iran exceeded 940 reported deaths, with U.S. service member losses at least six since the campaign's start and Israeli civilian fatalities around 11 from Iranian projectiles. No significant new nuclear facility degradation beyond earlier assessments occurred, as operations prioritized regime decision-making nodes, missile stocks, and leadership remnants amid indications the conflict could extend beyond initial projections.

  • AFRICOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that the United States Centers for Disease Control warned citizens about potential travel risks due to the spread of a fatal disease in 32 countries, including several popular vacation destinations. In geopolitical developments, Russian President Vladimir Putin held discussions with Central African Republic leader Faustin-Archange Touadera in Moscow, while increasing tensions in the Middle East raised concerns for Africa, with experts noting potential threats to military bases and disruptions to oil supply chains. The United States temporarily closed its embassy in Abuja, Nigeria, and rescheduled appointments due to the threat of protests. In a tragic incident, the government of the Democratic Republic of the Congo reported 200 fatalities after a landslide at a mine controlled by the M23 rebel group, which has declined to comment on the situation. Additionally, the conflict involving Israel, the US, and Iran reportedly influenced trade and security alliances throughout Africa.
