Open Source Intelligence (OSINT) Report

Date of Access: 24 December 2025

Date of Information: 24 December 2025

Reporting Period: 23 December - 24 December 2025

Distribution: Approved for public release; distribution is unlimited.

Combatant Commands: SOUTHCOM, EUCOM, PACOM, CENTCOM, AFRICOM

Intent: The intent of this report is to provide a high-level overview of significant military and geopolitical developments during the reporting period. The report consists of open-source reporting and updates on evolving conflicts, diplomatic initiatives, and strategic military actions involving countries of interest in the following Combatant Commands: SOUTHCOM, EUCOM, PACOM, CENTCOM, AFRICOM.

Executive Summary: During the reporting period, significant military and geopolitical developments occurred across multiple regions. In South America, U.S. President Donald Trump escalated economic pressure on Venezuela by announcing plans to keep or sell oil seized from tankers, while the U.S. Coast Guard confiscated another vessel suspected of transporting sanctioned oil. In Europe, Russia conducted a substantial drone and missile strike across Ukraine, resulting in casualties and power outages, prompting NATO nations to increase support for Ukraine’s air defense capabilities. In the Pacific, Trump proposed a new class of battleships to bolster naval strength amid tensions with China and Venezuela. Concurrently, Pakistan and Libya engaged in a major military equipment deal, signaling shifts in defense partnerships. In the Middle East, tensions rose as Israel faced scrutiny for its military actions in Lebanon and reaffirmed its presence in Gaza, while Iran conducted missile drills amid fears of conflict. In Africa, a significant law enforcement operation targeted cybercrime, and Sudan’s Prime Minister proposed a peace plan amid escalating violence, highlighting ongoing regional instability.

  • SOUTHCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that U.S. President Donald Trump announced intentions to keep or sell oil seized from two oil tankers off the coast of Venezuela, escalating economic pressure on the government of Nicolás Maduro. The U.S. Coast Guard had recently seized a second tanker suspected of carrying sanctioned oil, a development that Trump framed as part of a broader strategy against Maduro's regime. Trump warned Maduro not to “play tough” amid growing support for Venezuela from China and Russia, which discredited reports of an evacuation of Russian embassy staff. In response, Maduro rejected Trump’s warnings and criticized the U.S. military presence in the Caribbean, calling for its cessation. Concurrently, the U.S. military launched a strike against a drug trafficking vessel in the eastern Pacific Ocean, killing one individual, highlighting ongoing U.S. operations against drug trafficking in the region.

  • EUCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that Russia launched a significant drone and missile attack across Ukraine, resulting in at least three fatalities, including a child, and extensive power outages in multiple regions. Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelenskyy confirmed that the strikes affected homes and energy facilities, contributing to emergency power cuts as the nation prepared for Christmas celebrations. In response to the escalating situation, Polish military aircraft scrambled to secure airspace after strikes targeted western Ukraine near the Poland-Ukraine border. Amidst these developments, NATO nations pledged an additional $500 million to enhance Ukraine's air defense and energy infrastructure, highlighting the collective commitment to supporting Ukraine against Russian aggression. Additionally, a retired Taiwanese military officer advised Taiwan to enhance its defense capabilities by acquiring cost-effective and survivable weapons to counter potential threats from China, underscoring ongoing geopolitical tensions in the region.

  • PACOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that U.S. President Donald Trump announced plans for a new class of battleships, named the "Trump-class,” which will feature advanced technology and nuclear capabilities, emphasizing a revival of American shipbuilding to enhance naval strength against competitors like China. On 24 October, he warned Venezuelan President Nicolás Maduro to consider stepping down amid a U.S. naval blockade designed to curb oil exports from Venezuela, as tensions escalated with China and Russia supporting Maduro’s regime. In Taiwan, a retired admiral advocated for bolstering the island's defense capabilities with inexpensive, mobile weapons to counter increasing Chinese military threats. In addition, China condemned the U.S. for what it described as the arbitrary seizure of vessels, particularly following U.S. actions against a tanker bound for China while supporting Venezuela. Pakistan and Libya entered into a military equipment deal, valued at over $4 billion, amid rising geopolitical dynamics, highlighting a trend of countries reassessing defense partnerships and capabilities.

  • CENTCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that an American journalist injured during an Israeli airstrike in Lebanon called for accountability from Washington. In related developments, Israel faced criticism for its environmental impact in southern Lebanon, with reports highlighting destructive military tactics against local ecosystems. Israeli Defense Minister Israel Katz reaffirmed plans for a permanent military presence in the Gaza Strip, igniting fears of continued conflict and displacement among Palestinians. Concurrently, medical supplies remained severely restricted in Gaza, contributing to a rising death toll among patients awaiting treatment. Tensions escalated as Iran conducted missile drills amid fears of a potential conflict with Israel, prompting Israeli officials to raise alarms with U.S. counterparts. In Lebanon, the army dismantled an Israeli surveillance device, while clashes in Aleppo were reported as Kurdish-led forces faced pressure to integrate into the Syrian military.

  • AFRICOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that Bari Weiss, the editor-in-chief of CBS News, censored a segment of the 60 Minutes program discussing deported individuals in El Salvador. As a result, the segment circulated online. In a significant law enforcement operation, Operation Sentinel resulted in hundreds of arrests across 19 African countries and successfully recovered approximately $3 million linked to cybercrime. Meanwhile, the United States planned to recall 30 diplomats in a move criticized as politically motivated, as part of an "America First" initiative, with the American Foreign Service Association noting no reasons were provided. In response to ongoing violence, Sudan’s Prime Minister Kamil Idris proposed a peace plan to the United Nations Security Council to end the conflict, while reports surfaced about the dire conditions faced by thousands fleeing violence in the Democratic Republic of Congo. Additionally, Nigeria reported the release of 130 Catholic schoolchildren who were abducted, alongside troubling developments of continued targeted abductions of students by armed groups in the country.
