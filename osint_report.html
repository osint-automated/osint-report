Executive Summary: During the reporting period, significant military and geopolitical events unfolded globally. In Mexico, the killing of drug kingpin Nemesio “El Mencho” Oseguera Cervantes triggered retaliatory violence and increased concerns for tourism, especially in Puerto Vallarta. NATO officials acknowledged the need for enhanced weapons production capabilities due to Ukraine's ongoing conflictt.   • SOUTHCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that the killing of notorious drug kingpin Nemesio “El Mencho” Oseguera Cervantes occurred on 24 October during a precision military raid in Jalisco, Mexico. This operation sparked retaliatory violence in Mexico, predominantly involving American-made firearms. Following El Mencho’s death, concerns arose regarding the impact on tourism in Mexico, particularly in popular destinations like Puerto Vallarta, where Canadian airline regulators announced an investigation into recent flight disruptions. Reports highlighted that El Mencho maintained detailed records of bribes paid to local, state, and federal law enforcement, demonstrating extensive corruption. As the situation unfolded, discussions among bishops from the Texas-Mexico border addressed their pastoral roles amid the escalating violence. The ongoing influence of El Mencho's cartel in the United States persisted, raising alerts about narcotics trafficking continuity despite his removal.

  • EUCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated significant military and geopolitical developments impacting global security dynamics. NATO officials acknowledged Ukraine's ongoing war revealed the urgent need for improvements in the West's weapons production capabilities. Concurrently, diplomatic tensions escalated as world leaders expressed concerns over the U.S.-Israeli strikes on Iran, with several nations calling for restraint to prevent broader conflicts, especially in the Middle East. Russia condemned these actions as unprovoked aggression, warning of potential catastrophes stemming from the escalation. In Europe, Denmark's intelligence services revealed risks of foreign interference in its upcoming general election, primarily citing Russian threats. Furthermore, the situation in Ukraine continued to deteriorate, with reports of ongoing Russian operations in the Kharkov and Dnepropetrovsk regions and civilians increasingly suffering from the war's ramifications in Kyiv.

  • PACOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that a Chinese military spokesperson criticized the Philippines for allegedly disrupting peace and stability in the South China Sea by involving external countries in joint patrols. On 24 February, combined forces from Japan, the Philippines, and the United States engaged in a Multilateral Maritime Cooperative Activity in the Philippines' Exclusive Economic Zone, reinforcing their commitment to regional security. A U.S. think tank recommended that Taiwan adopt a "hellscape" strategy involving a significant drone presence in the Taiwan Strait to deter potential Chinese aggression.

  • CENTCOM: During the reporting period, the United States and Israel conducted sustained joint airstrikes under Operations Epic Fury and Roaring Lion against Iranian nuclear infrastructure, ballistic missile production facilities, IRGC command centers, air defense networks, and senior leadership sites, confirming the death of Supreme Leader Ayatollah Ali Khamenei along with multiple high-ranking military officials including Revolutionary Guards commander Mohammad Pakpour and security adviser Ali Shamkhani, and causing over 200 fatalities in Iran per Red Crescent reports, including significant civilian losses from a strike on a girls' elementary school in Minab that killed at least 148 children. Iran retaliated with successive barrages of ballistic missiles and drones targeting Israeli population centers—resulting in at least nine civilian deaths in Beit Shemesh and over 120 injuries across Jerusalem and Tel Aviv areas—and U.S. military installations throughout the Gulf region, including Al Udeid Air Base in Qatar, Al Dhafra in the UAE, Fifth Fleet headquarters in Bahrain, and facilities in Kuwait, Jordan, Iraq, and Saudi Arabia, supplemented by proxy drone strikes and maritime attacks near the Strait of Hormuz. U.S. Central Command assessed minimal damage to bases from successful intercepts with no operational disruptions, but confirmed three American service members killed in action and five seriously wounded during the overall campaign, alongside minor shrapnel injuries and concussions among others returning to duty; no additional American fatalities occurred from direct Iranian retaliatory strikes on regional bases.

  • AFRICOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated significant military and geopolitical developments. The United Nations cited that South Sudan's political and military leaders have taken actions undermining the 2018 Revitalized Peace Agreement, raising concerns over potential mass atrocities and renewed conflict. Tensions escalated as the UN Human Rights Council debated the deteriorating situation, amidst a humanitarian crisis exacerbated by the conflict in neighboring Sudan. Meanwhile, on the same day, Nigeria and Cameroon signed a new aviation safety agreement to enhance coordination for search and rescue operations in their shared airspace. Additionally, South African President Cyril Ramaphosa announced the deployment of military personnel in high-crime areas to assist police forces amid concerns over public safety. These developments highlight an interconnected landscape of military readiness and evolving geopolitics across multiple regions.
