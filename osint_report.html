Open Source Intelligence (OSINT) Report

Date of Access: 09 March 2026

Date of Information: 09 March 2026

Reporting Period: 08 March - 09 March 2026

Distribution: Approved for public release; distribution is unlimited.

Combatant Commands: SOUTHCOM, EUCOM, PACOM, CENTCOM, AFRICOM

Intent: The intent of this report is to provide a high-level overview of significant military and geopolitical developments during the reporting period. The report consists of open-source reporting and updates on evolving conflicts, diplomatic initiatives, and strategic military actions involving countries of interest in the following Combatant Commands: SOUTHCOM, EUCOM, PACOM, CENTCOM, AFRICOM.

Executive Summary: During the reporting period, the United States advanced regional security initiatives and sustained military operations across multiple combatant commands amid escalating conflict in the Middle East. In SOUTHCOM, President Donald Trump hosted the “Shield of the Americas” summit in Miami, establishing the 17-nation Americas Counter-Cartel Coalition to coordinate counter-narcoterrorism operations, intelligence sharing, and potential kinetic actions against cartel leadership, while U.S. Special Forces continued advisory support to Ecuadorian counter-cartel operations under Operation Southern Spear. In EUCOM, Ukrainian forces conducted precision missile strikes on Russian military infrastructure in occupied Donetsk while Russia executed large-scale drone and missile attacks against Ukrainian cities and energy targets, with only marginal frontline changes and a completed 500-for-500 prisoner exchange. In PACOM, China announced a 7 percent defense budget increase, condemned U.S.-Israeli strikes on Iran, and prepared for upcoming U.S.-China diplomatic engagements. In CENTCOM, U.S. and Israeli forces intensified strikes on Iranian military and energy infrastructure as Iran launched retaliatory missile and drone attacks against Israel and regional states, raising regional casualties and pushing oil prices above $100 per barrel. In AFRICOM, South Sudanese forces prepared offensive operations in Jonglei State and a jihadist attack in northern Benin killed 15 soldiers, while other African conflict theaters reported no significant operational changes.

  • SOUTHCOM: During the reporting period, U.S. President Donald Trump hosted the "Shield of the Americas" summit at Trump National Doral in Miami on 7 March, where leaders from approximately 12 aligned Latin American and Caribbean nations—including Argentina, Ecuador, Guyana, Honduras, Paraguay, El Salvador, Dominican Republic, Costa Rica, Bolivia, Chile, Panama, and Trinidad and Tobago—formalized the Americas Counter-Cartel Coalition, a 17-nation military partnership focused on eradicating drug cartels designated as narco-terrorist threats through joint operations, intelligence sharing, border security enhancements, and lethal force commitments. Trump signed a "Commitment to Countering Cartel Criminal Activity" declaration, offered U.S. missile strikes against cartel leaders, criticized Mexico as the epicenter of cartel violence under President Claudia Sheinbaum, and emphasized unilateral U.S. readiness while excluding major left-leaning economies such as Brazil, Mexico, and Colombia from participation amid efforts to counter Chinese influence in regional infrastructure, ports, lithium, and telecommunications. Ongoing U.S.-Ecuador joint operations under Operation Southern Spear persisted with U.S. Special Forces providing advisory, intelligence, and logistical support to Ecuadorian forces targeting groups like Los Lobos and Los Choneros to disrupt cocaine flows through Ecuadorian ports, though no new kinetic actions or escalations occurred in the period. Paraguayan President Santiago Peña visited U.S. Southern Command headquarters in Doral on March 6 to discuss collaborative anti-narcoterrorism efforts and strengthen bilateral defense ties. No reported major cartel retaliatory violence, territorial disputes advancements in Guyana-Venezuela Essequibo tensions, or new geopolitical shifts emerged in Central or South America beyond routine summit follow-ups and preparations, despite broader regional concerns over potential economic pressures from concurrent U.S. military commitments in the Middle East.

  • EUCOM: During the reporting period, Ukrainian forces conducted precision strikes on Russian assets in occupied Donetsk Oblast, using ATACMS and Storm Shadow/SCALP-EG missiles to target and destroy a Shahed drone storage and launch base at Donetsk airport, while additional drone operations damaged Russian military infrastructure including air defense systems and support facilities. Russia launched large-scale combined drone and missile barrages against Ukrainian targets, including energy and railway infrastructure across multiple regions such as Kharkiv (where a ballistic missile strike on a residential building killed at least 10 civilians and injured others) and central areas, with Ukrainian air defenses intercepting significant portions of the threats amid reports of 480 drones and dozens of missiles in one wave. Both sides completed a major prisoner-of-war exchange over two days, repatriating 500 personnel each (200 on the initial day and 300 on the second) through coordinated diplomatic channels. Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelenskyy announced the deployment of Ukrainian military experts and personnel with counter-Shahed drone experience to unspecified Middle Eastern states starting 9 March to share four years of operational knowledge against Iranian-origin drones, while postponing trilateral U.S.-Russia-Ukraine peace talks due to diplomatic priorities tied to concurrent U.S.-Israeli operations against Iran. Frontline positions showed marginal Russian advances in eastern sectors such as near Pokrovsk, Lyman, and Zaporizhzhia directions amid high attrition, with no confirmed major Ukrainian territorial gains or shifts in northern Sumy Oblast.

  • PACOM: During the reporting period, China announced a 7 percent increase in its 2026 defense budget during the National People's Congress Two Sessions to advance military modernization amid ongoing strategic competition with the United States. China condemned U.S.-Israeli military operations against Iran as unacceptable violations of sovereignty, regime-change attempts, and breaches of international law, with Foreign Minister Wang Yi urging an immediate cessation of strikes, protection of shipping through the Strait of Hormuz, prevention of escalation, and resumption of dialogue through diplomatic channels with regional and global actors. Beijing expressed grave concern over spillover effects disrupting energy security, global oil and LNG supplies critical to Chinese imports, and civilian infrastructure, while pledging protective measures for Chinese nationals via evacuations and preparedness. China signaled continued commitment to multi-level engagement with the United States, including thorough preparations for mid-March trade talks in Paris between USTR Scott Bessent and Vice Premier He Lifeng, as well as the anticipated Trump-Xi summit in Beijing at the end of March, while reaffirming defense of core interests on sovereignty, security, and development. No direct U.S.-China military incidents, confrontations, exercises, or developments occurred in the Indo-Pacific, Taiwan Strait, or South China Sea domains.

  • CENTCOM: During the reporting period, U.S. and Israeli forces conducted intensified airstrikes on Iranian targets, including a wide-scale wave against regime infrastructure in Tehran, Isfahan, and southern provinces that struck oil storage depots, refining facilities, fuel sites supporting military operations, drone bases, missile tunnels, and air defense elements, while satellite imagery confirmed destruction at Ahvaz drone facilities, Isfahan airport structures, and additional military garrisons. Israel executed renewed strikes on Hezbollah-linked sites in Beirut's southern suburbs and southern Lebanon, contributing to nearly 400 Lebanese deaths from cumulative operations. Iran launched fresh retaliatory missile and drone barrages toward Israel, triggering sirens in Tel Aviv, Jerusalem, and other areas with impacts or shrapnel causing injuries to at least six civilians in Tel Aviv and Petah Tikva, while additional salvos targeted Gulf states including Bahrain, Qatar, UAE (with attempts near Dubai airport), and Saudi Arabia, though defensive intercepts limited many effects amid prior pledges by President Masoud Pezeshkian to halt attacks on neighbors unless their territories facilitated strikes against Iran. Iran named Mojtaba Khamenei as the new supreme leader following the earlier killing of Ali Khamenei, prompting public celebrations in Tehran and vows from hardline officials to sustain prolonged resistance; the IRGC warned of an emerging energy war after strikes on Iranian assets. U.S. Central Command reported a seventh service member fatality from the campaign, with President Trump demanding Iran's unconditional surrender and signaling further dramatic escalation of firepower. Cumulative casualties exceeded 1,200 deaths in Iran, around 11 in Israel from Iranian projectiles, and nearly 400 in Lebanon; no major new nuclear facility strikes or ground incursions occurred beyond ongoing degradation of missile, drone, and energy capabilities as the conflict entered its second week with oil prices surpassing $100 per barrel amid regional disruptions.

  • AFRICOM: During the reporting period, South Sudan People's Defense Forces issued an evacuation order to UNMISS, NGOs, and civilians in opposition-held Akobo town in Jonglei state on 7-8 March, prompting thousands to flee amid preparations for a military assault on the area and surrounding regions following intensified confrontations since December 2025. The African Union Commission Chairperson condemned a jihadist attack in northern Benin near the Niger border that killed 15 soldiers, calling for accelerated deployment of the ECOWAS regional brigade under the African Standby Force to counter terrorism and violent extremism in West African border zones. The United States, European Union, and International Contact Group for the Great Lakes reiterated profound concern over continued ceasefire violations in eastern Democratic Republic of Congo involving M23 rebels, urging all parties to recommit to negotiations and halt fighting in alignment with prior Doha and Washington agreements. No major new kinetic engagements, territorial shifts, or confirmed operations materialized in Sudan (beyond prior recaptures like Bara in North Kordofan), Somalia against al-Shabaab, Libya, Ethiopia's Tigray region, or other active theaters, though indirect geopolitical risks persisted from the U.S.-Israeli conflict with Iran, including potential disruptions to Red Sea security, Horn of Africa stability, elevated energy costs affecting African economies, and possible resource diversion from counterterrorism initiatives in the Sahel and elsewhere. The East African Community advanced logistical preparations for its ordinary Summit in Arusha on March 7 without reported military developments.
