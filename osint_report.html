Intent: The intent of this report is to provide a high-level overview of significant military and geopolitical developments during the reporting period. The report consists of open-source reporting and updates on evolving conflicts, diplomatic initiatives, and strategic military actions involving countries of interest in the following Combatant Commands: SOUTHCOM, EUCOM, PACOM, CENTCOM, AFRICOM.

Executive Summary: During the reporting period, critical military and geopolitical developments unfolded across multiple regions. The U.S. military conducted operations against a Venezuela-linked oil tanker in the Indian Ocean, while Israeli airstrikes in Gaza disrupted a U.S.-brokered ceasefire amid rising tensions between Israel and Hamas. In Europe, Russia's delivery of stealth jets to Algeria prompted concerns of potential U.S. sanctions, and the Kremlin restored dialogue with Paris regarding Ukraine, though no peace talks were scheduled. In the Middle East, Iran faced economic downturns exacerbated by government crackdowns, while South Sudan's conflict led to a worsening humanitarian crisis. Somalia's defense agreement with Saudi Arabia and Ethiopia's alleged training of Sudanese forces indicated rising regional tensions. Additionally, Transparency International reported a global decline in anti-corruption efforts, including significant decreases in established democracies.

  • SOUTHCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that the United Nations awaited confirmation from the United States regarding its nearly $4 billion debt to the organization. The U.S. military conducted operations in the Indian Ocean, boarding a sanctioned oil tanker after a pursuit originating from the Caribbean, amidst accusations of "piracy" related to its blockade of Venezuelan oil. In Cuba, Russian officials promised to provide "all possible assistance" as the U.S. tightened its embargo, which has severely affected Cuba's fuel supplies. Additionally, Transparency International reported a decline in anti-corruption efforts among established democracies, including the United States, which recorded its lowest score under the current methodology. Cuban officials accused the U.S. of trying to undermine the political resolve of Cubans, while the U.S. State Department insisted its oil restrictions were not exacerbating the humanitarian situation on the island.

  • EUCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that Russian stealth jets arrived in North Africa, as captured on video by an Algerian farmer. In Ukraine, deaths from hypothermia rose due to Russian attacks on the energy system, with at least 10 fatalities reported. Senior Lieutenant Serhii Andrieiev described the operational challenges faced by drones on the Ukrainian frontlines in cold weather as akin to "Russian roulette." The U.S. government viewed the Russian aircraft deal with Algeria as problematic, threatening potential sanctions. On the diplomatic front, the Kremlin confirmed the restoration of technical-level dialogue with Paris and announced no date was set for the next round of peace talks on Ukraine. In NATO's eastern front, U.S. Vice President JD Vance pledged nearly $9 billion in potential investments to Armenia, Russia’s former ally, as Japan signed up for NATO’s Ukraine arms pipeline. Additionally, Russian Foreign Minister Sergei Lavrov warned of a full military response if provoked by NATO’s activities. The International Olympic Committee (IOC) blocked Ukrainian athlete Vladyslav Heraskevych from wearing a helmet tribute to war victims, prompting accusations of betrayal from the athlete.

  • PACOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated significant military and geopolitical developments. The United States planned to relinquish several senior NATO command posts to European countries, as outlined by diplomats on 9 February 2026 in Brussels, Belgium. Concurrently, the Philippines' ambassador to Washington expressed confidence that the U.S. would support Manila against China's assertiveness in the disputed South China Sea. On the technology front, China launched an emergency messaging service utilizing the BeiDou Navigation Satellite System on 10 February 2026 in Tokyo, Japan. In response to U.S. actions against Russian energy companies, Russian Foreign Minister Sergey Lavrov accused the U.S. of coercing other nations, including India, to reduce ties with Russian oil suppliers. In the Indian Ocean, U.S. forces boarded a tanker linked to Venezuela as part of continued efforts to target Venezuelan oil exports. Additionally, Japanese politician Sanae Takaichi's recent victory could reshape Japan's approach to geopolitical alignment in the Indo-Pacific.

  • CENTCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that significant military and geopolitical developments occurred in the Middle East. Israeli airstrikes in Gaza reportedly killed five Palestinians on 10 February, undermining a recently established US-brokered ceasefire. Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu traveled to Washington, D.C., for discussions with President Donald Trump focused on Iran and the ongoing conflict in Gaza, highlighting escalating tensions in the region. Meanwhile, accusations against Israel emerged regarding the use of chemicals to damage farmland in Syria and Lebanon, exacerbating the humanitarian situation. In Iran, authorities shut down private businesses linked to protests as the economy faced downturns following government crackdowns. The United Nations also announced plans to withdraw most of its peacekeepers from Lebanon by mid-2027 due to funding shortages, reflecting decreased support for stability efforts in the region.

  • AFRICOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated significant developments in military and geopolitical contexts across various regions. Heavy fighting between government forces and opposition fighters in South Sudan resulted in the displacement of civilians, contributing to a worsening humanitarian crisis due to impeded aid operations caused by attacks and looting. In the Horn of Africa, Somalia signed a defense agreement with Saudi Arabia amidst regional tensions involving the recognition of Somaliland by Israel, while Ethiopia allegedly hosted a secret training camp for Sudan's Rapid Support Forces. Meanwhile, Russia’s delivery of stealth jets to Algeria raised concerns in the U.S. government regarding potential sanctions. In Nigeria, the federal government reopened the Nigeria-Niger border at the Kamba corridor to enhance trade ties after disruptions, and the Nigerian Senate approved real-time election result transmission following public outcry. The Democratic Republic of the Congo saw a transition in peacekeeping efforts as South Africa planned to withdraw its troops after nearly three decades. Additionally, Transparency International reported a global decline in anti-corruption efforts, highlighting slipping scores in the U.S., UK, Canada, and Sweden.
