Open Source Intelligence (OSINT) Report

Date of Access: 19 November 2025

Date of Information: 19 November 2025

Reporting Period: 18 November - 19 November 2025

Distribution: Approved for public release; distribution is unlimited.

Combatant Commands: SOUTHCOM, EUCOM, PACOM, CENTCOM, AFRICOM

Intent: The intent of this report is to provide a high-level overview of significant military and geopolitical developments during the reporting period. The report consists of open-source reporting and updates on evolving conflicts, diplomatic initiatives, and strategic military actions involving countries of interest in the following Combatant Commands: SOUTHCOM, EUCOM, PACOM, CENTCOM, AFRICOM.

Executive Summary: During the reporting period, significant military and geopolitical events unfolded across key regions. In South America, U.S. military presence in the Caribbean increased with the deployment of the USS Gerald R. Ford and 15,000 troops, reflecting a stance on potential regime change in Venezuela amid diplomatic discussions. In Europe, the situation in Ukraine prompted concerns over Russian sabotage efforts, while Ukrainian President Zelenskyy sought to strengthen peace talks. In the South China Sea, Philippine forces faced interference from Chinese vessels, as Japan heightened alerts due to tensions over Taiwan. In the Middle East, a humanitarian crisis developed in Gaza, with the UN endorsing a peace plan linked to Hamas demilitarization, which was rejected by the group. Simultaneously, military developments in Lebanon and Jordan’s reinstatement of conscription were noted, and the situation in Africa highlighted growing violence in the Sahel and instability in the Democratic Republic of Congo, alongside looming food insecurity.

  • SOUTHCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that U.S. President Donald Trump maintained a firm stance on not ruling out military action against Venezuela amidst escalating tensions. The United States deployed the USS Gerald R. Ford, the world’s largest aircraft carrier, to the Caribbean and sent approximately 15,000 troops to the region. This military buildup raised concerns of a potential regime change in Venezuela, with former U.S. officials asserting that the operations appeared less focused on counter-narcotics and more aligned with regime change objectives. Concurrently, Trump expressed openness to discussing diplomatic solutions with Venezuelan President Nicolás Maduro, who also signaled a willingness to engage in talks. The U.S. further heightened pressure on Venezuela by designating the Cartel de los Soles as a terrorist organization, while opposition leader María Corina Machado spoke of a vision for a post-Maduro era amidst the mounting U.S. military presence.

  • EUCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that the drone conflict in Ukraine prompted urgent calls for innovation in defense technology, highlighting an industry struggle to keep pace with rapid changes. On 12 November, Polish Prime Minister Donald Tusk stated that two Ukrainian nationals, allegedly collaborating with Russian intelligence, were suspected of causing a railway explosion in Poland, which damaged a crucial line used for transporting goods to Ukraine. This incident raised alarms about Russian sabotage efforts in the region. Meanwhile, Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelenskyy planned to meet with U.S. envoy Donald Trump’s representative to intensify peace talks as Russia gained ground in Ukraine’s Zaporizhia region, specifically around Huliaipole. Additionally, the EU’s defense commissioner emphasized that Europe was unprepared to effectively respond to potential Russian drone attacks, underscoring a pressing need for stronger defense integration with Ukraine’s military capabilities.

  • PACOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that Philippine forces supplied a territorial outpost in a disputed shoal in the South China Sea, while Chinese government vessels interfered by jamming communications. Japan remained on alert, advising its citizens in China to adopt enhanced safety measures amid escalating tensions related to Japanese Prime Minister Sanae Takaichi's remarks about Taiwan, leading to heightened military and coastguard activities from Beijing in the region. The top U.S. admiral acknowledged China's significant naval expansion and emphasized the importance of collaboration with South Korea and Japan for countering Chinese influence, further underscoring regional security concerns. In response to ongoing geopolitical shifts, U.S. sanctions against Russian oil firms impacted Russian oil revenues, indicating broader economic tensions. Meanwhile, Russia reported increasing international interest in the Northern Sea Route, including participation from even non-friendly nations.

  • CENTCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated a deteriorating humanitarian situation in Gaza as winter rains raised fears of disease and death among displaced Palestinians, prompting aid agencies to call for additional shelter. The UN Security Council endorsed a peace plan proposed by former President Donald Trump, linking reconstruction efforts in Gaza to the demilitarization of Hamas, which the group rejected, stating that the resolution failed to meet their demands. Meanwhile, a water provider in Gaza suspended services after Hamas detained a company employee, affecting water access for over 1 million people. In military developments, Israel's military reported the killing of a Hezbollah operative in southern Lebanon. Jordan's parliament reinstated mandatory conscription for men starting in early 2026. Additionally, US President Trump welcomed Saudi Crown Prince Mohammed bin Salman to the White House, discussing trade, security, and potential arms sales, including F-35 jets, all while reinforcing Israel's stance against Palestinian statehood. Reports surfaced of Israeli actions against Palestinians in the West Bank, where violence continued to escalate, and claims emerged that a Polish company supplied explosives for Israel's operations in Gaza. These updates highlight ongoing tensions and the complexities surrounding reconstruction and peace efforts in the region.

  • AFRICOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated significant military and geopolitical developments across various regions. On 25 October, UN Secretary-General Antonio Guterres called for Sahel nations to unite against growing violence from armed groups, stressing the need for collaboration amid humanitarian crises. On 24 October, reports highlighted that China might ramp up military activities near Japan in response to comments from Japanese Prime Minister Sanae Takaichi, indicating escalating tensions between the two nations. Additionally, the UN World Food Programme (WFP) warned that approximately 318 million people could face acute food insecurity by 2026, reflecting a deepening global hunger crisis. Meanwhile, the International Criminal Court prepared to sentence Sudanese Ali Muhammad Ali Abd-Al-Rahman for his role in Darfur atrocities, where he maintained his innocence, claiming mistaken identity. In North Kivu, Democratic Republic of Congo, a terrorist attack on a church-run clinic resulted in around 20 civilian deaths, underscoring ongoing instability in the region. Finally, developments concerning the African Union included discussions around the need for debt relief, particularly for countries heavily burdened amidst recovery efforts from crises and conflicts.
