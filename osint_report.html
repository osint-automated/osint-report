Intent: The intent of this report is to provide a high-level overview of significant military and geopolitical developments during the reporting period. The report consists of open-source reporting and updates on evolving conflicts, diplomatic initiatives, and strategic military actions involving countries of interest in the following Combatant Commands: SOUTHCOM, EUCOM, PACOM, CENTCOM, AFRICOM.

Executive Summary: During the reporting period, authorities lifted sanctions on Venezuelan interim President Delcy Rodríguez on 1 April, while her administration prepared to seize control of PDVSA's U.S. subsidiaries. Concurrently, U.S. naval forces initiated a multinational maritime exercise in Panamanian waters. In Eastern Europe, Russian forces claimed full control of Luhansk Oblast, provoking Ukrainian denials and a retaliatory barrage of over 700 drones against Ukrainian infrastructure, resulting in civilian casualties and power outages. The situation in Taiwan escalated with Chinese military activities, prompting Taiwan to enhance its defenses amid legislative delays in critical procurement. In the Middle East, Israeli strikes targeted Iranian military infrastructure, prompting a strong retaliatory response from Iran and Hezbollah, escalating regional tensions and leading to surging oil prices. Furthermore, violent incidents surged in Burundi and Nigeria, prompting increased military and security responses, while South Africa deployed troops to combat crime. Analysts warned that ongoing Middle Eastern conflicts could hinder economic growth in Africa due to disrupted trade and energy supplies.

  • SOUTHCOM: During the reporting period, United States authorities lifted sanctions on Venezuelan interim President Delcy Rodríguez on 1 April, her administration simultaneously advanced preparations to seize control of state oil firm PDVSA's United States subsidiaries including Citgo Petroleum, and Secretary of State Marco Rubio underscored requirements for a transitional governance phase leading to free elections in Venezuela. A United States aircraft carrier and destroyer maneuvered into Panamanian territorial waters on 1 April to conduct a multinational maritime exercise. No additional geopolitical escalations or military engagements materialized across Central or South America.

  • EUCOM: During the reporting period, Russian Ministry of Defence forces declared complete territorial control over Luhansk Oblast on 1 April, prompting Ukrainian General Staff officials to issue immediate denials amid anticipation of US envoy-mediated discussions. Ukrainian President Zelenskyy directed US negotiators to transmit an Easter ceasefire proposal to Kremlin counterparts, which Russian Foreign Ministry spokespersons dismissed as a publicity operation. Russian Aerospace Forces responded with a massed drone barrage exceeding 700 Iranian-designed Shahed munitions launched in daytime operations against western and central Ukrainian infrastructure, including energy grid components and a postal terminal in multiple oblasts, resulting in at least five civilian fatalities, multiple injuries, and temporary power outages across affected sectors. Institute for the Study of War analysts noted the Luhansk claim echoed prior unsubstantiated assertions, while Ukrainian Air Force units intercepted portions of the drone swarm, mitigating further escalation in frontline engagements around Kharkiv, Sumy, and Zaporizhia regions.

  • PACOM: During the reporting period, People's Liberation Army forces dispatched eleven military aircraft and eight naval vessels into Taiwan's air defence identification zone on 1 April, prompting Taiwan's Ministry of National Defence to deploy surveillance assets for monitoring. Taiwan authorities announced enhanced defensive measures on the Pratas Islands amid escalated Chinese operational activities in the northern South China Sea. Taiwan's military leadership reported that legislative delays in budget approval jeopardised procurement of 2.4 billion dollars in weaponry and training programmes critical to countering Beijing's sovereignty claims. China issued diplomatic warnings to the United States against establishing an ammunition production facility in the Philippines, characterising it as an instigation of conflict within the Asia-Pacific theatre. New Zealand and the Cook Islands formalised a defence cooperation pact, with Chinese officials asserting the arrangement targeted no third parties including Beijing.

  • CENTCOM: During the reporting period, Israeli Defense Forces struck an Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps ground forces base, a mobile command post utilised by regime commanders in Tehran, and a ballistic missile storage site in the Tabriz area on 2 April; United States President Donald Trump delivered multiple addresses asserting decimation of Iran's navy, air force, leadership, and command structure while thanking allies including Israel, Saudi Arabia, Qatar, the United Arab Emirates, Kuwait, and Bahrain for support; Iran launched successive waves of ballistic missiles, cruise missiles, and drones targeting Israel including the Tel Aviv area and Gulf Arab states, with Hezbollah executing over 50 rocket barrages and 40 to 60 attacks on Israel's northern border and Beersheba; Iranian forces reportedly struck a United States airbase in Saudi Arabia destroying a surveillance jet; Houthi militants claimed joint missile operations with Iran and Hezbollah against Israel; Tehran issued warnings of more destructive retaliatory strikes against United States and Israeli targets; high-intensity exchanges persisted with Israel eliminating over 40 Hezbollah operatives; oil prices surged amid threats of intensified United States strikes.

  • AFRICOM: During the reporting period, Burundi's military ammunition depot in Bujumbura's Musaga district exploded on 1 April due to a fire from an electrical short circuit, killing 13 civilians and injuring 57 individuals including three soldiers while damaging nearby homes and vehicles, prompting the army to rule out coup involvement. South African National Defence Force personnel deployed 2200 troops to Cape Town's gang-ridden Cape Flats townships on 1 April to combat persistent violent crime and killings despite prior security measures. Nigeria's Plateau State authorities imposed and later eased a curfew in Jos North's Anguwan Rukuba community following a surge in violence on 1 April that claimed additional lives after a Palm Sunday attack, with President Tinubu visiting Jos to commiserate and Inspector-General Disu deploying security assets amid a 71 percent rise in regional hostilities. Analysts released data on 2 April revealing Burkina Faso and Mali junta forces killed more civilians than jihadists in 2025, while a report warned prolonged Middle East conflict threatened sharper African economic growth slowdown through disrupted trade and energy supplies.
