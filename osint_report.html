  • EUCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that the G20 summit held in Johannesburg, South Africa, featured discussions among Ukraine's allies regarding a U.S.-drafted peace plan for Ukraine, which President Donald Trump pressured Ukraine to accept by Thanksgiving. The 28-point plan proposed ceding approximately 20% of Ukraine's territory to Russia, a condition that President Volodymyr Zelenskyy warned could undermine Ukraine's dignity and security. Concurrently, U.S. officials indicated that failure to accept the plan could result in reduced intelligence sharing and weapons support for Ukraine. In military developments, Russia claimed to have captured several villages in eastern Ukraine, a statement that Kyiv did not confirm. Overall, Ukraine found itself navigating complex geopolitical dynamics, balancing U.S. demands with its own military and internal political challenges amid ongoing concerns over corruption within its government.
