Date of Information: 19 March 2026

Reporting Period: 18 March - 19 March 2026

Distribution: Approved for public release; distribution is unlimited.

Combatant Commands: SOUTHCOM, EUCOM, PACOM, CENTCOM, AFRICOM

Intent: The intent of this report is to provide a high-level overview of significant military and geopolitical developments during the reporting period. The report consists of open-source reporting and updates on evolving conflicts, diplomatic initiatives, and strategic military actions involving countries of interest in the following Combatant Commands: SOUTHCOM, EUCOM, PACOM, CENTCOM, AFRICOM.

Executive Summary: During the reporting period, significant military and geopolitical developments unfolded across multiple regions. Ecuador and Colombia engaged in discussions following the discovery of an unexploded explosive in Colombian territory stemming from a recent anti-cartel operation. Russian forces achieved territorial gains in Ukraine while suffering unprecedented personnel losses and executing extensive drone strikes across several Ukrainian regions. The People's Liberation Army intensified military activities around Taiwan, highlighting rising tensions in the region, concurrently, the U.S. released an assessment on Chinese intentions. In the Middle East, Israel conducted targeted strikes against Iranian assets, resulting in retaliatory missile attacks from Iran that caused civilian casualties. Nigerian forces successfully countered an Islamist assault, while Chadian authorities addressed cross-border incursions linked to the Sudan conflict. Overall, these events indicate escalating military confrontations and shifting geopolitical dynamics that necessitate heightened monitoring and strategic assessments.

  • SOUTHCOM: During the reporting period, Ecuadorian and Colombian authorities convened a virtual meeting on 19 March to probe an unexploded Ecuadorian Air Force explosive discovered in Colombian territory following a 3 March anti-cartel operation near the Sucumbíos border, with hypotheses centering on trajectory rebound or third-party displacement prompting a planned binational technical commission; the US Embassy in Brazil hosted a critical minerals forum in Sao Paulo on 18 March, which senior Lula administration officials snubbed amid escalating bilateral tensions ahead of presidential elections; Chilean officials marked the 96th anniversary of the Fuerza Aérea de Chile through a commemorative mass at Iquique Cathedral on 19 March; and Salvadoran authorities arrested two women on 19 March for drug trafficking after seizing cocaine portions, methamphetamine, cash, and distribution paraphernalia in a San Salvador raid.

  • EUCOM: During the reporting period, Russian forces advanced in the Slovyansk direction near northern Novoselivka and south of Kalenyky, captured Aleksandrovka and Kaleniki in Donetsk People's Republic, infiltrated positions near Oleksandrivka, and progressed north of Zatyshok in Pokrovsk direction while planting flags on Olgovka outskirts; Ukrainian forces counterattacked in Lyman direction employing innovative drone tactics to regain terrain, repelled assaults destroying Russian armored vehicles and motorcycles near Myrnohrad, Hryshyne, and Kotlyne, and struck Russian aviation facilities including Aviastar plant in Ulyanovsk Oblast on 16 March and 123rd Aircraft Repair Plant in Novgorod Oblast on 17 March plus Rubikon drone center, coordination hub, and depots in occupied Donetsk on 17-18 March alongside Crimea assets like Valdai radar and BK-16 boats; Russian forces launched 147-178 drones overnight 17-18 March targeting infrastructure in Chernihiv, Mykolaiv, Odesa, and other oblasts while striking rail stations in Sumy and Chernihiv, TRCs, and AFU positions in Kharkiv and Zaporizhia; Russian military incurred highest daily personnel losses of 2026 exceeding 1,700 casualties amid failed assaults; Russian Security Council Secretary Sergei Shoigu acknowledged effectiveness of Ukrainian long-range strikes on defense industrial base, General Valery Gerasimov exaggerated battlefield gains, and Spanish Prime Minister Pedro Sanchez announced €1 billion military aid package with joint production agreements on 18 March.

  • PACOM: During the reporting period, People's Liberation Army (PLA) forces conducted aerial and naval patrols around Taiwan, deploying 36 aircraft and eight People's Liberation Army Navy (PLAN) vessels plus one official vessel on 18 March, with 24 aircraft sorties crossing the Taiwan Strait median line into Taiwan's northern, central, southwestern, and eastern air defense identification zones (ADIZ). Taiwan's Ministry of National Defense monitored activities and responded accordingly. On 19 March, PLA dispatched 12 aircraft, nine PLAN vessels, and two official ships, with five aircraft crossing the median line into Taiwan's northern, central, and southwestern ADIZ; Taiwan again monitored and responded. No significant PLA activities occurred in the South China Sea or East China Sea, and US Director of National Intelligence released the 2026 Annual Threat Assessment on 18 March assessing PRC intentions to compete with the US while avoiding Taiwan invasion in 2027 and deepening South China Sea control.

  • CENTCOM: During the reporting period, Israel executed airstrikes on Iran's South Pars natural gas field on 18 March and initiated operations against naval assets at Bandar Anzali in northern Iran on 19 March, while United States forces deployed 5,000-pound guided bombs on Iranian missile sites near the Strait of Hormuz. Israel eliminated Intelligence Minister Esmail Khatib in a precision strike on Tehran overnight into 18 March, prompting Iranian Supreme Leader Mojtaba Khamenei to vow retribution. Iran launched multiple ballistic missile salvos at Israeli targets, inflicting fatalities including two civilians near Tel Aviv on 18 March, one foreign worker in Moshav Adanim, and three in the West Bank near Hebron on 19 March. Iran struck Gulf energy infrastructure, targeting Qatar's Ras Laffan gas facility with resultant fires and damage, UAE's Habshan complex and Bab oil field, and Saudi facilities via drones and missiles, which Saudi defenses intercepted. Qatar expelled Iranian military and security attachés, UAE denounced the assaults as escalatory, and Saudi Arabia reserved rights to military countermeasures. President Trump warned Israel against additional strikes on Iranian energy sites unless Iran renewed attacks on Qatar and affirmed United States readiness for unilateral response.

  • AFRICOM: During the reporting period, Nigerian forces repelled a coordinated Islamist insurgent assault on a military base in Borno State, eliminating at least 80 attackers including senior commanders through ground operations supported by aerial strikes; suspected suicide bombings in Maiduguri concurrently killed 23 civilians and wounded 108 others, prompting Vice President Kashim Shettima to visit the area and reaffirm counterinsurgency commitments on 19 March. Chadian authorities directed military retaliation against cross-border incursions by Sudanese belligerents following attacks linked to the ongoing Sudanese Armed Forces-Rapid Support Forces conflict. In Somalia, opposition elements seized Qansahdhere district amid heavy clashes involving artillery in Southwest State ahead of regional polls. Allied Democratic Forces rebels struck gold mining sites in Democratic Republic of Congo's Ituri province, inflicting multiple fatalities. Amhara Fano militias neutralised or captured numerous Ethiopian government troops, seizing weapons and vehicles in South Gonder. United States officials designated Sudan's Muslim Brotherhood a terrorist entity, while Rapid Support Forces captured a strategic Sudanese border town, escalating regional tensions.
