Intent: The intent of this report is to provide a high-level overview of significant military and geopolitical developments during the reporting period. The report consists of open-source reporting and updates on evolving conflicts, diplomatic initiatives, and strategic military actions involving countries of interest in the following Combatant Commands: SOUTHCOM, EUCOM, PACOM, CENTCOM, AFRICOM.

Executive Summary: During the reporting period, Colombian President Gustavo Petro accused Ecuadorian forces of conducting aerial bombings on Colombian territory, leading to troop deployments along the frontier and an investigation following the discovery of 27 charred bodies and an unexploded bomb on 17 March. Ecuadorian President Daniel Noboa denied any violations, asserting that Ecuadorian aircraft targeted narco-trafficking camps within its borders. Ukraine executed drone strikes against Moscow, marking the fourth consecutive day of operations deep into Russian territory, prompting concerns from Russian officials over escalating drone threats as Russian forces reported limited territorial gains in eastern Ukraine. The People's Liberation Army resumed large-scale operations around Taiwan, with multiple aircraft detected crossing the Taiwan Strait median line. In Iran, Israel conducted targeted airstrikes in Tehran, resulting in the deaths of key Iranian officials, which prompted retaliatory missile strikes against Israel and Gulf Arab states from Iran. In Nigeria, suspected ISWAP operatives executed suicide bombings in Maiduguri, resulting in multiple civilian casualties, while conflicts escalated between armed groups in the Democratic Republic of Congo and Sudan, further destabilizing regional security.

  • SOUTHCOM: During the reporting period, Colombian President Gustavo Petro accused Ecuadorian forces of executing aerial bombings on Colombian territory near the Ecuador-Colombia border on 17 March, where authorities discovered 27 charred bodies and an unexploded Mark-82 500-pound bomb, prompting Colombia to deploy troops to the frontier and initiate an investigation. Ecuadorian President Daniel Noboa denied violating Colombian airspace or conducting cross-border strikes, confirming instead that Ecuadorian aircraft executed precision airstrikes solely within Ecuadorian borders against narco-trafficking camps affiliated with Colombian criminal organizations, supported by United States Southern Command through joint operations under the ongoing anti-cartel coalition framework. Petro appealed directly to United States President Donald Trump to mediate with Noboa and avert escalation, while Ecuador intensified its domestic offensive by mobilizing 75,000 troops and police forces into curfew-enforced zones targeting designated terrorist entities such as Los Choneros and Los Lobos.

  • EUCOM: During the reporting period, Ukrainian forces executed drone strikes targeting Moscow with at least 40 unmanned aerial vehicles on 17 March, constituting the fourth consecutive day of such operations deep into Russian territory. Russia's former Defense Minister Sergei Shoigu assessed that Ukrainian long-range drones threatened the Ural region more than 1500 kilometers from the border, prompting Russian officials to declare no region safe from Ukrainian drone capabilities. Russian General Staff Army General Valery Gerasimov amplified tactical details and overstated Russian advances while minimizing Ukrainian territorial gains exceeding 400 square kilometers in eastern Zaporizhia Oblast since late January. Russian elements conducted limited incursions into Sumy and Kharkiv oblasts, capturing minor settlements including Bobylivka, Rohizne, Chervona Zorya, Mala Bobylivka, and Kruhle to propagate narratives of frontline collapse. President Zelenskyy characterized Iran and Russia as allies in adversarial drone technology proliferation, warned European states of prospective terrorist and criminal drone threats, and dispatched 200 anti-drone specialists to the Middle East amid reports of Russia furnishing Iran with satellite imagery and drone systems. EU foreign policy chief Kaja Kallas highlighted Russia's prospective benefits from elevated energy prices and redirected Western air defenses.

  • PACOM: During the reporting period, People's Liberation Army aircraft conducted resumed large-scale operations around Taiwan following a ten-day hiatus, as Taiwan's Ministry of National Defense detected thirty-six PLA aircraft, eight People's Liberation Army Navy vessels, and one official ship operating in the vicinity up until 0600 hours UTC+8 on 18 March, with twenty-four aircraft crossing the Taiwan Strait median line into Taiwan's northern, central, southwestern, and eastern air defense identification zones. Taiwan's forces monitored these activities and responded accordingly. Earlier on 17 March, detection recorded only two PLA aircraft entering the southwestern air defense identification zone alongside eight People's Liberation Army Navy vessels and one official ship up until 0600 hours UTC+8. No significant PLA naval or air force maneuvers occurred in the South China Sea, near the Philippines, or proximate to Japan during this interval.

  • CENTCOM: During the reporting period, Israel executed targeted airstrikes in Tehran on 17 March, eliminating Ali Larijani, secretary of Iran's Supreme National Security Council, and Gholamreza Soleimani, commander of the IRGC Basij force, as confirmed by Israeli Defense Minister Israel Katz and later by Iranian state television. Israel launched a wide-scale strike campaign across Tehran and intensified airstrikes against Hezbollah positions in Lebanon, where forces struck Kfar Sir village, killing one Lebanese soldier and wounding four others, while ground elements advanced into southern Lebanon. Iran responded with multiple missile and drone salvos targeting central Israel, including barrages toward Tel Aviv and regions south of the Sea of Galilee, alongside attacks on Gulf Arab states that damaged an oil facility in Fujairah, United Arab Emirates, and caused one civilian fatality from intercept debris over Abu Dhabi. Iran maintained operational control of the Strait of Hormuz, declaring passage open but denied to United States, Israeli, and allied vessels. United States President Donald Trump demanded deployment of warships by NATO allies to secure the strait, eliciting no immediate commitments.

  • AFRICOM: During the reporting period, suspected Islamic State West Africa Province (ISWAP) operatives executed multiple suicide bombings in Maiduguri, Borno State, Nigeria, on 17 March, targeting the post office, Monday Market, University of Maiduguri Teaching Hospital entrance, and Kaleri area, which killed 23 to 25 civilians and wounded over 100 others. Nigerian security forces from Operation Hadin Kai and the police Explosive Ordnance Disposal Team swiftly cordoned affected sites, restored normalcy, and intensified patrols amid reports of prior insurgent assaults on nearby military bases including Mile 4, Buratai, and Ajilari Cross, where attackers burned vehicles and killed personnel. In the Democratic Republic of Congo, Alliance Fleuve Congo/March 23 Movement (AFC/M23) forces repelled Burundian troops and Forces Démocratiques de Libération du Rwanda (FDLR) kamikaze drone strikes near Mikenke, Bidegu, and Minembwe on 17 March, while Kinshasa-aligned units bombarded Kabunambo along the Ruzizi plain with heavy artillery from 06:40, inflicting civilian casualties and displacements. Sudanese Armed Forces (SAF) drones struck civilian targets in West Kordofan and Darfur border markets on 17 March, exacerbating ongoing clashes with Rapid Support Forces (RSF) advances that isolated strategic positions.
