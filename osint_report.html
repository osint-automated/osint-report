Executive Summary: During the reporting period, United States naval assets, including an aircraft carrier and destroyer, arrived in Panama Canal waters on 4 April 2026 to conduct a multinational maritime exercise as Chinese entities delayed and flagged Panama-registered vessels in response to the cancellation of a Chinese-linked port concession. In Peru, Army forces intensified personnel preparations and deployments across key regions ahead of the 12 April general elections, while Guatemalan Army units maintained routine security operations. On the same day, the United States lifted sanctions on Venezuela's interim president amid stabilization efforts, and unconfirmed reports indicated potential military intervention in Nicaragua. In Ukraine, Russian forces launched 286 drone strikes against infrastructure, inflicting casualties and damage despite Ukrainian air defenses intercepting 260; Ukrainian forces executed successful mid-range strikes targeting Russian military assets. Geopolitically, Ukrainian President Zelenskiy met Turkish President Erdogan to enhance security cooperation, with scheduled U.S. engagement on security guarantees anticipated. In Iran, U.S. and Israeli forces targeted military sites and missile defense installations leading to escalated exchanges with Iran, which launched missiles at Israel, prompting retaliatory strikes. Additionally, tensions between Sudan and Ethiopia worsened, as reported Ethiopian drone strikes targeted Sudanese forces and concerns over regional instability emerged.

  • SOUTHCOM: During the reporting period, United States naval assets comprising an aircraft carrier and destroyer arrived in Panama Canal waters on 4 April 2026 to conduct a multinational maritime exercise, while Chinese entities delayed and flagged Panama-registered vessels in retaliation for the Supreme Court's cancellation of a Chinese-linked port concession at key terminals. Peruvian Army forces intensified personnel preparations and deployments across Iquitos, Puno, and Moquegua on 4 April 2026 to secure the 12 April general elections, and Guatemalan Army units maintained routine security patrols in Jacaltenango, Huehuetenango department. United States authorities lifted sanctions on Venezuela's interim president Delcy Rodríguez on 4 April 2026 amid post-regime change stabilization efforts, and unconfirmed reports indicated authorization of a targeted military intervention in Nicaragua. No kinetic military engagements, border incursions, or coup attempts materialized across Central or South America.

  • EUCOM: During the reporting period, Russian forces launched 286 drones targeting Ukrainian infrastructure, with Ukrainian air defenses intercepting 260 while strikes damaged facilities in Kyiv, Chernihiv Oblast, Sumy Oblast, and a market in Nikopol where five civilians died and 25 sustained injuries; Ukrainian forces executed mid-range strikes destroying Shahed drone hubs at Khalino airfield and Navlya, an S-400 radar near Feodosia in Crimea, a Tor-M2 system near Zachativka, logistics nodes in occupied Zaporizhia and Luhansk, and a chemical plant in Tolyatti, Samara Oblast, alongside advances in southwestern Vovchansk, northern Kupyansk, and northwestern Boikove. Russian grouping conducted assaults without confirmed territorial gains across northern Sumy Oblast near Hrabovske, Kharkiv sectors including Vovchansk and Lyptsi, Kupyansk along the Oskil River, Slovyansk toward Rai-Oleksandrivka, Kostyantynivka-Druzhkivka axis, Pokrovsk, Novopavlivka, Hulyaipole, and western Zaporizhia Oblast. Ukrainian drones struck Taganrog causing one fatality and four injuries plus damage to a foreign-flagged vessel, and industrial sites in Togliatti; reports indicated Ukrainian deployment of 7,000 robotic operations on the frontline and Russian Ministry of Defence claimed Ukrainian personnel losses exceeded 1,160 with hits on airbases and energy targets. Geopolitically, Ukrainian President Zelenskiy met Turkish President Erdogan in Istanbul on 4 April announcing enhanced security cooperation, while Kyiv anticipated a mid-April visit by US envoys Steve Witkoff and Jared Kushner to discuss security guarantees amid stalled peace talks.

  • PACOM: During the reporting period, Chinese People's Liberation Army Navy vessels maintained standard patrol patterns in the South China Sea without reported confrontations involving Philippine or US assets, while PLA Air Force aircraft registered zero incursions into Taiwan's air defense identification zone for the seventh consecutive day as tracked by Taiwan's Ministry of National Defense on 5 April. PLA forces executed no large-scale exercises, amphibious rehearsals, or gray-zone operations in the Taiwan Strait, Philippine Sea, or East China Sea, per USINDOPACOM monitoring and open-source intelligence. Beijing issued no official statements on Pacific theater deployments, and regional allies including Japan and the Philippines detected no anomalous submarine or carrier group transits beyond baseline activities. Coast Guard cutters conducted routine sovereignty assertions near Scarborough Shoal but avoided escalatory maneuvers against foreign vessels.

  • CENTCOM: During the reporting period, US and Israeli forces struck an S-300 surface-to-air missile defence site in Kahrizak, Tehran Province, targeted the Iran-Iraq border crossing in Shalamcheh, Khuzestan Province, and hit petrochemical facilities including Fajr, Rejal, and Amir Kabir companies in Bandar-e Imam Khomeini, Khuzestan Province, on 4 April; a projectile impacted near Bushehr Nuclear Power Plant, killing one protection staff member. Iran launched at least eight missiles at Israel, including a ballistic missile with cluster munitions that struck ten locations in central Israel, wounding six civilians and damaging sites near IDF headquarters in Tel Aviv and a school. US-Israeli combined operations eliminated Kataib Hezbollah headquarters in Anbar Province, Iraq, killing two Popular Mobilization Forces fighters, while Iranian-backed Iraqi militias struck North Rumaila oilfield in Basra, wounding three workers. Hezbollah executed 19 attacks against Israeli forces in southern Lebanon and 23 against northern Israeli communities, prompting IDF 84th Infantry Brigade airstrikes and raids that destroyed Hezbollah weapons caches and IDF 282nd Artillery Brigade shelling exceeding 400 rounds. Houthis fired a ballistic missile with cluster warhead and drones targeting Ben Gurion Airport and southern Israel, which IDF permitted to land in open areas. Iran targeted UAE with 56 drones and 23 ballistic missiles—all intercepted—along with attacks on Kuwait (19 drones, 8 missiles intercepted, sparking oil complex fire) and Bahrain (8 drones). President Trump issued a 48-hour ultimatum on 4 April demanding Iran reopen Strait of Hormuz, which Tehran rejected; Netanyahu confirmed strikes on Iranian petrochemical "money machine," and Trump announced multiple Iranian military leaders killed in Tehran strikes. US forces continued search for missing airman after Iran downed F-15E and A-10 on 3 April, with one crew rescued. Israel bombed southern Lebanon sites including Kfar Hatta and Maarakeh, killing five, amid one additional soldier death versus Hezbollah.

  • AFRICOM: During the reporting period, observers warned that Sudan's civil war risked merging with Ethiopia's ongoing conflict amid reports of Ethiopian drone strikes on Sudanese targets and Addis Ababa hosting Rapid Support Forces training camps; Darfur Governor Minni Arko Minawi urged the United Nations to replace the Adre border crossing due to its diversion for military logistics by Rapid Support Forces and United Arab Emirates; Russia delivered over twenty tons of weapons and equipment to Madagascar's army; geopolitical analysts highlighted Horn of Africa volatility from foreign military bases in Djibouti, South Africa's International Criminal Court action against Israel, and dependencies on regional chokepoints like Bab el-Mandeb Strait; Egyptian President Abdel Fattah al-Sisi appealed externally for support amid Nile water tensions with Ethiopia.
