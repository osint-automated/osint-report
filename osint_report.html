Date of Information: 29 March 2026

Reporting Period: 28 March - 29 March 2026

Distribution: Approved for public release; distribution is unlimited.

Combatant Commands: SOUTHCOM, EUCOM, PACOM, CENTCOM, AFRICOM

Intent: The intent of this report is to provide a high-level overview of significant military and geopolitical developments during the reporting period. The report consists of open-source reporting and updates on evolving conflicts, diplomatic initiatives, and strategic military actions involving countries of interest in the following Combatant Commands: SOUTHCOM, EUCOM, PACOM, CENTCOM, AFRICOM.

Executive Summary: During the reporting period, Brazilian officials unveiled the Embraer F-39 Gripen E, enhancing regional air defense capabilities on 28 March. Ecuadorian security forces escalated operations in Cotopaxi province against indigenous resistance linked to illegal mining, following prior tensions involving reported U.S. military involvement on 28 March. Concurrently, Russian forces launched over 270 drone strikes against Odesa Oblast, resulting in casualties at a maternity hospital and additional infrastructure damage, while Ukrainian forces retaliated with long-range strikes against key Russian assets, notably igniting the Yaroslavl Oil Refinery on 28 March. In Asia, Chinese diplomats engaged in high-level talks with Philippine authorities to stabilize relations amid maritime tensions, while the Houthi rebels initiated missile strikes targeting Israel, prompting Israeli and U.S. military responses aimed at Iranian threats on 28 March. Armed confrontations persisted in the Sahel region, with Niger formalizing regional self-defense units amidst ongoing violence in Mali, Burkina Faso, and Nigeria on 28 March.

  • SOUTHCOM: During the reporting period, Brazilian officials unveiled the Embraer F-39 Gripen E, Latin America's first domestically manufactured supersonic fighter jet, marking a significant advancement in regional air defense capabilities on 28 March. Ecuadorian security forces deployed drones, military vehicles, and personnel into Cotopaxi province to counter indigenous resistance against illegal mining operations, escalating tensions following prior incidents of reported Pentagon involvement and campesino detentions on 28 March. Mexican naval assets located and escorted two humanitarian aid sailboats carrying supplies from Mexico to Cuba, which safely arrived in Havana after temporary loss of contact amid ongoing U.S. oil blockade pressures on 28 March. Colombian authorities conducted a memorial ceremony honoring 69 security personnel killed in the 23 March Air Force C-130 crash near Putumayo, finalizing victim identifications and investigations on 28 March.

  • EUCOM: During the reporting period, Russian forces launched over 270 drones primarily against Odesa Oblast overnight 27-28 March, striking a maternity hospital in Odesa City where two personnel died and twelve sustained injuries amid sixty-plus drones targeting the city and damaging port, residential, and critical infrastructure; Ukrainian air defenses downed 252 drones while Russian forces executed additional strikes on residential, industrial, and energy facilities in Poltava Oblast and Kryvyi Rih on 28 March. Ukrainian forces prosecuted long-range strikes employing FP-5 Flamingo cruise missiles and FP-1 drones against Russian defense-industrial and oil-refining assets on 27-28 March, including the Promsintez explosives plant in Chapayevsk, Samara Oblast that provoked secondary detonations, the Yaroslavl Oil Refinery that ignited, and a fuel depot near Unecha, Bryansk Oblast, alongside drone attacks that set fires at Ust-Luga and Primorsk oil terminals in Leningrad Oblast. Russian elements marginally advanced near Brusivka southeast of Lyman and conducted limited assaults across Sumy, Kharkiv, Kupyansk, Lyman, Pokrovsk, Novopavlivka, Velyka Novosilka, and Zaporizhia axes on 28 March without confirmed territorial gains beyond Ukrainian-reported advances in eastern Lyman and Slovyansk directions; Ukrainian marines repelled a large-scale Russian assault on the Oleksandrivka front. Geopolitically, Ukraine concluded a ten-year defense pact with Qatar on 28 March covering air defenses, counter-drone systems, joint production, training, cybersecurity, and AI applications, following analogous agreements with Saudi Arabia on 27 March and pending one with the UAE; Russia directed preparations to halt all gasoline exports from 1 April amid domestic shortages intensified by Ukrainian strikes on energy infrastructure.

  • PACOM: During the reporting period, Chinese diplomats conducted high-level consultations with Philippine counterparts in Quanzhou, Fujian Province on 28 March, urging Manila to stabilize bilateral ties amid South China Sea disputes and lodging formal protests over Philippine maritime infringements, provocations, and rhetoric near Zhubi Reef. A PLA Navy vessel executed routine patrol near Zhubi Reef when a Philippine naval ship approached within 10 meters in a maneuver that Beijing characterized as dangerous, prompting Manila to release footage and reiterate concerns for personnel safety. The People's Liberation Army Air Force maintained obsolete J-6 supersonic fighters converted to attack drones at six air bases—five in Fujian Province and one in Guangdong Province—proximate to the Taiwan Strait, as detailed in a Mitchell Institute report published on 29 March.

  • CENTCOM: During the reporting period, Yemen's Houthi rebels, backed by Iran, launched their first direct missile and drone strikes against Israel in solidarity with Tehran, prompting Israeli forces to intercept most projectiles while expanding airstrikes on Iranian nuclear facilities and military infrastructure. Iranian forces fired a barrage of ballistic missiles targeting Tel Aviv, inflicting casualties and damage despite Israeli air defenses neutralizing the majority. United States and Israeli aircraft conducted precision strikes on Iranian ballistic missile sites and air defense systems in central and western Iran, degrading Tehran's retaliatory capabilities amid ongoing closure of the Strait of Hormuz. Israeli defense officials vowed escalation of operations against Iran following the Houthi incursion on 28 March, while United States Central Command repositioned Marine expeditionary units near the Persian Gulf to counter potential Iranian naval threats. Iranian state media reported dozens killed in Israeli strikes on nuclear-adjacent sites, as United States President extended the ultimatum for reopening the strait before targeting Iran's power grid on 28 March.

  • AFRICOM: During the reporting period, armed actors in Mali, Burkina Faso, and Nigeria mounted attacks on security positions while state forces executed counterattacks, arrests, and deployments on 28 March. Niger's government formalised regional self-defense units to enhance local force multiplication amid Sahel trends. Somali authorities reported troop movements and advanced plans for Southwest State elections, with President Hassan Sheikh Mohamud emphasising one-person, one-vote implementation in 2026. In eastern Democratic Republic of Congo, AFC/M23 forces alleged drone strikes targeted civilians and executed withdrawals around Minova on 28 March. Oromo Liberation Army clashed with Ethiopian government troops in Benishangul-Gumuz region, killing soldiers and incinerating facilities. Sudanese Armed Forces conducted an airstrike in South Kordofan on 28 March, which SPLM-N claimed killed seven civilians.
