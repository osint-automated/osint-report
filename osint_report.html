Intent: The intent of this report is to provide a high-level overview of significant military and geopolitical developments during the reporting period. The report consists of open-source reporting and updates on evolving conflicts, diplomatic initiatives, and strategic military actions involving countries of interest in the following Combatant Commands: SOUTHCOM, EUCOM, PACOM, CENTCOM, AFRICOM.

Executive Summary: During the reporting period, United States Southern Command forces conducted a precision strike against a drug-smuggling vessel in the eastern Pacific, neutralizing two suspects and detaining one survivor on 20 March. Geopolitical tensions rose as Venezuelan President Delcy Rodríguez dismissed senior military commanders amidst political instability, while Ecuador sought Colombian military reinforcements to address narcoterrorist threats. Russian forces executed extensive drone strikes against Ukrainian positions, damaging infrastructure and resulting in civilian casualties, while Ukraine launched retaliatory strikes against Engels Airbase and Russian oil depots. Concurrently, Iranian ballistic missiles targeted Jerusalem, leading to Israeli airstrikes against key Iranian military leaders and assets, compounded by U.S. naval reinforcements in the region. In Africa, Somali federal troops clashed with regional forces amid political unrest, while escalating tensions in Ethiopia and warnings of jihadist activities in Nigeria highlighted the ongoing instability across the region.

  • SOUTHCOM: During the reporting period, United States Southern Command forces executed a precision strike against a suspected drug-smuggling vessel in the eastern Pacific, neutralizing two suspects and detaining one survivor whom Costa Rican coast guard personnel subsequently processed on 20 March. Venezuelan President Delcy Rodríguez relieved several senior military commanders of their duties, prompting speculation regarding internal power consolidation amid ongoing post-intervention instability. Ecuadorian authorities formally requested Colombian military reinforcements along their shared border to counter escalating narcoterrorist threats and cross-border incursions. Guatemalan officials declared intentions to mediate and potentially intervene in the maritime territorial dispute between Belize and Honduras over the Sapodilla Cayes archipelago. Mexican civilian militias in cartel-contested regions repelled aggressors using AK-47 rifles and grenades, marking a tactical escalation in autonomous self-defense operations. Colombian security analysts highlighted intensified armed group rivalries across 13 territorial hotspots, underscored by 277 drone-delivered explosive incidents throughout 2025, though no major clashes materialized on 20 March. Nicaraguan opposition sources reported unconfirmed indications of United States naval assets maneuvering proximate to national coastlines, heightening regional vigilance.

  • EUCOM: During the reporting period, Russian forces executed multiple drone barrages totaling over 133 and 214 Shahed-type unmanned aerial vehicles against Ukrainian positions in Chernihiv, Volyn, Odesa, Dnipropetrovsk, Zaporizhia, Lviv, Khmelnytskyi, Sumy, Kyiv, and Odesa oblasts on 20 March and 21 March, inflicting damage on hospitals, residential structures, power infrastructure, and port facilities while wounding at least 20 civilians including four children and killing one man; Ukrainian air defenses intercepted 72 of 171 and 114 of the total drones launched. Ukrainian forces conducted drone strikes against Engels Airbase in Saratov Oblast and an oil depot in Krasnodar Krai on 20 March, prompting Russian declaration of emergency in Engels district, and marginally advanced west of Demidovka in Belgorod Oblast. Russian elements advanced north of Zhuravka in Sumy Oblast, northeast of Kupyansk near Dvorichna, along Enhelsa and Mykhaila Hrushevskoho streets in Toretsk, and within Tarasivka and Shevchenko east of Pokrovsk on 20 March and 21 March, while Ukrainian units progressed in Kostyantynivka-Druzhkivka, Pokrovsk, and Oleksandrivka directions. Russian Ministry of Defense reported destruction of 57 Ukrainian drones over Kursk and ignition at a Krasnodar oil facility on 20 March. Geopolitically, Russia and Ukraine exchanged 372 prisoners via UAE mediation on 20 March; Kremlin spokesperson Dmitry Peskov demanded Ukraine cease energy infrastructure attacks; Foreign Ministry spokesperson Maria Zakharova highlighted Ukrainian strikes on Turkish Stream and Blue Stream coinciding with Zelensky-NATO Secretary General Rutte meeting on 20 March; US Representative Luna affirmed ongoing US-Russia settlement talks; French Navy detained Mozambican-flagged tanker Deyna en route from Murmansk on 20 March; and US announced shuttle diplomacy in Riyadh on 23-24 March for Black Sea maritime ceasefire groundwork.

  • PACOM: During the reporting period, PLA Southern Theater Command navy and air force units tracked, monitored, warned, and expelled a Philippine C-208 aircraft that intruded into airspace over waters adjacent to Meiji Jiao in the Nansha Qundao on 20 March. PLA aircraft conducted four incursions into Taiwan's southwestern and eastern air defense identification zone while six aircraft and eight People's Liberation Army Navy vessels operated around Taiwan up to 0600 UTC+8 on 20 March. Chinese military aircraft registered zero incursions into Taiwan's air defense identification zone for the remainder of 20 March amid sustained low sortie levels averaging fewer than five daily incursions.

  • CENTCOM: During the reporting period, Iran fired ballistic missiles that fragmented and impacted Jerusalem's Old City near the Temple Mount and Al-Aqsa Mosque on 20 March, prompting Israeli condemnation of indiscriminate targeting. The Israel Defense Forces eliminated Mehdi Rastami Sh'mastan, a senior commander in Iran's Ministry of Intelligence responsible for global terrorist operations, and Esmail Ahmadi, head of the Basij Force Intelligence Division, along with several subordinates, through precision airstrikes in central Tehran on 20 March. Israeli forces executed additional airstrikes on Tehran while sustaining targeted ground operations in southern Lebanon against Hezbollah proxies, neutralizing over 120 command posts, 100 weapons depots, 130 missile launchers, and 570 terrorists since initiation. The United States deployed three additional warships and approximately 2,500 Marines to the Middle East, conducted strikes on Iranian naval assets in the Strait of Hormuz to restore maritime access, and announced reinforcements of thousands more troops amid Iran's continued assaults on Gulf oil infrastructure. President Trump declared intentions to wind down operations, proposing regional states assume policing duties in the Strait, as the conflict marked its 21st day without ceasefire indications.

  • AFRICOM: During the reporting period, Somali federal troops clashed with South West State forces in Baraawe on 20 March amid escalating political crisis, while Ethiopian forces contended with Amhara Fano battles in the Amhara region, hit-and-run attacks in Oromia, and heightened Tigray Defence Forces risks, exacerbating tensions with Eritrea and Sudan over alleged Rapid Support Forces support. United States mediators hosted Congo-Rwanda talks on 20 March to de-escalate Democratic Republic of Congo conflict, as a drone strike on the Chad-Sudan border killed 17 individuals. Nigerian security analysts warned of imminent jihadist assaults in Taraba and Southern Kaduna regions, citing armed infiltrations and staging near New Gboko on 20 March. Rapid Support Forces executed civilian killings in North Kordofan, Sudan, prompting Eid observances under ongoing civil war conditions on 21 March, while Sahel jihadist activity reaffirmed the region's status as global terrorism epicentre. Horn of Africa rivalries intensified over Ethiopian port ambitions, Nile waters, and Somaliland alignments.
