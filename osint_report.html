Intent: The intent of this report is to provide a high-level overview of significant military and geopolitical developments during the reporting period. The report consists of open-source reporting and updates on evolving conflicts, diplomatic initiatives, and strategic military actions involving countries of interest in the following Combatant Commands: SOUTHCOM, EUCOM, PACOM, CENTCOM, AFRICOM.

Executive Summary: During the reporting period, United States forces positioned CV-22B tiltrotors and MC-130J Commando II aircraft near Venezuela on 29 March, coinciding with Nicolas Maduro's first online communication from United States custody, reaffirming his stability amidst ongoing political turmoil. Russian forces conducted extensive missile and drone strikes against Ukraine, resulting in civilian casualties and infrastructure damage, while President Putin rejected peace negotiations in favor of countermeasures. The People's Republic of China enhanced its maritime presence around Scarborough Shoal, asserting territorial claims and deploying surveillance resources to counter United States submarine operations. Israeli forces executed significant airstrikes targeting Iranian military assets, prompting retaliatory missile attacks from Iran and heightening regional tensions as U.S. troop levels in the Middle East surpassed 50,000. In Somalia, intensified clashes between Somali National Army forces and Al-Shabaab militants raised alarms among UN officials regarding civilian security, with neighboring states indicating readiness to mediate the escalating conflict.

  • SOUTHCOM: During the reporting period, United States forces positioned CV-22B tiltrotors and MC-130J Commando II aircraft proximal to Venezuela on 29 March amid sustained operational oversight. Nicolas Maduro disseminated his inaugural online communique from United States custody on 29 March, confirming his stability with his spouse and acknowledging supporter solidarity following his January forcible extraction via military raid. Intelligence assessments documented prospective troop mobilizations adjacent to Venezuelan frontiers, while commentators scrutinized the Iran conflict's ramifications on regional economies in Colombia, Ecuador, Chile, Uruguay, Paraguay, and Panama. Brazilian representatives contested United States positions on e-commerce moratorium extensions at World Trade Organization deliberations extending into 30 March, heightening transhemispheric trade frictions. Public and official dialogues intensified over prospective United States troop withdrawals from Venezuela post-regime transition enforcement.

  • EUCOM: During the reporting period, Russian forces launched one Kinzhal aeroballistic missile and 442 drones, predominantly Shahed variants, against Ukraine on the night of 28 to 29 March; Ukrainian air defenses downed 380 drones, while 16 struck targets and debris affected 14 locations across Chernihiv, Kharkiv, Kyiv, Mykolaiv, Odesa, and Sumy oblasts, killing one child and injuring at least ten civilians, including eight children, at a Mykolaiv public recreation area. Russian aircraft executed five KAB-250 glide bomb strikes on Kramatorsk, Donetsk Oblast, on 29 March, killing three civilians, injuring thirteen, and damaging over thirty civilian infrastructure objects, followed by cluster munition artillery fire. Elements of the Russian 3rd Combined Arms Army halted advances near Kryva Luka and Zakitne east of Slovyansk after prior tactical penetrations, as 20th and 25th Combined Arms Armies progressed slowly in the Lyman direction and 8th Combined Arms Army with 3rd Army Corps advanced sluggishly toward Kostyantynivka. President Vladimir Putin rejected peace talks and ordered decisive countermeasures against Ukrainian strikes on Russian territory during a 29 March cabinet meeting. President Volodymyr Zelenskyy disclosed that Russian forces deployed 3000 drones, 1450 guided aerial bombs, and 40 missiles against Ukraine between 22 and 29 March.

  • PACOM: During the reporting period, People's Republic of China (PRC) Southern Theater Command forces executed joint patrols involving People's Liberation Army Navy (PLAN) surface combatants, People's Liberation Army Air Force (PLAAF) fixed-wing aircraft, and China Coast Guard (CCG) vessels around Scarborough Shoal (Huangyan Dao) in the South China Sea on 29 March. CCG units enforced maritime law in adjacent territorial waters after a Philippine vessel maneuvered proximate to the feature. PRC authorities sustained coast guard presence to assert administrative control over the disputed shoal amid Philippine resupply concerns. Intelligence assessments confirmed PRC deployment of 42 underwater surveillance vessels across South China Sea, Taiwan-adjacent waters, and western Pacific approaches to counter United States submarine operations. Philippine leadership initiated bilateral dialogue resets with PRC counterparts while signaling prospective joint patrols with regional partners.

  • CENTCOM: During the reporting period, Israeli Air Force executed over 150 airstrikes with fighter jets targeting weapons production facilities, a television station, and a petrochemical complex in Tehran and Tabriz on 29 March, while US and Israeli forces struck a southern Iranian port near the Strait of Hormuz, killing five personnel and inducing power outages across Tehran suburbs. Iran retaliated by launching multiple ballistic missile volleys at southern Israel, igniting fires at an industrial park near Beersheba, and fired 14 ballistic missiles alongside 12 drones into Kuwaiti airspace, injuring 10 Kuwaiti service members, damaging a logistics warehouse, and striking a power and desalination plant, resulting in one Indian worker fatality. Iran-backed Houthi forces conducted two salvos of ballistic missiles and drones against Israeli targets, with Israeli defenses intercepting most projectiles. United States Central Command deployed several hundred Special Operations forces, including Army Rangers and Navy SEALs, alongside 3,500 Marines and sailors aboard USS Tripoli arriving in the Middle East, elevating total US regional troop presence beyond 50,000 amid preparations for potential ground operations. Israel expanded its security zone in southern Lebanon against Hezbollah positions. Diplomatically, Saudi Arabia, Egypt, and Turkey convened in Islamabad on 29 March for de-escalation talks, while US President Trump announced Iran's agreement to permit 20 additional oil cargo ships through the Strait of Hormuz starting 30 March.

  • AFRICOM: During the reporting period, Somali National Army forces clashed intensely with Al-Shabaab militants near Daynuuney, 18 kilometres from Baidoa in South West Somalia on 29 March, sustaining casualties among troops whom field officers evacuated to Mogadishu amid disputed claims of territorial control by the Ministry of Defence. United Nations officials warned of heightened conflict risks to civilians in South West Somalia on 29 March, attributing escalations to federal government-state tensions that displaced nearly 50,000 individuals and exacerbated drought-induced vulnerabilities. Intergovernmental Authority on Development signalled readiness to facilitate dialogue between South West Somalia authorities and the federal government on 30 March, urging de-escalation to avert broader instability. Sudanese Armed Forces and Rapid Support Forces continued drone-enabled attrition operations across multiple fronts, with analysts noting Iranian logistical support sustaining belligerent entrenchment and potential spillover into Sahel states, though no discrete incidents materialised within the timeframe. Centre for Democracy and Development released assessments on 29 March highlighting persistent security deterioration and post-coup governance challenges in Sahel nations including Mali, Niger, and Burkina Faso, underscoring enduring jihadist threats despite regime popularity.
