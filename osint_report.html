Open Source Intelligence (OSINT) Report

Date of Access: 07 November 2025

Date of Information: 07 November 2025

Reporting Period: 06 November - 07 November 2025

Distribution: Approved for public release; distribution is unlimited.

Combatant Commands: SOUTHCOM, EUCOM, PACOM, CENTCOM, AFRICOM

Intent: The intent of this report is to provide a high-level overview of significant military and geopolitical developments during the reporting period. The report consists of open-source reporting and updates on evolving conflicts, diplomatic initiatives, and strategic military actions involving countries of interest in the following Combatant Commands: SOUTHCOM, EUCOM, PACOM, CENTCOM, AFRICOM.

Executive Summary: During the reporting period, significant military and geopolitical developments occurred across multiple regions. The U.S. military conducted a lethal strike on a suspected drug trafficking vessel in the Eastern Pacific on 5 November, part of a broader trend of intensified drug interdiction efforts resulting in 16 drone strikes since September. NATO engaged in military exercises in Romania and bolstered readiness along its eastern flank in response to heightened Russian aggression amid the ongoing Ukraine conflict, where Ukrainian forces faced intensified operations from Russia. Tensions in North Korea escalated as artillery rockets were fired ahead of U.S. Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth’s visit to the Korean border. In South Africa, President Cyril Ramaphosa launched an investigation into the recruitment of citizens as mercenaries in Ukraine. The U.S. also proposed a UN resolution for transitional governance in Gaza amidst increasing Israeli military spending and ongoing tensions in the region.

  • SOUTHCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that the U.S. military conducted a strike on 5 November 2025, targeting a boat suspected of drug smuggling in the Eastern Pacific, resulting in the deaths of two individuals. This incident marked the 16th attack by the Pentagon related to drug interdiction efforts since September, which included a total of 16 lethal drone strikes that killed five suspected drug smugglers. Secretary of Defense Pete Hegseth defended these actions, stating that the targets were labeled as narco-terrorists, highlighting a shift from previous non-lethal interdiction strategies. Concurrently, discussions continued regarding U.S. policy toward Venezuela, as President Donald Trump expressed mixed inclinations towards direct military interventions, amidst ongoing calls for regime change to secure control over Venezuelan oil. Additionally, Senate Intelligence Vice Chairman Mark Warner voiced support for the intelligence backing the strikes, while emphasizing the need for increased transparency from the Trump administration.

  • EUCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated significant military and geopolitical developments related to the ongoing Russia-Ukraine conflict. On 4 November, NATO conducted military exercises in Alba Iulia, Romania, emphasizing its readiness to bolster troop levels along its eastern flank in response to Russian aggression. Russian President Vladimir Putin announced potential reciprocal measures concerning nuclear testing after comments from US President Donald Trump suggested renewed American tests might occur. A report from the French Institute of International Relations noted that Europe would face challenges in mobilizing troops and weapons in the event of a direct confrontation with Russia. Concurrently, Germany's Chancellor Friedrich Merz convened the first meeting of a new National Security Council to address hybrid threats posed by Moscow. Meanwhile, Ukraine's defense authorities indicated the possibility of losing a critical city within a week, as Russian forces intensified their military operations. Additionally, a probe into the involvement of South African citizens fighting as mercenaries in Ukraine was ordered by President Cyril Ramaphosa.

  • PACOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that Philippine President Ferdinand Marcos Jr. declared a state of emergency on 24 October after Typhoon Kalmaegi caused at least 114 deaths and left hundreds missing across central provinces, notably Cebu, which experienced severe flooding. The typhoon, having regained strength, moved towards central Vietnam, prompting meteorologists to issue warnings for the region. Concurrently, North Korea fired multiple artillery rockets just prior to the visit of U.S. Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth to the Korean border, showcasing heightened military activity. In response to the growing threats from China, the United States deployed two new ground-based satellite jammers to counter China's expanding space-based intelligence and surveillance capabilities. Additionally, the U.S. military carried out a strike in the Eastern Pacific, targeting a vessel involved in drug trafficking, resulting in the deaths of two individuals. These developments underscore the escalating tensions and military activities in the Asia-Pacific region.

  • CENTCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that on 4 November 2025, thousands of Iranians commemorated the 46th anniversary of the 1979 storming of the US embassy in Tehran, displaying missile replicas and effigies of former US President Donald Trump and former Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu. In New York City, Israel's diaspora affairs minister Amichai Chikli warned Jewish Americans to consider emigration following the election of Muslim Mayor Zohran Mamdani, whom he labeled a "Hamas sympathizer." Amid escalating tensions, Israel's military expenditures rose to $76.3 billion since the onset of operations in Gaza in October 2023. Israeli Minister Avi Dichter stated that the current ceasefire only served to prepare for a military dismantling of Hamas. On the geopolitical front, the United States proposed a UN resolution aimed at establishing a transitional governance body in Gaza, while simultaneously engaging with the UN to lift sanctions on Syrian leader Abu Mohammed al-Jolani. In tactical operations, the US military conducted 16 drone strikes in the Eastern Pacific, striking alleged drug trafficking vessels and resulting in multiple fatalities. The reporting period also noted the resignation of New York City’s Jewish FDNY Commissioner Robert Tucker after Mamdani's election, highlighting community tensions exacerbated by political shifts.

  • AFRICOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated significant developments in military and geopolitical issues. South Africa's President Cyril Ramaphosa ordered an investigation into the recruitment of South African citizens as mercenaries in the ongoing conflict in Ukraine. Meanwhile, the United Kingdom reiterated its support for the United Nations Interim Security Force in Abyei, Sudan, underscoring its commitment to regional stability. In Venezuela, the United States received increased scrutiny for its regime-change tactics amid allegations of seeking control over the country's oil reserves, sparking debates over foreign intervention in Latin America. Additionally, US President Donald Trump claimed to have facilitated a peace agreement between India and Pakistan, asserting that his threats regarding trade deals had resolved their military tensions earlier in May. These reports highlight the ongoing complexities of international relations amid emerging security concerns.
