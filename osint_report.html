  • SOUTHCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that the United States seized a large oil tanker off the coast of Venezuela on 9 December. U.S. Attorney General Pam Bondi stated that the vessel was involved in shipping sanctioned oil from Venezuela and Iran. The seizure escalated tensions between the Trump administration and the Venezuelan government, which condemned the action as an "act of international piracy." President Donald Trump announced the operation publicly, asserting it was part of a broader anti-drug strategy. In response, Venezuela's officials accused the U.S. of theft and called for international condemnation of the seizure. Russia reiterated its support for Venezuela, while the seizure contributed to rising oil prices amid concerns of further supply disruptions. Additionally, the Pentagon expressed that the U.S. military remains unprepared for a potential conflict with China, suggesting ongoing geopolitical vulnerabilities.

  • EUCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated significant military and geopolitical developments in the ongoing Russia-Ukraine conflict. On 11 December, Russian Foreign Ministry spokeswoman Maria Zakharova urged the United Kingdom to disclose details regarding the activities of a British soldier killed in Ukraine, highlighting allegations of Britain's broader involvement. Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelenskyy convened urgent discussions with leaders from approximately 30 nations, emphasizing the need for continued support amid pressure for territorial concessions from both Russia and the United States. Ukraine executed a successful drone strike on Lukoil's Vladimir Filanovsky oil platform in the Caspian Sea and launched an attack on a Russian ship allegedly part of its shadow fleet. Ukraine's military faced a significant mechanized Russian assault in Pokrovsk, with Russia claiming control, while Ukraine contested this assertion. Additionally, a Polish court detained a Russian archaeologist, wanted in Ukraine for illegally excavating an ancient site in Crimea. Amid these actions, the U.S. House of Representatives approved an $800 million aid package for Ukraine, though divisions within the U.S. and European nations persisted regarding the use of frozen Russian assets.

  • PACOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that the Trump administration faced growing tensions in East Asia as U.S. nuclear-capable bombers joined Japanese fighter jets in a show of force over the Sea of Japan on 10 December, following joint military exercises by China and Russia. The U.S. and Japan reaffirmed their commitment to regional security in response to increased military activity by China, which included joint flights with Russian bombers. A Pentagon report highlighted concerns about the U.S. military's ability to deter China in a potential conflict over Taiwan, indicating the United States would struggle due to China's rapid weapon production capabilities. Additionally, the U.S. Navy and the Armed Forces of the Philippines conducted joint maritime activities within the Philippines' Exclusive Economic Zone, showcasing their coalition's commitment to regional stability amidst rising tensions. Countries in the region, including Japan and NATO allies, expressed serious concerns over the ongoing military maneuvers by China and Russia.

  • CENTCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that Amnesty International accused Hamas of committing crimes against humanity, including murder and torture, during and after the 7 October massacre. A severe winter storm, Storm Byron, struck the Gaza Strip, exacerbating the humanitarian crisis for displaced families living in flooded tent camps. Gaza activists accused Hamas of hoarding essential supplies, including baby formula, amid the ongoing hunger crisis, while Israel faced criticism for not adequately responding to the disaster affecting both Palestinians and its own citizens. Israeli forces reportedly opened fire on a UN peacekeeping patrol in southern Lebanon, prompting the United Nations Interim Force in Lebanon to demand an end to such aggressive actions. Meanwhile, Israel's Prime Minister Netanyahu faced scrutiny over comments praising ties with Russia, leading to the summoning of the Ukrainian ambassador. The United States advanced legislation that rolled back sanctions against Syria, further shifting the regional geopolitical landscape.

  • AFRICOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated significant developments in military and geopolitical dynamics in Africa and beyond. The South Sudan army initiated plans to secure the critical Heglig oilfield in Sudan, following the capture of the facility by the Rapid Support Forces (RSF) and a deadly drone attack that targeted Sudan's largest energy installation. Additionally, U.S. Republican Senator John Kennedy expressed views suggesting South Africa should be excluded from a prominent trade initiative, reflecting rising tensions between the United States and South Africa. Notably, the aftermath of President Donald Trump's announced peace deal did not alleviate ongoing conflicts in the Congo and Rwanda, as concerns about the practical implications of this diplomacy grew. Further, the Africa Cup of Nations, set to commence on 21 December 2025 in Morocco, generated excitement across the continent, with several nations, including Nigeria and Zimbabwe, finalizing their squads for the tournament.
