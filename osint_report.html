Date of Information: 20 March 2026

Reporting Period: 19 March - 20 March 2026

Distribution: Approved for public release; distribution is unlimited.

Combatant Commands: SOUTHCOM, EUCOM, PACOM, CENTCOM, AFRICOM

Intent: The intent of this report is to provide a high-level overview of significant military and geopolitical developments during the reporting period. The report consists of open-source reporting and updates on evolving conflicts, diplomatic initiatives, and strategic military actions involving countries of interest in the following Combatant Commands: SOUTHCOM, EUCOM, PACOM, CENTCOM, AFRICOM.

Executive Summary: During the reporting period, United States Southern Command Commander General Francis Donovan confirmed that no active military preparations for an invasion of Cuba existed despite heightened tensions. In Ecuador, President Daniel Noboa redeployed Armed Forces to address criminal threats in the Los Ríos province. Colombian President Gustavo Petro ratified key legislation regarding mercenary recruitment on 19 March, while Paraguay's President Santiago Peña authorized military support for flood relief on the same date. Ukrainian military operations resulted in significant territorial gains and strikes against Russian logistics, though Russian forces conducted extensive drone operations without notable advances. In East Asia, US intelligence noted China's non-kinetic approaches to Taiwan, countered by Taiwan's military readiness against ongoing Chinese buildup. Israel targeted Iranian facilities, leading to retaliatory strikes from Iran and escalating regional tensions. In Sudan, the Rapid Support Forces seized strategic cities, prompting a military response from Chadian leadership, while violence continued in Ethiopia and various other African nations experienced security challenges amidst international diplomatic efforts to de-escalate conflict.

  • SOUTHCOM: During the reporting period, United States Southern Command Commander General Francis Donovan denied active preparations or rehearsals for military invasion or takeover of Cuba amid heightened rhetorical tensions. Ecuadorian President Daniel Noboa directed the immediate redeployment of Armed Forces to ExpoQuevedo installations in Los Ríos province to counter criminal threats following prior withdrawal. Colombian President Gustavo Petro enacted legislation ratifying the United Nations International Convention against the Recruitment, Financing, and Training of Mercenaries on 19 March. Paraguayan President Santiago Peña ordered Armed Forces deployment to support humanitarian relief and infrastructure recovery in flood-impacted regions on 19 March. Colombian forces maintained reinforced postures along the Venezuela border due to persistent post-Maduro instability risks.

  • EUCOM: During the reporting period, Ukrainian forces destroyed a complete battery of six Russian Grad multiple launch rocket systems via drone strikes in the Pokrovsk direction southeast of Zhuravka, advanced in the Oleksandrivka direction liberating over 285 square kilometres since 19 February and northwest of Hulyaipole, repelled a Russian motorized assault on the southwestern outskirts of Kostyantynivka, and blocked a small Russian naval infantry and Spetsnaz group in the Pokrovsk direction. Russian forces conducted offensive operations across the northern Sumy, northern Kharkiv, Oskil River near Kupyansk, Slovyansk, Kostyantynivka-Druzhkivka, Dobropillya, Pokrovsk, Novopavlivka, Oleksandrivka, and Hulyaipole directions but achieved no confirmed advances, infiltrated positions in Kivsharivka southeast of Kupyansk and southern Kostyantynivka without altering control lines, and launched 133 drones overnight 18 to 19 March targeting Chernihiv, Volyn, Odesa, Dnipropetrovsk, Zaporizhia, and Lviv oblasts, of which Ukrainian air defences downed 109. Ukrainian forces executed strikes against Russian ammunition depots in occupied Donetsk City and Yalta, a material-technical warehouse near Vesele, personnel concentrations near Yalta and Striletska Bay, two Buk-M1 air defence systems, energy facilities, fuel and ammunition warehouses, Shahed drone communication towers, and satellite infrastructure in occupied Zaporizhia Oblast, an ammunition depot near occupied Kalanchak and fuel depot near occupied Novotroitske in Kherson Oblast, and the Granit Innovation Center air defence repair facility in occupied Sevastopol. A Russian Su-30 fighter violated Estonian airspace over the Gulf of Finland for one minute on 18 March. Geopolitically, Russian officials declared Ukraine peace talks in situational pause amid the Iran conflict, European Union leaders pressured Hungary's Viktor Orban to release a 103 billion dollar loan for Ukraine on 19 March, and Ukraine confirmed ongoing daily contacts with US and Russian negotiation teams despite the hold.

  • PACOM: During the reporting period, US intelligence agencies assessed that China refrained from planning a 2027 invasion of Taiwan and pursued non-kinetic control measures, while Taiwan officials warned of China's unabated military buildup across army, navy, air force, and rocket force units near the island. Taiwan President Lai Ching-te inspected the nation's submarine fleet amid heightened deterrence requirements. China accused the Philippines of instigating South China Sea disputes and criticized Japan for amplifying the issue to counter Beijing strategically. People's Liberation Army forces sustained development of invasion capabilities despite the US determination of no imminent 2027 operation, with Taiwan's Ministry of National Defense monitoring persistent encirclement activities on 20 March.

  • CENTCOM: During the reporting period, Israel executed strikes on Iran's South Pars natural gas field on 19 March, prompting Iran to launch missiles at Israel's Haifa oil refinery complex, Saudi Arabian refineries in Riyadh, and Qatar's Ras Laffan energy hub. United States officials received prior notification of Israel's South Pars operation but declined participation, while President Trump warned Iran of retaliatory destruction of the field should it target Qatar again. Israeli Prime Minister Netanyahu declared on 19 March that Iranian uranium enrichment and ballistic missile production capacities collapsed after twenty days of conflict, hinting at forthcoming ground operations alongside sustained airstrikes on 200 targets across western and central Iran. Qatar expelled Iran's military and security attachés within 24 hours, Brent crude prices surged to 115 dollars per barrel, and Hezbollah exchanged strikes with Israel amid Iran's renewed threats to dismantle regional energy infrastructure.

  • AFRICOM: During the reporting period, Rapid Support Forces seized the strategic cities of Bara in North Kordofan and Karnoi in North Darfur on 19 March, while RSF elements killed 12 civilians including six women in Shuraim Mima north of Bara and conducted a drone strike that killed 16 civilians at an Iftar gathering in Mabrouka on the Chad-Sudan border. Chadian President Mahamat Idriss Deby ordered military retaliation, placed forces on maximum alert, and closed the border with Sudan indefinitely following the incident. Fano militias launched a multi-pronged assault in South Gondar region of Amhara, Ethiopia, killing at least 650 Ethiopian National Defense Force soldiers and capturing 418 others. Nigerian troops repelled an insurgent attack on a military base, eliminating 80 fighters, amid ongoing security operations in Borno State where suspected suicide bombings in Maiduguri claimed 23 lives as reported on 19 March. United States officials hosted de-escalation talks between Democratic Republic of Congo and Rwanda representatives on 19 March to address eastern DRC tensions. Somali authorities issued warnings against alleged Israeli plans for a military base in Somaliland, exacerbating federal-regional frictions after Southwest State suspended cooperation with Mogadishu on 17 March. Kenyan and Ugandan authorities resolved a Turkana border standoff through a livestock exchange deal on 19 March, enabling herder crossings. South African National Assembly convened on 19 March to debate geopolitical implications of external conflicts on national interests. Djiboutian officials confirmed President Ismail Omar Guelleh faced a single challenger in the upcoming presidential election.
