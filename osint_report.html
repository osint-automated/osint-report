Open Source Intelligence (OSINT) Report

Date of Access: 07 March 2026

Date of Information: 07 March 2026

Reporting Period: 06 March - 07 March 2026

Distribution: Approved for public release; distribution is unlimited.

Combatant Commands: SOUTHCOM, EUCOM, PACOM, CENTCOM, AFRICOM

Intent: The intent of this report is to provide a high-level overview of significant military and geopolitical developments during the reporting period. The report consists of open-source reporting and updates on evolving conflicts, diplomatic initiatives, and strategic military actions involving countries of interest in the following Combatant Commands: SOUTHCOM, EUCOM, PACOM, CENTCOM, AFRICOM.

Executive Summary: During the reporting period, U.S. Southern Command hosted the inaugural Americas Counter Cartel Conference in Doral, Florida, where U.S. leadership urged regional partners to intensify military action against transnational criminal organizations and expanded coordination on intelligence sharing and border security while U.S. Special Forces continued advisory support to Ecuador’s Operation Southern Spear. Ukrainian forces conducted drone strikes on Russian military assets in occupied Crimea, while Russia maintained missile and drone attacks on Ukrainian infrastructure; both sides completed a 1,000-person prisoner exchange and frontlines remained largely unchanged. China announced a 7 percent defense budget increase, condemned U.S.-Israeli strikes on Iran, and emphasized diplomatic engagement while no Indo-Pacific military incidents occurred. U.S. and Israeli forces sustained large-scale strikes on Iranian military infrastructure and Hezbollah targets, reducing Iranian missile activity while limited retaliatory launches continued toward Israel. In Africa, Sudanese Armed Forces recaptured Bara from Rapid Support Forces, militants killed 15 soldiers in northern Benin, and no major operational shifts occurred across other regional conflict zones.

  • SOUTHCOM: During the reporting period, U.S. Southern Command hosted the inaugural Americas Counter Cartel Conference at its headquarters in Doral, Florida, where Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth addressed defense leaders from over a dozen Latin American and Caribbean nations—including Argentina, Honduras, and the Dominican Republic—and urged aggressive military offensives against drug cartels and transnational criminal organizations, warning that the United States would act unilaterally if partners failed to confront threats to hemispheric security and U.S. homeland interests. Hegseth signed a joint security declaration with attending representatives to advance coordinated efforts against narco-terrorist networks, emphasizing border security and intelligence sharing amid ongoing U.S. support for joint operations in Ecuador launched earlier in March under Operation Southern Spear, where U.S. Special Forces continued advisory roles in planning raids against groups such as Los Lobos and Los Choneros without direct combat participation. No new kinetic strikes, territorial changes, or major escalations occurred in Venezuela, Guyana's Essequibo dispute, Central American border zones, or other South American flashpoints, though preparations advanced for the upcoming "Shield of the Americas" summit in Miami to formalize U.S.-led alliances excluding Chinese infrastructure influence in ports, lithium, and telecommunications; Colombia, Brazil, and Mexico notably abstained from participation in the Counter Cartel Conference. No direct military engagements or geopolitical shifts materialized in response to concurrent U.S. operations against Iran.

  • EUCOM: During the reporting period, Ukrainian forces executed drone strikes on Russian targets in occupied Crimea, damaging facilities at the Yevpatoria Aircraft Repair Plant and destroying two Pantsir-S2 air defense systems near Dzhankoi airfield, while naval and airborne drones targeted the Syvash drilling platform in the Black Sea, where they struck equipment, personnel, and a Ka-27 helicopter attempting to land. Russia launched drone and missile barrages against Ukrainian infrastructure, including railway assets in Mykolaiv Oblast that damaged trains and killed civilians, with Ukrainian defenses intercepting multiple Shahed drones over Kharkiv Oblast and other regions amid reports of infrastructure hits. Both sides completed a large-scale prisoner exchange, repatriating 500 individuals each (200 on the first day and 300 on the second) in a coordinated swap. Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelenskyy postponed upcoming trilateral U.S.-Russia-Ukraine peace talks due to the diversion of diplomatic focus amid U.S.-Israeli operations against Iran, while directing Ukrainian support—including interceptor drones and specialists—to assist U.S. and Gulf air defense efforts against Iranian Shahed drones in the Middle East. Frontline dynamics showed no major territorial shifts in eastern or southern sectors such as Slovyansk, Pokrovsk, or Kostyantynivka-Druzhkivka areas, though localized positional adjustments and high attrition continued; northern Sumy Oblast remained stable without Russian advances.

  • PACOM: During the reporting period, China announced a 7 percent increase in its 2026 defense budget during the opening of the National People's Congress Two Sessions to advance military modernization amid strategic rivalry with the United States. China condemned U.S.-Israeli military strikes on Iran as unacceptable violations of sovereignty, regime-change efforts, and breaches of international law, with Foreign Minister Wang Yi and spokespersons urging an immediate halt to operations, prevention of escalation, protection of shipping through the Strait of Hormuz, and resumption of dialogue through diplomatic channels with regional actors including Russia, Saudi Arabia, and others. Beijing expressed grave concern over spillover risks to energy security, global oil and LNG flows critical to its imports, and civilian infrastructure, while pledging measures to protect Chinese nationals in the region through evacuations and preparedness warnings. China signaled continued commitment to multi-level communication with the United States, including preparations for mid-March trade talks in Paris and the anticipated Trump-Xi summit in Beijing at the end of March, while reaffirming defense of its core interests on sovereignty, security, and development. No direct U.S.-China military incidents, confrontations, or exercises occurred in the Indo-Pacific, Taiwan Strait, or South China Sea domains.

  • CENTCOM: During the reporting period, U.S. and Israeli forces sustained broad-scale airstrikes on Iranian infrastructure, including a major overnight wave targeting Tehran command centers, Mehrabad Airport as the primary domestic hub, ballistic missile production facilities, remaining launch sites, and deeply buried assets with penetrator munitions from B-2 bombers, while CENTCOM reported that Iranian ballistic missile attacks declined by approximately 90 percent since the campaign began. Israel conducted additional intense strikes on Hezbollah targets in Beirut's southern suburbs and southern Lebanon, killing at least 16 people according to Lebanese health authorities and prompting further civilian evacuations amid retaliatory rocket fire toward northern Israel. Iran launched reduced retaliatory missile barrages toward Israel, triggering air raid sirens in East Jerusalem and Tel Aviv with visible interceptions and minor impacts or injuries reported, while Iranian President Masoud Pezeshkian stated that attacks on Gulf neighbors would cease unless used to launch further strikes against Iran. Proxy elements including Hezbollah maintained supporting operations against Israel, but no large-scale new barrages or direct hits on U.S. bases materialized beyond prior patterns; defensive systems across Israel and Gulf states intercepted most threats, though regional disruptions to airspace, shipping, and energy flows persisted. Cumulative Iranian casualties from strikes exceeded 940 reported deaths with over 1,200 in some estimates, Israeli civilian fatalities remained around 11 from Iranian projectiles, and Lebanese deaths from related operations reached 217; President Trump vowed dramatically increased firepower, rejected unconditional surrender demands as unmet, and indicated consideration of previously untargeted areas or groups for complete destruction, as the conflict entered its second week without major new nuclear site damage or ground invasion announcements.

  • AFRICOM: During the reporting period, the Sudanese Armed Forces and allied Joint Forces recaptured the strategic city of Bara in North Kordofan state from Rapid Support Forces control following intense battles, drone strikes, and ground assaults originating from El Obeid, while RSF elements retaliated by looting and burning the nearby village of Umm Kuraidim after alleging civilian collaboration with SAF. Jihadist militants conducted a deadly attack in northern Benin that killed 15 soldiers, highlighting persistent escalation by groups such as JNIM along West African border zones including Benin, Niger, and Nigeria amid regional instability. The United States and European Union issued a joint statement expressing profound concern over continued ceasefire violations in eastern Democratic Republic of Congo involving M23 rebels and called for all parties to recommit to negotiations and halt fighting. No major new kinetic operations, territorial shifts, or direct engagements occurred in Somalia against al-Shabaab, the Sahel jihadist theaters, Ethiopia's Tigray region, or Libya, though ongoing concerns persisted regarding potential spillover from the U.S.-Israeli conflict with Iran, including risks to Red Sea security, Horn of Africa stability, energy prices impacting African economies, and indirect effects on counterterrorism support in regions like the Sahel. The East African Community prepared for an ordinary Summit in Arusha on 7 March amid budgetary strains and leadership transitions, with no associated military developments reported.
