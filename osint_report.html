Date of Information: 12 March 2026

Reporting Period: 11 March - 12 March 2026

Distribution: Approved for public release; distribution is unlimited.

Combatant Commands: SOUTHCOM, EUCOM, PACOM, CENTCOM, AFRICOM

Intent: The intent of this report is to provide a high-level overview of significant military and geopolitical developments during the reporting period. The report consists of open-source reporting and updates on evolving conflicts, diplomatic initiatives, and strategic military actions involving countries of interest in the following Combatant Commands: SOUTHCOM, EUCOM, PACOM, CENTCOM, AFRICOM.

Executive Summary: During the reporting period, the United States expanded counternarcotics military operations in the eastern Pacific with an additional strike on an alleged drug-smuggling vessel during joint efforts with Ecuador against narco-terrorist groups, while the administration advanced a 17-nation Americas Counter-Cartel Coalition to coordinate regional military and intelligence actions. Ukrainian forces conducted a long-range missile strike on a Russian missile component facility in Bryansk, degrading production capacity as Russian forces achieved limited tactical gains in eastern Ukraine amid continued Ukrainian deep strikes on energy infrastructure. The People’s Liberation Army resumed limited air activity near Taiwan and maintained routine naval patrols while satellite imagery indicated potential renewed island-building in the South China Sea. Iranian forces escalated hostilities through naval drone attacks on US-linked tankers, naval mine deployments in the Strait of Hormuz, and ballistic missile strikes on Israel, prompting US and Israeli retaliatory strikes and US naval interdiction of Iranian minelaying vessels. In Africa, M23-linked drone strikes in eastern Democratic Republic of Congo and Rapid Support Forces drone attacks in Sudan caused civilian casualties while both conflicts continued without significant territorial change.

  • SOUTHCOM: During the reporting period, the United States escalates military involvement in counternarcotics operations, with the US military conducting an additional strike on an alleged drug-smuggling boat in the eastern Pacific Ocean, killing six individuals and raising the total reported strikes to 45 since September amid ongoing joint operations with Ecuador against groups designated as narco-terrorists, including Los Choneros and Los Lobos, supported by US Southern Command intelligence and logistics. The Trump administration advances the Americas Counter-Cartel Coalition framework announced at the recent Shield of the Americas summit, with 17 countries signing on to coordinate military and intelligence efforts against transnational criminal networks, while Ecuador prepares expanded attacks on its criminal economy with US backing and severs diplomatic ties with Cuba. No significant interstate border escalations occur in the Venezuela-Guyana Essequibo dispute or other flashpoints, though Venezuela transfers seized oil from a US-apprehended tanker off Texas to a smaller vessel for onshore processing. In Haiti, persistent gang violence continues to displace populations and strain humanitarian access without reported major new incidents or territorial shifts in the immediate period, as broader regional focus remains on US-led anti-cartel militarization amid global distractions from the US-Israel-Iran conflict.

  • EUCOM: During the reporting period, Ukrainian forces conducted a successful strike on a key Russian missile component production plant in the Bryansk region using long-range missiles, including British-supplied Storm Shadow systems, disrupting Russian missile manufacturing capabilities while Russia accuses the United Kingdom of direct involvement in a "terrorist attack" and claims civilian casualties. Ukrainian drones targeted an oil hub in Russia's Krasnodar region, igniting facilities and further pressuring Russian energy infrastructure amid ongoing deep strikes. Russian forces advanced incrementally in eastern Ukraine, capturing the Krasnaya Zarya tract near the border and expanding control in the Grishina area under the Tsentr group, while repelling Ukrainian attempts to seize positions in the Zaporizhia direction near Andriyivka and Otradne. Russian artillery and drone operators supported defensive operations against Ukrainian counterattacks in multiple sectors, including Dobropillia and Gulyaipole, where positional battles continue along logistical routes. No major territorial shifts occurred beyond these localized gains, as both sides maintain attritional pressure with reduced intensity compared to prior weeks, amid broader geopolitical distractions from the concurrent US-Israel-Iran war.

  • PACOM: During the reporting period, the People's Liberation Army (PLA) resumed limited air activity around Taiwan after a nearly two-week pause in fighter jet incursions into the island's air defense identification zone (ADIZ), with Taiwan's Ministry of National Defense detecting five PLA aircraft operating in the Taiwan Strait, three of which crossed the unofficial median line, coinciding with a US Navy P-8A Poseidon patrol aircraft transit through the strait that the PLA Air Force tracked and monitored. Chinese naval vessels maintain daily presence around Taiwan without reported escalations. Satellite imagery indicates potential renewed island-building activity at Antelope Reef in the Paracel Islands of the South China Sea, expanding the surface area of the feature claimed by both China and Vietnam after a decade-long pause in such construction. No major PLA Navy deployments, ballistic missile tests, or direct confrontations occurred in the broader Western Pacific, amid ongoing US and allied exercises like Freedom Shield 26 in South Korea and preparations for overlapping Indo-Pacific drills, while analysts note Beijing's opportunistic observation of US resource diversions to the concurrent Iran conflict potentially creating windows for reduced deterrence in the region.

  • CENTCOM: During the reporting period, Iranian forces launched multiple retaliatory strikes, including naval drone attacks that destroyed two US-linked oil tankers near Iraq in the Persian Gulf, resulting in crew casualties and prompting Iraq to suspend oil terminal operations. Iran additionally deployed approximately a dozen naval mines in the Strait of Hormuz and conducted ballistic missile barrages under Operation True Promise 4, targeting Israeli cities such as Tel Aviv and causing structural damage. US and Israeli air operations continued with strikes on Iranian targets, including Tehran, while the US Navy destroyed 16 Iranian minelaying vessels near the Strait to counter the mining threat. Iran's new Supreme Leader Mojtaba Khamenei issued his first public statement, vowing to maintain the Strait closure as leverage and threatening attacks on US regional bases unless closed. The Pentagon reports a decline in Iranian missile launches over the period, marking the lowest volume in a 24-hour cycle, amid broader degradation of Iranian naval and air capabilities.

  • AFRICOM: During the reporting period, drone strikes attributed to M23 rebels (with alleged Rwandan backing) targeted Goma in eastern Democratic Republic of Congo, killing at least three people including a French aid worker and one UN staff member—the first such attack in the rebel-held city since M23's seizure last year—amid ongoing condemnation from the US and UN of Rwanda's support for the group and calls for withdrawal. In Sudan, Rapid Support Forces (RSF) drone strikes killed 17 people, including students, in White Nile State, while the Sudanese Armed Forces maintained defensive positions in Kordofan regions against RSF advances, with no major territorial changes reported as the civil war enters its third year amid escalating civilian targeting by both sides. Sahel jihadist activity remained limited in immediate reporting, with no confirmed large-scale attacks in Mali, Burkina Faso, or Niger over the period, though broader regional insecurity persists from Alliance of Sahel States (AES) joint operations against armed groups. No significant new coups, interstate clashes, or external military interventions materialized, as ongoing conflicts in Sudan and eastern DRC dominate amid global attention diverted to the US-Israel-Iran war and its secondary economic pressures on African energy and investment flows.
