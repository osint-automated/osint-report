Date of Information: 08 March 2026

Reporting Period: 07 March - 08 March 2026

Distribution: Approved for public release; distribution is unlimited.

Combatant Commands: SOUTHCOM, EUCOM, PACOM, CENTCOM, AFRICOM

Intent: The intent of this report is to provide a high-level overview of significant military and geopolitical developments during the reporting period. The report consists of open-source reporting and updates on evolving conflicts, diplomatic initiatives, and strategic military actions involving countries of interest in the following Combatant Commands: SOUTHCOM, EUCOM, PACOM, CENTCOM, AFRICOM.

Executive Summary: During the reporting period, U.S. Southern Command convened regional leaders in Miami to establish the Americas Counter Cartel Coalition and formalize expanded intelligence sharing and coordinated security operations targeting narco-terrorist networks, while ongoing advisory activities under Operation Southern Spear in Ecuador continued without new kinetic actions. In Europe, Ukrainian forces conducted precision drone strikes against Russian military infrastructure in occupied Crimea as Russia maintained drone and missile attacks on Ukrainian logistics and infrastructure, though front lines remained largely static and both sides completed a large prisoner exchange. In the Indo-Pacific, China signaled interest in stabilizing U.S. relations ahead of upcoming diplomatic engagements while announcing a defense budget increase and avoiding direct military escalation. In the Middle East, U.S. and Israeli forces intensified strikes against Iranian energy and military infrastructure as Iran conducted limited retaliatory missile and drone attacks against Israel and attempted strikes across the Gulf, while Israel simultaneously targeted Hezbollah positions in Lebanon amid rising regional casualties. In Africa, jihadist militants killed 15 soldiers in northern Benin during a cross-border insurgent attack linked to JNIM as international partners warned of continued instability in eastern Democratic Republic of Congo despite the absence of major new kinetic developments across other regional conflict zones.

  • SOUTHCOM: During the reporting period, U.S. Southern Command hosted the "Shield of the Americas" summit in Miami on March 7, where President Donald Trump convened leaders from at least a dozen Latin American and Caribbean nations—including Argentina, Ecuador, Guyana, Honduras, Peru, and others—to formalize the Americas Counter Cartel Coalition, a new U.S.-led military alliance focused on aggressive joint operations against drug cartels and transnational criminal organizations designated as narco-terrorist threats. Attendees signed a Joint Security Declaration committing to enhanced intelligence sharing, border security measures, and coordinated military actions, with Trump emphasizing unilateral U.S. readiness if partners hesitated and framing cartels as equivalent to groups like ISIS. The summit explicitly aimed to counter Chinese influence in regional infrastructure, ports, lithium resources, and telecommunications, excluding major nations such as Brazil, Mexico, and Colombia from participation amid their independent foreign policy stances. Ongoing U.S.-Ecuador joint operations under Operation Southern Spear continued without reported new kinetic actions in the period, as U.S. Special Forces provided advisory support, intelligence, and planning for raids targeting groups like Los Lobos and Los Choneros to disrupt cocaine flows through Ecuadorian ports. No new territorial disputes escalated in Guyana-Venezuela Essequibo tensions, no major cartel retaliatory violence or incidents surfaced in Mexico or elsewhere, and no direct military engagements or geopolitical shifts occurred in Central America beyond routine preparations; the events reinforced U.S. prioritization of hemispheric security and anti-China positioning despite concurrent Middle East commitments.

  • EUCOM: During the reporting period, Ukrainian forces executed targeted strikes in occupied Crimea, deploying drones to damage the Yevpatoria Aircraft Repair Plant's production facilities and destroy two Pantsir-S2 air defense systems near Dzhankoi airfield, while naval drones struck the Syvash drilling platform in the Black Sea, targeting equipment, personnel, and a Ka-27 helicopter. Russia launched multiple drone and missile attacks on Ukrainian infrastructure, hitting railway assets in Mykolaiv Oblast, damaging trains, and causing civilian casualties, with Ukrainian air defenses intercepting several Shahed drones over Kharkiv and other regions. Both sides completed a significant prisoner exchange, repatriating 500 personnel each (200 on the first day and 300 on the second) through coordinated negotiations. Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelenskyy postponed scheduled trilateral U.S.-Russia-Ukraine peace talks, citing diplomatic constraints from concurrent U.S.-Israeli operations against Iran, and authorized the deployment of Ukrainian interceptor drones and specialists to support U.S. and Gulf air defense efforts against Iranian Shahed drones in the Middle East. No major territorial changes occurred along frontline sectors including Slovyansk, Pokrovsk, or Kostyantynivka-Druzhkivka, though localized engagements persisted with high attrition rates; northern Sumy Oblast remained static without Russian advances.

  • PACOM: During the reporting period, China expressed hope that 2026 would become a landmark year for bilateral relations with the United States, as Foreign Minister Wang Yi stated during a press briefing on the sidelines of the National People's Congress annual session, emphasizing high-level exchanges between leaders as a strategic guarantee despite persistent differences, while urging management of divergences amid trade tensions and geopolitical issues. China reaffirmed its commitment to multi-level communication with the United States, including preparations for mid-March trade negotiations in Paris and the anticipated Trump-Xi summit in Beijing at the end of March. China condemned ongoing U.S.-Israeli military operations against Iran as unacceptable violations of sovereignty and international law, with officials calling for an immediate ceasefire, protection of shipping through the Strait of Hormuz, and prevention of escalation to safeguard energy security vital to Chinese oil imports, though Beijing provided no material support to Iran and focused on diplomatic signaling and citizen evacuations. China announced a 7 percent increase in its 2026 defense budget during the Two Sessions opening to advance military modernization amid strategic competition. No direct U.S.-China military incidents, confrontations, exercises, or developments occurred in the Indo-Pacific, Taiwan Strait, or South China Sea domains.

  • CENTCOM: During the reporting period, U.S. and Israeli forces expanded airstrikes on Iranian infrastructure, including a major overnight wave that targeted multiple oil depots and fuel storage facilities in Tehran and surrounding provinces, igniting large fires visible across the city, while additional strikes hit military sites, remaining missile infrastructure, and regime-linked assets with broad-scale deployments involving dozens of aircraft. Israel conducted renewed strikes on Hezbollah targets in Beirut's southern suburbs, southern Lebanon, and Beirut's Raouché area, killing at least 12 people in separate overnight operations and prompting further evacuations amid retaliatory rocket and drone fire from Lebanese territory toward northern Israel including Haifa. Iran launched reduced but continued retaliatory missile and drone barrages toward Israel, triggering air raid sirens in Tel Aviv, Jerusalem, Beersheba, and other areas with interceptions causing limited injuries from shrapnel or debris, while Iranian forces struck or targeted desalination plants and other sensitive infrastructure in Bahrain and attempted attacks on Gulf states including UAE and Qatar. Iran's President Masoud Pezeshkian issued an apology to neighboring Gulf nations for prior strikes and pledged to halt attacks on them unless their territories facilitated further operations against Iran, though fresh blasts occurred in Bahrain, Qatar, and UAE shortly afterward. U.S. Central Command urged Iranian civilians to remain indoors amid accusations that Iranian forces used populated areas for launches, and President Trump reiterated demands for Iran's unconditional surrender while signaling dramatically increased firepower and rejecting negotiations. Cumulative casualties included over 1,200 reported deaths in Iran from strikes, around 11 Israeli civilian fatalities from Iranian projectiles, and nearly 400 deaths in Lebanon from related Israeli operations against Hezbollah; no major new nuclear site strikes or ground operations materialized, as efforts focused on degrading energy, missile, and leadership capabilities amid Iran's indications of nearing a decision on a new supreme leader.

  • AFRICOM: During the reporting period, jihadist militants linked to JNIM executed a deadly attack on a military post in northern Benin near Kofouno village, close to the Niger border, killing 15 soldiers and underscoring persistent escalation along West African border zones involving Benin, Niger, and Nigeria. The African Union Commission Chairperson strongly condemned the terrorist incident and called for enhanced collective mobilization, including acceleration of the ECOWAS regional brigade under the African Standby Force to combat terrorism and violent extremism. In eastern Democratic Republic of Congo, the United States, European Union, and International Contact Group for the Great Lakes expressed profound concern over continued and recent ceasefire violations by parties including M23 rebels, urging immediate recommitment to negotiations and cessation of fighting in line with prior Doha and Washington agreements amid ongoing drone and ground threats to civilians. No confirmed major new kinetic operations, territorial recaptures, or direct engagements materialized in Sudan, Somalia against al-Shabaab, or other active theaters such as the Sahel or Horn of Africa, though indirect geopolitical concerns persisted regarding potential spillover from the U.S.-Israeli conflict with Iran, including risks to Red Sea maritime security, Horn stability, elevated energy prices straining African economies, and possible diversion of Western counterterrorism resources. The East African Community advanced preparations for its ordinary Summit in Arusha on 7 March amid budgetary constraints and leadership transitions, with no associated military developments reported.
