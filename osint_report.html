Date of Access: 14 March 2026

Date of Information: 14 March 2026

Reporting Period: 13 March - 14 March 2026

Distribution: Approved for public release; distribution is unlimited.

Combatant Commands: SOUTHCOM, EUCOM, PACOM, CENTCOM, AFRICOM

Intent: The intent of this report is to provide a high-level overview of significant military and geopolitical developments during the reporting period. The report consists of open-source reporting and updates on evolving conflicts, diplomatic initiatives, and strategic military actions involving countries of interest in the following Combatant Commands: SOUTHCOM, EUCOM, PACOM, CENTCOM, AFRICOM.

Executive Summary: During the reporting period, Guyana rejected Venezuela’s objection to planned offshore seismic exploration and reaffirmed sovereignty over disputed waters, while Ecuador imposed a curfew to support expanded counter–criminal organization operations conducted with U.S. Southern Command logistical and intelligence assistance. Within the EUCOM theater, Russian forces conducted a combined missile and drone strike against Ukrainian targets, while Ukrainian forces carried out retaliatory strikes on radar infrastructure in occupied Crimea and maintained defensive positions along active eastern and southern frontlines. In the PACOM region, the People’s Liberation Army conducted limited air incursions into Taiwan’s Air Defense Identification Zone following a U.S. patrol transit through the Taiwan Strait, while Chinese naval and maritime militia forces maintained routine regional presence without escalatory deployments. In the CENTCOM theater, Israeli and U.S. strikes targeted sites in Tehran as Iran launched additional ballistic missile barrages against Israel and continued disruptions to Strait of Hormuz shipping, prompting further U.S. Marine deployments to the region. In the AFRICOM region, Sudanese Armed Forces drone strikes and Rapid Support Forces shelling caused civilian casualties in Kordofan, while fighting in eastern Democratic Republic of Congo and Algerian counterterrorism operations continued without broader regional escalation.

  • SOUTHCOM: During the reporting period, Guyana's Ministry of Foreign Affairs rejected Venezuela's formal objection to Guyana's planned three-dimensional multi-client seismic exploration program covering approximately 25,000 square kilometers within its Exclusive Economic Zone, reaffirming unequivocal sovereign rights over the disputed maritime areas off the Essequibo region and emphasizing consistency with international law and the 1899 arbitral award on the land boundary. Ecuador's President Daniel Noboa decreed a nighttime curfew in four coastal provinces effective March 15 to support intensified military and security operations against criminal organizations, backed by U.S. Southern Command assistance under the ongoing joint framework targeting narco-terrorist infrastructure without new kinetic engagements reported in the interval. U.S. Southern Command continued logistical and intelligence support for Ecuadorian forces in counternarcotics operations following earlier lethal kinetic actions, amid the broader Americas Counter-Cartel Coalition framework established at recent regional summits. No significant cartel retaliatory violence, border incidents, or interstate military clashes emerged in Mexico, Colombia, or other Central/South American states, though diplomatic monitoring persisted for potential escalations linked to U.S.-led hemispheric counternarcotics efforts and post-Venezuela intervention stabilization dynamics.

  • EUCOM: During the reporting period, Russian forces launched an Iskander-M ballistic missile strike alongside approximately 126 drones—primarily Shahed-type—against targets across Ukraine, with Ukrainian air defenses intercepting 117 drones while the attacks inflicted casualties including at least three fatalities and 27 injuries in Donetsk, Sumy, Dnipropetrovsk, and Zaporizhzhia oblasts from shelling, drone impacts, and missile fragments. Ukrainian forces conducted targeted drone and cruise missile operations against Russian rear-area infrastructure, including strikes on radar installations in occupied Crimea that destroyed or damaged Oborona-14, Nebo-U, and dome-enclosed systems near Yevpatoria and Spokynye, alongside continued pressure on Black Sea oil export facilities that contributed to delayed loadings at Novorossiysk amid ongoing drone threats. Ground engagements persisted without major territorial shifts, as Russian assaults in the Konstantinivsky, Pokrovsk, and other eastern directions met Ukrainian resistance, while Ukrainian units maintained defensive positions and localized counterpressure in southern sectors near Hulyaipole and Oleksandrivka. Russian Ministry of Defense reported conducting group strikes on Ukrainian defense industry, fuel, transport, and UAV production sites in response to prior attacks, though no large-scale armored maneuvers or significant frontline breakthroughs occurred. Diplomatic fallout intensified over the U.S. temporary 30-day waiver on sanctions for stranded Russian oil tankers, with Ukrainian officials criticizing the measure as bolstering Russia's war economy amid elevated global energy prices.

  • PACOM: During the reporting period, the People's Liberation Army Air Force conducted limited incursions into Taiwan's Air Defense Identification Zone (ADIZ) following an extended lull, with Taiwan's Ministry of National Defense detecting five PLA aircraft operating around the island from 6:00 a.m. Wednesday to 6:00 a.m. Thursday, of which three crossed the Taiwan Strait median line and entered northern or southwestern ADIZ sectors in response to a U.S. Navy P-8A patrol aircraft transit through the strait. PLA Navy surface combatants maintained routine presence in the Taiwan Strait without reported increases in platform density, aggressive maneuvers, or major sorties beyond the First Island Chain. Analysts noted ongoing gray-zone maritime militia activity, as thousands of Chinese fishing vessels continued coordinated formations in the East China Sea—consistent with prior massing events in late 2025 and early 2026—potentially testing mobilization capabilities for crisis scenarios involving Taiwan or Japan, though no new large-scale clustering materialized in the immediate interval. In the South China Sea, PLA Southern Theater Command units sustained monitoring of U.S. and allied naval movements without new live-fire drills, major deployments, or escalatory incidents tied to Philippine or Vietnamese EEZ activities. No ballistic missile tests, carrier group operations, or joint exercises with external partners occurred in the Pacific theater during this window.

  • CENTCOM: During the reporting period, Israeli and U.S. forces executed airstrikes on multiple targets in Tehran and surrounding areas, including infrastructure near Ferdowsi Square during Quds Day rallies where thousands gathered in defiance, producing large explosions, smoke plumes, and at least one confirmed civilian fatality from shrapnel while senior officials including security chief Ali Larijani attended the event. Iran's Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps launched successive ballistic missile barrages—designated as additional waves in ongoing Operation True Promise iterations—targeting central and northern Israel, with impacts setting buildings ablaze in central sectors, striking residential areas near Nazareth causing injuries to dozens, and detonating projectiles in the Negev desert, though Israeli air defenses intercepted the majority without widespread casualties. Iranian authorities sustained Strait of Hormuz closure measures, maintaining disruptions to global oil transit and keeping Brent crude prices elevated above prior benchmarks. U.S. Central Command confirmed the deployment of additional Marine units to the Middle East amid escalating operations, while Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth reiterated claims of injuries to Iran's new Supreme Leader Mojtaba Khamenei from prior strikes; no major new ground incursions, nuclear facility escalations, or proxy activations beyond Hezbollah rocket exchanges materialized in the interval.

  • AFRICOM: During the reporting period, Sudanese Armed Forces drone strikes targeted civilian markets and infrastructure in West Kordofan and adjacent areas, killing at least 11 civilians and injuring over 20 near the Adikong market while igniting fuel reserves, as part of intensified aerial campaigns that have claimed more than 200 civilian lives in Kordofan and White Nile regions since early March according to UN reports. Rapid Support Forces artillery and allied shelling continued in South Kordofan, contributing to the mounting civilian toll without reported territorial shifts. In the Democratic Republic of Congo, a drone strike in rebel-held Goma killed a UN aid worker alongside two civilians, with M23 rebels attributing the incident to a combat drone operation amid ongoing FARDC aerial engagements targeting M23 positions near resource-rich zones like Rubaya. South African authorities deployed 2,200 SANDF troops to Johannesburg and surrounding areas to combat organized crime and illegal mining operations, marking a domestic military mobilization without external conflict involvement. Algerian army units eliminated seven militants in counter-terrorism clashes while sustaining three soldier fatalities. No major escalations occurred in the Sahel jihadist theaters of Mali, Burkina Faso, or Niger, nor in Somalia against Al-Shabaab or Ethiopia's Tigray border tensions, though monitoring persisted for spillover risks from broader regional instability.
