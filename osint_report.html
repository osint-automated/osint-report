Date of Information: 15 March 2026

Reporting Period: 14 March - 15 March 2026

Distribution: Approved for public release; distribution is unlimited.

Combatant Commands: SOUTHCOM, EUCOM, PACOM, CENTCOM, AFRICOM

Intent: The intent of this report is to provide a high-level overview of significant military and geopolitical developments during the reporting period. The report consists of open-source reporting and updates on evolving conflicts, diplomatic initiatives, and strategic military actions involving countries of interest in the following Combatant Commands: SOUTHCOM, EUCOM, PACOM, CENTCOM, AFRICOM.

Executive Summary: During the reporting period, the United States reopened its embassy in Caracas, Venezuela, restoring full diplomatic relations after seven years. Cuban protesters attacked a Communist Party office amid escalating blackouts linked to U.S. sanctions. Russian forces launched a major missile and drone strike against Ukrainian energy infrastructure, resulting in civilian casualties and infrastructure damage, while Ukrainian forces advanced significantly in Dnipropetrovsk Oblast. The European Union extended sanctions against Russia until September. In Taiwan, Chinese military activity resumed with numerous aircraft sorties and naval operations observed near the island. The U.S. conducted precision strikes on Iranian military sites and faced retaliation from Iran, which included missile strikes targeting U.S. personnel and infrastructure in the region. Rwanda signaled potential troop withdrawal from Mozambique due to funding issues, while tensions escalated in Sudan and experts warned of a risk of renewed conflict between Ethiopia and Eritrea.

  • SOUTHCOM: During the reporting period, United States officials raised the American flag over the reopened U.S. Embassy in Caracas, Venezuela, on 14 March, reestablishing full diplomatic operations seven years after closure amid prior regime hostilities. Cuban protesters assaulted a Communist Party office in Morón, central Cuba, early on 14 March, hurling rocks through windows, igniting fires with looted furniture, and targeting nearby state facilities over protracted blackouts worsened by U.S. oil embargoes following January's Venezuelan leadership transition; security forces detained five perpetrators, treated one for injuries, and President Miguel Díaz-Canel affirmed zero tolerance for vandalism.

  • EUCOM: During the reporting period, Russian forces launched a massive combined missile and drone assault overnight into 14 March, deploying 68 missiles—including 13 ballistic and Zircon hypersonic variants—alongside over 400 drones, primarily 250 Shahed types, against Ukrainian energy infrastructure such as the Trypillia Thermal Power Plant and a 750 kV substation near Kyiv, as well as military-industrial facilities and airfields across Sumy, Kharkiv, Dnipro, Mykolaiv, and Zaporizhzhia regions; Ukrainian air defenses and electronic warfare neutralized approximately 460 threats, though strikes killed six civilians—including five in Kyiv region and one in Zaporizhzhia—and injured at least 18 others while causing power outages in six oblasts. Ukrainian forces executed retaliatory long-range strikes, igniting the Afipsky oil refinery in Krasnodar Krai for the second time, destroying a fuel train locomotive east of Tokmak, targeting an Iskander missile site in Crimea, and damaging storage facilities plus two vessels—including a Kerch Strait ferry—at Port Kavkaz. Ukrainian ground units advanced decisively in Dnipropetrovsk Oblast, liberating nearly the entire region by pushing Russian positions back over 400 square kilometers since late January, with only three settlements remaining uncleared as of 14 March. Geopolitically, the European Union extended sanctions targeting 2,600 individuals and entities supporting Russia's invasion—encompassing asset freezes, travel bans, and economic restrictions—until 15 September, overcoming veto threats from Hungary and Slovakia, while EU Council President Antonio Costa condemned U.S. easing of Russian oil export sanctions amid surging Middle East conflict prices.

  • PACOM: During the reporting period, People's Liberation Army Air Force (PLAAF) executed 26 aircraft sorties around Taiwan, with 16 penetrating Taiwan's air defense identification zone (ADIZ) across northern, central, and southwestern sectors, marking resumption of large-scale operations after a prior lull; simultaneously, People's Liberation Army Navy (PLAN) deployed 7 vessels operating in Taiwan's vicinity, prompting Republic of China Armed Forces monitoring and response measures up to 0600 UTC+8 on 15 March. In the South China Sea, People's Armed Police Sea Guard (CCG) cutters targeted Philippine resupply vessels within the Philippine exclusive economic zone (EEZ) with radar locks near disputed reefs on 14 March, while multiple Chinese maritime militia vessels maintained presence in contested areas.

  • CENTCOM: During the reporting period, United States forces executed precision strikes against Iranian military installations on Kharg Island on 14 March, targeting air defence facilities, a naval base, the airport control tower, and an offshore oil company helicopter hangar while sparing oil infrastructure, accompanied by warnings of future attacks on energy sites should Iran disrupt Strait of Hormuz shipping. Israel conducted over 200 airstrikes across Iran within the preceding 24 hours, neutralizing missile launchers, defence systems, and weapons production facilities, and confirmed the elimination of two senior Iranian intelligence officials. Iran retaliated with a ballistic missile strike on the United States embassy helipad compound in Baghdad on 14 March, alongside coordinated drone and missile barrages with Hezbollah targeting Israeli territory that wounded seven personnel in Eilat and generated smoke plumes near Tel Aviv; additional Iranian projectiles prompted Saudi Arabia to intercept six drones, Qatar to neutralize one incoming missile, Bahrain to activate air raid sirens, and Oman to report two fatalities from falling drone debris, while intercepted drone remnants damaged an oil facility in Fujairah, United Arab Emirates. United States deployments intensified with 2,500 Marines aboard the USS Tripoli en route to the Middle East and plans for 10,000 interceptor drones, as Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth affirmed over 15,000 total strikes on Iranian targets since conflict initiation.

  • AFRICOM: During the reporting period, Rwanda warned of potential withdrawal of its troops from Mozambique's Cabo Delgado province on 14 March, citing unsustainable funding amid expiring European Union support and recent United States sanctions on its military; Sudanese Armed Forces leader Abdel Fattah al-Burhan rejected coexistence with Rapid Support Forces paramilitaries following a drone strike on a village; the United Nations High Commissioner for Human Rights expressed alarm over escalating deadly drone attacks in Sudan; United States Africa Command executed two airstrikes against militants in Somalia, targeting Islamic State affiliates in the Golis Mountains and al-Shabaab forces 37 miles from Mogadishu; Indian peacekeepers repelled rival faction assaults amid heavy fighting in Akobo, South Sudan; and experts cautioned that renewed Ethiopia-Eritrea hostilities risked igniting continent-wide conflict dubbed Africa's Second World War.
