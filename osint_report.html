Open Source Intelligence (OSINT) Report

Date of Access: 16 November 2025

Date of Information: 16 November 2025

Reporting Period: 15 November - 16 November 2025

Distribution: Approved for public release; distribution is unlimited.

Combatant Commands: SOUTHCOM, EUCOM, PACOM, CENTCOM, AFRICOM

Intent: The intent of this report is to provide a high-level overview of significant military and geopolitical developments during the reporting period. The report consists of open-source reporting and updates on evolving conflicts, diplomatic initiatives, and strategic military actions involving countries of interest in the following Combatant Commands: SOUTHCOM, EUCOM, PACOM, CENTCOM, AFRICOM.

Executive Summary: During the reporting period, the U.S. confirmed the deaths of four individuals in a military strike in the Caribbean as tensions with Venezuela escalated, prompting increased U.S. military presence in Puerto Rico, including the reopening of Naval Station Roosevelt Roads. In Europe, Russia launched a significant drone and missile attack on Ukrainian cities, coinciding with territorial gains and U.S. calls for direct negotiations with Moscow. Meanwhile, Israel's military construction along the Lebanon border heightened tensions with Turkey, as reports emerged of ongoing casualties in Gaza amid a fragile ceasefire. Concerns about resource management in the South China Sea intensified due to overfishing issues, and the UN Security Council prepared to vote on a resolution regarding Gaza, reflecting rising international scrutiny of the conflict dynamics.

  • SOUTHCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that the U.S. confirmed the deaths of four individuals in a strike against a vessel in the Caribbean, marking the 20th such strike amid rising military tensions with Venezuela. The Pentagon ramped up its military presence in Puerto Rico, strategically located north of Venezuela, and reopened Naval Station Roosevelt Roads, one of five operational bases in the territory. President Donald Trump acknowledged that he had "made up his mind" regarding actions in Venezuela, as senior U.S. officials engaged in multiple meetings to deliberate on military options. The administration intensified its rhetoric against Venezuelan President Nicolas Maduro, labeling him as the head of a drug-trafficking network. Reports indicated that U.S. forces were preparing for a potential attack on Venezuela while the regime in Caracas mobilized its forces in response to the increasing military buildup from the United States.

  • EUCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated significant military and geopolitical developments related to the ongoing conflict between Russia and Ukraine. On 24 October, Russia launched a large-scale barrage of drones and missiles targeting Kyiv, Odesa, and Kharkiv, resulting in damage to multiple apartment buildings. This attack coincided with Russia's recent incremental territorial gains in Ukraine. Concurrently, U.S. Vice President J.D. Vance advocated for direct negotiations with Russian President Vladimir Putin as part of Donald Trump's strategy. In Lithuania, Minister of National Defence Robertas Kaunas held discussions with NATO Military Committee Chairman Admiral Giuseppe Cavo Dragone, focusing on regional security and air defense support for Ukraine. The European Union expressed increasing concern over the corruption issues in Ukraine, raising questions about the impact on international aid. Additionally, a Ukrainian drone strike on Russia's Novorossiisk port disrupted oil exports, causing a notable spike in oil prices, reflecting heightened global supply concerns.

  • PACOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated military and geopolitical developments concerning the South China Sea. Fishermen and environmental advocacy groups raised alarms over overfishing and its impact on marine biodiversity, particularly affecting the large yellow croaker (Larimichthys crocea), a vital species in East Asia, which experienced genetic differentiation between wild and domesticated populations. Additionally, digital censorship in China was documented, as the Center for Democracy and Technology (CDT) released its “404 Deleted Content Archive” for October 2025, showcasing various instances of blocked content on platforms. These incidents reflect broader concerns regarding resource management and information control within the region.

  • CENTCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that the fragile ceasefire in Gaza, in effect for five weeks, has prompted both changes and continuities in the region's dynamics. The Israeli military constructed a concrete wall along the border near Yaroun, Lebanon, infringing on Lebanese territory and affecting access to over 4,000 square meters of the area. Tensions escalated between Israel and Turkey, with rising potential for military conflict despite existing mechanisms to prevent escalation. Concurrently, Congresswoman Rashida Tlaib, along with 20 Democratic colleagues, introduced legislation seeking to classify Israel's ongoing military actions in Gaza as genocide, amid reports of continued Palestinian casualties. The UN Security Council prepared to vote on a US-backed resolution concerning Gaza peace, reflecting heightened international attention to the region's instability.

  • AFRICOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated a growing risk of military conflict between Turkey and Israel, driven by a series of geopolitical tensions and territorial disputes. In the sports arena, Jasprit Bumrah's bowling performance contributed significantly to India's dominance over South Africa at Eden Gardens, as he secured five wickets for 27 runs, demonstrating India's prowess in the first Test. Concurrently, the U.S. Department of Agriculture reported projections of a record corn crop for the 2023 season, indicating a strong agricultural performance amidst evolving economic conditions. In the political sphere, President Donald Trump faced scrutiny after the Justice Department corrected replicated signatures on recent pardons. This correction highlighted ongoing controversies surrounding Trump's actions and policies.
