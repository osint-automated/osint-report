Open Source Intelligence (OSINT) Report

Date of Access: 13 March 2026

Date of Information: 13 March 2026

Reporting Period: 12 March - 13 March 2026

Distribution: Approved for public release; distribution is unlimited.

Combatant Commands: SOUTHCOM, EUCOM, PACOM, CENTCOM, AFRICOM

Intent: The intent of this report is to provide a high-level overview of significant military and geopolitical developments during the reporting period. The report consists of open-source reporting and updates on evolving conflicts, diplomatic initiatives, and strategic military actions involving countries of interest in the following Combatant Commands: SOUTHCOM, EUCOM, PACOM, CENTCOM, AFRICOM.

Executive Summary: During the reporting period, U.S. and Ecuadorian forces executed coordinated kinetic strikes against narco-terrorist infrastructure near the Ecuador-Colombia border while regional diplomatic tensions persisted but no major interstate conflict emerged in the SOUTHCOM theater. In EUCOM, Ukrainian forces conducted precision drone strikes against Russian fuel infrastructure in Krasnodar Krai as Russia continued large-scale drone attacks on Ukrainian targets and sustained heavy materiel and personnel losses amid ongoing high-intensity engagements without major territorial shifts. In PACOM, the PLA resumed limited air incursions into Taiwan's ADIZ and maintained routine naval patrols and maritime militia activity near Taiwan, though no major missile launches, carrier deployments, or escalatory military actions occurred across the broader Indo-Pacific. In CENTCOM, Israeli airstrikes targeted sites in Tehran while Iran launched multiple ballistic missile barrages under Operation True Promise 4 against Israeli and U.S.-linked targets and maintained closure of the Strait of Hormuz, intensifying regional military confrontation and energy market disruption. In AFRICOM, localized drone strikes, counterterrorism operations, and civil war violence continued in the Democratic Republic of the Congo and Sudan while other regions experienced limited militant activity and no significant interstate escalation.

  • SOUTHCOM: During the reporting period, the US, in coordination with Ecuadorian military units, executed targeted lethal kinetic operations against narco-terrorist infrastructure in northeastern Ecuador near the Colombian border, employing helicopters, aircraft, riverine assets, and drones to destroy a drug traffickers' training camp linked to the Comandos de la Frontera dissident group without reported casualties or captures. Guyana's Ministry of Foreign Affairs categorically rejected a Venezuelan communiqué objecting to Guyana's three-dimensional multi-client seismic exploration program in its Exclusive Economic Zone, reaffirming sovereignty over the disputed maritime space amid ongoing Essequibo territorial claims and preparations for International Court of Justice oral hearings in May 2026. No significant interstate military engagements, border clashes, or large-scale cartel retaliatory violence materialized in Mexico, Colombia, or other Central/South American theaters, though diplomatic tensions persisted between Ecuador and Colombia over trade tariffs and cross-border cocaine flows, and U.S.-Cuba talks advanced under economic pressure from an oil blockade while regional monitoring continued for potential escalations tied to broader hemispheric counternarcotics efforts.

  • EUCOM: During the reporting period, Ukrainian forces conducted a precision drone strike on the Tikhoretsk oil pumping station and associated fuel storage infrastructure in Russia's Krasnodar Krai, igniting multiple large fires at fuel tanks and disrupting southern Russian petroleum logistics critical for sustaining frontline operations. Russian forces launched approximately 94 Shahed-type and decoy drones targeting Ukrainian port infrastructure in the Odesa region, damaging a food warehouse facility while Ukrainian air defenses intercepted the majority without reported casualties. Ukrainian General Staff reported 122-154 combat engagements across the theater, with heightened Russian activity in the Gulaypole and Kostyantynivka directions but no confirmed territorial gains, while Ukrainian units advanced marginally in select sectors including near Kupyansk and western Zaporizhia Oblast. Russian forces sustained approximately 860 personnel losses along with destruction of 50 artillery systems, seven tanks, and over 2,000 unmanned aerial vehicles according to Ukrainian estimates. No major ballistic missile launches or large-scale armored assaults materialized, though ongoing drone and FPV systems continued to dominate tactical engagements and inflict significant attrition on both sides.

  • PACOM: During the reporting period, the People's Liberation Army Air Force resumed incursions into Taiwan's Air Defense Identification Zone (ADIZ) after a multi-day pause, with Taiwan's Ministry of National Defense detecting at least five PLA aircraft operating around the island, of which three crossed the Taiwan Strait median line and entered Taiwan's northern, central, or southwestern ADIZ sectors. PLA Navy surface vessels maintained routine patrols in the Taiwan Strait and adjacent Western Pacific waters without reported escalations in platform count or aggressive maneuvers. Reports indicated a massing of thousands of Chinese fishing vessels near Taiwan in the East China Sea, consistent with gray-zone maritime militia operations to assert presence and potentially complicate freedom of navigation. In the South China Sea, no major new PLA Navy deployments or live-fire activities occurred, though ongoing monitoring of U.S. and allied naval movements persisted amid heightened regional tensions linked to broader Indo-Pacific dynamics. No significant ballistic missile launches, carrier strike group sorties beyond the First Island Chain, or joint exercises with Russia materialized in the Pacific theater during this interval.

  • CENTCOM: During the reporting period, Israeli forces conducted extensive airstrikes on Tehran and other Iranian targets, including during al-Quds Day rallies, where explosions killed at least one civilian from shrapnel near march participants and caused widespread smoke coverage across the capital. Iran's Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps launched multiple missile barrages—designated as waves in Operation True Promise 4—targeting Israeli military and communications hubs in Tel Aviv and Haifa, as well as U.S. positions in Erbil and facilities associated with the U.S. Navy’s Fifth Fleet in Bahrain, employing advanced ballistic missiles such as Kheibar Shekan, Qadr, and Khorramshahr variants. Iranian authorities maintained closure of the Strait of Hormuz, disrupting global shipping and sustaining Brent crude oil prices above $100 per barrel. U.S. officials confirmed the loss of six airmen in a KC-135 refueling aircraft crash in western Iraq, while Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth claimed Iran's new Supreme Leader Mojtaba Khamenei sustained injuries. Iranian missile strikes impacted a residential area in northern Israel, and reports indicated ongoing power outages and targeted operations against IRGC and security institutions in Tehran.

  • AFRICOM: During the reporting period, Congolese Armed Forces (FARDC) elements and allied groups conducted a drone strike in rebel-held Goma, eastern Democratic Republic of the Congo, killing a French UNICEF aid worker, one other civilian, and injuring additional personnel near UN facilities, while M23 rebels attributed the attack to government forces targeting civilian infrastructure without confirmed territorial shifts. In Sudan, paramilitary Rapid Support Forces (RSF) launched a drone strike on a secondary school and healthcare center in White Nile State, killing at least 17 people including students amid ongoing civil war attrition, though no major ground offensives or territorial changes occurred. Algerian army units executed a counter-terrorism operation eliminating four militants in a border-adjacent engagement without reported further escalation. Ethiopian authorities managed humanitarian responses to recent landslides and floods that killed over 60 people in southern regions, with no new military developments in Tigray or Amhara despite persistent tensions. No significant jihadist attacks materialized in the Sahel belt involving Mali, Burkina Faso, or Niger forces, and no interstate border clashes or major geopolitical shifts involving external actors emerged across the continent in the interval.
