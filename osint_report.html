Additionally, the United States appointed a new envoy focused on Tibetan human rights, drawing criticism from China, which accused the U.S. of interfering in its internal affairs.

  • EUCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that Russia intensified its hybrid warfare efforts, particularly against Poland and NATO, utilizing tactics such as rail sabotage, drone assaults, and cyber strikes. Polish officials highlighted concerns about "disposable agents" creating fear and undermining support for Ukraine. In Geneva, Switzerland, US-mediated peace talks between Ukraine and Russia resumed but concluded on 18 February with no meaningful progress after just two hours. Both sides described the negotiations as challenging, with Ukraine accusing Russia of delaying tactics. Amidst these developments, Ukrainian forces reported significant territorial gains, reclaiming over 200 square kilometers from Russian control. Additionally, a controversial decision allowed Russian and Belarusian athletes to compete under their flags at the 2026 Paralympics, which provoked strong reactions from Ukrainian officials.

The Philippines and the United States reaffirmed their alliance, emphasizing a commitment to defend freedom of navigation in light of escalating tensions in the South China Sea. In addition, the U.S. expanded missile deployments in northern Philippines, positioning missile systems within 1,000+ miles of Chinese military assets. Russia, China, and Iran conducted joint naval exercises in the Gulf of Oman, showcasing their military cooperation amid ongoing geopolitical tensions. Meanwhile, U.S. military strikes against alleged drug boats in Latin America killed 11 individuals, raising the total death toll to at least 145 since September.

International pressure had little effect on Israel's policies, with over 80 UN member states condemning Israel's annexation of West Bank land and militants in Gaza preparing for a potential escalation amid a fragile ceasefire. In the political realm, Australia barred a citizen affiliated with ISIS from returning from Syria, while Turkey's parliament discussed advancing peace processes regarding the PKK. Iranian and US officials indicated progress in nuclear negotiations despite ongoing tensions, and multiple artistic figures criticized the Berlin Film Festival for remaining silent on the Israeli military campaign in Gaza. Additionally, American artist U2 released a politically charged EP addressing various global issues, including conflicts in Gaza and Ukraine.

  • AFRICOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that tensions escalated between Ethiopia and Eritrea, raising concerns about a potential renewed conflict that could pit the Ethiopian government against Tigray's leadership. In South Africa, authorities faced backlash from residents in Johannesburg following comments made by a senior politician amid an ongoing water crisis in the city's struggling infrastructure. Meanwhile, Gabonese authorities enforced an indefinite suspension of Facebook and TikTok, citing risks to social cohesion. The UN refugee agency launched a $1.6 billion appeal to support millions affected by the ongoing war in Sudan, which continued to drive regional displacement amid a humanitarian crisis. Additionally, Burkina Faso's foreign minister reported that Russian instructors had aided the country in reclaiming 74% of territory from militant groups.
