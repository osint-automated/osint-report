Intent: The intent of this report is to provide a high-level overview of significant military and geopolitical developments during the reporting period. The report consists of open-source reporting and updates on evolving conflicts, diplomatic initiatives, and strategic military actions involving countries of interest in the following Combatant Commands: SOUTHCOM, EUCOM, PACOM, CENTCOM, AFRICOM.

Executive Summary: During the reporting period, significant military and geopolitical developments emerged across multiple regions. In Latin America, Colombian President Gustavo Petro expressed fears of U.S. military intervention, while the U.S. seized five oil tankers from Venezuela, which subsequently released opposition figures likely at U.S. urging. In Europe, Russia launched a large-scale attack using hypersonic missiles against Ukraine, resulting in casualties and renewed calls for Western support. In East Asia, Japan's Prime Minister recognized the need for military readiness regarding Taiwan amidst escalating tensions with China, while Japan negotiated arms deals and addressed its strategic relationship with the region. Iran faced intensified protests and government crackdown strategies. Concurrently, geopolitical tensions grew as Iranian and Chinese naval forces conducted joint military drills in South Africa, and humanitarian crises deepened in Sudan and Syria.

  • SOUTHCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated significant military and geopolitical developments involving the United States and Latin America. Colombian President Gustavo Petro stated that the U.S. posed a "real threat" of military action against Colombia, reflecting Trump's historical threats of intervention. The U.S. continued its campaign in Venezuela, successfully seizing five oil tankers as part of efforts to exert pressure on the Maduro regime. Amidst these tensions, Venezuela released several high-profile opposition figures, reportedly at the request of the U.S. In a broader context, Trump's foreign policy prompted discussions in Europe about geopolitical alignments, as evidenced by Italian Prime Minister Giorgia Meloni's assertion against a U.S. military move on Greenland and a call for NATO to maintain a strong Arctic presence. Concurrently, experts warned that the U.S. and Russia's ideologies might align over Greenland's future, potentially destabilizing NATO.

  • EUCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that Russia utilized its Oreshnik hypersonic missile in a large-scale attack on Ukraine, marking the second deployment of this weapon since it was first used in November 2024. The missile targeted significant infrastructure in Ukraine's western Lviv region, situated near the border with Poland, and was reported to have caused at least four fatalities in Kyiv. Ukrainian authorities characterized the missile strike as a "grave threat" to European security, prompting calls for increased pressure on Moscow from Ukraine's Foreign Minister Andrii Sybiha. Concurrently, Ukrainian military sources highlighted concerns over potential Russian strikes aimed at disrupting Starlink satellite communications, an essential tool for their operational communications since the onset of the conflict in 2022. Meanwhile, British and French defense officials reaffirmed their commitment to provide security guarantees to Ukraine, reinforcing transatlantic support during this escalated military engagement.

  • PACOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that the world’s oceans absorbed unprecedented levels of heat in 2025, reaching a record not seen in the last eight years. In geopolitical developments, the Trump administration's focus on acquiring Greenland continued, despite uncertainty about its commercial viability for resources. Japan faced strategic challenges, particularly in light of its relationship with China and an outdated postwar framework, which Prime Minister Sanae Takaichi acknowledged while suggesting military readiness regarding Taiwan. In Latin America, Argentine President Javier Milei affirmed his intention to maintain trade relations with China, despite geopolitical tensions. Meanwhile, tensions escalated between Japan and China after Takaichi's comments regarding military action over Taiwan, and Taiwan's Air Force launched a search for a missing F-16 pilot after a routine training exercise. In response to the instability within Venezuela, including a surge of crude oil held at sea due to U.S. intervention, Pakistan negotiated a $1.5 billion arms deal with Sudan to bolster its military against paramilitary threats. Finally, Iran's internal unrest led to concerns over potential disruptions in oil supply, influencing global market dynamics.

  • CENTCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that Iranian Supreme Leader Ali Khamenei planned to address the ongoing protests in Iran, which included instances of violent demonstrations and government attempts to suppress dissent through an internet blackout. As protests intensified, protesters across Iran chanted anti-government slogans and set buildings on fire in Tehran, demonstrating widespread discontent with the economic situation. In Aleppo, Syria, heavy fighting persisted after negotiations concerning the integration of the Syrian Democratic Forces into the national army collapsed, resulting in casualties and renewed tensions between Kurdish groups and government forces. Meanwhile, Israeli military operations in Gaza resulted in substantial Palestinian casualties, with airstrikes killing at least 14 individuals, including children, after retaliatory actions followed a failed rocket launch. On the geopolitical front, Israel's military targeted Hezbollah installations in Lebanon amidst rising fears of regional destabilization and Turkish scrutiny over Israeli activities in the Eastern Mediterranean. Yemen's Southern Transitional Council announced its dissolution after its leadership fled to the United Arab Emirates, signaling ongoing instability in the region.

  • AFRICOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated heightened military and geopolitical activity in the African region. Chinese and Iranian warships arrived at Simon's Town, South Africa, for joint naval drills scheduled from 9 to 16 January, coinciding with rising tensions following U.S. military actions in Venezuela. Concurrently, Algeria and Nigeria prepared for a critical quarter-final match at the Africa Cup of Nations (AFCON) 2025 amidst reports of internal disputes within the Nigerian squad regarding unpaid bonuses. Furthermore, twenty-two Islamic countries and the Organization of Islamic Cooperation condemned Israel's foreign minister Gideon Sa’ar for his visit to the Somaliland region of Somalia, labeling it illegal and a violation of Somali sovereignty. Yemen's Southern Transitional Council announced its dissolution following unrest, showcasing the fragility of political structures in the region. Meanwhile, the UN reported escalating hunger crises and civilian casualties in Sudan and Syria, highlighting ongoing humanitarian concerns amidst regional conflicts.
