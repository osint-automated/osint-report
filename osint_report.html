Date of Information: 19 November 2025

Reporting Period: 18 November - 19 November 2025

Distribution: Approved for public release; distribution is unlimited.

Combatant Commands: SOUTHCOM, EUCOM, PACOM, CENTCOM, AFRICOM

Intent: The intent of this report is to provide a high-level overview of significant military and geopolitical developments during the reporting period. The report consists of open-source reporting and updates on evolving conflicts, diplomatic initiatives, and strategic military actions involving countries of interest in the following Combatant Commands: SOUTHCOM, EUCOM, PACOM, CENTCOM, AFRICOM.

Executive Summary: During the reporting period, significant military and geopolitical tensions emerged globally. In South America, U.S. President Donald Trump indicated potential troop deployment to Venezuela amid regional military buildups and plans to designate the Cartel de los Soles as a terrorist organization, while Ecuadorians rejected U.S. military bases. In Europe, Russian advancements in the Zaporizhia region heightened concerns, with warnings from the EU defense chief about preparedness for Russian drone attacks, while Ukraine sought to strengthen defense through talks with Turkey and potential Rafale jet acquisitions from France. In the Pacific, Philippine forces successfully delivered supplies under duress from Chinese ships, raising alarms over military tensions related to Taiwan and Japan's response. In the Middle East, worsening humanitarian conditions in Gaza prompted a UN resolution for an international stabilization force rejected by Hamas and met with resistance from Israel, while diplomatic engagement continued between the U.S. and Saudi Arabia amid escalating tensions involving Hezbollah in Lebanon. In Africa, a massacre linked to Mali's military raised international concerns as humanitarian incidents marked the region.

  • SOUTHCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that tensions escalated between the United States and Venezuela. President Donald Trump announced he would not rule out the deployment of U.S. troops to Venezuela amidst a military buildup in the region, including the arrival of the USS Gerald Ford aircraft carrier in the Caribbean. Concurrently, the Trump administration planned to designate the Cartel de los Soles, linked to the Venezuelan government, as a foreign terrorist organization in a bid to intensify pressure on President Nicolás Maduro's regime. Trump also expressed a willingness to engage in talks with Maduro, despite the looming threat of military action. In a separate incident, U.S. forces carried out an airstrike in the eastern Pacific that killed three individuals as the military presence in the area continued to grow. In related news, Ecuadorian voters rejected a proposal to allow the return of U.S. military bases, indicating a regional reluctance toward U.S. intervention.

  • EUCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated a significant escalation in military and geopolitical tensions across Europe and Ukraine. Russian forces reportedly gained ground in the Zaporizhia region, using glide bombs and concealed troop movements to advance on the strategic town of Huliaipole. In Poland, Prime Minister Donald Tusk accused two Ukrainian citizens, who had entered from Belarus and had purportedly worked for Russia, of involvement in a recent railway sabotage that damaged a line crucial for transporting goods to Ukraine. Concurrently, the EU defense chief warned that Europe was ill-prepared for a potential Russian drone attack, emphasizing the need to integrate Ukraine's operational capabilities for defense. In the broader context of the conflict, Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelensky sought to reinvigorate peace talks with Turkey, while simultaneously pursuing a major air defense deal with France, aiming to acquire up to 100 Rafale fighter jets in the coming decade.

  • PACOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that Philippine forces successfully delivered supplies, including food, fuel, and personnel, to a contested outpost in the South China Sea, despite interference from Chinese government ships that jammed communications during the mission. Concerns arose about potential escalated military activity near Japan following remarks by Japanese Prime Minister Sanae Takaichi regarding Taiwan, which strained diplomatic relations. The U.S. Chief of Naval Operations described China's naval expansion as "impressive" and emphasized the importance of collaboration with allies like South Korea and Japan to counter this growth. Additionally, Japan issued safety warnings to its citizens in China due to rising tensions linked to these remarks. Meanwhile, the U.S. Treasury implemented sanctions targeting Russian oil revenues, demonstrating continued international efforts to address geopolitical challenges involving Russia and China.

  • CENTCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that urgent humanitarian conditions worsened for displaced Palestinians in Gaza as winter approached, prompting calls from aid agencies for increased shelter provisions. In geopolitical developments, the United Nations Security Council approved a US-drafted resolution supporting the formation of an international stabilization force in Gaza, which garnered mixed reactions. Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu reaffirmed his opposition to Palestinian statehood prior to the vote. Hamas rejected the resolution, stating it did not meet the political and humanitarian demands of the Palestinian people. Meanwhile, President Donald Trump welcomed Saudi Crown Prince Mohammed bin Salman to the White House for discussions on security and trade, coinciding with Trump's announcement of plans to sell F-35 jets to Saudi Arabia. Israel reported killing a Hezbollah operative in southern Lebanon amidst ongoing tensions, and Jordan's parliament reinstated military conscription for men, marking a significant policy shift. Concurrently, Israeli forces abducted four family members in Syria's Quneitra province, and reports highlighted worsening conditions in Tehran due to a severe water crisis, affecting the capital's residents.

  • AFRICOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that prosecutors sought life imprisonment for Ali Muhammad Ali Abd-Al-Rahman, convicted of atrocities in Darfur during the 2000s, who claimed victimhood due to mistaken identity. In a significant humanitarian incident, a late-night attack on a Catholic health facility in northeastern Democratic Republic of Congo resulted in approximately 20 civilian fatalities. Relations between China and Japan deteriorated as Beijing threatened increased military activity near Japan following heightened tensions linked to comments made by Japanese Prime Minister Sanae Takaichi. The UN Security Council extended the mandate of the United Nations Interim Security Force for Abyei, reinforcing its commitment to peacekeeping in that region. Furthermore, Mali’s military actions raised international concern as at least 31 civilians were killed in a massacre attributed to the military and allied militias in the central region.
