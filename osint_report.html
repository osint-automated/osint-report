Intent: The intent of this report is to provide a high-level overview of significant military and geopolitical developments during the reporting period. The report consists of open-source reporting and updates on evolving conflicts, diplomatic initiatives, and strategic military actions involving countries of interest in the following Combatant Commands: SOUTHCOM, EUCOM, PACOM, CENTCOM, AFRICOM.

Executive Summary: During the reporting period, Ecuador implemented a 100 percent tariff on Colombian imports amidst escalating tensions, recalled its ambassador from Bogotá, and engaged in naval exercises with the US. Colombian forces intensified operations against the FARC dissidents. Venezuela's riot police dispersed protests demanding better wages and pensions, while lawmakers confirmed a new attorney general and passed a mining law to attract foreign investment. Cuba's President Díaz-Canel resisted US threats, tightened oil blockade measures, and received a Russian oil shipment. Argentina expelled an Iranian diplomat over terrorist organization designations, while Costa Rica designated multiple groups as terrorist entities and linked an assassination plot to Nicaragua. Brazil and the US collaborated against organized crime, and the US sought diplomatic engagement with Peru to counter Chinese influence. In Ukraine, Russian strikes targeted fuel, energy, and civilian infrastructure, resulting in casualties. A ceasefire for Orthodox Easter was ordered but frontline clashes persisted. China’s President Xi Jinping hosted Taiwan opposition leaders, reaffirming military options while monitoring heightened naval activity in nearby waters. Israel conducted airstrikes on Hezbollah targets, complicating US-Iran ceasefire talks. Turkey deployed military instructors to Niger to enhance counterinsurgency efforts, while Nigeria faced separatist violence and Boko Haram assaults. Djibouti expanded operations into Somalia amid territorial disputes, and Ethiopia extended the mandate of the Tigray Interim Administration to prevent renewed conflict.

  • SOUTHCOM: During the reporting period, Ecuador heightened tariffs on Colombian imports to 100 percent amid escalating bilateral tensions, recalled its ambassador from Bogotá over remarks concerning former Vice President Jorge Glas, and participated in bilateral naval training exercises with the US Nimitz Carrier Strike Group. Colombian forces intensified military operations against the FARC General Staff dissident faction. Venezuelan riot police dispersed protesters demanding wage increases and pensions, while lawmakers confirmed Devoe as attorney general and approved a mining law to attract foreign investment. Cuban President Miguel Díaz-Canel resisted escalated US threats and tightened oil blockade measures, urged unconditional dialogue with Washington, received a second Russian oil shipment, and hosted US FBI agents investigating a reported armed infiltration attempt via speedboat. Argentine authorities expelled an Iranian diplomat following a dispute over designating the Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps as a terrorist organization. Costa Rican officials declared Hezbollah, Hamas, the Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps, and Houthi militants as terrorist entities, and linked the assassination of military officer Roberto Samcam to a plot originating in Nicaragua. Brazil and the United States initiated joint operations against organized crime, while the United States advanced diplomatic overtures to Peru to counter Chinese influence ahead of its presidential election. Panama's president pursued de-escalation with China. Demonstrations surged in Cuba and Venezuela.

  • EUCOM: During the reporting period, Russian forces struck Ukrainian fuel and energy infrastructure, enemy deployment sites, and civilian targets in Sumy region, inflicting at least four fatalities and 38 injuries across multiple oblasts. Ukrainian air defenses intercepted Shahed drones launched by Russia, while Ukrainian drones targeted an oil pumping station in Krasnodar Krai. Russia transferred approximately 1000 bodies of deceased soldiers to Ukraine on 9 April as part of a repatriation exchange. President Putin ordered a ceasefire for Orthodox Easter on 9 April, which President Zelenskyy accepted following prior advocacy, though both sides reported ongoing frontline clashes near Pokrovsk where Russian units advanced using troops drawn from strategic reserves. Zelenskyy disclosed that unnamed international partners requested Ukraine cease attacks on Russian oil refineries amid stalled trilateral talks and fading U.S. security guarantees.

  • PACOM: During the reporting period, People's Republic of China President Xi Jinping hosted Taiwan opposition leader Cheng Li-wun in Beijing on 10 April, reiterated the one-China principle and 1992 Consensus, invoked Taiwan independence as a threat, and affirmed military force as an option for reunification while expressing openness to cross-strait dialogue. People's Liberation Army deployed nearly 100 naval and coast guard vessels across South China Sea and East China Sea, prompting Taiwan security officials to report heightened warship and warplane activity around the island. PLA Eastern Theater Command maintained persistent surveillance operations near Taiwan Strait approaches. PLA Southern Theater Command spokesperson condemned Philippines resupply missions at Second Thomas Shoal on 9 April, accusing Manila of provocation in Spratly Islands chain. No live-fire exercises, aerial incursions exceeding air defense identification zone norms, or amphibious maneuvers occurred.

  • CENTCOM: During the reporting period, Israel executed multiple waves of airstrikes targeting Hezbollah infrastructure in Lebanon, neutralising approximately ten missile launchers and inflicting over 250 fatalities while injuring hundreds more on 9 April, thereby jeopardising the nascent US-Iran ceasefire agreement. Prime Minister Netanyahu authorised direct negotiations with Lebanon on 9 April to address the escalating border tensions. Vice President Vance departed for Islamabad on 10 April to conduct peace talks with Iranian representatives amid disputes over ceasefire scope. President Trump directed Netanyahu to moderate strikes in Lebanon and issued warnings to Iran demanding reopening of the Strait of Hormuz, where Tehran sustained blockades disrupting commercial shipping. Iranian negotiators accused the US and Israel of violating ceasefire stipulations, particularly concerning Hezbollah protections, while US officials insisted the truce excluded Lebanese operations. No direct US-Iran or Israel-Iran kinetic exchanges occurred, though proxy confrontations intensified regional instability.

  • AFRICOM: During the reporting period, Turkish defense officials deployed military instructors to Niger following a bilateral training agreement signed on 8 April, enhancing counterinsurgency capabilities against Sahel jihadists. Nigerian forces executed heavy troop deployments and patrols with armored vehicles across Abia State in Southeast Nigeria on 9 April, triggering gunfire exchanges, property destruction, and disputed casualties during operations targeting separatist elements. Boko Haram militants assaulted a military base in Borno State, Nigeria, on 9 April, neutralizing one army general and multiple soldiers while seizing materiel. Djibouti army units crossed into Saylac, Awdal region, Somalia, on 9 April, demolishing local farms amid territorial disputes with Gadabuursi clans. Unconfirmed field intelligence reported jihadist forces ambushing a Nigerien military convoy in western Niger, striking during a Niger River transit in Tillabéri, and seizing a position in eastern Burkina Faso on 9 April. Ethiopian federal authorities extended the Tigray Interim Administration's mandate by one year on 8 April, preempting Tigray People's Liberation Front mobilization and averting renewed hostilities.
