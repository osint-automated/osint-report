Executive Summary: During the reporting period, significant military and geopolitical developments occurred across multiple regions. In Mexico, cartel violence resulted in at least 70 deaths, prompting the U.S. State Department to update its travel advisory for Americans. In Ukraine, indigenous Flamingo missiles struck an Iskander factory in Russia, and Russia returned 1,000 Ukrainian soldiers' bodies amid ongoing negotiations with U.S. peace envoys. In the South China Sea, the China Coast Guard expelled Philippine vessels, while the Philippines increased military cooperation with the U.S. In the Middle East, U.S. and Iranian officials engaged in nuclear negotiations, coinciding with heightened military posture from the U.S. and Israeli strikes in Lebanon. Meanwhile, in Africa, Islamic militants intensified attacks in Nigeria, and South Africa boosted army funding as regional instability persisted, particularly in South Sudan and the Central African Republic.

  • SOUTHCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated significant cartel violence in Mexico, which resulted in at least 70 deaths. FIFA President Gianni Infantino voiced his full confidence in Mexico as a co-host for the upcoming World Cup, despite the violence that erupted following the death of notorious drug kingpin Nemesio Oseguera Cervantes, known as El Mencho. In response to the escalating violence, the U.S. State Department updated its guidelines for Americans traveling in Mexico, transitioning from a "shelter in place" order to a milder travel advisory. In related developments, Canadian authorities emphasized the responsibility of air carriers to promptly return stranded passengers impacted by the violence. The turmoil prompted safety concerns for travelers, especially with spring break approaching, leading some to reconsider trips to popular Mexican destinations. Meanwhile, mass graves containing human remains were discovered near a soccer stadium in Guadalajara, further highlighting the crisis.

  • EUCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that Ukraine employed its indigenous Flamingo missiles to strike an Iskander factory in Russia, achieving a distance of nearly 900 miles from the launch site. This attack formed part of Ukraine's significant long-range assault into Russian territory. Concurrent to these developments, Russia announced the transfer of 1,000 bodies of Ukrainian soldiers back to Ukraine amid ongoing negotiations between Ukrainian representatives and U.S. peace envoys in Geneva. Russian forces conducted a substantial overnight attack during this period, targeting military-industrial facilities, energy infrastructures, and military airfields across Ukraine. Additionally, law enforcement in Kenya arrested individuals accused of trafficking citizens to fight for Russia in Ukraine, with reports suggesting that over 1,000 Kenyans had been recruited for this purpose. Tensions escalated as Russia claimed that the United Kingdom and France were attempting to smuggle nuclear weapons into Ukraine, a move they deemed a violation of non-proliferation treaties that could lead to severe repercussions. This marked day 1,463 of the ongoing conflict between Russia and Ukraine, a war that has now lasted four years.
