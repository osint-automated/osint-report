Open Source Intelligence (OSINT) Report

Date of Access: 01 December 2025

Date of Information: 01 December 2025

Reporting Period: 30 November - 01 December 2025

Distribution: Approved for public release; distribution is unlimited.

Combatant Commands: SOUTHCOM, EUCOM, PACOM, CENTCOM, AFRICOM

Intent: The intent of this report is to provide a high-level overview of significant military and geopolitical developments during the reporting period. The report consists of open-source reporting and updates on evolving conflicts, diplomatic initiatives, and strategic military actions involving countries of interest in the following Combatant Commands: SOUTHCOM, EUCOM, PACOM, CENTCOM, AFRICOM.

Executive Summary: During the reporting period, significant military and geopolitical developments emerged across various regions. In Venezuela, President Trump closed airspace over the country, prompting a U.S. military buildup that involved increased aircraft patrols and simulated airstrikes, which Venezuela condemned, with discussions of President Maduro potentially fleeing to Brazil. In Ukraine, U.S. officials met with Ukrainian negotiators to pursue peace amid ongoing conflict, while Russian forces intensified hostilities with a strike on Kyiv, leading to Ukrainian drone attacks on Russian oil tankers. In the South China Sea, China conducted combat-readiness patrols, exacerbating regional tensions following the withdrawal of a U.S. missile battery in Japan. In Syria, Israeli strikes resulted in multiple casualties, while Iran seized a vessel carrying smuggled fuel, further escalating tensions in the Middle East. In Africa, Nigeria faced a cholera outbreak and increasing kidnappings, while violence in Sudan continued to rise, complicating the geopolitical landscape as international leaders convened to address these issues.

  • SOUTHCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated significant military and geopolitical developments involving Venezuela. President Donald Trump declared the airspace over and around Venezuela closed on 24 October, escalating tensions between the United States and the South American nation. Concurrently, the U.S. military reportedly increased its presence, with aircraft patrolling international airspace near Venezuela and conducting simulated airstrikes. This military buildup followed a campaign against suspected drug trafficking vessels. Venezuelan authorities, led by President Nicolás Maduro, strongly condemned the U.S. actions, with Vice President Delcy Rodriguez announcing new travel measures for citizens due to the threats. Additionally, sources suggested Maduro may consider fleeing to Brazil in anticipation of further military actions from the U.S. Both the House and Senate commenced inquiries into a reported strike against an alleged drug boat in the Caribbean, reflecting a heightened legislative focus on military operations in the region.

  • EUCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that officials from the Trump administration met with Ukrainian negotiators in Florida, aiming to facilitate a resolution to the ongoing conflict in Ukraine. Turkish Foreign Minister Hakan Fidan announced that both Russia and Ukraine displayed a willingness to negotiate peace. However, hostilities intensified as Russian forces executed a deadly strike on Kyiv, resulting in casualties and structural damage. In response, Ukraine launched drone strikes on two Russian oil tankers in the Black Sea, prompting condemnation from Turkey regarding the attacks. The geopolitical landscape further evolved as President Zelensky of Ukraine ramped up diplomatic efforts amid Russian aggression and internal corruption scandals while also appointing a new head for his delegation for negotiations with the U.S. and its allies. As these developments unfolded, discussions about a potential peace plan emerged, although skepticism regarding its viability remained prevalent among Ukrainian officials and constituents.

  • PACOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated significant military and geopolitical developments. The Malaysian Meteorological Department issued a tropical depression advisory that warned of strong winds and rough seas in the South China Sea on 29 November. Concurrently, the Chinese People's Liberation Army (PLA) Southern Theater Command conducted combat-readiness patrols in the territorial waters and airspace of Huangyan Dao, also on 29 November. On the same day, the Chinese PLA Navy's 989 Task Group arrived at Port Klang, Malaysia, for a four-day friendly visit. In Japan, authorities informed the Iwakuni city government of the Pentagon’s withdrawal of the Typhon missile battery from the U.S. Marine Corps air station, raising concerns about heightened risks of conflict with China. Additionally, a devastating fire in Hong Kong resulted in at least 146 fatalities, with further investigations underway. These developments underscore increasing tensions in the region, particularly in the context of maritime security and international military dynamics.

  • CENTCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that Israeli forces killed 13 people, including two children, in Beit Jinn, Syria, following local resistance to their incursion on 24 November. In a separate operation, the Israel Defense Forces (IDF) successfully killed a Hamas battalion commander and other militants who emerged from Rafah tunnels during nighttime strikes. Iran seized an Eswatini-flagged vessel carrying 350,000 liters of smuggled fuel off Bushehr, detaining its 13 crew members amid efforts to combat fuel smuggling. The United Arab Emirates condemned Israeli strikes on the suburbs of Damascus, calling for international action to halt the attacks. Tensions escalated further as Iranian and several other Gulf nations denounced Israel's military actions in southern Syria. Concurrently, the humanitarian crisis continues in Gaza, with the death toll surpassing 70,000 reported by the Gaza Health Ministry, amidst accusations from Hamas regarding Israel's ongoing military operations. The U.S. military also conducted strikes in Somalia, further illustrating the region's volatility.

  • AFRICOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated significant developments in military and geopolitical landscapes. In Nigeria, the government made four emergency requests for cholera vaccines as the country faced a rising death toll from an outbreak. A resurgence in kidnappings marked the region, with the release of 12 young girls and the final group of schoolgirls abducted in Borno State. Meanwhile, in Sudan, fighting escalated as the army and Rapid Support Forces sought control over additional territories despite international calls for a truce. Reports highlighted the influence of European migration policies and arms supplies in empowering Sudanese warlords, further complicating the conflict. The U.S. continued its military operations in Somalia, targeting alleged militants with airstrikes that raised concerns over civilian casualties. Lastly, in a broader geopolitical context, European leaders convened in Angola to strengthen ties amid increasing global tensions, highlighting Africa's strategic importance in current international affairs.
