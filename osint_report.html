Open Source Intelligence (OSINT) Report

Date of Access: 08 December 2025

Date of Information: 08 December 2025

Reporting Period: 07 December - 08 December 2025

Distribution: Approved for public release; distribution is unlimited.

Combatant Commands: SOUTHCOM, EUCOM, PACOM, CENTCOM, AFRICOM

Intent: The intent of this report is to provide a high-level overview of significant military and geopolitical developments during the reporting period. The report consists of open-source reporting and updates on evolving conflicts, diplomatic initiatives, and strategic military actions involving countries of interest in the following Combatant Commands: SOUTHCOM, EUCOM, PACOM, CENTCOM, AFRICOM.

Executive Summary: During the reporting period, U.S. military operations in the Caribbean raised tensions with Venezuela, culminating in a September airstrike on a drug-smuggling vessel, which resulted in casualties among designated military targets. Turkish President Erdoğan called for dialogue between the U.S. and Venezuela as the State Department maintained a Level 4 travel advisory for Venezuela. In the Russia-Ukraine conflict, Ukrainian forces faced a Russian attack in Kremenchuk, while President Zelenskyy resisted endorsing a U.S.-proposed peace plan amid concerns about U.S. support declining under a potential Trump administration. In East Asia, military tensions escalated between China and Japan, marked by aggressive military maneuvers and Japan's protests. In the Middle East, Israel prepared to shift to the second phase of a ceasefire with Hamas, while Iranian military drills showcased advanced missile capabilities. In Africa, a new Russian military unit in Mali committed severe human rights abuses, while Sudan faced a humanitarian crisis exacerbated by violence. Lastly, a coup attempt in Benin was condemned by regional authorities, stressing the need for stability.

  • SOUTHCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that U.S. military operations in the Caribbean heightened tensions with Venezuela as President Donald Trump intensified pressure on Nicolás Maduro's regime. On 2 September, the U.S. military executed an airstrike on an alleged drug-smuggling vessel, which resulted in casualties among individuals listed as military targets by the U.S. Navy. Following this, both U.S. Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth and other officials defended the military's actions against what they termed drug trafficking threats, despite calls for an inquiry into potential war crimes. Concurrently, the U.S. State Department reaffirmed its Level 4 travel advisory for Venezuela, cautioning against travel due to rampant violent crime and health risks. President Recep Tayyip Erdoğan of Turkey urged both the U.S. and Venezuela to maintain open dialogue amid rising fears of U.S. military engagement. Meanwhile, supporters of Venezuelan opposition leader Maria Corina Machado rallied globally in response to her nomination for the Nobel Peace Prize, showcasing international solidarity against the Maduro government amidst escalating geopolitical strife in the region.

  • EUCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated significant developments in the ongoing military and geopolitical landscape surrounding the Russia-Ukraine conflict. On 3 December, officials in Ukraine reported that a Russian overnight attack targeted the industrial city of Kremenchuk, leading to casualties among the local infrastructure. Additionally, Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelenskyy faced increasing pressure following a difficult negotiation with U.S. representatives to discuss a proposed peace plan authored by the United States, which he indicated he was not ready to endorse. In a separate incident, Zelenskyy narrowly escaped a suspected drone attack near Dublin Airport, attributed to hybrid warfare tactics. Meanwhile, U.S. officials described the potential for a peace deal as being "really close," although the Kremlin demanded major alterations to the proposals. Internationally, concerns arose as European leaders speculated that U.S. support for Ukraine might diminish under a potential Trump administration, affecting the country's ability to confront ongoing Russian aggression.

  • PACOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated heightened military tensions between China and Japan, highlighted by multiple incidents where Chinese fighter jets directed fire-control radar at Japanese aircraft near the Okinawa Islands. Japan's Defense Ministry characterized this behavior as dangerous and launched formal protests against China for these confrontations over international waters. Concurrently, China deployed its largest naval presence yet in East Asian waters, coinciding with Japan's increased militarization of the Ryukyu island chain amidst ongoing concerns over Taiwan. Additionally, the geopolitical landscape evolved as the United States, the European Union, and Saudi Arabia revived the India-Middle East-Europe Economic Corridor (IMEC), a strategic initiative aimed at countering China's influence and pulling India closer to the West. In this context, Chinese officials maintained that their naval training operations did not target any specific countries, despite increased scrutiny and escalating tensions in the region.

  • CENTCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu announced an impending transition to the second phase of the ceasefire with Hamas, expected to involve the disarming of Hamas after the return of remains of Israeli hostages. German Chancellor Friedrich Merz reaffirmed support for Israel during his inaugural visit, emphasizing continued cooperation despite tensions due to the Gaza conflict. Meanwhile, Iran conducted military drills in the Persian Gulf, showcasing advanced missile technology, while Hezbollah's Secretary-General warned of Israeli aggression in Lebanon. In Iraq, the government retracted a previous designation of Hezbollah and Ansarallah as terrorist organizations following intense U.S. pressure, highlighting the country's precarious political situation. In Gaza, reports indicated ongoing Israeli airstrikes, leading to additional casualties among Palestinians despite a declared ceasefire.

  • AFRICOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that a new Russian military unit known as the Africa Corps committed serious human rights abuses in Mali, including beheadings and rapes, as it replaced the Wagner mercenary group. In Sudan, the World Food Programme warned of a severe humanitarian crisis, exacerbated by ongoing violence that prevented aid from reaching millions in need. A drone strike attributed to the Rapid Support Forces and allied groups resulted in over 100 deaths at a preschool in Sudan. In Benin, military personnel announced a coup attempt to oust President Patrice Talon, but the presidency asserted that the government remained in control. The Economic Community of West African States condemned the coup attempt, threatening to deploy a standby force to restore order. Additionally, Nigeria's government expressed solidarity with Benin and condemned the coup attempt, emphasizing the importance of regional stability. Lastly, South Africa experienced multiple mass shootings, resulting in at least 23 fatalities over the weekend amid ongoing gang violence.
