Intent: The intent of this report is to provide a high-level overview of significant military and geopolitical developments during the reporting period. The report consists of open-source reporting and updates on evolving conflicts, diplomatic initiatives, and strategic military actions involving countries of interest in the following Combatant Commands: SOUTHCOM, EUCOM, PACOM, CENTCOM, AFRICOM.

Executive Summary: During the reporting period, U.S. forces conducted a lethal strike on a narco-submarine in the Caribbean Sea on 25 March, neutralizing four operatives and marking a significant escalation in Operation Southern Spear against drug trafficking. In Ukraine, air defenses intercepted 97 percent of Russian drones during mass strikes on 25 March, while Ukrainian forces dealt damage to a Russian warship in the Baltic, indicating a shift in naval capabilities. The People's Liberation Army intensified air incursions near Taiwan, exploiting U.S. military redeployments to the Middle East. Iran categorically rejected a U.S.-proposed ceasefire, resulting in missile barrages targeting Israel and U.S. bases, as retaliatory actions intensified in the region. Congolese armed forces launched drone strikes against M23 positions amidst rising tensions and civilian casualties, while European nations provided military assistance to Ghana, reflecting regional security dynamics.

  • SOUTHCOM: During the reporting period, U.S. forces executed a lethal strike against a suspected narco-submarine in the Caribbean Sea on 25 March, neutralizing four operatives and destroying the vessel as part of Operation Southern Spear. Pentagon spokespersons confirmed that joint U.S.-Ecuadorian kinetic operations near the Colombia-Ecuador border, conducted under Operation Total Extermination, marked the onset of an expanded counter-cartel campaign across Latin America, with potential for additional ground assaults. Ecuadorian authorities enforced ongoing curfews in four Amazonian provinces through 30 March amid elevated violence from Los Lobos factions. Thousands rallied in Buenos Aires, Argentina, on 25 March to commemorate the 50th anniversary of the 1976 military coup, directing protests against President Milei's administration. Colombian leadership scrutinized the armed forces' reliance on secondhand aircraft following a fatal crash on 25 March. Argentine military planners adjusted the Atlantic Dagger special operations exercise on 25 March due to U.S.-Iran hostilities diverting regional assets. A solidarity flotilla delivered aid to Havana, Cuba, on 25 March, challenging the U.S. energy blockade.

  • EUCOM: During the reporting period, Ukrainian air defenses intercepted 97 percent of Russian kamikaze drones during a rare mass daytime strike on 25 March. Ukrainian forces damaged the Russian FSB-operated icebreaker Pruga at Vyborg port in Leningrad Oblast with maritime drones, achieving the first confirmed strike on a Russian warship in the Baltic Sea roughly 1000 kilometres from Ukraine, while separate drone attacks ignited fires at a major natural gas terminal in Ust-Luga port in the same oblast. Russian air defenses neutralised 389 Ukrainian drones over 13 regions and occupied Crimea overnight into 25 March, with 56 downed specifically over Leningrad Oblast. Two Ukrainian drones deviated into NATO territory, detonating harmlessly in a field near Latvia's Kraslava region and inflicting minimal damage to a chimney at Estonia's Auvere power station without casualties or disruptions. Russian authorities mandated an internet shutdown in Moscow due to unspecified security threats linked to the drone incursions. Ukrainian General Staff reported Russian forces incurred 1220 casualties on 25 March, elevating cumulative losses to 1,280,960 since 24 February 2022.

  • PACOM: During the reporting period, People's Liberation Army (PLA) aircraft conducted 16 sorties around Taiwan on 25 March, with 13 crossing the median line into Taiwan's southwestern and eastern air defense identification zone (ADIZ), while PLA Navy (PLAN) vessels numbered 10 alongside two official ships in the vicinity; Taiwan's Ministry of National Defense (MND) detected and responded to these activities. PLA aircraft executed four additional sorties on 26 March up to 0600 hours UTC+8, all crossing the median line into Taiwan's northern and southwestern ADIZ, accompanied by 11 PLAN vessels and one official ship, prompting Republic of China Armed Forces (ROCAF) monitoring and response measures. Taiwanese officials assessed Beijing's resumed large-scale air incursions—initiated mid-March amid a prior lull—as opportunistic exploitation of United States force redeployments to the Middle East conflict, with Chinese state media critiquing American weapons efficacy to undermine Taiwan's deterrence posture. No escalated PLA naval maneuvers or live-fire drills occurred in the South China Sea or Philippine Sea sectors, though PLAN maintained routine patrols within the first island chain.

  • CENTCOM: During the reporting period, Iran dismissed a United States-proposed 15-point ceasefire plan as maximalist and unreasonable, prompting Iranian Revolutionary Guards Corps forces to launch missile barrages targeting central and northern Israel, United States military bases in Kuwait, Jordan, and Bahrain, and Gulf Arab countries on 25 March. United States and Israeli forces sustained airstrikes on Iranian military infrastructure, including Islamic Revolutionary Guards Corps targets, while Israeli Defense Forces conducted intensified operations against Hezbollah positions in Lebanon. United States Central Command executed strikes on an Iran-aligned military site in Iraq, and Iran released footage claiming the downing of a United States jet over Iranian airspace. President Trump warned Iran of escalated retaliation absent a deal, as United States deployments augmented troop presence in the Gulf region amid Iranian defensive preparations on Kharg Island. Israel accelerated attacks to degrade Iran's ballistic missile and nuclear capabilities before potential negotiations.

  • AFRICOM: During the reporting period, Congolese armed forces (FARDC) executed multiple drone strikes targeting M23 positions near Uvira in South Kivu Province, Democratic Republic of Congo, which killed civilians including 95-year-old Diriyani Rudaga and destroyed livestock on 25 March; Islamist terrorists raided Aso community in Nasarawa State, Nigeria, during the morning of 25 March; Nigerien leader General Abdourahamane Tiani reaffirmed rejection of foreign military bases to preserve national sovereignty on 25 March; European states supplied drones, anti-drone systems, bomb disposal vehicles, and motorcycles to Ghanaian military forces on 25 March; analysts highlighted escalating Burundi troop concentrations near Uvira alongside FARDC preparations for offensives against M23, risking regional spillover involving Rwanda and Tanzania on 25 March.
