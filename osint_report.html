Open Source Intelligence (OSINT) Report

Date of Access: 31 March 2026

Date of Information: 31 March 2026

Reporting Period: 30 March - 31 March 2026

Distribution: Approved for public release; distribution is unlimited.

Combatant Commands: SOUTHCOM, EUCOM, PACOM, CENTCOM, AFRICOM

Intent: The intent of this report is to provide a high-level overview of significant military and geopolitical developments during the reporting period. The report consists of open-source reporting and updates on evolving conflicts, diplomatic initiatives, and strategic military actions involving countries of interest in the following Combatant Commands: SOUTHCOM, EUCOM, PACOM, CENTCOM, AFRICOM.

Executive Summary: During the reporting period, Ecuadorian Armed Forces executed Operation Fuego Letal, employing advanced weaponry to eliminate illegal mining operations on 30 March. In Mexico, security forces neutralised a top criminal figure and swiftly countered retaliatory assaults. Russian military advancements occurred across various regions, while Ukraine launched successful strikes on Russian facilities and proposed a temporary ceasefire that was dismissed by Moscow. The People's Liberation Army displayed provocations near Taiwan and engaged in near-collisions with Philippine vessels in the South China Sea. Tensions escalated in the Middle East as Israel struck Iranian targets extensively, leading to Iranian retaliatory attacks and significant casualties. In Africa, violent incidents at a gold mine in South Sudan and a gun attack in Nigeria raised security concerns, while Egypt's President Sisi highlighted the risks of external conflicts to regional stability.

  • SOUTHCOM: During the reporting period, Ecuadorian Armed Forces executed Operation Fuego Letal in El Chical parish, Carchi province, along the Colombian border, where units deployed three AMX-13 light tanks firing 105mm rounds, 81mm mortars expending 40 grenades, a C3 armed helicopter with 0.50 calibre munitions, and infantry discharging over 5500 rounds of 7.62mm ammunition to destroy 50 illegal mining shafts on 30 March. Mexican security forces neutralised the most wanted criminal figure in the Americas, countered his organisation's retaliatory attacks, and reestablished control within 48 hours as confirmed on 30 March. Costa Rican authorities ratified a bilateral agreement with the United States to receive 25 deportees weekly under a third-country arrangement on 30 March.

  • EUCOM: During the reporting period, Russian forces advanced near Hulyaipole in southeastern Myrne, in the Kostyantynivka-Druzhkivka tactical area at southwestern Stepanivka south of Druzhkivka, and in western Zaporizhia Oblast at southeastern Luhivske southeast of Orikhiv, while Ukrainian forces advanced near Slovyansk in southern Yampil southeast of Lyman and east of Kostyantynivka along the T-0504 highway; Russian forces launched 164 drones and one Iskander-M ballistic missile overnight on 29 to 30 March targeting energy infrastructure in Chernihiv Oblast, caused power outages across Odesa, Zaporizhia, Dnipropetrovsk, Khmelnytskyi, Kharkiv, Chernihiv, and Sumy oblasts, and executed 70 glide bomb strikes on Kostyantynivka, whereas Ukrainian forces conducted long-range strikes on Russian drone production facilities in Taganrog Rostov Oblast, a chemical plant in Tolyatti Samara Oblast, Baltic Sea oil infrastructure including the Kinef refinery in Kirishi and Ust-Luga, manpower concentrations near Basivka northeast of Sumy City and Nova Tavolzhanka in Belgorod Oblast, a drone command post near Hulyaipole, personnel near Berezove, and an S-400 launcher near Hvardiiske in occupied Crimea; Azov Brigade elements captured Russian soldiers near Dobropillia on 30 March; Ukrainian General Staff reported Russian personnel losses exceeded 870 on 30 March alongside 48 artillery systems; President Zelensky proposed a temporary ceasefire for Orthodox Easter on 30 March encompassing a full halt or moratorium on energy infrastructure strikes, which Russian officials dismissed; Ukraine finalised defence cooperation agreements on air defence systems with Qatar, Saudi Arabia, and the United Arab Emirates amid regional tensions, while the European Commission approved a 1.5 billion euro package on 29 March to bolster European and Ukrainian defence industries, Ukraine signed a bilateral security pact with Bulgaria for joint drone production on 30 March, and Latvia allocated 6.8 million euros for Ukrainian energy grid and drone support.

  • PACOM: During the reporting period, People's Liberation Army (PLA) forces conducted routine gray-zone operations around Taiwan, as Taiwan's Ministry of National Defense detected one PLA aircraft sortie that entered Taiwan's southern Air Defense Identification Zone (ADIZ), alongside six People's Liberation Army Navy (PLAN) vessels and one official ship operating in the vicinity until 6 a.m. UTC+8 on 31 March; Republic of China Armed Forces monitored and responded accordingly. PLA warships and warplanes executed provocative combat patrols near Scarborough Shoal in the South China Sea on 30 March, heightening tensions with the Philippines. Chinese and Philippine warships experienced a near-collision incident in the South China Sea on 30 March. China imposed sanctions on Japanese lawmaker Keiji Furuya, a close aide to Prime Minister Sanae Takaichi, on 30 March for alleged collusion with Taiwan independence forces; Japan rejected the measures as unacceptable. No large-scale exercises, blockades, or kinetic engagements occurred.

  • CENTCOM: During the reporting period, United States President Donald Trump issued renewed ultimatums threatening obliteration of Iranian power plants, oil facilities, desalination infrastructure, and Kharg Island unless Tehran accepted ceasefire terms and reopened the Strait of Hormuz. Israeli Defense Forces conducted airstrikes on 170 Iranian targets, neutralizing approximately seventy percent of Iran's ballistic missile launchers and additional weapons production sites. Iranian Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps forces launched retaliatory missiles and drones that struck a Kuwaiti oil tanker at Dubai port, a critical water and electrical plant in Kuwait, an oil refinery in Israel, and the Thuraya Telecommunications building in Sharjah, United Arab Emirates; United Arab Emirates defenses intercepted eleven missiles and twenty-seven unmanned aerial vehicles. United States and Israeli coalition aircraft executed sustained strikes across Iranian territory, inflicting at least six civilian fatalities. Three United Nations Interim Force in Lebanon peacekeepers perished from Iranian-linked attacks in southern Lebanon. Gulf Cooperation Council states, including Saudi Arabia and the United Arab Emirates, pressed Washington to maintain offensive operations and consider ground invasion options amid stalled multilateral talks hosted by Pakistan.

  • AFRICOM: During the reporting period, unidentified assailants killed over 70 individuals at a gold mine near Juba in South Sudan on 30 March amid resource disputes, prompting opposition accusations against government forces and ongoing military investigations. Gunmen attacked a university community in Plateau State's Angwan Rukuba district, Nigeria, on 30 March, killing at least 30 civilians and injuring others, which compelled authorities to impose a 48-hour curfew and suspend examinations at the University of Jos. Somali army units captured a strategic city from militants, coinciding with the resignation of a regional leader. Egyptian President Sisi warned of oil supply disruptions linked to external conflicts and asserted that only external mediation could halt ongoing wars impacting African stability.
