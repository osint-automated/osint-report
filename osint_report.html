Intent: The intent of this report is to provide a high-level overview of significant military and geopolitical developments during the reporting period. The report consists of open-source reporting and updates on evolving conflicts, diplomatic initiatives, and strategic military actions involving countries of interest in the following Combatant Commands: SOUTHCOM, EUCOM, PACOM, CENTCOM, AFRICOM.

Executive Summary: During the reporting period, critical military and geopolitical developments emerged from multiple regions. The United States and Israel accused Iran of planning to assassinate the Israeli ambassador to Mexico, indicating escalating tensions. In the Russia-Ukraine conflict, large-scale Russian attacks severely impacted Ukraine's energy infrastructure, resulting in casualties and intensified territorial battles. Concurrently, Turkey issued arrest warrants for Israeli officials, including Prime Minister Netanyahu, related to actions in Gaza, as tensions between Israel and Hamas persisted. In the Indo-Pacific, China commissioned its third aircraft carrier and a research vessel docked in the Cook Islands, raising regional security concerns. Lastly, Sudan's Rapid Support Forces agreed to a U.S.-backed ceasefire following a prolonged civil conflict.

  • SOUTHCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated a range of significant military and geopolitical developments. On 3 November, the United States and Israel accused Iran of orchestrating a plot to assassinate the Israeli ambassador to Mexico, alleging an escalation of tensions in the region. Meanwhile, in response to concerns over drug trafficking from Venezuela, a group of U.S. Senators sought to halt President Trump's military action against drug vessels associated with Hezbollah's reported plans to extend its operations in Venezuela. The situation in the Indo-Pacific also remained tense, with ongoing debates surrounding U.S. military presence amid escalating local conflicts. In the oil sector, predictions estimated that South America, driven by production from Brazil, Guyana, and Argentina, would remain a crucial player in non-OPEC+ oil supply leading up to 2030, reinforcing geopolitical interests in the area.

  • EUCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated significant military and geopolitical developments stemming from the ongoing Russia-Ukraine conflict. Large-scale Russian attacks on Ukraine's energy infrastructure resulted in widespread power outages and casualties, with at least two reported deaths from the strikes. Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelenskyy confirmed over 300 Russian soldiers had entered the eastern city of Pokrovsk, intensifying the battle for territory, with Ukrainian forces deploying elite units to respond. In parallel, NATO chief Mark Rutte expressed confidence in the alliance's nuclear deterrence capabilities following recent drills, amidst rising tensions from US nuclear brinkmanship towards Russia. Additionally, the United States granted Hungary a one-year exemption from sanctions on Russian oil, a move that followed Hungarian Prime Minister Viktor Orbán's appeals for relief. The European Union responded to the ongoing conflict by tightening visa restrictions for Russian citizens.

  • PACOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that a Chinese research vessel, the Da Yang Hao, docked in Avatiu, Cook Islands, on 4 November while exploring the nation's deep-sea mining potential, attracting interest from both Beijing and Washington. Furthermore, on 8 November, China officially commissioned its third aircraft carrier, the Fujian, enhancing its maritime capabilities and positioning it as the second-largest naval power globally. Meanwhile, the upcoming COP30 summit in Brazil, described as an 'implementation COP', is anticipated to focus on climate initiatives without expecting major decision-making.

  • CENTCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that Turkey's Istanbul Chief Public Prosecutor’s Office issued arrest warrants for 37 Israeli officials, including Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu, on charges of genocide and crimes against humanity related to actions in Gaza. The United States, alongside Israel, accused Iran's Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps of plot to assassinate Israel's ambassador to Mexico. Additionally, leaked U.S. intelligence revealed that Israeli military lawyers had recognized evidence of potential war crimes in Gaza, sparking further international scrutiny. In a related development, Turkey coordinated the transfer of hostage remains amidst the ongoing conflict, highlighting the fragile truce between Israel and Hamas.

  • AFRICOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated significant military and geopolitical developments. On 8 November, former President Donald Trump announced the U.S. would boycott the G20 summit in South Africa, citing the country's treatment of white farmers and reiterating claims of a white genocide, which have been widely debunked. Concurrently, Sudan's Rapid Support Forces agreed to a U.S.-backed ceasefire after 2.5 years of civil conflict, which has been characterized by a dire humanitarian crisis, described by the U.N. as the largest globally. Additionally, on the same day, Turkey issued arrest warrants for Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu and 36 other Israeli officials on genocide charges linked to the ongoing conflict in Gaza. These events underscore rising tensions in various regions and the complexities of contemporary geopolitical issues.
