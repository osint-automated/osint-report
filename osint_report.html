Intent: The intent of this report is to provide a high-level overview of significant military and geopolitical developments during the reporting period. Executive Summary: During the reporting period, critical military and geopolitical updates revealed a significant escalation in violence and instability across multiple regions. The United States and Israel jointly launch major coordinated strikes on Iran on 28 February, under Operation Epic Fury (U.S.) and Roaring Lion (Israel). In Mexico, cartel-related violence surged following the death of a cartel leader, prompting cruise lines to skip Puerto Vallarta and the escape of 23 inmates from a prison, facilitated by cartel gunmen. Meanwhile, Ukraine demonstrated effective military operations by destroying enemy drones and received an $8.1 billion loan package from the International Monetary Fund for post-war recovery. Tensions heightened in the South China Sea, where Chinese military activity increased alongside joint exercises involving U.S., Philippine, and Japanese forces. In Gaza, Israeli airstrikes resulted in civilian casualties as international tensions over the Israel-Palestine conflict deepened, with growing American sympathy for Palestinians. Africa faced rising instability, with South Sudan on the brink of renewed conflict, significant jihadist violence in Burkina Faso, and escalating tensions in Ethiopia's Tigray region.

  • SOUTHCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that several cruise lines opted to skip stops in Puerto Vallarta, Mexico, following increased violence after the death of cartel leader Nemesio "El Mencho" Oseguera. This week, 23 inmates escaped from a prison in Puerto Vallarta, with cartel gunmen facilitating the break, highlighting the escalating chaos. The U.S. State Department replaced its "shelter in place" order for Americans in Mexico with a less severe "travel advisory." In related developments, the U.S. offered $10 million for information leading to the capture of Sinaloa cartel leaders in Tijuana and cartel bosses Rene and Alfonso Arzate Garcia. Meanwhile, FIFA President Gianni Infantino expressed confidence in Mexico as a host for the upcoming World Cup, despite acknowledging the ongoing violence. Mexican President Claudia Sheinbaum confirmed her commitment to hosting the event safely, responding to Infantino's reassurances.

  • EUCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that Ukrainian helicopter crews effectively utilized machine guns to destroy eight Shahed and Gerbera drones in a single day, as confirmed by footage released by the Ukrainian navy on 24 October. The U.S. military shot down a Customs and Border Protection drone using a laser near El Paso, Texas, leading to the closure of nearby airspace, as confirmed by members of Congress. Russian forces gained control of the Krasnoznamenka settlement in the Dnepropetrovsk region, according to the Russian Defense Ministry. The International Monetary Fund approved an $8.1 billion loan package for Ukraine, unlocking immediate access to $1.5 billion, aimed at assisting post-war recovery efforts. Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelenskyy indicated readiness for peace talks with Russian President Vladimir Putin but maintained that Ukraine would not relinquish territory. Foreign casualties from the conflict included at least 55 confirmed deaths of Ghanaians, representing the highest toll from a single African nation. Additionally, Hungary's Prime Minister Viktor Orban requested EU assistance to investigate the condition of the damaged Druzhba oil pipeline.

  • PACOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that China successfully retrieved the Long March 10 rocket booster from the South China Sea following a test flight on 16 February 2026. Concurrently, Chinese military drones conducted regular flights in the South China Sea while using false transponder signals to disguise their movements, potentially rehearsing for a future operation involving Taiwan. Philippine, U.S., and Japanese aircraft participated in joint military exercises over the Bashi Channel, which separates the Philippines from Taiwan, enhancing regional cooperation. In parallel, Taiwan reported the detection of eight aircraft and six vessels from the People's Liberation Army near its territory, with many sorties crossing into its air defense identification zone. Additionally, India announced plans to build a military airfield in the Andaman and Nicobar Islands, aiming to counter China's growing influence in the region. As tensions escalated, Sweden also disrupted a suspected Russian drone near the French aircraft carrier Charles de Gaulle, participating in NATO exercises.

  • CENTCOM: During the reporting period, the United States and Israel jointly launch major coordinated strikes on Iran on 28 February, under Operation Epic Fury (U.S.) and Roaring Lion (Israel), targeting Iranian nuclear facilities, ballistic missile infrastructure, military bases, and senior leadership compounds across cities including Tehran, Isfahan, Qom, Karaj, and Kermanshah. Strikes hit the compound of Supreme Leader Ayatollah Ali Khamenei, along with other high-value political and military figures, while aiming to degrade Iran's nuclear program and missile capabilities following failed indirect nuclear negotiations mediated by Oman. Iran immediately retaliates with waves of ballistic missiles and drones under "Truthful Promise 4," targeting Israeli territory and U.S. military installations throughout the Middle East, including Al Udeid Air Base in Qatar, Al Dhafra Air Base in the UAE, Al Salem Air Base in Kuwait, the U.S. Fifth Fleet headquarters in Bahrain, and sites in Jordan and Saudi Arabia. Air defenses in Gulf states intercept many incoming projectiles, though explosions and at least one civilian fatality from debris occur; Iran declares all American and Israeli assets in the region legitimate targets, with ongoing missile and drone exchanges escalating regional tensions.

  • AFRICOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that South Sudan faced an increased risk of returning to full-scale war as the UN warned of escalating atrocity risks and widespread impunity amid renewed fighting. In Burkina Faso, coordinated attacks by the Al-Qaeda affiliate JNIM resulted in over 130 deaths, highlighting the surge of jihadist violence across the Sahelian state. Tensions escalated in Ethiopia's Tigray region, where residents feared an imminent return to conflict as fragile calm persisted in Mekelle amid rising hostilities between Ethiopia and Eritrea. The US reported a historic low in citizens' support for Israel amid the ongoing Gaza conflict, with polls showing a shift in sympathy towards the Palestinian cause.
