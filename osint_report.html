Open Source Intelligence (OSINT) Report

Date of Access: 27 November 2025

Date of Information: 27 November 2025

Reporting Period: 26 November - 27 November 2025

Distribution: Approved for public release; distribution is unlimited.

Combatant Commands: SOUTHCOM, EUCOM, PACOM, CENTCOM, AFRICOM

Intent: The intent of this report is to provide a high-level overview of significant military and geopolitical developments during the reporting period. The report consists of open-source reporting and updates on evolving conflicts, diplomatic initiatives, and strategic military actions involving countries of interest in the following Combatant Commands: SOUTHCOM, EUCOM, PACOM, CENTCOM, AFRICOM.

Executive Summary: During the reporting period, significant military and geopolitical developments occurred across multiple regions. In South America, U.S. military presence in Venezuela expanded amid rising tensions, with a naval force deployed to pressure President Nicolás Maduro, while Russia's involvement became evident with a top general leading troops in the country. In Europe, Ukraine tentatively accepted a U.S.-brokered peace proposal amidst ongoing conflict with Russia, as NATO ally Romania heightened military readiness following Russian airstrikes. Concurrently, Taiwan announced a $40 billion arms budget to bolster defenses against China, and the Philippine Navy reported increased Chinese naval activity in contested waters. In the Middle East, Israeli airstrikes in Beirut targeted Hezbollah leadership, while escalating violence in Gaza exacerbated humanitarian crises. In Africa, reports of atrocities by Russian mercenaries in Mali emerged, as Nigeria's government faced ongoing insurgent-related kidnappings and South Sudan dealt with a humanitarian aid crisis following an aid flight crash.

  • SOUTHCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that tensions increased in Venezuela, with the U.S. expanding military presence in the region. The Trump administration deployed a naval force, including three destroyers and approximately 4,000 troops, allegedly under the guise of a counter-narcotics operation, intensifying pressure on President Nicolás Maduro. Cuba condemned the U.S.'s "aggressive" stance, suggesting it sets the stage for a potential violent intervention. Amid these developments, reports surfaced of a top Russian general leading several hundred soldiers in Venezuela, highlighting the geopolitical stakes involved. Concurrently, President Gustavo Petro of Colombia argued that the U.S. military campaign against Venezuela revolved around its oil resources. Additionally, the Trump administration designated the Cartel de los Soles as a foreign terrorist organization, complicating operations for foreign oil companies and further isolating Maduro's government.

  • EUCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that significant developments transpired regarding the ongoing Russia-Ukraine conflict and associated geopolitical dynamics. Ukraine tentatively accepted a U.S.-sponsored peace proposal following covert negotiations in Abu Dhabi and Geneva, despite Russian skepticism about the deal's viability. U.S. President Donald Trump confirmed plans for his special envoy, Steve Witkoff, to visit Moscow on 25 November, underlining continued U.S. efforts to mediate the conflict. Concurrently, a senior Ukrainian defense official revealed military modifications in Russia's Shahed drones, aimed at improving their effectiveness against Ukrainian interceptors. Amid these military escalations, NATO member Romania took preventive measures by deploying fighter jets after Russian airstrikes prompted drone crashes on its territory and that of neighboring Moldova. Additionally, Polish officials expressed concerns over potential Russian sabotage activities following a rail explosion attributed to Moscow.

  • PACOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated significant military and geopolitical developments. Taiwan announced a special budget of $40 billion for arms purchases, intended to strengthen its defense capabilities amid ongoing threats from China, with expenditures planned from 2026 to 2033. In the West Philippine Sea, the Philippine Navy reported monitoring 30 Chinese military and coast guard ships near four Philippine-held features, raising tensions in the region. Meanwhile, Ukraine signaled support for a potential U.S.-led peace framework concerning Russia, leading to a drop in oil prices, as analysts predicted a crude surplus for the following year. In the United States, several senators proposed the "Strategic Subsea Cables Act of 2025" to enhance the security of undersea fiber-optic cables, reflecting ongoing concerns over critical infrastructure.

  • CENTCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that Pope Leo XIV prepared for his inaugural foreign pilgrimage to Turkey and Lebanon amid escalating tensions in the Middle East. On 24 October, Israeli forces conducted an airstrike in Beirut, marking the first such attack in months, which resulted in the death of a top Hezbollah military commander. In response to ongoing violence, Pope Leo urged both Israel and Hezbollah to seek dialogue instead of armed conflict. Meanwhile, Israeli operations expanded in the West Bank, particularly in Tubas, displacing numerous families as part of what the military termed a counter-terrorism effort. The situation in Gaza remained dire, with ongoing Israeli airstrikes and severe humanitarian challenges exacerbated by harsh weather conditions. Aid organizations reported an urgent need for housing for displaced Palestinians. Additionally, reports emerged of strengthened military ties between Hezbollah and Hamas, and between Hamas and Islamic Jihad, raising concerns about regional stability.

  • AFRICOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that Russian mercenaries committed significant atrocities in Mali, as verified by eyewitness accounts shared with the BBC. Concurrently, Nigeria’s government announced the rescue of 24 schoolgirls taken by gunmen from a school in Kebbi State, while hundreds of other children remained missing due to ongoing insurgent attacks across the country. Furthermore, the surge in violence prompted Human Rights Watch to call on Nigerian authorities to investigate the alarming rise in abductions. In South Sudan, an aid flight tragically crashed, resulting in the deaths of all three crew members. Meanwhile, Somalia declared a national drought emergency as more than two million people faced starvation due to delayed aid. Lastly, South Africa's dominance in the cricket series against India was marked by a historic 408-run victory, completing a 2-0 series sweep, while South Africa's central bank acknowledged concerns about stablecoins potentially impacting financial stability and draining deposits from emerging markets.
