Executive Summary: During the reporting period, significant military and geopolitical updates emerged across multiple regions. The U.S. announced that India would switch its oil imports from Venezuela to replace Russian supplies amid sanctions. Venezuela's government released activist Javier Tarazona under U.S. pressure, while U.S. diplomatic efforts in Caracas resumed with the arrival of Laura Dogu. In the ongoing Russia-Ukraine conflict, Russian drone strikes killed 12 mine workers, as Ukraine faced power outages and limited progress in peace talks. The Chinese military conducted patrols around Huangyan Dao, asserting territorial control, while violence in Pakistan led to nearly 200 deaths linked to separatist conflicts. In Syria, authorities detained a group connected to rocket attacks on a military airport, as Iran engaged in discussions with the U.S. to reduce military tensions. Meanwhile, the Nigerian army eliminated a Boko Haram commander, Sudan's airport resumed flights, and the Islamic State claimed responsibility for an assault in Niger.

  • SOUTHCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that U.S. President Donald Trump announced that India would begin purchasing oil from Venezuela instead of Iran, as India sought to replace Russian oil imports amid U.S. sanctions. Venezuela's government released rights activist Javier Tarazona, who had been imprisoned on charges of terrorism and conspiracy, marking a continuation of prisoner releases under U.S. pressure for reforms. Newly-appointed U.S. charge d'affaires Laura Dogu arrived in Caracas, Venezuela, to reopen the diplomatic mission after seven years, coinciding with the country's efforts to privatize its oil sector. President Trump also indicated that the U.S. was initiating discussions with Cuban leaders while enforcing restrictions on oil supplies to the island. Amid geopolitical tensions, the Pope called for dialogue between the U.S. and Cuba to avoid further conflict. The U.S. welcomed investments from China and India in Venezuelan oil, a strategic move following the recent removal of Nicolás Maduro by U.S. forces.

  • EUCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that Elon Musk announced the success of SpaceX's measures to prevent unauthorized Russian access to Starlink internet services, as of 1 February. The ongoing Russia-Ukraine conflict continued to witness significant military developments, with Russian drone strikes resulting in the deaths of 12 mine workers and injuries to seven others in Dnipropetrovsk, Ukraine, also reported on 1 February. Ukrainian officials faced severe challenges due to widespread power outages affecting major cities amid winter conditions, while U.S.-backed trilateral peace talks were set to resume in Abu Dhabi on 4 and 5 February, following previous negotiations that yielded limited progress. Additionally, Russian Deputy Foreign Minister Alexander Grushko emphasized Russia's stance against NATO troop deployment in Ukraine, stating it could not be accepted under security guarantees. Meanwhile, tens of thousands rallied in Prague, Czech Republic, supporting President Petr Pavel amidst political disputes.

  • PACOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that the Chinese military conducted combat readiness patrols around Huangyan Dao on 14 October, asserting control over the territorial waters and airspace of the contested region. In Balochistan, Pakistan, nearly 200 individuals, including 31 civilians, lost their lives amid escalating violence linked to ongoing separatist conflicts. The current geopolitical tensions in both regions reflect underlying socio-political struggles that could have broader implications for regional stability.

  • CENTCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that Syria detained a group responsible for recent rocket attacks on the Mezzeh military airport in Damascus on 1 February, with the weaponry traced back to Hezbollah. Iran initiated serious discussions with the United States to avert escalating military tensions while also preparing for live-fire naval exercises in the Strait of Hormuz, a crucial shipping route. Tensions rose as Iran labeled all European Union militaries as "terrorist groups" in response to the EU's designation of the Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps. In Gaza, Israel partially reopened the Rafah crossing with Egypt on 1 February, which had been closed for nearly two years, allowing limited movement as thousands awaited medical treatment. Meanwhile, Israeli airstrikes in southern Lebanon resulted in one casualty, marking ongoing violations of a ceasefire agreement established in November 2024.

  • AFRICOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated significant military and geopolitical developments across multiple regions. On 1 February, the Nigerian army announced the elimination of a prominent Boko Haram commander, Abu Khalid, along with ten militants during a night raid in Borno State, Nigeria. Meanwhile, in Sudan, Khartoum International Airport welcomed its first scheduled flight since the onset of the ongoing conflict, signaling a small step towards normalization in the region. In West Africa, leaders convened at the Accra Summit, where they expressed a united commitment to combat rising terrorism. Additionally, the Islamic State claimed responsibility for a recent assault on Diori Hamani International Airport in Niamey, Niger, which is also home to a military airbase, though Niger’s authorities reported that the attack was repelled. In a broader context, U.S. NATO envoy Matthew Whitaker emphasized the importance of avoiding a Libyan scenario in Iran, reflecting ongoing geopolitical concerns.
