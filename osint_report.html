Intent: The intent of this report is to provide a high-level overview of significant military and geopolitical developments during the reporting period. The report consists of open-source reporting and updates on evolving conflicts, diplomatic initiatives, and strategic military actions involving countries of interest in the following Combatant Commands: SOUTHCOM, EUCOM, PACOM, CENTCOM, AFRICOM.

Executive Summary: During the reporting period, key military and geopolitical updates included U.S. military actions against a drug smuggling vessel near Venezuela, leading to bipartisan scrutiny and increased tensions between the U.S. and Venezuela. President Trump called for a closure of Venezuelan airspace, while Maduro faced domestic and international pressure amid widespread protests. In Europe, Russia claimed territorial gains in Ukraine as Ukrainian resistance persisted, with significant drone attacks on Russian infrastructure reported. China's naval capabilities continued to grow, raising concerns for U.S. forces in the Pacific, and tensions flared between Japan and China over a coast guard incident. In the Middle East, Israel maintained military operations in Gaza despite a ceasefire, while the UK expressed concern over humanitarian conditions in Gaza. Lastly, in Africa, the U.S. Treasury investigated potential funding links between welfare misuse in Minnesota and al-Shabaab in Somalia, and the International Criminal Court confirmed the arrest of a Libyan war crimes suspect.

  • SOUTHCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that the White House confirmed a U.S. admiral, acting under Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth's authority, ordered a "double-tap" military operation against an alleged drug smuggling vessel near Venezuela. This second strike, conducted on the survivors of an initial attack, drew bipartisan scrutiny and raised concerns over the legality of the action. Concurrently, tensions between the U.S. and Venezuela escalated as President Donald Trump called for a closure of Venezuelan airspace following President Nicolás Maduro's refusal to leave the country. U.S. military presence increased in the Caribbean, with a carrier strike group reportedly deployed near Venezuela, while Congress initiated bipartisan inquiries into the military strikes. Meanwhile, Maduro faced mounting pressure domestically and internationally, as thousands rallied in Caracas against potential U.S. military action, and key regional allies distanced themselves from his regime.

  • EUCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that Kelluu, a Finnish company located 50 miles from the Russian border, was developing small airships for Arctic warfare, spurred by ongoing jamming efforts from Russia. Russian forces reportedly continued to utilize black market Starlink terminals for their drones despite international efforts to restrict access since 2024. Concurrently, President Vladimir Putin claimed that Russian troops captured the Ukrainian cities of Pokrovsk and Vovchansk, which Ukraine disputed. Ukrainian troops expressed skepticism about the prospects of achieving lasting peace, fearing Russia's determination to retain control of captured territories. Meanwhile, Ukraine conducted extensive drone attacks on Russian energy infrastructure in the Black Sea, targeting vessels connected to Russia’s “shadow fleet.” U.S. envoy Steve Witkoff held talks with Putin in Moscow, with discussions centering on peace proposals for Ukraine, amid rising tensions regarding military strategies and territorial disputes.

  • PACOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that China's navy continued to enhance its capabilities, posing a potential peer challenge to U.S. naval forces in the Pacific region. Japan and China experienced rising tensions following a coast guard incident in disputed waters, with both sides presenting conflicting accounts. Informal discussions between Japan and the Philippines centered on the potential export of missile technology, which may exacerbate relations with China. As the United States intensified its stance against Venezuelan President Nicolás Maduro, global alliances around Venezuela's political situation were highlighted, with various nations indicating their positions. In India, the commissioning of the INS Aridhaman, the third nuclear-powered ballistic missile submarine, marked a significant advancement in its naval deterrent capabilities. Additionally, geopolitical risks influenced the oil market, with prices remaining elevated in response to drone strikes on infrastructure in the Caspian region and ongoing tensions over Venezuela. Finally, NATO leadership suggested the need to analyze the possibility of pre-emptive strikes as a defensive strategy against Russia.

  • CENTCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that the UK’s Foreign Secretary, Yvette Cooper, criticized delays in aid reaching Gaza, highlighting dire conditions for 1.5 million people requiring immediate shelter. Israeli forces killed two suspected Palestinian attackers in the West Bank on 24 October, following earlier incidents that resulted in injuries to three Israelis. Israel's Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu expressed that a deal with Syria was possible but insisted on establishing a buffer zone. Meanwhile, the Israeli military continued its operations in Gaza, conducting a drone strike that killed a Palestinian man, which violated a ceasefire agreement. Analysts asserted that Israeli military actions had not ceased despite a designated ceasefire. In cyber warfare, Iran-linked actors targeted multiple sectors within Israel, employing sophisticated tools to breach security. The situation remained tense as the U.S. envoy warned Iraq about the potential for an Israeli offensive against Hezbollah.

  • AFRICOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that the U.S. Treasury Department investigated the potential diversion of Minnesota welfare funds to the al-Shabaab terror group in Somalia. In Libya, the International Criminal Court confirmed the arrest of Khaled Mohamed Ali El Hishri for alleged war crimes committed at Mitiga Prison between 2015 and 2020. In Sudan, the armed forces denied claims that the Rapid Support Forces had taken control of the key city of Babnusa amidst ongoing tensions in the region. Meanwhile, Nigeria granted asylum to Fernando Dias, a presidential candidate from Guinea-Bissau, citing imminent threats to his life after a coup. In Central African Republic, the Defense Minister reported unprecedented development due to Russian aid.
