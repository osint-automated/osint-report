Open Source Intelligence (OSINT) Report

Date of Access: 16 December 2025

Date of Information: 16 December 2025

Reporting Period: 15 December - 16 December 2025

Distribution: Approved for public release; distribution is unlimited.

Combatant Commands: SOUTHCOM, EUCOM, PACOM, CENTCOM, AFRICOM

Intent: The intent of this report is to provide a high-level overview of significant military and geopolitical developments during the reporting period. The report consists of open-source reporting and updates on evolving conflicts, diplomatic initiatives, and strategic military actions involving countries of interest in the following Combatant Commands: SOUTHCOM, EUCOM, PACOM, CENTCOM, AFRICOM.

Executive Summary: During the reporting period, significant military and geopolitical developments occurred across multiple regions. In South America, a JetBlue flight narrowly avoided a midair collision with a U.S. Air Force tanker near Venezuela, prompting military deployments by the U.S. amid escalating tensions and a seized Venezuelan oil tanker. In Europe, Ukraine expressed readiness to abandon NATO aspirations in favor of U.S. security guarantees, while Russia emphasized the need for a legally binding renunciation of NATO accession in peace talks. In Asia, the Philippines protested against China's aggressive actions in the South China Sea, and Japan announced increased defense spending amid rising regional security concerns. Concurrently, in the Middle East, Hamas commemorated the October attacks and warned of ceasefire violations, while Israel conducted airstrikes in southern Lebanon. In Africa, Sudan faced severe humanitarian crises due to ongoing violence, and ECOWAS threatened sanctions against Guinea-Bissau's military government. Overall, these developments underscore rising geopolitical tensions and military preparedness globally.

  • SOUTHCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that a JetBlue flight, ascending from Curaçao, narrowly avoided a midair collision with a U.S. Air Force refueling tanker near Venezuela on 15 December. The JetBlue pilot reported that the military plane's lack of transponder activation made the encounter "outrageous." Concurrently, tensions between the United States and Venezuela rose significantly, prompting the U.S. military to deploy various aircraft, including bombers and surveillance drones, along the Venezuelan coast. The U.S. government seized a Venezuelan oil tanker, escalating accusations of American military actions aimed at securing oil interests in the region. In political developments, Chile elected far-right candidate José Antonio Kast as president, marking a swift shift to the right amid public concerns over crime and migration.

  • EUCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated significant developments in the ongoing Russia-Ukraine conflict. Ukraine announced its willingness to abandon its NATO membership aspirations in exchange for security guarantees from the United States and European partners, marking a pivotal shift in its diplomatic stance. Concurrently, Russian officials underscored the necessity of a legally binding renunciation of Ukraine's NATO ambitions as a critical aspect of peace negotiations in Berlin. The MI6 chief, Blaise Metreweli, warned of an aggressive and expansionist Russia, as tensions remained high, particularly following reports of Russia utilizing foreign-made components in missile-armed drones aimed at Ukrainian forces. Lithuania declared a state of emergency due to suspected hybrid threats involving balloon incursions from Belarus. Additionally, discussions continued on the financial support and reparations for Ukraine amidst complicated negotiations involving frozen assets. While peace talks progressed, territorial disputes between the involved nations remained unresolved, suggesting ongoing volatility in the region.

  • PACOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated significant military and geopolitical developments in Asia. On 15 December, the Philippines announced plans to formally protest against China's Coast Guard after an incident on 13 December where three Filipino fishermen were injured due to aggressive actions involving water cannons in a disputed area of the South China Sea. In response to rising tensions, the U.S. characterized China's actions in the region as provocative and increasingly dangerous. Concurrently, Japan's government revealed intentions to escalator its defense budget to a record-high 9 trillion yen (approximately $60.3 billion) for the 2026 fiscal year, signaling a potential resurgence in militaristic policies amid regional security concerns. Meanwhile, the China National Offshore Oil Corporation (CNOOC) commenced full operations at the Liuhua Oilfield in the South China Sea, further asserting its presence in the contested area. These developments illustrate a continuing trend of regional militarization and geopolitical rivalry.

  • CENTCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated significant developments in military and geopolitical situations across various regions. On 14 December, Hamas commemorated its founding anniversary by tying it to the 7 October attacks, alleging Israeli violations of the ceasefire agreements. The European Union's foreign policy chief, Kaja Kallas, stated on 11 December that negotiations for financial support for Ukraine faced increasing difficulties. In Australia, a mass shooting at Bondi Beach resulted in at least 15 fatalities and raised concerns about rising antisemitism following the Gaza conflict. Meanwhile, the Israeli military conducted airstrikes in southern Lebanon, resulting in at least three deaths amid accusations of ceasefire violations. On the diplomatic front, serious tensions arose as Hamas warned that Israeli military actions threatened the viability of the current ceasefire, while Iran reiterated its commitment to supporting Hezbollah in the region. Additionally, the International Criminal Court upheld its investigation into possible war crimes committed in Israel, impacting legal proceedings against Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu and former Defense Minister Yoav Gallant.

  • AFRICOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that escalating violence and famine in Kadugli, Sudan, severely impacted civilians, leaving them trapped and fearful. Humanitarian conditions deteriorated as nearly three years of conflict displaced 14 million people and pushed 21 million into acute hunger. The UN condemned drone attacks on a peacekeeping base in Sudan’s Kordofan region that killed six peacekeepers from Bangladesh. The Economic Community of West African States (ECOWAS) warned Guinea-Bissau’s military government could face sanctions if it did not swiftly return to civilian rule. In the military sphere, the International Criminal Court sentenced former Sudanese militia leader Ali Kosheib to 20 years for war crimes. Additionally, Algeria's football team prepared for the upcoming Africa Cup of Nations while discussions about the continent's recent coup turmoil intensified, highlighting the need for a return to democratic governance across several countries.
