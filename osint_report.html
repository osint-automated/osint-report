Intent: The intent of this report is to provide a high-level overview of significant military and geopolitical developments during the reporting period. The report consists of open-source reporting and updates on evolving conflicts, diplomatic initiatives, and strategic military actions involving countries of interest in the following Combatant Commands: SOUTHCOM, EUCOM, PACOM, CENTCOM, AFRICOM.

Executive Summary: During the reporting period, Cuban authorities released 2,010 prisoners as a humanitarian measure amid intensified US economic pressure, while US naval assets conducted multinational maritime exercises in Panamanian waters, enhancing regional security cooperation. Russian forces launched a large-scale aerial assault across Ukraine, resulting in civilian casualties and significant interceptions by Ukrainian air defenses. Ukrainian forces repelled numerous attacks, achieving slight territorial gains and demonstrating a resilient frontline position. The People's Liberation Army executed live-fire drills near Taiwan, while maintaining air and maritime operations that raised regional tensions. US and Israeli forces conducted extensive airstrikes on Iranian military infrastructure, resulting in casualties and further degradation of Iranian capabilities despite rapid reparations. In Africa, Burkina Faso's junta leader dismissed democratic aspirations amid jihadist threats, and regional conflicts revealed ongoing violence and operational challenges, with significant civilian casualties reported in the Democratic Republic of Congo.

  • SOUTHCOM: During the reporting period, Cuban authorities initiated the release of 2,010 prisoners on 3 April as a humanitarian measure amid intensified US economic pressure via oil blockade, while US naval assets including an aircraft carrier and destroyer conducted multinational maritime exercises in Panamanian waters on 3 April to enhance regional security cooperation. US Senator Marco Rubio condemned Chinese detention of Panama-flagged vessels following a canal dispute on 3 April, escalating bilateral tensions, and Russian vessels delivered additional fuel shipments to Cuba on 3 April to counter the US-imposed energy restrictions. US forces executed a precision strike against a suspected narco-submarine in the eastern Pacific Ocean off Central American coasts on 3 April, neutralizing six suspects and disrupting transnational criminal networks. Paraguayan legislators ratified enhanced US military access protocols earlier in the month, with implementation activities observed on 4 April, bolstering hemispheric defense postures. No major interstate conflicts or troop mobilizations transpired across Central or South America.

  • EUCOM: During the reporting period, Russian forces executed a large-scale daytime aerial assault employing drones and missiles against civilian infrastructure in Zhytomyr, Kyiv, Kharkiv, and Donetsk regions, killing six civilians including three in Kramatorsk via bomb strike and two in Kharkiv alongside over two dozen injuries, while Ukrainian air defenses intercepted 515 of 542 drones and 26 of 37 missiles launched since the previous evening. Ukrainian General Staff forces repelled 230 combat clashes predominantly near Pokrovsk in Donetsk Oblast and Hulaipole in Zaporizhzhia Oblast, destroying 10 Russian tanks, 18 armored vehicles, and eight automotive units amid Russian massing efforts that Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelenskiy characterized as thwarted offensives yielding the most favorable frontline posture for Kyiv in 10 months with slight territorial net gains of 20 square kilometers. Zelenskiy accused Moscow of Easter escalation through intensified daytime tactics designed to maximize civilian casualties and disrupt infrastructure, as affirmed by Prime Minister Yulia Svyrydenko, while inviting U.S. negotiators to Kyiv amid stalled peace talks; Russian forces continued offensive operations across northern Sumy, Kharkiv, Kupyansk, Pokrovsk, Novopavlivka, Oleksandrivka, Hulyaipole, and western Zaporizhia sectors on 3 April but achieved no confirmed advances.

  • PACOM: During the reporting period, People's Liberation Army (PLA) Eastern Theater Command executed its inaugural live-fire drill featuring a Type 055 Renhai-class destroyer near Taiwan, with the Anqing engaging multiple targets in a complex operation to enhance war readiness. Taiwan Ministry of National Defense detected two PLA aircraft sorties that penetrated southwestern and eastern air defense identification zones (ADIZ), alongside seven People's Liberation Army Navy (PLAN) vessels and two official ships operating around the island up to 6 a.m. UTC+8 on 3 April. PLA maintained persistent maritime presence with YJ-62 anti-ship missile transporter-erector-launchers deployed approximately 320 km from Taiwan, bolstering sea denial capabilities in the region. No significant South China Sea patrols or broader Indo-Pacific escalations occurred, though Institute for the Study of War reported ongoing assessments of PLA threats to Taiwan and US infrastructure.

  • CENTCOM: During the reporting period, United States and Israeli forces executed over seventy airstrikes targeting military infrastructure across central and western Iran, Israel Defense Forces reported on 3 April. Iranian state media claimed forces downed a United States A-10 aircraft, while United States outlets confirmed rescuers retrieved one crew member from a separate downed fighter jet over Iranian territory on 3 April. Bahraini air forces intercepted and destroyed sixteen Iranian drones within the preceding twenty-four hours. Casualties from a United States-Israeli strike on the B1 Bridge near Tehran climbed to thirteen dead, Iranian sources updated on 4 April. United States intelligence assessed Iranian technicians rapidly repaired missile bunkers despite prior degradation. Iranian ballistic missile and drone launches registered their lowest volume in the conflict's duration over the last twenty-four hours. President Donald Trump delineated the forthcoming operational phase, asserting United States strikes would intensify against remaining Iranian assets. Tehran rejected ceasefire overtures, activated reinforcements from its Axis of Resistance proxies, and issued warnings to regional allies amid escalating tensions.

  • AFRICOM: During the reporting period, Burkina Faso's junta leader Captain Ibrahim Traoré declared that citizens should abandon democratic aspirations in favour of sustained military governance amid jihadist threats. Human Rights Watch documented systematic civilian abuses by Burkina Faso and Mali militaries and their allies, revealing higher civilian casualties from state forces than from jihadists. Zimbabwe's ZANU-PF escalated pre-electoral repression through arrests, abductions, and armed militia deployments against opposition elements on 3 April. Congolese army forces reported Allied Democratic Forces militants executed an attack killing 43 civilians in northeast Democratic Republic of Congo on 2 April, with operations ongoing. Darfur Governor Minni Arko Minawi urged United Nations replacement of the Adre crossing due to Rapid Support Forces and United Arab Emirates military diversions. Libyan National Army commander Khalifa Haftar integrated Chinese and Turkish combat drones at Al Khadim airbase, violating United Nations arms embargo provisions. Burundi army spokespersons confirmed a munitions depot explosion at Musaga base destroyed infrastructure and killed at least 13 personnel following an electrical fault.
