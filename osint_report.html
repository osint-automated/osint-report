Combatant Commands: SOUTHCOM, EUCOM, PACOM, CENTCOM, AFRICOM

Intent: The intent of this report is to provide a high-level overview of significant military and geopolitical developments during the reporting period. The report consists of open-source reporting and updates on evolving conflicts, diplomatic initiatives, and strategic military actions involving countries of interest in the following Combatant Commands: SOUTHCOM, EUCOM, PACOM, CENTCOM, AFRICOM.

Executive Summary: During the reporting period, significant military and geopolitical tensions emerged, particularly related to the U.S.-Iran conflict. Joint U.S.-Israeli forces conducted sustained airstrikes across Iran targeting command centers, air defenses, missile systems, weapons storage sites, IRGC infrastructure, and the Natanz nuclear facility while establishing local air superiority, Israel struck Hezbollah and Quds Force assets in Lebanon, Iran launched repeated ballistic missile and drone barrages against Israel, U.S. forces, and Gulf state targets causing civilian and military casualties and regional infrastructure disruption, Iranian-backed militias expanded attacks in Israel and Iraq, and defensive intercepts limited but did not prevent casualties as operations focused on degrading Iran’s remaining missile and leadership capabilities amid continued retaliation threats. In Europe, France announced plans to expand its nuclear arsenal, while discussions about peace talks between Russia and Ukraine continued amid ongoing conflict. In West Africa, ECOWAS deployed troops in response to challenges from Niger, Mali, and Burkina Faso, while violence in South Sudan raised fears of returning to civil war. The geopolitical landscape remained tense, affecting global markets and regional stability, with varying international responses to the U.S.-Iran situation.

  • SOUTHCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that several military and geopolitical tensions intensified. The Brazilian National Team resumed training in Toluca, Mexico, ahead of a friendly match against Venezuela, while the ongoing U.S.-Iran conflict raised questions about regional stability and potential ripple effects across international sports and diplomatic relations.

  • EUCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that the Kremlin asserted its interest in continuing peace talks with Ukraine, emphasizing the importance of dialogue. French President Emmanuel Macron announced plans to expand France’s nuclear arsenal, allowing a temporary deployment of nuclear-armed aircraft to European allies, indicating a shift towards a distinct nuclear-security relationship outside NATO arrangements. Ukrainian Defense Minister Mikhail Fedorov revealed efforts to recruit foreign fighters to address a manpower crisis amid ongoing conflict in Ukraine. Meanwhile, hundreds of thousands of German parts were discovered in Russian drones deployed against Ukraine, raising concerns over sanctions effectiveness. The United States and Israel intensified military actions against Iran which prompted discussions about possible ramifications for Russia's stance in the Ukraine conflict. German Chancellor Friedrich Merz traveled to Washington for crisis talks with President Donald Trump amidst this geopolitical escalation. Additionally, potential U.S.-brokered talks between Russia and Ukraine, initially set for Abu Dhabi, could shift venues to Turkey or Switzerland due to the instability in the region.

  • PACOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated significant military and geopolitical developments. Asian countries, particularly Japan, faced heightened vulnerability to energy supply challenges. In the South China Sea, provocation arose from a close encounter between a Philippine vessel and a China Coast Guard ship, reflecting ongoing maritime tensions in the region.

  • CENTCOM: During the reporting period, joint U.S.-Israeli forces conduct sustained airstrikes across Iran, targeting command centers in Tehran, air defense systems (over 200 destroyed), ballistic missile launchers (approximately 200 eliminated or damaged), weapons storage facilities, the Natanz nuclear site in Esfahan Province, and IRGC infrastructure, while establishing local air superiority over western Iran and Tehran to enable direct "stand-in" munitions drops. Israel simultaneously strikes Hezbollah positions in Beirut's southern suburbs and southern Lebanon, killing a Quds Force commander responsible for Lebanese operations and targeting command centers, resulting in dozens of deaths and displacement of over 30,000 in Lebanon. Iran launches multiple ballistic missile and drone barrages—exceeding several waves per day—targeting Israel (with interceptions in Tel Aviv, Jerusalem, Haifa, and other areas causing civilian casualties including deaths from direct hits on residential buildings and shrapnel injuries) and U.S. military facilities or allied sites in Gulf states (Bahrain's Fifth Fleet headquarters hit, impacts near ports/airports in UAE, Qatar, Kuwait, Saudi Arabia, Oman, and others, disrupting oil shipments, international travel, and causing civilian damage to residential towers, hotels, and infrastructure across all GCC members). Iran-backed Iraqi militias and Hezbollah contribute with additional rocket, drone, and missile attacks on northern Israel and U.S. positions in Iraq. Defensive intercepts by Gulf states and Israel limit many impacts, but confirmed casualties include U.S. service members at regional bases and civilian deaths/injuries in Israel and Gulf locations; no major new nuclear facility strikes beyond Natanz occur, though operations prioritize degrading remaining missile and leadership capabilities amid Iran's vows of continued ferocious retaliation.

  • AFRICOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that tensions escalated in West Africa as the Economic Community of West African States (ECOWAS) deployed thousands of soldiers amid strained relations with neighboring Niger, Mali, and Burkina Faso, complicating efforts against armed groups. In South Sudan, ongoing violence resulted in the deaths of at least 169 people following insurgent attacks in the Ruweng area, raising concerns about the fragile peace established in 2018 after a five-year civil war. Additionally, intensifying attacks resulted in the United Nations warning that the country could be on the brink of returning to civil war, with children, elderly, and women among the casualties.
