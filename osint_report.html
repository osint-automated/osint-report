Intent: The intent of this report is to provide a high-level overview of significant military and geopolitical developments during the reporting period. The report consists of open-source reporting and updates on evolving conflicts, diplomatic initiatives, and strategic military actions involving countries of interest in the following Combatant Commands: SOUTHCOM, EUCOM, PACOM, CENTCOM, AFRICOM.

Executive Summary: During the reporting period, Colombian authorities confirmed 69 fatalities from the crash of a Hercules C-130 military transport aircraft near the Peru border on 26 March, while a military convoy faced an improvised explosive device assault in Norte de Santander. United States forces initiated Operation Southern Seas, involving multinational naval maneuvers with 10 Latin American nations to enhance counter-narcotics operations. In Ukraine, Russian forces executed 153 drone strikes, injuring civilians and disrupting infrastructure, while Ukrainian forces liberated key settlements and targeted Russian oil terminals. In the Middle East, Israel launched airstrikes in Tehran, killing key Iranian military personnel, as tensions escalated with Iran retaliating against U.S. assets. Chinese warnings to the U.S. over military construction in the Philippines coincided with intensified surveillance operations in the Pacific, while regional conflicts in eastern Democratic Republic of Congo and the ongoing violence in Sudan and Niger further heightened geopolitical instability.

  • SOUTHCOM: During the reporting period, Colombian authorities elevated the confirmed fatality count to 69 following the crash of a Hercules C-130 military transport aircraft shortly after takeoff near the Peru border on 26 March, while unidentified perpetrators executed an improvised explosive device assault against a military convoy transiting from Sardinata to Cúcuta in Norte de Santander department on 26 March, targeting personnel in multiple vehicles along the western ring road. United States forces commenced multinational naval maneuvers under Operation Southern Seas, deploying the USS Nimitz carrier alongside contingents from 10 Latin American nations including Colombia, Peru, Chile, and Ecuador, to enhance interoperability and counter-narcotics capabilities in southern maritime domains as announced on 26 March. In Venezuela, former leader Nicolás Maduro appeared before a New York federal court on 26 March, where prosecutors presented arguments alleging resource plundering amid ongoing geopolitical tensions over sanctions and regime transitions.

  • EUCOM: During the reporting period, Russian forces executed 153 drone strikes targeting infrastructure across multiple Ukrainian oblasts, including Kharkiv where attacks injured nine civilians, Dnipro where fires erupted, and Mykolaiv where power outages occurred, while Ukrainian air defenses intercepted 56 of 117 incoming drones; Ukrainian forces conducted drone strikes on Russian oil terminals at Ust-Luga and Primorsk in Leningrad Oblast overnight on 27 March, marking the third consecutive night of attacks alongside prior hits on the Kirishi refinery that halted shipments at affected ports and disrupted approximately 40 percent of Russia's oil export capacity. Russian troops advanced in the Slovyansk direction toward Riznykivka and Kalenyky, infiltrated positions in Kupyansk, and progressed west of Pokrovsk, whereas Ukrainian forces liberated Berezove southeast of Oleksandrivka in Zaporizhia Oblast, regained terrain in the Oleksandrivka sector liberating nine settlements across Dnipropetrovsk and Zaporizhia oblasts, and advanced in northern Kharkiv near Vovchansk and western Zaporizhia near Prymorske, Stepnohirsk, and Richne. Ukrainian drones struck a Russian shadow fleet tanker carrying 140,000 tons of crude oil in the Black Sea near the Bosphorus Strait. President Zelenskyy visited Saudi Arabia on 26 March to offer Ukrainian drone expertise and secure support amid stalled trilateral negotiations where Russia demanded SWIFT reconnection for a Black Sea truce and Ukraine rejected territorial withdrawals from Donbass for U.S. guarantees; both sides accused each other of violating energy infrastructure moratoriums, France pledged two billion euros in additional military aid, and U.S. officials weighed diverting aid to the Middle East while allocating 25 million dollars for rehabilitating abducted Ukrainian children.

  • PACOM: During the reporting period, China issued stern warnings to the United States against constructing an ammunition storage facility in the Philippines on 26 March, citing risks of escalation in the South China Sea. Beijing's research vessels intensified deep-sea mapping and surveillance operations across the Pacific Ocean, deploying hidden sensor networks to monitor submarine warfare environments on 26 March. State media analyzed South China Sea sovereignty disputes, suggesting potential diplomatic resets with Manila amid ongoing territorial frictions on 26 March. Chinese maritime militia vessels positioned near Second Thomas Shoal harassed Philippine resupply missions, prompting Manila to formalize a military logistics pact with France on 27 March.

  • CENTCOM: During the reporting period, Israel executed airstrikes on central Tehran infrastructure and eliminated Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps Navy Commander Alireza Tangsiri, who directed the Strait of Hormuz blockade, while United States and Israeli forces degraded Iranian naval assets and reduced missile stockpiles by over ninety percent. Iran launched ballistic missile salvos at Israeli territory, inflicted retaliatory strikes on Gulf state positions hosting United States assets, and conducted attacks injuring nine in Israel alongside two fatalities in Abu Dhabi. United States President Donald Trump extended the deadline for Iran to fully reopen the Strait of Hormuz to 6 April, postponed strikes on Iranian energy facilities by ten days, and claimed Tehran desperately sought a deal despite Iranian rejection of a fifteen-point United States proposal and issuance of a counterproposal via Pakistani mediation. A joint United States-Israeli airstrike targeted residential sites in Qom, killing six civilians, as Tehran dismissed ceasefire overtures and hardened its negotiating stance through intermediaries including Egypt and Turkey.

  • AFRICOM: During the reporting period, United Nations officials warned the Security Council that conflict escalated in eastern Democratic Republic of Congo with deployment of heavy weapons and drones; Sudanese army forces clashed with Founding Forces in Blue Nile region on 25 and 26 March, displacing 73,000 civilians; Chadian President Mahamat Idriss Deby authorised military retaliation against Sudanese actors following a deadly drone strike at a border funeral; armed groups ambushed Nigerian troops in Kebbi State, killing nine soldiers; Jama'at Nusrat al-Islam wal-Muslimin executed attacks in north-central Burkina Faso; geopolitical tensions intensified between Rwanda and Democratic Republic of Congo after an incident involving the DRC First Lady at a United States hotel; Rapid Support Forces advanced into North Darfur; and Ugandan forces alongside March 23 Movement withdrew from Ituri province in Democratic Republic of Congo.
