Intent: The intent of this report is to provide a high-level overview of significant military and geopolitical developments during the reporting period. The report consists of open-source reporting and updates on evolving conflicts, diplomatic initiatives, and strategic military actions involving countries of interest in the following Combatant Commands: SOUTHCOM, EUCOM, PACOM, CENTCOM, AFRICOM.

Executive Summary: During the reporting period, the United States intensified its military actions against Venezuela, pursuing sanctioned oil tankers in the Caribbean to disrupt its oil industry, prompting Venezuelan officials to threaten international legal action. A Russian general was killed in Moscow, raising suspicions of Ukrainian involvement, while NATO reported concerns about Russia developing weapons to target Starlink satellites. In the Middle East, Israeli military operations escalated, with strikes in Lebanon and the approval of new settlements in the occupied West Bank, amid warnings of potential Iranian missile attacks. Additionally, Turkey's diplomatic and military visits to Syria highlighted pressing integration issues concerning Kurdish forces. In the geopolitical landscape, Iran expanded its naval presence to South Africa, and Nigeria celebrated the release of schoolchildren from a mass kidnapping.

  • SOUTHCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that the United States intensified its military engagement with Venezuela by pursuing a third sanctioned oil tanker in the Caribbean Sea. This development followed the seizure of a second oil tanker as part of President Donald Trump's blockade aimed at crippling Venezuela's vital oil industry. U.S. Coast Guard officials confirmed the active pursuit as part of Washington's pressure campaign against Venezuelan President Nicolás Maduro. Venezuelan officials condemned these actions, labeling them as "theft" and "hijacking," and pledged to bring their grievances to the United Nations. Tensions continued to escalate with accusations of piracy arising from the U.S. tactics, while economic implications saw oil prices fluctuate amid concerns over supply disruptions. Lawmakers, including some from Trump's own party, expressed concerns that these maritime confrontations could be seen as provocations potentially leading to war. Meanwhile, talks between U.S., European, and Ukrainian officials in Florida focused on diplomatic strategies to end ongoing conflicts in Ukraine amidst these rising geopolitical tensions.

  • EUCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that a Russian general, Fanil Sarvarov, was killed on 24 October in Moscow by a bomb planted under his car. Investigators from Russia's Investigative Committee pursued various leads, including a potential link to Ukrainian special forces. In diplomatic developments, discussions occurred in Miami between U.S. officials and Ukrainian negotiators, with attendees describing the talks as productive despite no significant breakthroughs. Additionally, Vice President JD Vance remarked on the increasing acknowledgment within Ukraine that it may not secure victory in the Donbass region. Meanwhile, NATO intelligence services reported suspicions that Russia is developing a new weapon designed to target Starlink satellites, further complicating the conflict's technological landscape. Lastly, Russian officials condemned claims of expansive ambitions, asserting such narratives as propaganda used to justify military escalation.

  • PACOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated significant military and geopolitical developments. The United States escalated its maritime operations in the Caribbean, boarding several oil tankers as part of an intensified blockade against Venezuela, which contributed to a rise in oil prices with Brent crude reaching around $61 a barrel. Concurrently, President Donald Trump’s administration reshaped the deep-sea mining industry, facing opposition from various global entities. Additionally, Pakistan secured a $4 billion military equipment deal with the Libyan National Army, marking one of its largest arms sales. On the European front, French President Emmanuel Macron announced plans to replace the aging Charles de Gaulle aircraft carrier with a more modern nuclear-powered vessel. In the Pacific, CNOOC achieved a significant production milestone in the South China Sea with a new oilfield development, while Russian liquefied natural gas exports to China reached a record high in November, further illustrating shifting energy dynamics.

  • CENTCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that Turkey’s top diplomat and military officials visited Syria on 18 December as a deadline approached for the integration of Kurdish forces. In the context of escalating tensions in the Middle East, Israeli officials warned the United States that missile exercises by Iran’s Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps (IRGC) could signal preparations for a surprise attack. On the ground, Israel intensified its military operations, striking targets in Lebanon and demolishing residential buildings in East Jerusalem, which displaced numerous Palestinians. Meanwhile, a Russian general was killed in Moscow by an explosive device, with investigations suggesting Ukrainian involvement. Additionally, Israel approved the establishment of 19 new settlements in the occupied West Bank, facing significant international condemnation.

  • AFRICOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that Nigerian authorities confirmed the release of the last 130 schoolchildren abducted from a Catholic school on 21 November in Niger State, Nigeria, marking the conclusion of one of the country's largest mass kidnappings. In diplomatic developments, the Trump administration initiated a recall of nearly 30 career diplomats from various posts worldwide, including the ambassador to Nigeria, as part of its “America First” foreign policy approach. On the broader geopolitical front, Iran dispatched naval flotillas 103 and 104 to South Africa, indicating an expansion of its maritime presence beyond its traditional areas of operation. Additionally, Ghana's parliament legalized cryptocurrency trading, empowering its central bank to regulate the market, which aims to modernize financial practices and enhance innovation.
