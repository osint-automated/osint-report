Intent: The intent of this report is to provide a high-level overview of significant military and geopolitical developments during the reporting period. The report consists of open-source reporting and updates on evolving conflicts, diplomatic initiatives, and strategic military actions involving countries of interest in the following Combatant Commands: SOUTHCOM, EUCOM, PACOM, CENTCOM, AFRICOM.

Executive Summary: During the reporting period, significant military and geopolitical developments unfolded globally. Moscow threatened nuclear action against NATO states aiding Ukraine, while the U.S. increased military presence in the Caribbean amidst tensions with Venezuela, despite legal challenges over operations against drug trafficking. Russia escalated recruitment of Ukrainian saboteurs, and a deadly missile strike in Ternopil caused significant casualties, complicating the humanitarian situation. In the South China Sea, Malaysia ramped up maritime patrols in response to regional island-building by Vietnam and China. Concurrently, the U.K. prepared military responses to threats posed by Russian naval actions. In the Middle East, airstrikes resumed in Gaza amid ongoing violence, while diplomatic tensions rose over proposed legislation impacting Palestine. Additionally, international cooperation efforts materialized with a joint U.S.-Saudi declaration on nuclear initiatives and Germany's contribution to Brazil's environmental funds.

  • SOUTHCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that Moscow threatened to launch nuclear warheads against NATO states providing assistance to Ukraine, escalating tensions in global military dynamics. The U.S. military built up forces in the Caribbean amid increasing pressure on Venezuela, leading to strikes against alleged drug-smuggling boats. However, legal concerns arose within the U.S. Southern Command, where the senior military lawyer challenged the legality of these operations but was overruled by higher authorities. This military engagement followed an ongoing trend of U.S. involvement in various conflicts, transitioning from Afghanistan to escalating situations in Ukraine, Gaza, and Iran. Moreover, Venezuelan migrants faced difficulties while seeking refuge in Mexico, supported by the UNHCR and the Irish embassy in Mexico City.

  • EUCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that Russia significantly escalated its recruitment efforts for Ukrainian saboteurs via Telegram, targeting children and teenagers to conduct sabotage and bombings within Ukraine, as reported by the Ukrainian government. Additionally, a deadly Russian attack on two nine-story apartment buildings in Ternopil, Ukraine, resulted in at least 25 deaths, further intensifying the humanitarian crisis. The geopolitical landscape grew more complex as President Donald Trump reportedly approved a controversial 28-point peace plan demanding significant territorial concessions from Ukraine, which caught U.S. congressional supporters of Ukraine off guard. Meanwhile, tensions between Russia and NATO heightened, with the UK accusing a Russian spy ship of endangering pilots by pointing lasers at them, prompting discussions of military responses, while the EU unveiled plans for a military mobility area to enhance troop movements near Russia's border.

  • PACOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that Malaysia intensified maritime patrols and enforcement operations in the South China Sea, affirming its stance on sovereignty amidst ongoing island-building activities by Vietnam and China. In the United Kingdom, Defence Secretary John Healey condemned actions by the Russian spy ship Yantar for allegedly pointing lasers at pilots observing the vessel, stating that Britain was prepared to respond. Meanwhile, the Philippines sentenced a local mayor, identified as a 'Chinese spy,' to life imprisonment after authorities discovered one of the country's largest scam operations in her town of Bamban.

  • CENTCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that the U.K. Covid inquiry prepared to publish its second set of findings detailing the political decisions made throughout 2020 regarding the Covid response. In Israel, air strikes killed four individuals in Gaza, as Hamas reported that over 300 people died in the region since the ceasefire was established. The Knesset advanced a bill to block the United Nations Relief and Works Agency's access to utilities and reclaim Israeli land. Meanwhile, Ireland moved forward with a bill banning goods and services from Judea, Samaria, and eastern Jerusalem, which drew warnings from U.S. and Jewish leaders about potential diplomatic consequences. Additionally, U.S. and Saudi officials signed a joint declaration for civilian nuclear cooperation, moving forward with Saudi Arabia's nuclear ambitions.
