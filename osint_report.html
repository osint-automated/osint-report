Intent: The intent of this report is to provide a high-level overview of significant military and geopolitical developments during the reporting period. The report consists of open-source reporting and updates on evolving conflicts, diplomatic initiatives, and strategic military actions involving countries of interest in the following Combatant Commands: SOUTHCOM, EUCOM, PACOM, CENTCOM, AFRICOM.

Executive Summary: During the reporting period, Ecuadorian President Daniel Noboa initiated a large-scale U.S.-supported military offensive against narcotrafficking gangs along the coastal provinces, mobilizing approximately 75,000 personnel. In Ukraine, Russian forces engaged in 152 combat encounters and captured key settlements, while Ukrainian defenders repelled assaults, inflicting notable personnel losses on Russian troops. The conflict dynamics shifted as Ukraine achieved territorial recaptures, disrupting Russian offensives. The People's Liberation Army Air Force resumed provocative sorties near Taiwan, enhancing their gray-zone operations amidst fluctuating geopolitical tensions. In the Middle East, Israel escalated military operations against Hezbollah and Iranian targets, prompting retaliatory strikes from Iran towards U.S. interests, including a significant missile launch towards Israel. Concurrently, Nigeria faced bombings in Maiduguri attributed to insurgent activity, and South Africa deployed troops to combat gang violence, underscoring regional security challenges.

  • SOUTHCOM: During the reporting period, Ecuadorian President Daniel Noboa launched the first full day of a U.S.-backed military offensive and nighttime curfew operation across four coastal provinces—Guayas, Los Ríos, El Oro, and Santo Domingo de los Tsáchilas—targeting narcotrafficking gangs such as Los Choneros and Los Lobos under the Shield of the Americas framework. Ecuadorian forces deployed extensively, with government sources claiming 75,000 personnel mobilized nationwide, while reports confirmed explosions in Guayaquil on 16 March amid heightened security measures including checkpoints. U.S. Southern Command provided intelligence, logistics, and advisory support to Ecuadorian units executing raids, marking the doctrine's inaugural sustained kinetic application post-Doral summit. No additional geopolitical tensions, border incidents, coups, or military engagements transpired in Central or South America.

  • EUCOM: During the reporting period, Russian forces conducted 152 combat encounters across multiple fronts, including assaults on Petropavlivka, Kurilivka, and Kupianska in the Kupiansk direction, Drobishevo and Lyman in the Lyman direction, and Bilitske, Rodinske, and Novooleksandrivka in the Pokrovsk direction, while Ukrainian defenders repelled nine assaults in the Lyman sector and inflicted losses of 56 Russian personnel killed and 26 wounded near Pokrovsk on 16 March. Chief of the Russian General Staff Valery Gerasimov announced that Russian troops seized 12 settlements in eastern and southern Ukraine during the first two weeks of March, advanced toward Sloviansk, established control over 60 percent of Kostiantynivka amid ongoing street battles, progressed westward in Zaporizhia Oblast, and created buffer zones along the Kharkiv and Sumy borders, as reported on 16 March. Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelenskiy declared that Ukrainian armed forces disrupted Russia's planned March offensive, recaptured approximately 400 square kilometres and eight settlements in Zaporizhia, and constrained Moscow's reinforcement efforts, while the DeepState analytical project confirmed Russian breakthroughs near Sloviansk and Kostiantynivka. Russian air defences intercepted 250 Ukrainian drones approaching Moscow over the weekend, constituting the largest such incursion in a year according to Moscow Mayor Sergei Sobyanin, who reported no casualties following the action on 16 March. Kremlin spokesman Dmitry Peskov dismissed a Financial Times report alleging the stagnation of trilateral peace talks due to U.S. President Donald Trump's focus on Iran, affirmed Russia's continued interest in negotiations, and noted the absence of a confirmed venue or date for the next round on 16 March.

  • PACOM: During the reporting period, People's Liberation Army Air Force units executed 26 sorties proximate to Taiwan until 0600 hours Taipei time on 16 March, with 16 aircraft penetrating Taiwan's northern, central, and southwestern Air Defense Identification Zones. People's Liberation Army Navy vessels simultaneously operated seven surface combatants around the island, prompting Republic of China Armed Forces to deploy fighter interceptors, naval assets, and coastal missile systems for monitoring and response. This activity marked resumption of gray-zone coercion following a 16-day lull in incursions since late February, amid President Trump's postponement of a summit with CCP General Secretary Xi Jinping due to the Iran conflict. United States Indo-Pacific Command sustained two carrier strike groups in the western Pacific, while Pentagon analysts assessed low probability of Chinese exploitation of U.S. distractions for Taiwan contingencies.

  • CENTCOM: During the reporting period, Israel expanded its ground offensive in southern Lebanon against Iran-backed Hezbollah forces, launched fresh airstrikes on Tehran and Beirut targeting government infrastructure, and initiated new waves of attacks across multiple Iranian provinces including Bushehr. Iranian forces conducted missile and drone strikes on Gulf states' oil and transport infrastructure, U.S. assets, and military bases in the UAE and Iraq, with drones striking the U.S. embassy in Baghdad and prompting temporary UAE airspace closure on 16 March before resumption amid explosions in Dubai. Iran claimed its first wartime launch of a Sejjil ballistic missile against Israel and deployed Russian-produced Shahed drones targeting U.S. positions. U.S. President Trump criticised allies including China, France, Japan, South Korea, and the UK for rejecting requests to secure the Strait of Hormuz, urged their rapid naval involvement amid halted tanker traffic, and received prior warnings of Iranian retaliation against Gulf partners on 17 March.

  • AFRICOM: During the reporting period, insurgents detonated multiple bombs across Maiduguri, Borno State capital of Nigeria, targeting the post office in the city centre, Monday Market, University of Maiduguri Teaching Hospital, and Kaleri neighbourhood on 16 March, resulting in several civilian fatalities and injuries as emergency teams cordoned off affected areas. Nigerian joint security forces, comprising FAAN, NAF, Army, Police, and Amotekun, arrested bandits who pursued farmers onto Akure Airport runway on 16 March. South African soldiers deployed to Johannesburg to counter gang violence and organised crime. Kenyan and Russian authorities confirmed an agreement prohibiting recruitment of Kenyans for the Ukraine conflict on 16 March.
