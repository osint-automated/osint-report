Reporting Period: 06 December - 07 December 2025

Distribution: Approved for public release; distribution is unlimited.

Combatant Commands: SOUTHCOM, EUCOM, PACOM, CENTCOM, AFRICOM

Intent: The intent of this report is to provide a high-level overview of significant military and geopolitical developments during the reporting period. The report consists of open-source reporting and updates on evolving conflicts, diplomatic initiatives, and strategic military actions involving countries of interest in the following Combatant Commands: SOUTHCOM, EUCOM, PACOM, CENTCOM, AFRICOM.

Executive Summary: During the reporting period, significant military and geopolitical developments emerged across various regions. In South America, the U.S. military conducted strikes against drug traffickers near Suriname, while Argentina received its first six F-16 fighter jets from Denmark. Notably, NATO heightened its readiness in response to Russian missile strikes on Ukraine, as ongoing discussions sought to establish a security framework to address the conflict. In the Middle East, Hezbollah attacked UN peacekeepers in Lebanon amid direct Israel-Lebanon talks mediated by the U.S. Canada and the U.S. removed Syria from their lists of state sponsors of terrorism. In Africa, the influx of Sudanese refugees into Chad strained resources, while Nigeria faced threats against Christians. Overall, these developments reflected a shifting geopolitical landscape characterized by emerging alliances and rising tensions.

  • SOUTHCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated significant military and geopolitical developments. On 6 December, FIFA President Gianni Infantino awarded President Donald Trump a newly established Peace Prize, generating controversy regarding FIFA's neutrality amid ongoing military tensions. Additionally, reports confirmed the U.S. military executed strikes against suspected drug traffickers near Suriname in September, with ongoing congressional inquiries seeking clarification on the operations and their legal justifications. Argentina received its first six F-16 fighter jets from Denmark, marking a resurgence of their air combat capabilities. Moreover, the Trump administration expanded its travel ban to include over 30 countries, citing national security concerns, amidst an ICE crackdown on the Somali community in Minnesota, seen as racially motivated. Political dynamics in South America intensified as a far-right candidate emerged, potentially aligning with Trump against socialist-led Venezuela, while Venezuelan opposition leader Maria Corina Machado prepared to accept her Nobel Peace Prize in Oslo, despite living in hiding.

  • EUCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that NATO scrambled fighter jets and put air defense systems on alert in Poland in response to extensive missile and drone strikes launched by Russia against Ukraine. Simultaneously, U.S. and Ukrainian officials continued discussions in Miami to develop a security framework aimed at ending the ongoing conflict. Russian President Vladimir Putin ruled out any reentry into the G7, signaling a shift towards the BRICS alliance, while European leaders expressed growing concern over perceived shifts in U.S. policy toward Ukraine. The International Atomic Energy Agency reported that drone strikes attributed to Russia had compromised the protective shield at the Chornobyl nuclear plant, raising safety alarms. Furthermore, Russian forces conducted significant attacks targeting Ukraine's military and energy infrastructure, resulting in widespread blackouts across the region.

  • PACOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that a drone strike by Ukraine on an oil-loading facility near Russia's Black Sea coast disrupted Kazakh oil exports, highlighting Kazakhstan's vulnerability due to its dependence on a single pipeline for oil shipments. Reports detailed heightened tensions across Ukraine as the entire nation went under air raid alert for potential Russian missile attacks on 24 October, although the alerts were subsequently lifted. Meanwhile, oil prices surged to a two-week high driven by expectations of U.S. Federal Reserve rate cuts that could bolster economic growth and energy demand, alongside ongoing geopolitical tensions involving Russia and Venezuela. Additionally, criticisms arose within India directed at Prime Minister Modi as the U.S. softened its stance on Pakistan, reflecting a notable shift in the U.S. National Security Strategy as compared to previous years.

  • CENTCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu sought a pardon amid rising opposition and corruption charges. Hezbollah gunmen attacked UN peacekeepers in southern Lebanon, prompting the Lebanese army to arrest six suspects. Concurrently, Israel and Lebanon conducted historic direct talks under U.S. mediation, the first face-to-face diplomatic engagement in over four decades. Canada removed Syria from its list of state sponsors of terrorism, following a similar move by the United States and acknowledging Syrian government efforts toward stability. In relation to the ongoing Gaza conflict, Qatari Prime Minister Sheikh Mohammed bin Abdulrahman Al Thani remarked that ceasefire negotiations were at a critical juncture, while Arab and Muslim nations rejected an Israeli proposal for a one-way exit from Gaza at the Rafah crossing. Germany deployed Israel's Arrow 3 missile defense system as part of its military modernization effort in response to threats posed by Russia.

  • AFRICOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that military and geopolitical tensions escalated in various regions. In Chad, the influx of Sudanese refugees strained resources and infrastructure as millions fled Sudan's civil war, prompting a humanitarian emergency. The Rapid Support Forces (RSF) accused Sudan's army of attacking a vital border crossing near Chad, exacerbating the crisis. In Nigeria, warnings emerged of planned attacks against Christians on Christmas Day, amid ongoing violence linked to armed gangs. Meanwhile, Nigeria's Foreign Minister expressed hope for the country's full membership in the BRICS association in coming years, signaling a potential shift in geopolitical alliances. In South Africa, a mass shooting at a bar in Pretoria resulted in at least 11 deaths, including three children, as police launched an investigation into the incident. Additionally, skepticism emerged over a recently brokered peace deal between Rwanda and the Democratic Republic of Congo, underlining the region's complex security dynamics.
