Reporting Period: 09 November - 10 November 2025

Distribution: Approved for public release; distribution is unlimited.

Combatant Commands: SOUTHCOM, EUCOM, PACOM, CENTCOM, AFRICOM

Intent: The intent of this report is to provide a high-level overview of significant military and geopolitical developments during the reporting period. The report consists of open-source reporting and updates on evolving conflicts, diplomatic initiatives, and strategic military actions involving countries of interest in the following Combatant Commands: SOUTHCOM, EUCOM, PACOM, CENTCOM, AFRICOM.

Executive Summary: During the reporting period, key military and geopolitical developments included heightened tensions in Southern Lebanon, where Israeli airstrikes against Hezbollah occurred, complicating the regional conflict, while Turkey issued arrest warrants for Israeli officials related to alleged atrocities against Palestinians. In Ukraine, drone strikes targeted Russian infrastructure following Russian attacks on residential areas, prompting calls for increased Western support for Ukraine's energy sector. In the South China Sea, China's military criticized Philippine-U.S. relations, coinciding with the commissioning of China's new aircraft carrier. In Latin America, U.S. immigration policies influenced migration patterns, and mass defections among Venezuelan boat crews highlighted narco-terrorism concerns. In the Middle East, U.S. military presence in Syria increased amid ongoing Israeli-Hamas violence, culminating in a high death toll in Gaza. Meanwhile, Sudan experienced military tensions with drone interceptions, and military developments continued in South Korea and Turkey's engagement in humanitarian initiatives for Gaza.

  • SOUTHCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated a significant migration shift in Latin America, where thousands of individuals redirected their journeys southward in response to the immigration policies enacted by former President Donald Trump. Additionally, a report from the Associated Press confirmed that U.S. airstrikes targeting vessels labeled as drug runners did not involve any shipments of fentanyl to the United States, casting doubt on the Trump Administration's assertions regarding these operations. The United Nations chief condemned U.S. airstrikes in the Caribbean and Pacific, labeling them as violations of international humanitarian law. Tensions escalated in southern Lebanon as fighting resumed between Israel and Hezbollah, threatening to alter the region's power dynamics. Meanwhile, reports highlighted mass defection among Venezuelan boat crews facing accusations of narco-terrorism, revealing a complex reality that involved individuals from diverse backgrounds struggling economically. In Oklahoma, U.S. Immigration and Customs Enforcement conducted a sting operation that led to the arrest of 34 illegal alien truck drivers, raising concerns about inadequate English proficiency and the implications for safety regulations in the transportation sector.

  • EUCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that Ukrainian forces executed drone and missile strikes disrupting power and heating in several regions of Russia, including Belgorod, affecting major cities near the Ukrainian border. These actions followed a significant wave of Russian drone strikes targeting residential buildings in Eastern Ukraine, killing three individuals and injuring over a dozen others. Meanwhile, the U.S. military condemned North Korea's recent ballistic missile launch, asserting it posed no immediate threat but reaffirming consultation with allies. Russian Foreign Minister Sergey Lavrov stated that dialogue between Washington and Moscow on bilateral issues was ongoing but progressing slowly. Concurrently, Ukraine struggled to restore its energy infrastructure after Russian attacks had reduced its power generation capacity to zero, prompting Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelenskyy to call for increased support from Western allies. In Belgium, the UK military deployed personnel and equipment to assist following drone sightings near airports, amidst discussions regarding NATO’s response to the ongoing conflict.

  • PACOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that tensions in the South China Sea escalated as China accused the Philippines of collaborating with outside powers, including the United States and Canada, to provoke regional instability. China's Defense Ministry spokesperson Jiang Bin condemned these actions, while a military spokesperson reiterated demands for the Philippines to cease provocations. In naval developments, China's first aircraft carrier equipped with electromagnetic catapults, the Fujian, was commissioned in Sanya, Hainan Province, with the PLA Navy asserting that its modernization efforts did not target any specific country. Additionally, South Korea advanced its military capabilities following U.S. approval to develop nuclear-powered submarines, amidst its ongoing conflict with nuclear-armed North Korea. Meanwhile, the Philippines faced a humanitarian crisis as super typhoon Fung-wong approached, prompting the evacuation of over a million residents just days after damaging Typhoon Kalmaegi.

  • CENTCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that tensions escalated in the Middle East, with Israeli Defense Forces (IDF) intensifying strikes against Hezbollah positions in southern Lebanon, violating UN Resolution 1701. The Lebanese President Joseph Aoun condemned these actions as “fully fledged crimes.” In retaliation, Hezbollah vowed to resist, further complicating the fragile truce. Concurrently, Turkey issued arrest warrants for Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu and 36 other senior officials, accusing them of genocide against Palestinians. Meanwhile, U.S. military presence in Syria increased as Syrian President Ahmed al-Sharaa arrived in Washington, D.C., for talks with President Donald Trump following the lifting of sanctions against him. On the ground in Gaza, the death toll surpassed 69,000 as both Israel and Hamas engaged in body exchanges under a tenuous ceasefire agreement, amid ongoing Israeli air raids and demolitions in the region.

  • AFRICOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that Guinea’s Supreme Court released a provisional list of presidential candidates for the upcoming December elections, marking a significant development in the West African nation's electoral politics. On 8 November, the Sudanese army intercepted a drone attack launched by the Rapid Support Forces on El-Obeid, a key southern city, highlighting ongoing military tensions in Sudan. In a move affecting international diplomacy, U.S. President Donald Trump announced that no U.S. officials would attend the G20 summit scheduled for South Africa later in November. Additionally, Alparslan Bayraktar, Turkey's Energy and Natural Resources Minister, confirmed the completion of the first phase of operations at a new gold field in Niger, representing a milestone in the country's efforts to expand its gold production abroad. Further reports indicated that Türkiye expressed its commitment to increasing humanitarian aid to Gaza amid ongoing regional tensions.
