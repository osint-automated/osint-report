Reporting Period: 12 November - 13 November 2025

Distribution: Approved for public release; distribution is unlimited.

Combatant Commands: SOUTHCOM, EUCOM, PACOM, CENTCOM, AFRICOM

Intent: The intent of this report is to provide a high-level overview of significant military and geopolitical developments during the reporting period. The report consists of open-source reporting and updates on evolving conflicts, diplomatic initiatives, and strategic military actions involving countries of interest in the following Combatant Commands: SOUTHCOM, EUCOM, PACOM, CENTCOM, AFRICOM.

Executive Summary: During the reporting period, significant military and geopolitical developments emerged across multiple regions. In South America, Venezuelan forces mobilized 200,000 troops in response to U.S. military actions, including airstrikes against drug trafficking boats that resulted in 76 casualties, prompting a suspension of intelligence sharing from the UK and Colombia. In Ukraine, Russian forces advanced in the Zaporizhzhia region, while intercepted attacks decreased significantly, and Ukraine's energy infrastructure faced renewed strikes ahead of winter. In Asia, China showcased its new carrier amid regional military developments, including Japan's proposed security triangle with Australia and the Philippines activating supersonic missiles. In the Middle East, Israel intensified operations against Hezbollah, experienced a soldier shortage, and faced internal violence while Iran's nuclear stockpile raised alarm. Lastly, the humanitarian crisis in Libya highlighted ongoing migrant tragedies, while instability in Sudan escalated demographic displacements.

  • SOUTHCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that Venezuelan military forces conducted a massive mobilization in response to the increased U.S. military presence in the Caribbean, particularly following the arrival of the USS Gerald R. Ford aircraft carrier. Concurrently, the U.S. military executed air strikes against vessels suspected of drug trafficking in Latin American waters, resulting in at least 76 casualties since September. This escalation drew criticism, prompting both the United Kingdom and Colombia to suspend intelligence sharing with the U.S. over concerns regarding the legality of the strikes. In Panama, authorities reported the seizure of 13 tons of cocaine bound for the U.S., marking one of the largest drug hauls in the country's waters. As tensions heightened, Venezuela announced large-scale military exercises involving around 200,000 troops, signaling its readiness amid rising fears of conflict. Meanwhile, the U.S. partially reopened a jungle warfare training site in Panama in line with its regional counter-narcotics efforts.

  • EUCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that Ukraine's interception rates of Russian drones and missiles declined, with approximately 20% of Russian drones and 45% of missiles remaining undefeated in October. The Russian army made significant advances, overrunning three settlements in the southern Zaporizhzhia region of Ukraine. Ukrainian forces reported heavy artillery fire and retreated under intense pressure from Russian attacks. Additionally, Ukraine unveiled its first 'drone wall' aimed at protecting critical infrastructure from Russian threats, such as glide bombs and Shahed-type drones. Amidst a corruption scandal that led to the resignation of several key Ukrainian ministers, the country faced renewed Russian strikes against its energy infrastructure, causing rolling blackouts ahead of winter. International dynamics shifted as Serbia's president warned of an inevitable direct conflict between the West and Russia, while Russia established unmanned systems forces and reported foiling an alleged Ukrainian-UK plot to hijack a missile-carrying military jet.

  • PACOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated significant military and geopolitical developments. China's new Fujian aircraft carrier, showcased by President Xi Jinping, underscored the nation's efforts to modernize its naval capabilities. In Asia, Japan's ambassador to Australia proposed an "equilateral" security triangle with the United States and Australia, emphasizing security collaboration. The Philippines activated its first battery of Indian-made supersonic cruise missiles, a move aimed at enhancing defense against threats in the South China Sea. Concurrently, Australian intelligence accused Chinese hackers of targeting critical infrastructure, indicating potential sabotage risks. In response to increasing tensions in Venezuela, the Trump administration put military resources on high alert as uncertainty regarding U.S. strategies in the region persisted.   • CENTCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that former German Chancellor Angela Merkel visited the Gaza Strip border in Israel on 24 October. The European Union and Indian navies took control of a ship off the coast of Somalia that was involved in piracy against a Malta-flagged tanker. The International Atomic Energy Agency (IAEA) reported concerns over Iran's uranium stockpile, emphasizing the urgency of inspections at nuclear facilities not targeted by Israeli attacks. In Israel, President Isaac Herzog condemned escalating settler violence in the West Bank as "shocking" and called for its cessation. Analysts noted that Israel intensified its military operations against Hezbollah in Lebanon, potentially preparing for further conflict. Meanwhile, the Israeli Defense Forces faced a critical soldier shortage as operations in Gaza and Lebanon persisted. Israel opened the Zikim Crossing in northern Gaza to facilitate humanitarian aid following increased scrutiny over aid access. In Iraq, preliminary election results showed traditional political powers maintaining influence, as US and Israeli pressures on Baghdad to confront Iran-backed militias increased. Finally, Lebanese Hezbollah reaffirmed its commitment to armed defense, rejecting disarmament demands from the US and Israel.

  • AFRICOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that at least 42 migrants were presumed dead after a boat capsized off the coast of Libya, with only seven individuals rescued following six days adrift, highlighting ongoing humanitarian crises in the region. Additionally, the European Union and Indian navies seized a ship off the coast of Somalia, which was being used by pirates to capture a Malta-flagged tanker, emphasizing the persistent security challenges in international waters. In Southern Africa, South Africa announced an investigation into 17 citizens who found themselves engaged as mercenaries in Ukraine, following distress signals sent by these individuals. Furthermore, the United Nations reported that Sudan was facing the world's largest displacement crisis, with over 10 million people forced to flee their homes, underscoring significant geopolitical instability in the region.
