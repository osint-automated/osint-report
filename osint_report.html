Date of Access: 22 March 2026

Date of Information: 22 March 2026

Reporting Period: 21 March - 22 March 2026

Distribution: Approved for public release; distribution is unlimited.

Combatant Commands: SOUTHCOM, EUCOM, PACOM, CENTCOM, AFRICOM

Intent: The intent of this report is to provide a high-level overview of significant military and geopolitical developments during the reporting period. The report consists of open-source reporting and updates on evolving conflicts, diplomatic initiatives, and strategic military actions involving countries of interest in the following Combatant Commands: SOUTHCOM, EUCOM, PACOM, CENTCOM, AFRICOM.

Executive Summary: During the reporting period, Russian forces executed mass drone strikes against Ukrainian energy infrastructure, resulting in power outages for over 400,000 subscribers, while Ukrainian forces launched one of their largest drone attacks targeting multiple Russian regions. Ground combat intensified in several areas of Ukraine, with reported personnel losses on both sides. In Taiwan, PLA aircraft and naval vessels engaged in provocative maneuvers, prompting monitoring by ROC Armed Forces. Concurrently, US and Israeli forces conducted precision strikes on Iranian military sites, eliciting a retaliatory response from Iran, which included missile strikes on Israel and threats against US assets in the region. Meanwhile, in Sudan, an airstrike on a hospital resulted in significant civilian casualties, and the US intelligence community identified Africa as a growing center of jihadist activity. Additionally, Nigeria and South Africa strengthened their defense cooperation amidst rising security challenges.

  • SOUTHCOM: During the reporting period, the Nuestra América flotilla delivered thousands of pounds of humanitarian aid, including medical supplies, to Havana, Cuba, on 21 March, navigating US blockade restrictions amid severe energy shortages and blackouts exceeding twenty hours daily in eastern provinces. Brazilian President Luiz Inácio Lula da Silva condemned renewed foreign interference and colonial ambitions targeting Latin American sovereignty during a Community of Latin American and Caribbean States summit in Colombia on 21 March. Colombian President Gustavo Petro accused Ecuadorian forces of conducting an airstrike within Colombian territory near the border, prompting troop reinforcements to the frontier zone. The US Embassy in Havana issued a demonstration alert for potential protests along the Malecón seawall on 21 March. Turkish defense firm Baykar showcased its AI-assisted K2 unmanned combat aerial vehicle to Latin American delegations on 21 March, signaling prospective regional military technology acquisitions. No kinetic military engagements, coups, or border incursions materialized across Central or South America.

  • EUCOM: During the reporting period, Russian forces conducted mass Geran drone strikes against Ukrainian energy infrastructure in Chernihiv Oblast, causing power outages for over 400,000 subscribers including Chernihiv city and Slavutych, while targeting a locomotive depot in Trostyanets and a substation near Mykolaiv; Ukrainian forces launched nearly 300 to over 400 drones in one of their largest attacks against multiple Russian regions including Moscow, Rostov, Kursk, Saratov, and Ufa, where Russian air defenses downed most with minor civilian infrastructure damage and two wounded. Russian Ministry of Defense reported Ukrainian Armed Forces suffered 1,310 personnel losses, while Ukrainian General Staff claimed Russian forces incurred 1,240 personnel losses, 39 artillery systems, and 1,781 UAVs destroyed on 20 March as reported 21 March. Russian ground forces advanced in sixteen sectors near Sumy and Glukhov, conducted battles for Kaleniky toward Rai-Aleksandrivka on Slavyansk axis prompting partial civilian evacuation from Slavyansk, and progressed near Hryshino and Belitske toward Dobropillia; positional fighting persisted near Volchansk, Kupyansk, Chasiv Yar, Fedorivka Druha, Hulyaipole, and Zaporizhia fronts including Prymorske, Stepnohirsk, and Rechne. Geopolitically, US and Ukrainian negotiators met on 21 March in Florida amid ongoing civilian deaths from Russian attacks including four killed in drone strikes, Russia declined participation in US-Ukraine talks scheduled to continue on 22 March, and reports emerged of Russia proposing to limit Iran support in exchange for halted US aid to Ukraine.

  • PACOM: During the reporting period, People's Liberation Army (PLA) aircraft executed two sorties around Taiwan on 21 March, with one aircraft crossing the median line into Taiwan's eastern air defense identification zone while eight People's Liberation Army Navy (PLAN) vessels operated in adjacent waters; Republic of China Armed Forces monitored these activities and responded accordingly. PLAN forces deployed six vessels and one official ship around Taiwan up to 0600 hours on 22 March, absent any detected PLA aircraft incursions. In the South China Sea, Chinese personnel launched flares at a Philippine Coast Guard aircraft carrying Rear Admiral Jay Tarriela during surveillance of ongoing land reclamation at Mischief Reef within the Philippines' exclusive economic zone on 21 March.

  • CENTCOM: During the reporting period, US and Israeli forces executed precision strikes on Iran's Natanz uranium-enrichment facility, ballistic missile production sites including an IRGC missile components complex near Tehran, Malek Ashtar University, and the Research Institute of Petroleum, while Israel separately targeted regime facilities in Tehran and Hezbollah positions in Beirut on 21 March. Iran retaliated with eight ballistic missile barrages against Israel, impacting Arad where emergency services reported 74 injuries including seven serious cases, Dimona with several casualties from a direct hit, and Rishon Lezion via cluster munitions damaging multiple sites. Iran additionally launched two long-range ballistic missiles at the US-UK base on Diego Garcia in the Indian Ocean, one of which the US intercepted while the other failed in flight. US President Donald Trump issued a 48-hour ultimatum on 21 March demanding Iran reopen the Strait of Hormuz or face obliteration of its power plants, as US Central Command confirmed degradation of Iranian threats there following prior strikes and announced deployment of thousands more Marines and warships to the Middle East. Israel's defense minister declared that US-Israeli strikes on Iran would intensify significantly that week, amid reports of Saudi Arabia and UAE intercepting Iranian drones and missiles near their territories.

  • AFRICOM: During the reporting period, the World Health Organization verified an airstrike on Al Deain Teaching Hospital in East Darfur, Sudan, which occurred late on 20 March and killed 64 civilians including 13 children, two nurses, and one doctor while injuring 89 others and rendering the facility non-functional; the United States intelligence community released its 2026 Annual Threat Assessment on 21 March, designating Africa as the epicenter of global jihadist expansion with intensified operations by al-Qaida and Islamic State affiliates in the Sahel, West Africa, and Somalia; Nigeria and South Africa expanded their defence pact through high-level meetings of the Bi-National Commission Defence and Security Committee on 21 March to enhance military cooperation and continental stability.
