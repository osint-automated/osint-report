Open Source Intelligence (OSINT) Report

Date of Access: 13 December 2025

Date of Information: 13 December 2025

Reporting Period: 12 December - 13 December 2025

Distribution: Approved for public release; distribution is unlimited.

Combatant Commands: SOUTHCOM, EUCOM, PACOM, CENTCOM, AFRICOM

Intent: The intent of this report is to provide a high-level overview of significant military and geopolitical developments during the reporting period. The report consists of open-source reporting and updates on evolving conflicts, diplomatic initiatives, and strategic military actions involving countries of interest in the following Combatant Commands: SOUTHCOM, EUCOM, PACOM, CENTCOM, AFRICOM.

Executive Summary: During the reporting period, significant military and geopolitical developments unfolded across multiple regions. In South America, the U.S. intensified actions against Venezuela, seizing an oil tanker and imposing sanctions on the Maduro regime amidst rising tensions. In Europe, Ukraine's President Zelenskyy announced aspirations for EU membership by January 2027, prompting a stern response from Russia, which reaffirmed its military presence in eastern Ukraine. Asian-Pacific tensions escalated as U.S. and Japanese defense leaders criticized China's military activities near Taiwan. In the Middle East, Israeli airstrikes targeted Hezbollah in Lebanon while humanitarian conditions in Gaza worsened due to severe flooding. In Africa, Benin thwarted a coup attempt, highlighting regional instability, while Eritrea faced rising tensions with Ethiopia and the UK imposed sanctions related to violence in Sudan.

  • SOUTHCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that the Danish intelligence agency warned of an increased military threat from the United States under President Trump, who reportedly sought to leverage economic power to assert U.S. influence globally. The Venezuelan government announced that the U.S. had unilaterally suspended deportation flights scheduled to arrive on 24 October, while the U.S. seized an oil tanker off the Venezuelan coast, with intentions to transport it to a U.S. port. Reports surfaced that this vessel had allegedly manipulated its location data to evade detection. In response to U.S. actions, the Trump administration imposed new sanctions on members of Venezuelan President Nicolás Maduro's family and targeted six oil tankers. Additionally, Adm. Alvin Holsey, who commanded U.S. military operations in the Caribbean, retired amid rising tensions, and Trump signaled forthcoming military strikes to intercept narcotics shipments from Venezuela. The U.S. actions intensified fears of a broader conflict as tensions escalated, with Russian support for Maduro reaffirmed amidst the U.S. military buildup.

  • EUCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelenskyy announced plans for Ukraine to join the European Union in January 2027 as part of a U.S. strategy to end the ongoing conflict with Russia. The Kremlin responded by stating that Russian police and National Guard forces would remain in eastern Ukraine's Donbas region, regardless of any peace agreements. Concurrently, the European Union prepared to indefinitely freeze hundreds of billions of euros in Russian assets to prevent Hungary and Slovakia from vetoing their allocation for Ukraine. Additionally, Russian authorities pursued legal action against Belgium's Euroclear in connection with efforts by the EU to utilize frozen Russian funds for Ukraine. Ukrainian troops reported significant advancements in the conflict, successfully retaking parts of Kupiansk, while NATO emphasized the need for preparedness against a potential large-scale conflict with Russia.

  • PACOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that tensions in the Asia-Pacific region heightened as U.S. Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth and Japan's Defense Minister agreed that China's recent military actions were "not conducive to regional peace." This discussion followed an incident where Chinese aircraft locked radar on Japanese jets near Taiwan. Concurrently, a Danish intelligence report warned that the U.S. under President Trump escalated its military assertions, threatening both allies and adversaries through economic intimidation. In Southeast Asia, experts pointed out the inherent power asymmetry complicating resolution efforts for South China Sea disputes. Lastly, the U.S. prepared to enhance pressure on Venezuelan President Nicolás Maduro by increasing seizures of oil tankers, following an initial intervention that aimed to curb Maduro's access to oil revenue.

  • CENTCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated significant military and geopolitical developments in the Middle East. Heavy rains exacerbated the humanitarian crisis in Gaza, where at least 12 Palestinians died or went missing due to flooding, as UN agencies noted inadequate aid access. In southern Lebanon, Israeli airstrikes targeted regions purportedly to pressure Hezbollah to disarm, while Lebanon's Foreign Minister warned of an impending major Israeli military operation. Iranian authorities arrested Nobel Peace Prize laureate Narges Mohammadi during a memorial in Mashhad, marking a continued crackdown on dissent. Meanwhile, the U.S. faced increasing diplomatic tension with Israel over its military actions in Syria, as stated by U.S. officials frustrated with the lack of progress in security talks. The UN General Assembly overwhelmingly backed a resolution urging Israel to cooperate with UNRWA for Gaza aid, which Israel rejected as biased. Satellite imagery revealed potential manipulation of data from a seized tanker off the coast of Venezuela, as the U.S. enacted stricter maritime policies targeting sanctioned vessels.

  • AFRICOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that Benin's government successfully thwarted a coup attempt with the assistance of loyalist troops and air support from Nigeria, marking a critical moment for the nation's democratic stability. Despite this intervention, regional concerns regarding the proliferation of coups in West Africa persisted. In Eritrea, tensions escalated with Ethiopia as Eritrea withdrew from the Intergovernmental Authority on Development (Igad), citing failures in the bloc's founding principles. Additionally, a report from Sudan accused the Rapid Support Forces (RSF) of systematic sexual violence during the ongoing civil war, significantly impacting the civilian population. The United Kingdom imposed sanctions on RSF commander Al-Fateh Abdullah Idris, following connections to violent acts against civilians. Meanwhile, countries in Central Africa exhibited some electoral progress and security improvements despite challenges from climate shocks and terrorism, as highlighted by a senior UN official addressing the Security Council.
