Open Source Intelligence (OSINT) Report

Date of Access: 05 November 2025

Date of Information: 05 November 2025

Reporting Period: 04 November - 05 November 2025

Distribution: Approved for public release; distribution is unlimited.

Combatant Commands: SOUTHCOM, EUCOM, PACOM, CENTCOM, AFRICOM

Intent: The intent of this report is to provide a high-level overview of significant military and geopolitical developments during the reporting period. The report consists of open-source reporting and updates on evolving conflicts, diplomatic initiatives, and strategic military actions involving countries of interest in the following Combatant Commands: SOUTHCOM, EUCOM, PACOM, CENTCOM, AFRICOM.

Executive Summary: During the reporting period, significant military and geopolitical developments unfolded across various regions. In South America, U.S. President Donald Trump stated on 31 October that airstrikes on Venezuela were not being considered, while the U.S. military targeted drug trafficking boats in the Caribbean and initiated upgrades on a naval base, signaling potential military escalations. In Europe, Ukrainian drones struck deep within Russia, marking an escalation in conflict, as Russia intensified its military efforts in strategic areas like Pokrovsk. In Asia, China warned the U.S. against crossing "red lines" regarding Taiwan, while South Korea advanced its nuclear submarine program in response to North Korean threats. In the Middle East, the situation in Gaza deteriorated with thousands of casualties amid ongoing Israeli airstrikes, as regional tensions escalated with Iran arming militias and U.S. diplomatic efforts aimed at de-escalation. Finally, in Africa, violence surged in Sudan, prompting U.S. evacuations from Mali and humanitarian concerns following recent conflicts.

  • SOUTHCOM: During the reporting period, President Donald Trump stated on 31 October that the United States was not considering airstrikes on Venezuela, amid concerns in Caracas regarding a possible military buildup aimed at regime change. Concurrently, the U.S. military announced the killing of three individuals in a strike against alleged drug trafficking boats in the Caribbean on 2 November, signaling ongoing operations in the region. Additionally, the Pentagon commenced upgrades on a Cold War-era naval base in the Caribbean, indicating preparations for increased military action near Venezuela. Meanwhile, the Iranian Foreign Ministry confirmed that while messages were exchanged between Iran and the U.S. through intermediaries, no formal negotiations had begun, amid continued tensions in the region related to Israel. These developments reflect a complex landscape of military engagement and geopolitical maneuvering, particularly in relation to Latin America and the Middle East.

  • EUCOM: During the reporting period, Ukrainian long-range drones struck an industrial plant approximately 1,300 kilometers (800 miles) inside Russia on 4 November, marking a significant escalation in Ukraine's drone capabilities as the conflict continued. Reports indicated that Ukraine aimed to intensify long-range attacks on key Russian infrastructure. Concurrently, Ukraine's military adapted its strategy by preparing to mass-produce interceptor drones to counter Russian Shahed drones. On the ground, Russian forces intensified their efforts to encircle Ukrainian troops in Pokrovsk, a city of strategic importance. Meanwhile, North Korea sent roughly 5,000 construction troops to Russia to assist with infrastructure reconstruction, according to South Korean intelligence. In the realm of international relations, China and Russia pledged to bolster their cooperation in various sectors, while Hungarian Prime Minister Viktor Orban publicly rejected any moral obligation for Hungary to support Ukraine financially amid the ongoing war.

  • PACOM: During the reporting period, significant military and geopolitical updates emerged globally. On 26 October, China urged the United States to avoid crossing defined "red lines" regarding Taiwan and human rights, as articulated by Chinese Ambassador to the U.S. Xie Feng. Meanwhile, Bangladesh's accession to the UN Water Convention raised concerns about potential tensions with India, creating a ripple effect across South Asia. In the Philippines, Typhoon Kalmaegi caused widespread devastation, resulting in at least 46 fatalities as it brought severe flooding across central regions, particularly affecting the island of Cebu. Additionally, South Korea intensified its military capabilities by advancing its nuclear submarine program in response to North Korea's nuclear threats. The defense ministers of the Philippines, Australia, Japan, and the United States expressed collective concern over China's destabilizing actions in the East China Sea and South China Sea. In a related development, a newly signed defense pact between the Philippines and Canada aimed at addressing China's military presence faced criticism for lacking substantial military significance.

  • CENTCOM: During the reporting period, significant geopolitical and military developments unfolded in the Middle East. The Gaza Strip saw a devastating escalation, with nearly 70,000 Palestinian casualties reported since October 2023, predominantly affecting women and children. The situation was exacerbated as Israel continued airstrikes despite a fragile ceasefire. On 3 November, Gaza's Health Ministry reported the transfer of 45 additional bodies from Israeli authorities, raising the total to 270. Concurrently, Israeli forces arrested a former top military lawyer after she resigned amid allegations related to a leaked video showing soldier misconduct towards a Palestinian detainee. Additionally, Iran actively armed Iraqi militias in anticipation of potential conflicts with Israel. Diplomatic tensions also rose, with U.S. Ambassador to Türkiye asserting that Türkiye and Israel would avoid war, while Iranian officials declared an ongoing "real regional war" with Israel. Further, the mobilization of Lebanese forces occurred near the Israeli border, following military movements by Israel. In a separate development, Hamas stated it had recovered the body of an Israeli soldier previously held captive and was facilitating its return.

  • AFRICOM: During the reporting period, tensions escalated in Sudan as the paramilitary Rapid Support Forces (RSF) intensified fighting in the oil-rich Kordofan area. Claims emerged of a drone strike on a funeral that killed numerous mourners, further highlighting the ongoing violence. Reports also indicated that RSF forces rounded up hundreds of men and boys near El-Fasher, contributing to humanitarian concerns. The U.S. Department of State ordered the evacuation of all non-emergency personnel and their families from Mali due to ongoing armed conflicts with al-Qaida-linked terrorists. In Nigeria, Foreign Minister Yusuf Taggar rejected claims made by former President Donald Trump regarding Christian persecution in the country, while the Nigerian government dismissed suggestions of a U.S. military intervention. Norway announced an additional contribution of USD 4.5 million in humanitarian assistance for Sudan, amidst worsening conditions as the UN chief called for an end to the violence.
