Intent: The intent of this report is to provide a high-level overview of significant military and geopolitical developments during the reporting period. The report consists of open-source reporting and updates on evolving conflicts, diplomatic initiatives, and strategic military actions involving countries of interest in the following Combatant Commands: SOUTHCOM, EUCOM, PACOM, CENTCOM, AFRICOM.

Executive Summary: During the reporting period, significant military and geopolitical developments occurred across multiple regions. In Mexico, the death of Jalisco New Generation Cartel leader Nemesio "El Mencho" on October 24, facilitated by U.S. intelligence, sparked violent retaliatory actions, prompting travel advisories and public safety concerns ahead of the FIFA World Cup. In Europe, the ongoing Russia-Ukraine conflict saw Ukrainian troops increasingly relying on drones, while President Zelenskyy confirmed a meeting with U.S. officials to outline a postwar recovery plan. Simultaneously, Hungary raised military readiness amid perceived threats from Ukraine. In Asia, Japan announced plans to deploy missile systems near Taiwan, escalating tensions with China, while U.S. F-22s were deployed to Israel amid rising tensions with Iran. In Africa, high-level diplomatic visits aimed at forging alliances highlighted regional security dynamics, while the UN condemned atrocities in Sudan and warned of a severe hunger crisis in Somalia.

  • SOUTHCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that the death of Nemesio “El Mencho” Oseguera Cervantes, leader of the Jalisco New Generation Cartel (CJNG), on 24 October marked a significant turning point in Mexico's ongoing battle against organized crime. U.S. intelligence played a critical role in the operation that led to his death, which resulted in violent retaliatory actions by CJNG, particularly in Jalisco state, compelling over 26,000 Canadians to shelter in place and resulting in flight cancellations in tourist areas like Puerto Vallarta. In the aftermath, Mexican President Claudia Sheinbaum assured the public of safety for the upcoming FIFA World Cup, despite concerns raised by ongoing cartel-related violence. Multiple governments, including Canada and Ghana, issued travel advisories citing high risks due to crime. The situation prompted calls for legal action against Elon Musk after his allegations that Sheinbaum’s administration was influenced by drug cartels. The power vacuum left by El Mencho may lead to increased infighting among criminal organizations, further complicating the security landscape in the region.

  • EUCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated significant military and geopolitical developments related to the ongoing conflict between Russia and Ukraine. Ukrainian troops relied heavily on drones for combat operations despite the potential effectiveness of other weaponry. President Volodymyr Zelenskyy announced that a Ukrainian delegation would meet American envoys in Geneva on 26 October to discuss a possible postwar recovery plan and prepare for upcoming trilateral talks with Russia. NATO Secretary General commended Ukraine and highlighted the fourth anniversary of Russia's invasion during a ceremony at NATO headquarters in Brussels. Russia claimed to have liberated the settlement of Grafskoye in the Kharkov region. Meanwhile, Hungary heightened military readiness in response to perceived threats of Ukrainian attacks and ordered troops to secure critical infrastructure. The U.S. abstained from a UN vote regarding an anti-Russia resolution, while Russian officials, including Dmitry Medvedev, warned of potential nuclear retaliation should NATO supply Ukraine with nuclear arms.

  • PACOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that Japan planned to deploy surface-to-air missile systems on one of its remote islands near Taiwan by March 2031, escalating tensions with China. Israeli President Isaac Herzog visited Addis Ababa, Ethiopia, following high-level delegations from Turkiye and Saudi Arabia, as discussions emerged regarding potential alliances in the region. In the Philippines, a U.S. military plane crashed into a concrete barrier during a training exercise, injuring all five personnel on board. Moreover, Russian and Iranian oil producers significantly reduced prices to secure market share in China after India scaled back its Russian crude imports. Reports confirmed that a Philippine Navy official denied claims of a Chinese military base being constructed in Scarborough Shoal within the West Philippine Sea.

  • CENTCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated significant military and geopolitical developments. The U.S. Air Force deployed approximately a dozen F-22 stealth fighters to Israel, emphasizing a bolstered military presence in the region amid rising tensions with Iran. President Donald Trump delivered a State of the Union address on 5 February, warning Iran and expressing a preference for diplomatic resolutions despite a hardline stance. In related developments, the Australian government ordered the families of diplomats to evacuate from Israel and Lebanon due to fears of escalating conflict, while India’s Prime Minister Narendra Modi visited Israel to discuss enhancing security and economic ties, which analysts suggested could put pressure on Pakistan's diplomatic efforts. Violent incidents also occurred, including the wounding of four Palestinians in Hebron, West Bank, following attacks by Israeli settlers, as well as deadly clashes in Latakia, Syria, involving pro-Assad forces. Furthermore, Israel issued a warning to Lebanon regarding severe strikes should Hezbollah engage in any conflict with the U.S.

  • AFRICOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated several key military and geopolitical developments. Israeli President Isaac Herzog visited Addis Ababa, Ethiopia, amid efforts to engage the country in potential alliances against common adversaries, following recent high-level delegations from Turkiye and Saudi Arabia. In Sudan, the United Nations Security Council condemned atrocities in the Kordofan region, calling for an end to the ongoing conflict marked by mass killings and attacks on aid workers. South African President Cyril Ramaphosa expressed gratitude to Russian President Vladimir Putin for assisting in the repatriation of 11 South African men involved in the Russia-Ukraine conflict. The UN also warned of a severe hunger crisis in Somalia, affecting 6.5 million individuals due to a prolonged drought. Concurrently, the U.S. Africa Command launched Exercise Justified Accord 2026 in Djibouti, Kenya, and Tanzania, reinforcing military cooperation and readiness in the region.
