Intent: The intent of this report is to provide a high-level overview of significant military and geopolitical developments during the reporting period. The report consists of open-source reporting and updates on evolving conflicts, diplomatic initiatives, and strategic military actions involving countries of interest in the following Combatant Commands: SOUTHCOM, EUCOM, PACOM, CENTCOM, AFRICOM.

Executive Summary: During the reporting period, significant military and geopolitical developments occurred, particularly involving U.S.-Iran relations and regional escalations. U.S. and Israeli air strikes against Iran intensified following the assassination of Iranian Supreme Leader Ayatollah Ali Khamenei, leading to Iranian missile retaliations targeting U.S. and Israeli interests across the region and the death of four American service members. The Iranian Revolutionary Guard Corps announced the closure of the Strait of Hormuz, a vital route for global oil supplies, prompting maritime disruptions and concerns over rising oil prices. In Ukraine, a Russian strike resulted in casualties while Latvia's Foreign Minister emphasized Russia's lack of peace negotiation intent. Additionally, Belgian forces intercepted an oil tanker linked to Russia's illicit operations, contributing to heightened geopolitical tensions.

  • SOUTHCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that the killing of notorious drug kingpin Nemesio “El Mencho” Oseguera Cervantes during a precision military raid in Jalisco, Mexico sparked retaliatory violence in Mexico. Following El Mencho’s death, concerns arose regarding the impact on tourism in Mexico, particularly in popular destinations like Puerto Vallarta, where Canadian airline regulators announced an investigation into recent flight disruptions. Reports highlighted that El Mencho maintained detailed records of bribes paid to local, state, and federal law enforcement, demonstrating extensive corruption. As the situation unfolded, discussions among bishops from the Texas-Mexico border addressed their pastoral roles amid the escalating violence. The ongoing influence of El Mencho's cartel in the United States persisted, raising alerts about narcotics trafficking continuity despite his removal.

  • EUCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that fighting in the Middle East intensified despite an Olympic truce, with significant flight disruptions occurring as athletes and families arrived in Italy for the Winter Paralympic Games. The U.S. and Israel carried out air attacks against Iran, prompting American citizens in the region, particularly in Turkey, to seek safe evacuation. Latvia’s Foreign Minister noted that Russian President Vladimir Putin showed no genuine intent to negotiate peace in Ukraine, advocating for sustained pressure on Moscow. Belgian forces seized an oil tanker believed to be part of Russia’s illicit operations, while a Russian strike in Dnipropetrovsk, Ukraine, resulted in one death and several injuries. The geopolitical climate worsened as the U.S.-Iran conflict escalated, threatening significant disruptions in global oil and gas markets, while thousands of flights faced cancellations due to ongoing air strikes in the Middle East.

  • PACOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated significant military and geopolitical developments affecting global oil and security dynamics. The Iranian Revolutionary Guard Corps (IRGC) announced the closure of the Strait of Hormuz, a critical maritime corridor for 20-30 percent of global oil and gas supplies, prompting British and other vessels to turn back. Rising tensions in the region prompted warnings of increased freight and insurance costs, alongside a potential spike in oil prices. Concurrently, Israel conducted strikes in Tehran, and the Iranian paramilitary forces pledged to launch intensified operations against U.S. and Israeli interests. Chinese People's Liberation Army naval and air units engaged in readiness patrols around Huangyan Dao, signaling increased military activity in the South China Sea. The international community expressed concerns over Japan's military initiatives, cautioning against neo-militarism. Despite these developments, Indian officials reported sufficient oil stocks to mitigate immediate supply disruptions.

  • CENTCOM: During the reporting period, Israel and the United States conducted additional airstrikes, targeting residual Iranian ballistic missile sites, air defense systems, fighter aircraft at bases including Tabriz (destroying one F-4 and two F-5 jets) and Shiraz, mobile radars at IRGC facilities in Zahedan, and central Tehran command structures to maintain air superiority and degrade retaliatory capabilities following the prior elimination of Supreme Leader Ayatollah Ali Khamenei. Iran launched multiple waves of ballistic missiles and drones toward Israel—causing impacts in Jerusalem and Tel Aviv areas with civilian casualties reported—and directed strikes at U.S. military installations across the Gulf, including Al Udeid in Qatar, Al Dhafra in the UAE, Fifth Fleet headquarters in Bahrain, facilities in Kuwait (with apparent hits on the U.S. Embassy compound and friendly-fire incidents downing three American F-15E aircraft), Jordan, Iraq, and Saudi Arabia, supplemented by Hezbollah rocket and drone attacks on Israeli targets in retaliation for Khamenei's death. U.S. Central Command assessed that successful intercepts limited damage to bases with no operational disruptions from direct Iranian strikes, but confirmed four American service members killed in action overall (including one who succumbed to wounds from initial attacks) and additional serious injuries during the campaign.
