Executive Summary: During the reporting period, significant geopolitical updates occurred across various regions. In Mexico, violence surged after the death of Jalisco Cartel leader El Mencho on 22 February, leading to a wave of retaliatory attacks, resulting in at least 26 fatalities and prompting U.S. and Canadian embassies to issue shelter-in-place orders. In Europe, Russia conducted extensive missile and drone strikes in Ukraine, causing civilian casualties, while internal pressures grew on Ukrainian President Zelensky to sustain military efforts. The EU faced divisions over sanctions against Russia, with Hungary blocking a new package tied to oil pipeline conditions. In Asia, Philippine Senator Lacson asserted territorial sovereignty in discussions on the West Philippine Sea, amidst shifting dynamics as U.S. tariffs on China faced legal challenges. CENTCOM reported heightened tensions in the Middle East, with ongoing humanitarian issues in Gaza and operations by U.S. forces in Syria amid escalating violence in Lebanon. Lastly, Chad indefinitely closed its border with Sudan due to security concerns stemming from the latter's civil conflict.

  • SOUTHCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated significant violence erupted across Mexico following the death of Nemesio Rubén Oseguera Cervantes, known as "El Mencho," leader of the Jalisco New Generation Cartel. The Mexican military, with assistance from U.S. intelligence, killed El Mencho in a raid in the western state of Jalisco on 22 February. In response, cartel members initiated a series of retaliatory attacks that swept through more than a dozen states, resulting in the burning of vehicles, blocked highways, and violence in major cities such as Guadalajara and Puerto Vallarta. Reports indicated at least 26 fatalities, including members of the security forces, as chaos ensued, prompting both the U.S. and Canadian embassies to issue "shelter in place" orders for tourists. Authorities postponed several soccer matches, and American citizens found themselves stranded as airlines canceled flights amid the unrest. President Claudia Sheinbaum encouraged citizens to remain calm while the situation unfolded, which many feared would escalate further in the days to come.

  • EUCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that Russia continued to feel the economic and social consequences of its war against Ukraine, now in its fourth year. On 23 October, Russia launched large-scale missile and drone attacks across various Ukrainian regions, resulting in casualties, including the deaths of three civilians. The Ukrainian president, Volodymyr Zelensky, remained resolute, asserting Ukraine's commitment to continue fighting "for another three years" despite facing increasing internal pressures. Meanwhile, Russian military intelligence accused Britain of orchestrating an assassination attempt on a Russian general linked to the poisoning of Sergei Skripal, showcasing the escalating rhetoric between the two nations. Additionally, Hungary blocked the 20th package of EU sanctions against Russia, pivoting on the re-opening of the Druzhba oil pipeline as a condition, which caused friction within the EU. In geopolitical analyses, some Finnish politicians proposed a reversion of NATO to its 1997 borders as a means to establish lasting peace in Ukraine. Meanwhile, over 1,000 individuals from various African nations, including Kenya, were reportedly fighting for Russia, many allegedly recruited under false pretenses.

  • PACOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that Senator Panfilo Lacson of the Philippines reaffirmed that any discussions with the Chinese Embassy regarding the West Philippine Sea (WPS) would not result in compromises of Philippine territory. Similarly, the Supreme Court of the United States invalidated former President Donald Trump's tariffs, enhancing China’s negotiating position ahead of a leaders’ summit planned for April, where Beijing is expected to seek concessions on Taiwan. Additionally, Chancellor Friedrich Merz of Germany embarked on a significant inaugural visit to China, reflecting Germany's aim to establish new global partnerships following the perceived retreat of U.S. influence.

  • CENTCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that European Union diplomats convened in Brussels for discussions with the director of the Board of Peace regarding the future of Gaza. Reports confirmed that a Palestinian child died after Israel denied permission for medical evacuation through the Rafah crossing, underscoring ongoing restrictions affecting thousands. India's Prime Minister Narendra Modi's planned visit to Israel sparked protests back in India, highlighting tensions as India realigned its diplomatic stance towards Israel. Meanwhile, Hamas condemned Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu's proposal for a regional alliance, labeling it as a threat to Arab interests. U.S. military forces initiated a withdrawal from a key base in northeastern Syria following the control of the region by Syrian forces. Additionally, violence escalated in Lebanon, with Hezbollah officials stating they had no choice but to defend themselves after Israeli strikes resulted in multiple fatalities. Iran condemned remarks by the U.S. ambassador to Israel endorsing expansionist policies, reiterating its commitment to defending itself against perceived aggressions.

  • AFRICOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that Chad closed its border with Sudan indefinitely on 24 October, citing incursions by armed groups from Sudan's civil war as a measure to safeguard its citizens. The border remained shut due to repeated violations stemming from conflict in Sudan. In South Africa, former President Robert Mugabe's son faced attempted murder charges following a shooting incident in Johannesburg. Meanwhile, a large recruitment of individuals from Kenya and 36 other African nations into the Russia-Ukraine conflict continued, with reports suggesting many were misled into participating.
