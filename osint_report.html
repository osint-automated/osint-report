Executive Summary: During the reporting period, significant military and geopolitical developments emerged across multiple regions. Venezuelan authorities released French national Julien Fevrier, further complicating diplomatic relations with France. In Ecuador, the withdrawal of the ambassador from Colombia following inflammatory remarks marked heightened interstate tensions. Ukraine demonstrated tactical proficiency by neutralizing Russian military capabilities and participating in multinational discussions to enhance maritime defense, while fatality reports revealed substantial losses among African mercenaries in the conflict. In East Asia, Japan scrambled fighter jets in response to aggressive PLA maneuvers, as China's military conducted nuclear simulations in preparation for potential conflicts in the Taiwan Strait. The situation in the Middle East escalated following Israel's extensive airstrikes against Hezbollah, which prompted Iran to disrupt oil shipments through the Strait of Hormuz. Additionally, ongoing violence in Sudan and Nigeria showcased the deteriorating security landscape, with increased drone strikes and militant attacks resulting in significant civilian casualties. The United Nations continued efforts to facilitate dialogue amid escalating conflicts, highlighting the urgent need for diplomatic resolutions in volatile regions.

  • SOUTHCOM: During the reporting period, Venezuelan authorities released French national Julien Fevrier after 15 months of detention on 8 April, prompting French Foreign Ministry confirmation of his return. Ecuador withdrew its ambassador from Colombia on 8 April following critical remarks on former Vice President Jorge Glas, heightening interstate diplomatic frictions. Argentine legislators approved a bill authorizing mining in glacier zones on 9 April, which demonstrators contested amid environmental concerns. Colombian Army units engaged Jaime Martínez dissidents in Valle del Cauca clashes involving drones on 8 April, resulting in soldier injuries. Mexican Federal Forces repelled a Jalisco New Generation Cartel assault in Michoacán on 8 April, securing a substantial weapons cache. No large-scale military operations or border incidents transpired elsewhere in Central or South America.

  • EUCOM: During the reporting period, Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelenskyy praised the US-Iran ceasefire for enabling diplomatic pathways and urged Russian forces to cease strikes on Ukrainian infrastructure while affirming Kyiv's readiness for reciprocal de-escalation. Zelenskyy announced Ukraine's participation in multinational discussions on the Strait of Hormuz, wherein Ukrainian specialists proffered Black Sea operational expertise to bolster collective maritime defense capabilities. Ukrainian defense units established new benchmarks for neutralizing Russian military personnel and materiel, thereby inducing operational instability across Russian command structures. Independent monitors disclosed that fatalities among African mercenaries recruited by Russian President Vladimir Putin surpassed official disclosures, with Cameroon confirming 16 deaths but analysts estimating at least 94 Cameroonian and over 300 total African losses in Ukrainian theaters. Ukrainian precision strikes targeted Russian oil export facilities, enforcing economic sanctions and curtailing petroleum revenues that sustained Moscow's war effort. Putin extended matériel assistance to Madagascar's military hierarchy, advancing Russian geopolitical leverage in Africa amid protracted conflict dynamics. NATO's senior command credited Ukrainian engagements with refining alliance protocols for drone-centric and hybrid warfare training scenarios.

  • PACOM: During the reporting period, Japan's Air Self-Defense Force scrambled F-15 fighters from the Southwest Air Defense Command to intercept People's Liberation Army aircraft approaching East China Sea airspace on 8 April. People's Liberation Army Eastern Theater Command conducted nuclear response simulations oriented toward the Taiwan Strait and Japan-facing directions. Assessments identified over 100 PLA Navy warships executing large-scale gray-zone armada operations in the Western Pacific. Beijing reserved extensive offshore airspace for 40 days starting early second-quarter 2026 to support elevated PLA operational activities.

  • CENTCOM: During the reporting period, Israel launched over 100 airstrikes against Hezbollah targets in Lebanon on 8 April, striking residential and commercial areas in Beirut and two key border crossings, which killed 112 civilians and militants including a Hezbollah leader's secretary. Iran responded by closing the Strait of Hormuz to tankers, halting oil shipments and announcing alternative shipping routes amid sea mine risks. Netanyahu declared Israel's readiness to resume full-scale operations at any moment, while US forces maintained positions encircling Iran until final negotiations concluded. Trump affirmed the two-week ceasefire brokered via Pakistan held tentatively despite discrepancies in terms, as Iran formalised new Hormuz tolls and the US paused energy infrastructure attacks. Hezbollah fired retaliatory drones toward Israel, intercepted without casualties, and Gulf states reported minor missile interceptions linked to Iranian proxies.

  • AFRICOM: During the reporting period, Rapid Support Forces conducted a drone strike on the Chadian border town of Tine on 8 April, killing 17 civilians as Sudan's civil war spilled over. Boko Haram and Islamic State West Africa Province launched simultaneous assaults on Maiduguri in Nigeria on 8 April. Unknown actors executed a drone strike on Kutum in North Darfur, Sudan, killing 12 civilians including six children. Sudanese Armed Forces and Rapid Support Forces intensified drone and loitering munition attacks on civilian infrastructure in Darfur and Kordofan, contributing to 480-500 fatalities since January. United Nations Personal Envoy Pekka Haavisto met Rapid Support Forces commander Mohamed Hamdan Dagalo in Nairobi on 8 April to pursue de-escalation pathways. James Swan assumed command of MONUSCO in the Democratic Republic of Congo on 8 April to revitalize peace initiatives amid eastern insurgencies. Kenyan security forces dismantled an Al-Shabaab logistics network in Nairobi's Eastleigh district on 8 April, confiscating camouflage uniforms and apparel. Ethiopian federal police arrested human trafficking ringleader Yetbarek Dawit and nine associates in Shire on 8 April for orchestrating Libya-bound operations involving torture and deaths. A Yale University analysis disclosed Ethiopian military provision of sanctuary and support to Rapid Support Forces. Zimbabwean opposition parties condemned Zanu-PF's constitutional amendments on 8 April, which sought to eliminate presidential elections and prolong incumbency terms. Central African Republic authorities released detained aid worker Joseph Figueira Martin on 8 April after nearly two years on espionage charges.

    Executive Summary: During the reporting period, regional combatant commands maintained generally stable operational postures amid localized kinetic activity, with SOUTHCOM supporting Ecuadorian counter-narcotics enforcement operations that interdicted a semi-submersible vessel and reinforced curfew compliance, while diplomatic tensions between Guyana and Venezuela persisted without escalation; EUCOM observed intensified but contained Russia-Ukraine hostilities characterized by limited missile and drone strikes, sustained ground engagements without territorial change, and reciprocal long-range strikes impacting logistics nodes; PACOM recorded routine PLA air and maritime activity within expected parameters across the Taiwan Strait and South/East China Seas, with continued gray-zone operations absent escalation; CENTCOM monitored continued Israel-Iran exchanges involving precision strikes and ballistic missile retaliation, with effective Israeli missile defense mitigating impacts and Iran sustaining maritime disruption operations in the Strait of Hormuz; and AFRICOM reported ongoing low- to միջ-intensity conflicts and counterterrorism operations across Sudan, the DRC, and Algeria, with no significant regional escalations or shifts in force posture across broader African theaters.

    • SOUTHCOM: During the reporting period, Ecuadorian armed forces, with integrated U.S. Southern Command reconnaissance and logistics support, enforced the newly imposed nighttime curfew across four coastal provinces through joint helicopter and riverine patrols that interdicted one semi-submersible vessel carrying 1.2 metric tons of cocaine hydrochloride precursor chemicals, resulting in six detentions without exchange of fire. Guyana’s Ministry of Foreign Affairs transmitted a formal diplomatic note to the United Nations and the International Court of Justice reiterating sovereign rights over the 25,000-square-kilometer seismic exploration block in its Exclusive Economic Zone and rejecting Venezuela’s latest objection as inconsistent with the 1899 arbitral award. Venezuelan naval units conducted a routine presence patrol near the disputed maritime boundary line but initiated no approaches or challenges to Guyanese-flagged vessels. No additional kinetic operations against narco-terrorist infrastructure, border incidents, or interstate military movements occurred across Mexico, Colombia, or other Central/South American theaters, though monitoring of post-intervention stabilization in Venezuela continued under the Americas Counter-Cartel Coalition framework.

    • EUCOM: During the reporting period, Russian forces executed a combined strike package of one Iskander-M ballistic missile and 148 Shahed-type drones against Ukrainian energy infrastructure and rear-area logistics hubs in Kharkiv, Dnipropetrovsk, and Mykolaiv oblasts, with Ukrainian air defenses downing 131 drones and the remaining impacts causing localized power outages and minor damage to two transformer substations without civilian fatalities reported in the interval. Ukrainian forces responded with long-range drone strikes on Russian oil storage facilities at Tuapse and a command node near Belgorod, igniting secondary fires that disrupted southern logistics flows for 18 hours according to satellite imagery assessments. Ground combat intensified along the Pokrovsk axis with 137 reported engagements, where Russian motorized rifle units supported by FPV drones attempted limited advances near Shevchenko but Ukrainian defensive positions held firm without territorial changes, while localized Ukrainian counterattacks in the Vuhledar sector inflicted attrition on Russian armor. Ukrainian General Staff estimates placed Russian personnel losses at 1,020 alongside destruction of 42 artillery pieces, nine armored fighting vehicles, and over 1,800 unmanned systems. No large-scale missile barrages, naval operations in the Black Sea, or diplomatic developments tied to sanctions waivers emerged in the 24-hour window.

    • PACOM: During the reporting period, the People's Liberation Army Air Force executed routine incursions into Taiwan's Air Defense Identification Zone, with Taiwan's Ministry of National Defense detecting six PLA aircraft operating around the island from 6:00 a.m. Tuesday to 6:00 a.m. Wednesday, of which four crossed the Taiwan Strait median line and penetrated northern and southwestern ADIZ sectors in apparent response to a concurrent U.S. Navy P-8A transit. PLA Navy surface combatants maintained standard patrol density in the Taiwan Strait and adjacent waters without increases in platform count, aggressive maneuvering, or extensions beyond the First Island Chain. Chinese maritime militia assets continued gray-zone operations through coordinated fishing vessel formations in the East China Sea, sustaining presence near disputed zones without fresh large-scale clustering or direct interference with commercial shipping. In the South China Sea, PLA Southern Theater Command units persisted in passive monitoring of U.S. and allied naval movements without new live-fire drills, major surface deployments, or incidents involving Philippine or Vietnamese EEZ claims. No ballistic missile tests, carrier strike group sorties, or joint exercises with external partners occurred in the Pacific theater during the interval.

    • CENTCOM: During the reporting period, Israeli Air Force assets executed follow-on precision airstrikes against remaining IRGC ballistic missile assembly sites and command bunkers in southern Tehran suburbs, producing targeted secondary detonations that further degraded launch infrastructure without reported additional civilian casualties beyond residual shrapnel effects from prior Quds Day engagements. Iranian Revolutionary Guard Corps forces responded with a limited barrage of two dozen medium-range ballistic missiles—including Kheibar Shekan and Sejjil variants—directed at Israeli military logistics hubs near Tel Aviv and Dimona, though Israeli Arrow-3 and David's Sling systems intercepted all but three projectiles that caused localized infrastructure damage. Iranian naval patrol vessels sustained full enforcement of the Strait of Hormuz closure protocol, boarding and diverting three additional commercial tankers while maintaining Brent crude prices above $105 per barrel. U.S. Central Command assets conducted defensive patrols in the northern Arabian Sea in support of Israeli operations, with no new Marine deployments or ground incursions reported. Iranian state media confirmed ongoing power grid instability in affected Tehran districts and acknowledged minor injuries to senior IRGC personnel, while Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth reiterated prior assessments of Supreme Leader Mojtaba Khamenei's condition from initial strike waves. No proxy force activations, nuclear site engagements, or large-scale naval surface actions materialized in the interval.

    • AFRICOM: During the reporting period, Sudanese Armed Forces conducted follow-on drone strikes against Rapid Support Forces supply nodes in West Kordofan, destroying additional fuel depots and causing at least nine civilian fatalities near market infrastructure while Rapid Support Forces artillery units returned fire in South Kordofan without producing territorial shifts. Democratic Republic of Congo Armed Forces executed targeted combat drone operations against M23 rebel positions in the Goma sector, resulting in one additional UN-affiliated aid worker death and two civilian casualties amid ongoing engagements near resource extraction zones. South African National Defence Force units sustained the 2,200-troop domestic deployment in Johannesburg and surrounding districts to interdict organized crime and illegal mining networks. Algerian army counter-terrorism elements neutralized six militants in border-area clashes while incurring one soldier fatality. No major escalations occurred in Sahel jihadist theaters across Mali, Burkina Faso, or Niger, nor in Somali operations against Al-Shabaab or Ethiopian border tensions, though monitoring persisted for potential spillover from persistent regional instability.
