Open Source Intelligence (OSINT) Report

Date of Access: 10 March 2026

Date of Information: 10 March 2026

Reporting Period: 09 March - 10 March 2026

Distribution: Approved for public release; distribution is unlimited.

Combatant Commands: SOUTHCOM, EUCOM, PACOM, CENTCOM, AFRICOM

Intent: The intent of this report is to provide a high-level overview of significant military and geopolitical developments during the reporting period. The report consists of open-source reporting and updates on evolving conflicts, diplomatic initiatives, and strategic military actions involving countries of interest in the following Combatant Commands: SOUTHCOM, EUCOM, PACOM, CENTCOM, AFRICOM.

Executive Summary: During the reporting period, significant military and geopolitical developments unfolded across various regions. Joint Task Force Southern Spear conducted a lethal maritime strike in the Eastern Pacific on 8 March that destroyed a narco-trafficking vessel operated by designated terrorist organizations and killed six combatants, while U.S. forces continued advisory cooperation with Ecuador against criminal groups disrupting cocaine flows through regional ports. In the European theater, Ukrainian forces executed localized counterattacks in western Zaporizhia Oblast that pushed Russian units from several forward positions and conducted drone strikes against Russian air defense infrastructure in occupied Crimea, while Russia launched large missile and drone barrages against Ukrainian civilian and energy targets and claimed limited gains in eastern Donetsk amid continued attritional operations; both sides also completed a 500-per-side prisoner exchange. In the Indo-Pacific, China emphasized diplomatic engagement with the United States, condemned ongoing U.S.-Israeli strikes against Iran, and announced a 7 percent defense budget increase while avoiding direct military confrontations in regional flashpoints. In the U.S. Central Command theater, U.S. and Israeli forces intensified strikes against Iranian regime infrastructure, missile systems, and proxy elements, while Iran conducted retaliatory missile and drone attacks against Israel and several Gulf states amid leadership transition following the elevation of Mojtaba Khamenei and escalating rhetoric signaling prolonged conflict. In the Africa Command area, South Sudanese forces prepared an offensive in Jonglei state prompting civilian evacuations, regional leaders condemned a jihadist attack that killed 15 soldiers in northern Benin, and international actors reiterated concern over ceasefire violations in eastern Democratic Republic of Congo, while other regional theaters remained operationally static despite broader geopolitical spillover risks.

  • SOUTHCOM: During the reporting period, U.S. Southern Command executed a lethal kinetic strike on March 8 in the Eastern Pacific under Operation Southern Spear, where Joint Task Force Southern Spear targeted and destroyed a vessel operated by Designated Terrorist Organizations along known narco-trafficking routes, resulting in the deaths of six male narco-terrorists with no U.S. military casualties reported. The command continued joint advisory and support operations with Ecuadorian forces against groups such as Los Lobos and Los Choneros as part of ongoing efforts to disrupt cocaine flows through Ecuadorian ports, though no new kinetic actions materialized on March 9 or 10. Trinidad and Tobago's Prime Minister Kamla Persad-Bissessar visited SOUTHCOM headquarters on March 9 to discuss bilateral defense cooperation and regional security alignment within the newly established Americas Counter-Cartel Coalition. No major escalations, territorial changes, or new incidents occurred in other Central or South American flashpoints, including the Guyana-Venezuela Essequibo dispute (which remained static pending ICJ proceedings), Venezuela's post-Maduro stabilization, or cartel-related violence in Mexico, Colombia, or elsewhere; the focus persisted on implementing the coalition framework formalized at the March 7 Shield of the Americas summit, with emphasis on intelligence sharing, border security, and countering transnational criminal networks amid broader U.S. hemispheric priorities.

  • EUCOM: During the reporting period, Ukrainian forces conducted successful counterattacks primarily in western Zaporizhia Oblast and southern sectors, pushing Russian units out of positions near Prymorske, Novoyakolivka, northern Lukyanivske, and Pavlivka south of Zaporizhzhia City while halting Russian advances near Orikhiv and in the Richne-Prymorske direction, forcing elements of the Russian 58th Combined Arms Army to shift to defensive postures and disrupting potential operational linkages between the Hulyaipole and Orikhiv axes. Ukrainian drone operators struck Russian assets, including a 64N6 Nadgorbok radar station in occupied Crimea that guided S-300PM and S-400 systems. Russia launched combined missile and drone barrages overnight, firing Iskander-M ballistic missiles and over 100 drones (including Shahed variants) that struck energy and civilian infrastructure in regions such as Kharkiv and Chernihiv, with Ukrainian defenses downing the majority but allowing some impacts that caused casualties and damage. Russian forces claimed marginal territorial gains in eastern Donetsk Oblast, including near Ryznykivka and other gray-zone areas, amid continued grinding pressure in directions like Pokrovsk and Kostyantynivka-Druzhkivka, though overall progress remained slow. Both sides completed the second phase of a large prisoner-of-war exchange, repatriating an additional 300 personnel each to conclude a 500-per-side swap initiated earlier in the week. Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelenskyy visited eastern frontline positions to assess troop morale and operations while postponing trilateral U.S.-Russia-Ukraine peace talks due to diplomatic focus on the concurrent U.S.-Israeli conflict with Iran; no major shifts occurred in northern Kharkiv or Sumy Oblast, where Russian efforts stalled without advances.

  • PACOM: During the reporting period, China expressed hope that 2026 would become a landmark year for bilateral relations with the United States, as Foreign Minister Wang Yi stated during a press briefing on the sidelines of the National People's Congress annual session, highlighting high-level leader exchanges as a strategic guarantee for managing differences and promoting mutual respect, peaceful coexistence, and win-win cooperation. China condemned ongoing U.S.-Israeli military operations against Iran as unacceptable violations of sovereignty, regime-change efforts, and breaches of international law, with officials urging an immediate cessation of strikes, protection of shipping through the Strait of Hormuz, prevention of escalation, and resumption of dialogue through diplomatic outreach to regional and global parties. Beijing reaffirmed its commitment to multi-level engagement with the United States, including preparations for mid-March trade negotiations in Paris and the anticipated Trump-Xi summit in Beijing at the end of March, while pledging measures to safeguard Chinese nationals and energy security amid conflict spillover risks to oil and LNG supplies. China announced a 7 percent increase in its 2026 defense budget during the Two Sessions to support military modernization and advanced combat capabilities amid strategic rivalry. No direct U.S.-China military incidents, confrontations, exercises, or developments occurred in the Indo-Pacific, Taiwan Strait, or South China Sea domains.

  • CENTCOM: During the reporting period, U.S. and Israeli forces escalated airstrikes on Iranian targets, with Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth announcing that March 10 marked the most intense day of U.S. operations since the conflict began, deploying the highest number of fighters and bombers to hit regime infrastructure in Tehran and other provinces, including command centers, remaining missile sites, and energy-related assets amid reports of explosions and fires in the capital. Israel conducted additional waves against Hezbollah in Beirut's southern suburbs and southern Lebanon, targeting command posts and weapons storage in response to rocket and drone launches toward northern Israel. Iran launched fresh missile and drone barrages toward Israel, with strikes hitting central areas including Tel Aviv and causing civilian casualties and injuries, while retaliatory salvos targeted Gulf states such as Qatar, UAE, Bahrain, and Saudi Arabia, though intercepts limited many impacts; Iran's IRGC rejected U.S. claims of destroyed missile capabilities and vowed to deploy heavier projectiles with over-one-ton warheads. Iran named Mojtaba Khamenei as the new supreme leader following the earlier death of Ali Khamenei, prompting public rallies in Tehran and hardline statements that Iran—not the U.S. or Israel—would determine the war's end, with officials warning of readiness for prolonged conflict and rejecting negotiations after prior "bitter experiences." President Trump delivered mixed signals, describing the campaign as "very complete" and "far ahead of schedule" in some statements while emphasizing decisive defeat of the enemy on U.S. terms and threatening overwhelming retaliation if Iran disrupted Strait of Hormuz shipping; cumulative casualties included over 1,300 deaths in Iran, around a dozen in Israel from projectiles, and hundreds in Lebanon from related operations, with seven confirmed U.S. service member fatalities from regional strikes and no major new nuclear site targeting reported as focus remained on degrading missile, drone, and leadership elements.

  • AFRICOM: During the reporting period, South Sudan People's Defense Forces issued an evacuation order to UNMISS, NGOs, and civilians in opposition-held Akobo town in Jonglei state, prompting thousands to flee as forces prepared for an imminent offensive on the area and surrounding regions following escalated confrontations since late 2025, with reports of looting at humanitarian facilities including an MSF hospital. The African Union Commission Chairperson condemned a recent jihadist attack in northern Benin near the Niger border that killed 15 soldiers, urging accelerated deployment of the ECOWAS regional brigade under the African Standby Force to counter terrorism and violent extremism in West African border zones. The United States, European Union, and International Contact Group for the Great Lakes reiterated profound concern over continued ceasefire violations in eastern Democratic Republic of Congo involving M23 rebels and associated militias, calling for recommitment to negotiations and cessation of fighting in alignment with prior Doha and Washington agreements amid ongoing clashes and drone threats. No major new kinetic operations, territorial changes, or direct engagements occurred in Sudan (beyond prior escalations in Kordofan), Somalia against al-Shabaab, Libya, Ethiopia's Tigray region, or other active theaters, though indirect geopolitical concerns persisted regarding spillover from the U.S.-Israeli conflict with Iran, including risks to Red Sea maritime security, Horn of Africa stability, elevated energy prices straining African economies, and potential diversion of counterterrorism resources in the Sahel and elsewhere. The East African Community continued logistical preparations for its ordinary Summit in Arusha without associated military developments reported.
