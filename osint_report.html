Executive Summary: During the reporting period, significant military and geopolitical developments unfolded globally. In Venezuela, U.S. military actions led to the capture of President Nicolás Maduro, drawing condemnation from Russia, which accused the U.S. of violating international law. Concurrently, Iran warned of repercussions against U.S. bases amid escalating protests that resulted in over 2,500 deaths. In Ukraine, the deployment of U.S. missile systems successfully targeted over 20 Russian drones, while Ukrainian leaders addressed severe energy crises stemming from Russian strikes. Additionally, Danish military readiness to defend Greenland was confirmed in response to increased U.S. military presence and tensions over the territory. The U.S. State Department announced a suspension of visa processing for 75 countries amid ongoing unrest in Gaza and humanitarian concerns.

  • SOUTHCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated significant military and geopolitical tensions surrounding U.S. actions in Venezuela and Iran. On 14 January, Russian Foreign Minister Sergei Lavrov accused the United States of fragmenting the global order with its military operation in Venezuela, labeling it a "flagrant violation" of international law. Concurrently, Russia's Roszarubezhneft announced full ownership of Venezuelan oil assets in defiance of U.S. claims to revive the Venezuelan energy sector through Western companies. The U.S. military operation, which resulted in the capture of Venezuelan President Nicolás Maduro, prompted both condemnation and strategic partnership statements from Russia and other nations. Reports also confirmed the release of American detainees in Venezuela, facilitated by a U.S. State Department team. In the context of Iran, Tehran warned that it would strike U.S. bases if military interference occurred during ongoing protests.

  • EUCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that U.S. Vice President JD Vance met with Denmark's foreign minister and Greenlandic officials in Washington, D.C. to discuss the geopolitical implications surrounding Greenland. In Ukraine, the air command reported that a newly deployed American missile-mounted vehicle, called Tempest, successfully struck over 20 Russian Shahed drones, showcasing tactical advancements in Ukraine's defense capabilities. Russian Foreign Minister Sergei Lavrov condemned U.S. actions in Venezuela and Iran as destabilizing, while asserting Russia's openness to future peace talks regarding Ukraine despite rejecting the feasibility of a ceasefire. Additionally, Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelenskyy announced intentions to declare a state of emergency in the energy sector following intensified Russian strikes that left citizens in Kyiv enduring extreme cold without power or water. Russia reportedly aimed missile strikes at a power plant in Kharkiv, resulting in casualties and further straining Ukraine's already vulnerable energy infrastructure.

  • PACOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that densely populated river deltas, including those of the Nile and Amazon, sank faster than global sea levels rose, threatening 236 million residents with increased flooding risks. Danish military leadership confirmed readiness to defend Greenland, mandating a military response to any U.S. aggression, amidst heightened tensions fueled by former President Donald Trump's insistence on acquiring the territory. In the South China Sea, Chinese officials rejected claims from the Philippine National Maritime Council, asserting that they distorted maritime law and risked escalating conflict in the region. Concurrently, U.S. plans to strengthen its military presence in Greenland surfaced as a strategic move in the Arctic amidst warnings from Danish intelligence regarding Russian and Chinese military objectives.

  • CENTCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated significant military and geopolitical developments globally. The death toll from protests in Iran surpassed 2,500, with President Donald Trump threatening to take "very strong action" if Iran executed detained protesters. Concurrently, Iran utilized military jammers, reportedly supplied by Russia, to shut down access to Starlink Internet, marking a new phase in its digital blackout efforts amidst ongoing unrest. The U.S. military continued its strategic drawdown from Al Udeid Air Base in Qatar amid increasing tensions with Iran, while the U.S. State Department announced a halt on visa processing for 75 nations due to public charge risks. In Gaza, the Trump administration announced the second phase of its peace plan, transitioning from ceasefire to demilitarization efforts as concerns over ongoing violence and humanitarian crises persisted.

  • AFRICOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that the U.S. State Department announced the suspension of immigrant visa processing for 75 countries, including Somalia, Iran, Russia, Afghanistan, Nigeria, Yemen, Thailand, and Brazil, effective 21 January 2026. This decision aimed to address concerns over applicants potentially becoming public charges. Separately, reports surfaced regarding actions taken by U.S. Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) agents in Minnesota, where they allegedly inquired about the locations of Asian residents, aligning with Vice President JD Vance's directive for door-to-door immigration enforcement. In the context of the African continent, Burkina Faso dismissed national football team coach Brama Traore after a disappointing exit from the Africa Cup of Nations, while serious concerns grew over the deteriorating security situation in the Sahel region, where jihadist groups increasingly threatened national sovereignty.
