Intent: The intent of this report is to provide a high-level overview of significant military and geopolitical developments during the reporting period. The report consists of open-source reporting and updates on evolving conflicts, diplomatic initiatives, and strategic military actions involving countries of interest in the following Combatant Commands: SOUTHCOM, EUCOM, PACOM, CENTCOM, AFRICOM.

Executive Summary: During the reporting period, a Colombian Air Force C-130 Hercules transport aircraft crashed during takeoff in Putumayo province, resulting in 66 soldier fatalities, the deadliest aviation disaster in recent Colombian military history. U.S. Southern Command deployed the USS Nimitz aircraft carrier and USS Gridley destroyer for joint military exercises with Ecuador and regional allies, prioritizing counter-narcotics operations in Ecuador. On 24 March, Russian forces executed a significant drone and missile attack against Ukraine, resulting in civilian casualties and infrastructure damage while Ukrainian forces launched counterattacks and strikes in Russian territory. In the Pacific, China enhanced its undersea operations, challenging Philippine vessels and condemning Japan's missile upgrades. In the Middle East, the U.S. redirected approximately 2000 soldiers from the 82nd Airborne Division and faced escalating hostilities between Israel and Iran, with both sides exchanging strikes. Concerns over potential regional conflict surged in the Horn of Africa following Ethiopian advances into Sudan and arms deliveries to Sudan’s Rapid Support Forces by UAE operators, alongside critical fuel shortages in Kenya due to ongoing hostilities.

  • SOUTHCOM: During the reporting period, a Colombian Air Force C-130 Hercules transport aircraft crashed immediately after takeoff from a runway in Putumayo province near the Ecuadorian and Peruvian borders, resulting in 66 soldier fatalities and marking the deadliest aviation disaster in the Colombian military's recent operational history. United States Southern Command announced deployment of the USS Nimitz aircraft carrier and USS Gridley destroyer to the region for joint military exercises with Ecuador and nine allied nations, designating Ecuador as the priority target in counter-narcotics operations. Thousands of Argentinians marched in Buenos Aires and other cities to commemorate the 50th anniversary of the 24 March 1976 military coup that established a seven-year dictatorship responsible for widespread human rights violations and the subsequent Falklands conflict. Mexican naval authorities confirmed the suicide of Lieutenant Commander Gloria Gómez, the first woman to command a Mexican Navy vessel.

  • EUCOM: During the reporting period, Russian forces launched a massive daytime drone and missile barrage against Ukraine on 24 March, deploying over 400 drones and additional missiles in one of the war's largest assaults, which Ukrainian air defenses partially intercepted while the strikes killed seven civilians, wounded at least 50 others, and damaged civilian infrastructure including water systems and buildings in historic city centers. Russian ground units intensified assaults along frontline sectors, capturing minor positions and signaling initiation of spring offensive preparations amid Ukrainian counterattacks in southern directions that forced Russian redeployments of naval infantry divisions. Ukrainian forces conducted strikes on Russian military targets in Belgorod and Bryansk oblasts, targeting command posts and air defense systems. Diplomatically, Ukrainian President Zelenskyy conferred with Latvia's Defense Minister on 24 March to coordinate sustained military support, as Irish parliament debated enhanced aid commitments amid reports of Russian escalation.

  • PACOM: During the reporting period, China intensified undersea domain awareness operations across the Pacific Ocean, deploying research vessels such as Dong Fang Hong 3 to map ocean floors and install sensors near Taiwan, Guam, the Philippines, Hawaii, and chokepoints like the Malacca Strait approaches, enhancing People's Liberation Army Navy submarine warfare capabilities against United States forces. People's Liberation Army warships challenged Philippine coast guard vessels in contested South China Sea waters on 24 March, prompting Manila to accuse Beijing of repeated maritime bullying. Beijing's People's Liberation Army Daily condemned Japan's upgrades to Type-12 surface-to-ship missiles as offensive elements of a 'kill network' amid looming long-range deployments near Chinese waters. Ocean University of China expanded deep-sea observation systems in the South China Sea basin to support these strategic surveys.

  • CENTCOM: During the reporting period, the US Pentagon directed the deployment of approximately 2000 soldiers from the 82nd Airborne Division to the Middle East to enhance military options, while the Trump administration transmitted a 15-point ceasefire proposal to Iran via diplomatic channels. Israel executed multiple airstrikes on Tehran on 25 March, collapsing an eight-story residential building in the Enderzgu district, targeting two gas facilities and a pipeline, and prompting Iranian media reports of 12 killed and 28 wounded. Iran launched several waves of ballistic missiles at Israel overnight into 25 March, striking Tel Aviv and reportedly aiming at the Dimona nuclear facility, which inflicted damage on buildings and vehicles. US President Donald Trump announced a five-day postponement of strikes on Iranian energy infrastructure amid claims of progress in indirect talks, though Iranian officials rejected negotiation assertions and vowed retaliation against Gulf energy and water facilities. US and Israeli forces conducted additional strikes on Iranian military targets in Iraq and Lebanon, sustaining the conflict's intensity without abatement.

  • AFRICOM: During the reporting period, analysts assessed heightened risks of a regional mega-war in the Horn of Africa after Ethiopian forces advanced into Sudanese territory, prompting Sudanese Armed Forces alignments with Eritrean and Tigrayan factions on 24 March. United Arab Emirates operators executed covert cargo flights through Ethiopia and the Central African Republic to deliver arms to Sudan's Rapid Support Forces amid ongoing civil conflict. Kenyan retailers encountered acute fuel shortages attributable to Red Sea shipping disruptions from Middle East hostilities, while South African officials projected petroleum reserves to exhaust by end April 2026. Iranian war spillovers exacerbated continental security vulnerabilities through elevated energy costs and logistical strains across the Sahel and East Africa.
